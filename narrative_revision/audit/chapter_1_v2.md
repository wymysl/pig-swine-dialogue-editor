## Playtest findings

Three findings drive the rewrite:

1. **The first issue feels like a fetch quest.** The original Beat 2-3 structure (Mr. Pig panic → Murrow briefing → Procedural Binder pickup) gives the player legal tools before the player has met or felt the case's stakes. The client is offstage by design in v1; the player works on a case file rather than for a person.
2. **Pacing feels rushed.** Each beat delivers its function and exits. There is no breathing room for character or consequence to register.
3. **Characters appear punch-line-y.** Each NPC is doing a register-job (Pig panics, Murrow explains, Crab gates-then-engages, Whimsy flourishes) and exiting. The phase-4 reframings (theatrical-cynic, leverage-aware, etc.) are right on register but were applied as labels rather than as space to unfold.

Mechanics, world structure, and the legal spine itself were not flagged. The procedural-defect / procedural-reset legal spine remains canonical.

## Decision: rewrite scope

The rewrite addresses all three findings. Two new beats are added; all existing beats receive breathing-room expansion.

**New beats added:**
- Beat 8 — Client meeting at office (N)
- Beat 9 — Archive Room research (N)

**Existing beats expanded (not restructured):**
All beats with a speaking NPC receive *character-dwell* dialogue options in addition to load-bearing options. Character-dwell options have no quest impact; each surfaces a register-revealing line. Pattern set as project-wide standard for Ch2-5 dialogue work.

**Beats unchanged in structure but renumbered downstream by +2:**
Old Beats 1-7 become new Beats 1-7 (no renumbering for the recruitment phase; the new beats slot between recruitment and readiness check). Old Beat 8 (court readiness) becomes new Beat 10 (slimmed; some content moves to Beat 9). Old Beats 9-12 (walk-to-court, court, return-to-office, postcard) become new Beats 11-14.

**New beat count: 14.** Up from canonical 12.

**Beat 5 gate tightening (phase-8 user decision).** The pre-phase-8 canon had Crab's recruitment gate as *binder collected OR Murrow visited* (either-or). Phase-8 tightens to *binder collected AND Murrow visited* (both required). The change makes the chronology cleaner — Cula approaches Crab with concrete case content (Murrow's eviction briefing) AND concrete legal tools (the binder) in hand, not just one or the other. The eviction-pitch register lands cleanly because both halves of the pitch's substantive grounding are present. Phase-7 V1.2 voice-pack drafts that reference the old *either* gate require update during Stage 7. Crab's refusal/engagement line shapes from `bibles/crab.md` interior-range section still apply; the canonical refusal line ("The binder is not in the room. I cannot check service without the binder.") is unaffected — it names binder specifically, which works under either gate logic.

## New plants introduced

Three new load-bearing plants are introduced. Each requires phase-5 re-traversal map registration.

**1. Halina Sikorska as character.**
A 71-year-old retired Polish-language teacher; widowed in 2009; one adult son in Wrocław (rare contact); tenant in a pre-war apartment building in central Warsaw since 1987 with inherited tenancy from a 1962 lease in her father's name. Lives alone. On a teacher's pension that does not quite cover groceries plus utilities plus medication; has been drawing down a small savings account for three years.

Voice register: educated, formal Polish, slightly dated phrasing (a teacher's diction shaped by four decades of reading mid-century literature aloud). Polite but precise. Does not dramatize her situation. Carries herself with dignity even when frightened. Does not weep. Does not plead. Tired in a way that is specific to her age and her situation, not generic. May make literary references in passing that Cula either catches or misses depending on stance.

The wrong-address mechanism: in 2015 the landlord (private investor who acquired the building in the late 1990s and has been trying to clear long-term tenants ever since) renovated common areas and renumbered the units. Halina's flat went from #7 to #5. The eviction notice was served to apartment #7 — the *current* #7, now occupied by a different renter who took the notice, did not know what to do with it, and passed it along to Halina three weeks late, after the ex parte hearing had produced the eviction order. The defect: service to a unit demonstrably renumbered by the landlord himself is bad service. The landlord knew about the renumbering. He authorized it. The notice to #7 was at minimum careless and at maximum strategic.

Appears in new Beat 8 (client meeting) and reappears in Ch4 (corridor sighting at courthouse, ~5 lines exchanged).

**2. The cardiologist plant.**
During Beat 8, in any of the three dialogue stances, Halina mentions in passing that she had to reschedule a cardiology follow-up due to eviction stress. One sentence. No italics, no Cula reaction line, no clutched chest. Phase-7 voice work specifies exact language. The plant pays off in the Ch4 reappearance — by then the cardiac strain has compounded.

**3. The fee plant.**
Halina agrees to a fixed fee of 5,000 PLN. She has the money; she has calculated; she pays. The fee discussion is not a player choice (would open haggling questions that complicate the spine). Cula's *internal reaction* to the figure can vary by stance carried into the meeting — character-dwell texture, not load-bearing branching. Quest flags: `chapter1.client_fee_agreed = true` (set during Beat 8); `chapter1.client_fee_collected = true` (set by Beat 10 readiness check). Beat 13 Pig's celebration references the fee specifically as one of the things being celebrated about (firm-survival framing per v1 audit).

The fee plant compounds the cardiac plant in Ch4: by then, Halina has had her health degrade *and* drawn down 5,000 PLN of savings on a procedural reset that ultimately did not save her tenancy.

Combined: the three new plants converge in Ch4 to deliver the β recoloring — the procedural reset was correct, the legal system functioned as designed, and it was insufficient. The corridor sighting is a duration made visible, not a plot beat.

## Article 135-bis § 2 KPC (fabricated)

The Procedural Binder contains a fabricated KPC article that Murrow narrates during Beat 9 (Archive Room research). Sits plausibly near real KPC Art. 135 (residence service). Drafted text:

> **Art. 135-bis § 2 KPC.** Service is invalid when the address used has been altered, renumbered, or otherwise made non-current to the knowledge of the serving party. A hearing held upon invalid service shall, on motion of the party not properly served, be set aside and rescheduled, provided the motion is filed within fourteen days of actual notice. The appearance of a third party at the served address shall not be considered as curing service unless that party held the express authority of the served party.

The text does three jobs: states the wrong-address rule cleanly; builds the 14-day window into the case's time pressure (Halina is inside the window but barely — three weeks late from #7's occupant means actual notice happened recently); preempts the landlord's likely defense (third-party tenant accepting misdirected notice does not cure).

**Project convention on article numbering.** The rewrite establishes a convention: real KPC article numbers may be cited where their real content fits the case (e.g., Crab's reference to Art. 132 in V1.3 stands); fabricated numbers use the -bis suffix and sit plausibly near real KPC topical neighbors. Mixing real numbers with obviously-implausible ones would feel inconsistent. This convention applies forward to Ch2-5 legal texture.

## Breathing-room expansion: project-wide register standard

**Pattern.** Every NPC scene in Ch1 receives *character-dwell* dialogue options in addition to existing load-bearing options.

**Definitions:**
- *Load-bearing options* — advance the quest or surface evidence. These already exist in the canonical structure.
- *Character-dwell options* — 2–3 per scene, no quest impact, but produce a register-revealing line. Mannerism, worldview, memory, tic.

**Discipline.** Character-dwell is not filler. Each option must produce a line that surfaces something specific about the character. Generic "tell me more" branches that produce paraphrased exposition are scrub-mode failures and should be flagged in phase-7 audit as such.

**Sincerity rule respected.** The "one canonical sincere line per character per chapter" rule (V1.3 §A) is preserved. Character-dwell options must respect register interior range without breaking sincerity rationing. Whimsy can have more space without becoming sincere; the cynic register has interior range. Same for Crab (opportunist has range — bored-professional, mildly-amused-professional, scenting-leverage-professional), Pig (panic has range — maritime-flailing, distractable-anxious, briefly-paternal), Murrow (practical-mentor has range — terse-instructional, archival-aside, dry-collegial), Asia (cheerful-while-drowning has range — warm-direct, briskly-prioritizing, briefly-conspiratorial). Bibles must be revisited to specify each register's interior range. This is a minor phase-3 re-touch, not a re-bibling.

**Project-wide implication.** Ch2-5 dialogue is not yet drafted. The breathing-room standard set by this Ch1 rewrite becomes the standard for all subsequent dialogue work. Phase-7 voice packs for Ch2-5 should plan dialogue volume accordingly (likely 2-3× the original phase-4 estimates).

**Phase-7 audit scrub-list addition.** Add to the existing scrub list (V1.3 §A): *"Character-dwell options that produce paraphrased exposition rather than register-revealing line."* Flag as scrub-mode failure during dialogue audit.

## English-version dialogue convention (phase-8 sharpening)

The English version of the game uses Polish only for proper names of people and places (Halina, Sikorska, Wójcik, Borowski, Plotek, Iwaszkiewicz, Reymont, Warsaw, Wrocław, etc.) and PLN currency. The phase-8 rewrite sharpens the V1.3 §A.5 "zero-to-one Polish flavor word per scene" rule: in the English version, no Polish flavor words appear in dialogue at all.

- **No Polish greetings.** *Dzień dobry* → *Good morning* / *Good afternoon*.
- **No Polish address forms.** *Pani* → *Ms.* / *Pan* → *Mr.*
- **No Polish common nouns in dialogue.** *kamienica* → *pre-war apartment building* / *aplikatura* → *apprenticeship* / *aplikant* → *apprentice* / *kancelaria* → *the firm* / *patron* → *master* (already specified in V1.3 §A.5; phase-8 confirms scope extends to all dialogue).
- **No Polish idioms or epigrams.** Idioms appear in English with their force preserved through line construction, not through Polish texture. Halina's canonical line *"You go to a lawyer like you go to a doctor: too late."* is delivered in English; the doctor/lawyer compression is the device.

The project's eventual Polish-version translation can restore Polish texture; the English version reads cleanly for an English-speaking player without requiring a glossary.

**Scope.** This convention applies to all phase-7 voice work going forward (V1.1, V1.2, V1.3 updates and V1.4 new draft) and to all phase-8 deliverables (audit, beat sheet, bibles, story.txt, world.txt). Pre-existing canonical content authored before phase 8 (e.g., Pig's *aplikatura* lecture sample shapes in `pig.md` line 31) is **not retroactively fixed by this audit**. A separate cleanup pass through pre-phase-8 canon may be desired; flag if so.
