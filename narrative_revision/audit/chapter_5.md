**This is the recoloring chapter.** The chapter-4 inflection plants in the player; the chapter-1/2/3 spaces and lines must read differently when the player returns to them in chapter 5. The chapter as written **does not make this explicit** — the player goes to new spaces (airport, embassy, penthouse) and then returns to the office. Phase 4 must add retraversal beats inside the existing structure, especially in Beat 0, Beat 10, and Beat 16, where the player is back in the Pig & Swine office.

**Critical spine integration point**: the **Pro-Bono Transfer Stub** in Beat 8 (penthouse) is the chapter's load-bearing spine moment. It partially redeems Swine and complicates the chapter-4 inflection — the firm IS broke, but partly because Swine has been redirecting money to pro-bono work. The half-right portrait pattern continues: both "Mr. Pig is performing crisis to suppress wages" and "Swine has been quietly subsidizing rights cases without authorization" are true at once. The Pro-Bono reveal does not undo the chapter-4 inflection; it adds a layer. Mr. Pig's blind spot remains; Swine's hidden idealism complicates without redeeming.

## Audit conclusion

Chapter 5 is **structurally sound for the spine** but requires **significant phase-4 work to add retraversal beats**. The chapter as written takes the player to new spaces and returns to the office without explicitly using those returns as recoloring surfaces. Phase 4 must add the retraversal beats inside Beat 0, Beat 10, and Beat 16.

The chapter's spine integration hinges on the **Pro-Bono Transfer Stub (Beat 8)** as the partial-redemption-of-Swine artifact that *complicates without resolving* the chapter-4 inflection. The half-right portrait of Mr. Pig requires that he be *genuinely surprised* by Swine's redirections — he didn't know, his austerity is sincere, his blind spot about wage suppression remains intact regardless. The chapter does not redeem Mr. Pig; it adds layers to the firm's situation that he himself has not seen.

Load-bearing items, in order of importance:
1. **Beat 8 Pro-Bono Transfer Stub** specifics (recipient, scale, Pig's awareness). Phase 4 high-priority.
2. **Mr. Pig's reactions** to the Pro-Bono reveal and Beat 16 partial truth. The half-right portrait stands or falls here.
3. **Retraversal beats at Beat 0 / 10 / 16.** The recoloring technique requires this; the chapter as written does not stage it.
4. **Swine's Beat 16 partial truth** — cheerful-evasion-that-cannot-quite-hide register.
5. **The penthouse multistability** (Beat 8) — visible visual contradictions plant Swine's full complexity.

**Phase-4 work for chapter 5 is the second-highest-stakes of the audit** (after chapter 4). The retraversal beats are the chapter's quiet structural backbone; the Pro-Bono Stub is the visible spine artifact. Both must be specified explicitly before phase-7 dialogue work begins.
