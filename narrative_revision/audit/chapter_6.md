**This is the highest-stakes chapter for the spine** because it carries the highest risk of cathartic-resolution drift. The chapter as written *mostly* avoids the failure mode — the office stays shabby, total victory is forbidden, returning NPCs cohere with their voices, the printer apology is honored, STUB callbacks remain comic-deflationary. But several beats sit close to the line and require explicit phase-4 calibration:

1. **Mr. Pig as "moral heart"** (NPC roles table). The phrase is dangerous if read as "moral resolution." Phase 4 must specify that Pig embodies the firm's idealism *while remaining the half-right figure he is* — moral heart, not moral resolution.
2. **Round 5 "social utility" argument.** If the firm wins because of social utility, the spine collapses. The win must be *procedurally and substantively grounded* (defective notice, lack of individual assessment, procurement conflict, suppression) — social utility is supportive, not load-bearing. Phase 4 high-priority.
3. **Beat 10 Swine reveal.** The chapter-5 partial-redemption completes here. Phase 4 must keep Mr. Pig's wage-suppression blind spot intact through this scene; Swine's redirections are *one* layer of the firm's complexity, not its resolution.
4. **Beat 14 ending.** The chapter omits the wage question from its closing, which is correct, but doesn't *invite* the unresolution. Phase 4 should add a small final image that lands the question still posed.

The cathartic-exposure failure mode — "firm exposed, juniors win, union triumphs" — does not appear in the chapter as written. The chapter ends with the firm shabby-and-alive, the consolidation scheme set aside, the structural conditions of labor unaddressed. **This is the right shape.** Phase 4's job is to make the unresolution *visible* without editorializing.

Chapter 6 is **structurally sound for the spine** and **largely correctly tuned**. **Phase-8 addition: Kacper's β corridor cameo is staged within this chapter** (see section above; lines drafted in `bibles/kacper_mazurek.md` Ch6 section). The chapter's load-bearing items, in order of importance:
1. **Mr. Pig's Round 5 sincere line** ("We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them"). Load-bearing for the half-right portrait at full intensity.
2. **The "office should not become rich or polished" rule** (Beat 14). Load-bearing for the no-cathartic-resolution discipline.
3. **Round 5 remedy structure** (scheme falls on legal defects, not on social-utility argument). Phase 4 must specify with precision.
4. **Beat 10 Swine reveal** (Pig's wage-suppression blind spot intact through the scene).

6. **Mr. Pig's role description** ("moral heart" needs phase-4 revision to clarify the half-right framing).

**Phase-4 work for chapter 6 is the third-highest-stakes of the audit** (after chapters 3 and 4). The Round 5 remedy specifics, the Beat 10 Swine reveal calibration, and the Beat 14 invitation-to-unresolution are the three phase-4 deliverables that make or break the chapter's spine integration. Get these right and the game ends in the shape the spine commits to; get them wrong and the chapter resolves the question the spine commits to leaving open.
