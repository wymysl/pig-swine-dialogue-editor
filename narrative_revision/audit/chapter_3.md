## Why this chapter exists

Three structural functions, each load-bearing:

1. **Filling the criminal-defense register gap.** The current spine is all civil law (procedure → eviction merits → printer conspiracy → contract → district consolidation). Plotek brings white-collar criminal in Ch5 (was Ch4 pre-phase-8) but the player has no prior criminal-defense register to anchor against. The compact chapter introduces criminal-defense mechanics with an indigent defendant, so Plotek's case lands as a register-twist: same procedural mode, opposite client class. The contrast — defending a homeless 19-year-old vs. defending a wealthy white-collar client — is dramatically rich and otherwise absent from the spine.

2. **Extending the β technique to a different mode.** Halina's case in Ch1 plants the "winning is losing" mechanic via her Ch4 corridor sighting (β recoloring). Kacper's case extends the same technique into criminal defense: the firm wins (suspended sentence) but the win is partially Pyrrhic — keeping Kacper out of prison sends him back to the homelessness he was trying to escape. The two β returns rhyme: a wronged client and an over-helped client both end up worse than where Cula's intervention left them. The Ch6 finale gets a quiet chord that pulls Ch1 + new Ch3 together.

3. **Transitional function in the spine.** The chapter sits between "firm at its best" (current Ch2 / Waldek) and "the inflection" (Ch4 / printer conspiracy). The Ch2 audit warned: "if chapter 2 reads with sour undertones, the player has nothing to compare the doubt against." Kacper's case provides the player's first taste of doubt *after* having seen the firm's clean work. The doubt doesn't destabilize Ch2's reading — it adds the first crack. By the time the inflection chapter arrives, the player has seen: clean win (Ch2) → ambiguous win (new Ch3) → premise begins to fail (Ch4). The progression is paced.

## Chapter function in the spine — detailed

**Beat-count target:** 6 beats. Compact case discipline.

**Runtime target:** 25–30 minutes. Tighter than canonical chapter (35–45 min) because no world expansion, no multiple witnesses, single detention scene, single hearing (phased mechanically but one court session narratively).

**Legal spine line** (proposed; Stage 11 beat sheet finalizes): *"The win that keeps the client out of prison sends him back to the place he committed the crime to escape."*

**Comedy spine** (proposed): The chapter's comedy is restrained. Pig's fury is funny. Asia's deadpan delivery of the rate structure is funny. The detention visit and hearing are not comic territory. The chapter's primary register is *measured-and-grim* with comic relief at the office bookends — opposite to the Ch2 register (warmth + Beton's bureaucratic absurdism in roughly equal measure).

**Emotional arc** (proposed):
```text
office anger → bureaucratic resignation → uneasy approach → encounter (the "2 years easy" pivot)
→ procedural prep → court pressure → procedural win → ambiguous aftermath
```

The arc inverts Ch2's: Ch2 climbs from client panic to public ripple (bottom up to top). New Ch3 descends from office anger to ambiguous aftermath (top to bottom). The descent prepares the renumbered-Ch4 inflection.

## Hearing structure — phased mechanic

**Phase 1: Contest the *włamanie* element.**
- Argument: was it actually breaking? The back door's lock was already broken. Polish doctrine on *kradzież z włamaniem* requires breaking of an entry barrier; if the barrier was already non-functional, the case should arguably be *kradzież szczególnie zuchwała* (audacious theft, Art. 278 § 3a) which carries a much lower sentence range.
- Best evidence: lock condition photo / patrol officer's testimony about the door state.
- Argument tag (per `battle_mechanics.txt` update in Stage 14): `breaking_element_dispute`.
- Outcome: even partial success on this element supports Phase 3's suspended-sentence argument.

**Phase 2: Contextualize the act.**
- Argument: mitigating circumstances. Homelessness, hunger, no violence, no flight, taken items minor (coat for warmth, food for survival, ~150 PLN — not a sophisticated theft).
- Best evidence: Kacper's social-services file (he aged out of state foster care; documented homelessness); the inventory of items taken; the patrol report showing no flight.
- Argument tag: `mitigating_circumstances`.
- Outcome: establishes the human picture without pleading for mercy. The cynic register can't work here; only direct factual presentation.

**Phase 3: Argue for suspended sentence.**
- Argument: statutory criteria for *warunkowe zawieszenie wykonania kary* (KK Art. 69 § 1). Post-2015 reform caps suspended imprisonment at **one year** — not exceeding. First-offender, non-violent offense, age, circumstances, prospects for rehabilitation. Probation period under KK Art. 70 § 2 is 2-5 years for *młodociany* (offender under 21 at offense, under 24 at trial — Kacper at 19 fits).
- Best evidence: prior record (none), the contextualization from Phase 2.
- Argument tag: `suspended_sentence_criteria`.
- Outcome: court grants 1 year imprisonment **(statutory minimum for Art. 279 § 1; maximum suspendable under Art. 69 § 1 — the two constraints converge on exactly one year)**, suspended for 3 years' probation. Cula has won. The win is the chapter's pyrrhic victory.

**Legal-accuracy note for Stage 13/14.** Polish KK Art. 69 § 1 (post-2015 reform) caps suspendable imprisonment at one year. Earlier draft of this audit and the beat sheet had referenced 1 year 6 months — that was incorrect. Phase-7 V3.* voice work and Stage 14 battle_mechanics.txt update must use **exactly 1 year imprisonment, suspended for 3 years' probation** as the canonical outcome. Cula's Phase 3 argument should cite Art. 69 § 1 and Art. 70 § 2 specifically; phase-7 voice work specifies whether the citation is in dialogue or in the judge's verdict announcement.

**Remedy discipline preserved.** Suspended sentence is the "modest" outcome here — Kacper is not acquitted, he has a criminal record, he has supervision conditions to meet for three years. Just as Halina's procedural reset bought a rehearing (not victory on the merits), Kacper's suspended sentence keeps him out of prison (not absolution from the act). The no-cathartic-resolution discipline applies at the chapter scale.
