Chapter 2 has the lightest reframing load of any chapter and that's *correct*. This is the chapter where the firm is at its best — Waldek's home is saved, the poster ripples publicly, the case is unambiguously good work. The spine *needs* this chapter to play straight. Doubts arrive in chapter 4 and bite in chapter 5; if chapter 2 reads with sour undertones, the player has nothing to compare the doubt against, and the chapter-5 recoloring loses its weight. Show the firm working before showing what the work costs.

Most of the audit is therefore confirmation: the existing material is already well-calibrated for what the spine wants from this chapter.

## Audit conclusion

Chapter 2 is **structurally sound for the spine** with minimal reframing. The chapter does what the spine needs from this moment in the game: it shows the firm at its best, with a client whose win is unambiguously good and a public ripple that lands sincerely. **Do not introduce sour undertones here.** The doubts arrive in chapter 4 and bite in chapter 5; chapter 2 must hold the line on "the firm's work has real value" so that the chapter-5 question — "and the work has costs paid by people who don't choose to pay them" — is a complication, not a refutation.

Load-bearing items, in order of importance:
1. **The three-tier victory states** (strong / standard / weak — all save Waldek). Canonical remedy discipline at chapter scale.
2. **The poster-multiplication payoff** (Beat 15 apartment payoff). Public ripple, sincere, unambiguous.
3. **The Beat 0 normalization** (Mr. Pig's continuing financial worry as background hum). Saturates "we're broke" so the inflection has something to puncture.
4. **The coffee-machine "may be solidarity" line** (Beat 15 office payoff). Primes STUB/PUTKA chapter-4 founding.
5. **Waldek's voice rules** (style_canon §2). The chapter's emotional anchor depends on him reading as a person, not a cause.

Phase-4 beat-sheet work for chapter 2 is the lightest of the five. Most of it is voice-reference alignment (phase 7) rather than structural change.
