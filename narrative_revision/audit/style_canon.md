### Crab — REFRAMING

- (K) Visual canon (mid-brown short cropped hair, rumpled suit, sleeves rolled, tie loose). Reads as *unfussed-professional*, which works for opportunist.
- (R) Voice rule: "terse, observational, **suspicious of optimism**. Two-sentence rhythm: observation, then implication." The *terse-observational-two-sentence* survives intact — it's exactly how a competent-detached opportunist talks. **"Suspicious of optimism" needs to soften toward "professionally detached from outcomes."** The opportunist isn't invested enough to be suspicious; he's clear-eyed because he's not emotionally engaged. Suggested replacement: "terse, observational, professionally indifferent to morale. Two-sentence rhythm: observation, then implication."
- (K) Vocabulary (plain, concrete nouns; trusts facts).
- (R) "Notices first: the inconsistency. Always." Survives but reframe. The opportunist notices inconsistency *because inconsistency is leverage*, not because he's outraged by it. Same observation, different motivation. No line change; voice references should reflect the motivation distinction.
- (R) "Will not say: anything reassuring while a fact remains unexplained." Survives. The opportunist also doesn't reassure — but for a different reason than the cynic would. The opportunist doesn't reassure because reassurance would be a commitment, and he doesn't make commitments to outcomes he isn't accountable for.

## Cross-cutting items added by the spine (canon additions to draft in phase 3+)

These don't exist in `style_canon.txt` yet but should be added once the bibles stabilize:

1. **Half-right portrait clause**: explicit canon language that Mr. Pig's voluntary austerity is in-scope as a *blind spot, not a corruption*. Distinguishes the new theme from the existing "no bribery/fraud/malice" rule.

2. **Stance-trio canon**: brief codification that the juniors carry three stances toward the firm's central question — Cula (true believer), Whimsy (cynic), Crab (opportunist) — and that scenes should respect this differentiation. This is the writing-discipline aid that prevents stance drift across chapters.

3. **Funny-in-hindsight rule**: a section in §3 (running jokes) explicitly protecting the technique. "Surface text must be funny on first read; meaningful on second read. Re-traversed spaces and recurring NPCs must support recoloring; one-shot gags that cannot recolor are demoted to flavor."

4. **Inflection clause**: a brief note in §6 or §7 codifying that chapter 4 contains the player's frame-shift moment, after which prior content reads differently. The specific beat is pinned in phase 4.

5. **No-cathartic-resolution rule**: a § note (probably in §6 court design or §7 quest design) explicitly forbidding the cathartic-exposure ending shape (firm exposed, juniors win, union triumphs). Currently implied by the remedy-discipline rule but not explicit.

These are not action items for this audit. They are deliverables for *after* phase 3 (bibles), when the contradictions are pinned and we know exactly what the canon language needs to protect.

## Audit conclusion

`style_canon.txt` survives the spine with *light reframing concentrated on the junior-stance reassignments*. There are no cuts. The canon was written with characters-having-contradictions and arc-discipline in mind, which is exactly what the spine requires; the spine sharpens what was already there.

The most load-bearing pre-existing canon items, in order of importance:
1. Mr. Pig's Ch 5 sincere-line arc rule (§2).
2. Remedy discipline — modest, procedural, never total victory (§6).
3. STUB/PUTKA tonal rules — comic-deflationary, never effective (§3).
4. Quest design — failure is comic, not punitive (§7).

These four already protect against the failure modes the spine fears most. The audit's main job for phase 3 is to make the protections *explicit* and to align Crab and Whimsy's voice rules with their pinned stances.
