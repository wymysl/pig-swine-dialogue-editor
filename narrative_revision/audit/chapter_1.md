The audit's main work is **identifying which existing beats need to plant specific recolorable content** (without changing what they currently do on first read) and **aligning Crab and Whimsy's recruitment beats with their pinned stances**.

## Audit conclusion

Chapter 1 survives the spine with light reframing concentrated on **planting density** for chapter-5 recoloring. There are no cuts. The chapter as written reads correctly as a starter-town financial-crisis story; the work for the spine is making sure the seeds for the half-right portrait, the Crab/Whimsy stance reframings, and the chapter-4 inflection are present *as plants* without tipping the hand.

The chapter's load-bearing items for the spine, in order of importance:
1. The remedy-discipline rule (Beat 10 Round 3) — protects no-cathartic-resolution at chapter scale.
2. Mr. Pig's specific financial claims (Beat 2) — needs reframing to add specificity for chapter-4 cross-reference.
3. The Murrow ledger line (Beat 11) — chapter-4 inflection seed.
4. Asia's overwork (Beat 1) — chapter-5 recoloring seed.
5. The Murrow-first-name beat (Beat 3) — labor-warmth-among-juniors plant.

**Phase 4 beat-sheet work for chapter 1 is the hardest** because every beat must do double work: read correctly on first pass for a true-believer protagonist, and seed something specific for chapter 5 recoloring. The chapter is the proof that the spine can sustain itself; if it doesn't work here, it doesn't work anywhere.
