# Pig & Swine RPG — Literary Lines Catalogue

> Companion to `literary_references.md`. Where that file names the
> *outside ear*, this one is a working menu of **actual lines** drawn
> from those writers, mapped to the character and beat where they
> could land. Each entry has:
>
> 1. **SOURCE** — the original quote (English translation by default;
>    Polish where the original cadence is load-bearing).
> 2. **PLACEMENT** — character + chapter/beat.
> 3. **IN-GAME LINE** — an adapted candidate ready to drop into the
>    JSON dialogue files. This is the deliverable; the source is the
>    provenance note.
> 4. **NOTES** — style-canon checks (legal referent? Latin? cap
>    watch?), and the "posture-only vs. quotable" call.
>
> This is a **menu**, not a slot-everywhere list. The style_canon
> cap holds: **one literary planted reference per beat, three per
> chapter total (including art-direction passes).** Pick from this
> menu against that budget.
>
> Style discipline carried in from `literary_references.md`:
>
> - **No author may be named in dialogue.** Characters may hold or
>   quote an epigram (Halina-style), but cannot attribute it.
> - **No Latin, real or fake.** Mortimer's English-only discipline
>   extends even to genuine quotations.
> - **One reference per chapter beat; three per chapter.** Includes
>   book-spine art passes.
> - **Pass-test bar:** every adapted line carries a real legal
>   referent and sounds spoken, not dictated.

---

## How to read an entry

```
> SOURCE — [author / work / English translator if relevant]
>   "..."
>
> PLACEMENT — character, chapter/beat
> IN-GAME LINE — "..."
> NOTES — posture-only | direct-quotable | window-dressing |
>          legal referent: <which> | cap-watch: <chapter>
```

`posture-only`: do not transcribe; the source recalibrates the
character's cadence. The IN-GAME LINE is original to P&S.

`direct-quotable`: the IN-GAME LINE is close to or identical with
the source (minus author attribution). Only Halina has the
canonical permission for this register; one other character is
flagged below where it works.

`window-dressing`: the line never speaks; it sits on a spine, a
poster, a docket. No reading required to land.

# Joseph Heller — *Catch-22*

The patron saint of the joke where escape from a procedure is
itself a procedure. Reach for him on Casebook tag-combat and any
court round where the legal answer to a procedural trap is itself a
procedural trap. The cadence is plain English, the rhythm is the
infinite regress, the deadpan never breaks.

### 1. The Catch-22 itself
```
> SOURCE — Heller, Catch-22
>   "There was only one catch and that was Catch-22, which specified
>   that a concern for one's own safety in the face of dangers that
>   were real and immediate was the process of a rational mind."
>
> PLACEMENT — Murrow, Ch3 (fee scene) OR Ch4 (STUB procedural)
> IN-GAME LINE — "The rule says any tenant frightened enough to
>   request emergency relief is rational. Rational tenants are
>   presumed capable of waiting. So emergency relief, by the rule,
>   is for tenants who don't ask."
> NOTES — posture-only | legal referent: emergency interim relief /
>   standing | cap-watch: Ch4 if STUB beat also borrows from Lem
```

### 2. Cathcart's missions
```
> SOURCE — Heller, Catch-22
>   "Catch-22 says they have a right to do anything we can't stop
>   them from doing."
>
> PLACEMENT — Crab, Ch4 (STUB intake / receipt-of-summons beat)
> IN-GAME LINE — "Notice that what's lawful here is whatever the
>   clerk hasn't been stopped from doing yet. We have not stopped
>   the clerk. So."
> NOTES — posture-only | legal referent: clerk's discretion under
>   procedure | Crab cadence: diagnose-then-truncate, as in
>   "postal theatre"
```

### 3. Major Major's office

# Jaroslav Hašek — *The Good Soldier Švejk*

Passive-aggressive proceduralism as survival. Mr. Swine's mode by
canon; usable in any beat where apparent compliance *is* the
resistance. Parrott's "Beg to report, sir" is the signature; in
P&S English, a softer "Sir, I should mention…" or "If I may…" lands
the same way.

```

>   charge of forgetting things on purpose. You are welcome to ask
```

# Forbidden, restated

- **No author named in dialogue.** Ever. Halina holding the
  cadence is the canonical permission; she does not say
  "Iwaszkiewicz wrote." Same rule extends to every entry above.
- **No Latin.** Includes real Latin from Borges, Mortimer,
  Bulgakov's epigraphs, or anywhere else.
- **Cap holds: one per beat, three per chapter.** If a chapter
  already has Hłasko docket #22, the Iwaszkiewicz Halina-shelf
  drop, and Bajtek's Gogol-named printer, it is *out of budget*
  even if another entry above would also fit.
- **Cross-easter-egg discipline:** if Bajtek's printer carries an
  Akaky-cognate AND the cardiologist is Stawska AND the docket is
  1957/8 *in the same chapter*, the player who catches one starts
  hunting; the deniable register collapses. Distribute across
  chapters.
