# Adversarial pressure test

**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Phase:** 6
**Method:** Read the chapter beat sheets, bibles, audits, and re-traversal map with hostile eyes. Mark every failure-mode hit. Provide a remediation plan for each. Severity rated: **Critical** (blocks the spine; must remediate before phase 7) · **Significant** (risks weakening the spine; should remediate) · **Minor** (worth noting; doesn't block).

**Hostility caveat.** The pass is honest, not protective. Items below are the places I tried hardest to dislike the work. Most of the spine survives. The hits are concentrated in places where the writing has been over-engineered, where staging is doing too much authorial work, or where the recoloring devices have accreted past the player's tracking budget. Not all hits warrant remediation — some are flagged for awareness only.

---

## What survives the pressure test (counterweight)

Before the failure modes, what holds:

- **The spine itself.** "Does it matter why someone helps you, if they help?" remains a real, posable, unanswerable question. The half-right portrait of Mr. Pig is internally consistent across the bibles and beat sheets; he reads as neither admirable nor venal at any chapter boundary.
- **The chapter-4 inflection's content.** The 285,400 PLN reserve as a steady-state number (not a hidden windfall) makes the inflection structural rather than gotcha-shaped. The inflection content is sound; the staging has issues (see below).
- **The STUB-founding restraint.** "Two callbacks across the rest of the game; not a recurring punchline" survives. The canon's discipline holds. The union does not slide into noble-collective-action territory; phase-4 work strengthened rather than weakened this.
- **The Beat 14 office-stays-shabby commitment.** The chapter-6 ending does not enrich the firm. The structural conditions stay unaddressed. The cathartic-exposure failure mode does not appear in the chapter-6 endpoint.
- **Asia and Murrow's stances.** Both bibles hold without strain. The asymmetric warmth/restraint pair works.
- **Plotek as structural-not-personal threat.** The bible's discipline holds — Plotek is polite, conversational, structural. The chapter-2 Beat 8.5 first visit needs adjustment (see below) but the character framing is not the failure mode.
- **The remedy-discipline rule** across all five chapters' courts. Modest, procedural, partial. No total victories. This is the spine's mechanical-level protection and it holds.

The work is sound at the level of design intent. The failure modes are at the level of execution and accretion.

## Failure-mode hits — chapter 1

### 1. Beat 2 "back in my day" lecture timing — Significant

**Failure mode:** writer signaling distance from caring (protective irony) AND Mr. Pig flattening to obtuse rather than blind.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_1.md` Beat 2, the new "first lecture" insertion.

**Description.** Phase 4 places Pig's first lecture immediately after his three-claim financial panic — so within sixty seconds of meeting Cula, Pig pivots from maritime crisis about the rent into a lecture about how he had it harder. The juxtaposition is too neat. A player who notices the pivot will read it as authorial setup rather than as Pig being Pig. The chapter-5 family-photo discovery's recoloring depends on the lectures landing as ordinary across the game; Beat 2 is too soon to make that work.

**Remediation.** Relocate the first lecture out of Beat 2. Recommended placement: as part of the **Beat 8 readiness check** — Pig delivers a brief lecture aside while Murrow runs through the requirements. The lecture sits inside an existing scene's downtime rather than pivoting from his own panic. Phase 4 beat sheet to be updated; the chapter-1 first-lecture beat moves from Beat 2 to Beat 8.

### 2. Beat 2 financial claims load — Significant

**Failure mode:** structural overload risking that the chapter-4 inflection's anchoring goes unnoticed.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_1.md` Beat 2, three specific financial claims wrapped in maritime metaphors.

**Description.** Phase 4 has Pig naming three specific items (rent overdue six weeks, printer lease just doubled, missing Swine retainer) in his Beat 2 panic, all wrapped in maritime metaphors. This is a lot of specific content for the player's first encounter with Pig in panic mode. The starter-town chapter is already loaded with introductions and tutorials; asking the player to retain three specific bills for chapter-4 cross-reference is optimistic. If the player doesn't retain them, the chapter-4 inflection's anchoring is weaker than the spine wants.

**Remediation.** Distribute the claims across multiple Pig moments in chapter 1. Beat 2 keeps one specific claim (the rent — the most concrete and emotionally legible). The printer lease becomes a Beat 8 readiness-check aside or a Beat 11 post-court anxiety. The missing Swine retainer becomes the Beat 11 framing line. The player encounters each claim in a moment with breathing room around it. Cumulatively the three items still anchor the chapter-4 inflection but the player has had three separate moments to register each.

### 3. Crab's recruitment line "There is no engagement letter on this. I work on engagements." — Significant

**Failure mode:** characters contradicting each other randomly (in this case, characters *announcing* their stance rather than embodying it).

**Location:** `beats/chapter_1.md` Beat 5, recommended phase-7 line shape for Crab's first resistance.

**Description.** The recommended line tells the player directly that Crab has a transactional relationship with the firm. This is the voice equivalent of a name tag — the opportunist stance gets diagnosed by Crab's own dialogue, which violates the writing rule from Crab's bible ("his stance is the moral core, and the joke is that it works" — i.e., shown not said). The line is also too literally about engagement letters; aplikants in Polish practice don't typically demand engagement letters for internal-firm work assignments.

**Remediation.** Revise toward a less self-explaining shape. The opportunism should be in the *limitation* of his commitment, not in his announcing his commitment style. Recommended replacement: *"The binder cites Article 132. I can verify the service issue."* — refers to work, not to motivation. The transactional posture lives in what Crab is willing to do (a defined deliverable) versus what he isn't (open-ended firm support). Phase 7 voice work writes the exact text against this shape.

## Failure-mode hits — chapter 2

**Failure mode:** central question getting answered rather than posed; the moral juxtaposition reads as deliberate authorial lesson.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_2.md` Beat 1, Plotek courier arrives immediately after Waldek leaves.

**Description.** The juxtaposition (sympathetic client + repugnant client, both accepted on principle) is the chapter's spine work. Phase 4 stages it back-to-back. **Remediation.** Insert two minutes of neutral office texture between Waldek's intake and Plotek's. Pig has a moment with Asia about a coffee cup; Murrow notes a procedural item; the new aplikant Cula is asked to fetch something from the archive. Two minutes of breathing room. Then the courier arrives with Plotek's case file. The juxtaposition is still there — same chapter, same Pig accepting on principle — but the staging is no longer adjacent. Phase 4 beat sheet to be updated.

### 6. Cula visits Plotek alone in chapter 2 — Significant

**Failure mode:** the protagonist getting authorial responsibility he hasn't earned in-game.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_2.md` Beat 8.5, Plotek first detention visit. Personnel: Cula and Plotek, alone.

**Description.** Lawyer-client privilege justifies the solo meeting in legal terms. But Cula is in his second chapter; the player has had perhaps 90 minutes with him as an aplikant fresh from the doctorate. Sending him alone into a high-menace meeting with a structurally-dangerous client is asking the protagonist to carry weight he hasn't earned narratively. It also denies the player the buffering that would make the scene work — Murrow's archival neutrality, the formal procedural register a senior would bring.

**Remediation.** **Murrow accompanies Cula on the first visit.** Murrow is silent in the scene but present; he is training Cula on the case. Plotek's menace operates in the air despite (and slightly *because of*) Murrow's presence — Plotek can be polite to Cula while Murrow watches. The chapter-4 second visit Cula goes alone, having earned the responsibility. This matches how junior lawyers actually handle high-stakes client meetings and removes the authorial bravado. Phase 4 beat sheet and Plotek bible to be updated.

### 7. Plotek's mother-mention at Saska Kępa in chapter 2 — Significant

**Failure mode:** the white-collar client tipping toward personal menace; structural threat over-loaded too early.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_2.md` Beat 8.5, Plotek's specific biographical reference to Cula's mother's district.

**Description.** Phase 4 has Plotek delivering a specific biographical reference (district where Cula's mother lives) on the very first visit. The bible's recipe for Plotek's menace is "accumulating small unsettling touches"; phase 4 makes the first touch a maximally-loaded biographical specificity. The character risks tipping into personal-menace mode (the failure the bible explicitly forbids) because the first interaction already shows full access.

**Remediation.** Chapter 2's first visit shows Plotek's *legal-landscape access*, not his *biographical access*. He demonstrates unusual depth of reading on the warrant motion's likely outcome — perhaps cites a recent procedural decision the firm hasn't yet engaged with. The mother-mention moves to chapter 4's second detention visit, when Cula is alone and the menace can escalate naturally. The chapter-2 visit lands as "this man knows the law better than expected"; the chapter-4 visit lands as "this man knows things about my family." Two stages of access, one per chapter. Plotek bible and chapter 2/3 beat sheets to be updated.

## Failure-mode hits — chapter 4

### 8. Beat 11 staging — Murrow leaves with the ledger open — Significant

**Failure mode:** characters acting out of character to enable plot beats.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_3.md` Beat 11 inflection, Murrow steps out for a stamp leaving the ledger visible.

**Description.** The inflection requires the ledger to be open in Cula's path during a brief Murrow absence. But Murrow is archival, particular, careful. Leaving the ledger open while he steps out — even briefly — feels uncharacteristic. The staging is doing the inflection's work at the cost of Murrow's bibled discipline. Hostile read: the character is bending around the plant.

**Remediation.** Restage so Murrow doesn't *leave* with the ledger open — he is *interrupted* mid-update. The phone rings (Asia transferring a courier call), or the printer demands attention, or Pig calls him with a panic. Murrow steps away expecting to return immediately; the interruption is the reason the ledger stays open. The interruption is in-character (Murrow responds to procedural needs) and the ledger's openness is plausible (he intends to come right back). Phase 4 chapter-4 beat sheet to be updated.

Alternative: Murrow has a *separate document* on the desk (a printed bank statement, a recent receipt, a working sheet) that Cula glimpses. The ledger itself stays appropriately closed. The number is on the working sheet. This shifts the surface but preserves the inflection's content. Phase 4 picks A or B.

### 10. Beat 11 eye-contact moment — Minor

**Failure mode:** authorial staging risking wink-to-player.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_3.md` Beat 11, Murrow returns and shares a two-second look with Cula.

**Description.** Two seconds of eye contact between two characters who haven't been intimate readers of each other before, staged at the chapter's most spine-loaded moment, risks landing as a wink to the player rather than as a real interpersonal beat. ### 11. Inflection vs STUB-founding proximity — Significant

**Failure mode:** characters contradicting each other randomly (in this case, the *staging* contradicts the *intent* — the writer says the beats are independent but the staging makes them feel linked).

**Location:** `beats/chapter_3.md` Beat 11 (inflection) and Beat 11.5 (STUB founding) happen in the same scene, in the same office, within minutes of each other.

**Description.** Phase 4 explicitly commits that the two beats must read as causally independent. But spatially and temporally they are extremely close. Even with no dialogue linking them, the player will likely connect them — "the juniors were tired this month AND Cula just saw the books are healthier than Pig says, and these two things produced the union" reads as a single causal event rather than two parallel ones. The independence claim is structurally fragile.

**Remediation.** Insert separation. Move STUB-founding to a slightly later moment within the chapter. Recommended: STUB-founding happens *after* the urgent filing has been sent — possibly during a coffee-break beat between the deadline-dash cutscene departure and Cula's arrival at the District Court. The juniors are at the office; the work is sent; they are tired; they make the napkin. Murrow is at the courier dispatch overseeing the delivery; Pig is asleep at his desk. The geographical and temporal separation from the inflection is real — the inflection happened during the prep, the union founding happens after the prep is complete. Phase 4 chapter-4 beat sheet to be updated.

## Failure-mode hits — chapter 5

### 12. Beat 0 retraversal density — Significant

**Failure mode:** easter eggs reading as a checklist rather than as recoloring.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_4.md` Beat 0 retraversal — six surfaces (case board, cork board, Pig idle line, Murrow ledger, Asia desk, framed family photo) in 60-90 seconds.

**Description.** Phase 4 commits the player encountering six recoloring surfaces in the chapter-5 opening retraversal. Hostile read: this is a checklist of "things the player should re-examine now that they have new context." A player who's not paying attention will skip past most of it; a player who is paying attention will register the staging-density. Either way, the beat is doing too much.

**Remediation.** Reduce Beat 0 to one focal moment plus ambient texture.
- **Focal:** the framed family photo. Interactive, with Cula's specific observation if examined, generic flavor if not. **This is the chapter's biographical-blind-spot reveal moment and deserves the focal slot.**
- **Ambient:** the case board, cork board, Murrow's ledger area, Asia's desk are all present in the scene as ordinary visible texture. No specific Cula lines play. The player who's looking sees the chapter-4 plants holding; the player who isn't moves through normal traversal.
- **Pig's idle line** (firm-survival continuation, possibly maritime-flavored) plays once and lands.

Beat 10 retraversal can carry the case-board / cork-board observations more explicitly if the player is by then primed. Phase 4 beat sheet to be updated. Beat 0 becomes leaner; Beat 10 can absorb the displaced weight.

### 13. Pro-Bono Stub recipient too neat — Significant

**Failure mode:** authorial closing-of-the-loop; the redirections feel staged rather than organic.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_4.md` Beat 8, Pro-Bono Stub recipient is Stowarzyszenie Pomocy Lokatorom Warszawskim (Warsaw Tenants' Aid Association) — connecting cleanly to Waldek's chapter-2 case.

Real philanthropy is diffuse — people who quietly redirect funds for years across multiple causes don't typically pick a single recipient that thematically mirrors their firm's most prominent recent case. - A refugee legal aid center
- A domestic violence shelter's legal fund

The visible Stub at Beat 8 shows one specific transfer to the tenants' aid (3,400 PLN, 18 months ago) — which lands the chapter-2 connection — but Crab's Beat 10 Pro-Bono Ledger placement at chapter 6 reveals the diversified recipient list. Swine cared diffusely. The chapter-2 thematic connection is one of several; the player feels it without the writer underlining it. `bibles/swine.md`, `00_decisions.md`, and chapter 5/5 beat sheets to be updated.

### 15. Cula's optional Beat 16 dialogue choice deflates the moment — Significant

**Failure mode:** mechanical interruption of emotional rhythm.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_4.md` Beat 16, Cula gets a three-option dialogue menu (A/B/C) at a high-emotional moment.

**Description.** Beat 16 carries Pig's conflicted reaction to the Pro-Bono reveal, Swine's partial truth, the team processing. Adding a player-choice menu in the middle of this scene's emotional rhythm risks deflating it — the player drops out of immersion to evaluate three options; the moment gets cataloged.

**Remediation.** Make the choice less menu-shaped. Two options:
- **(a) Silent player choice.** Cula either speaks once or stays silent based on a single button press; no menu surfaces. The branch-flagging happens internally; the scene reads continuous.
- **(b) Distributed choice.** No single Beat-16 choice. Instead, multiple smaller Cula choices accumulate across chapters 3-4 (whether Cula examined the family photo at Beat 0, whether Cula spoke to Asia about the audit on his mother, etc.); the chapter-6 branch resolves from accumulated flags rather than a single menu.

Recommended: **(b) distributed**. The branch is more truly player-shaped if it reflects accumulated behavior rather than a single late-chapter menu. Phase 4 chapter-5 and chapter-6 beat sheets, Cula bible, and the flag taxonomy to be updated. The chapter-6 Beat 14 final dialogue choice (which is also a menu) gets the same treatment — silent or distributed.

## Failure-mode hits — chapter 6

### 16. Beat 14 final image too similar to inflection — Significant

**Failure mode:** the image landing as repetition rather than as ending punctuation.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_5.md` Beat 14 final image, Cula passes Murrow's desk, ledger open, sees, says nothing.

**Description.** Cula has been doing this exact action since chapter 4 — passing Murrow's desk, seeing the ledger, saying nothing. The chapter-6 final image as written introduces nothing visually new; it repeats the inflection. As the closing frame of the game, it risks landing as "the writer using the same image again" rather than as "the structure persists" punctuation.

**Remediation.** Make the chapter-6 ledger visibly different from the chapter-4 version. Recommendations:
- **Murrow is at his desk now** (not absent like in chapter 4). The image is intimate rather than illicit; Cula passes, Murrow is working, the ledger is open as ordinary professional activity. Cula sees the page; Murrow sees Cula see; nothing is said. The intimacy is the change.
- **A new entry has been made.** The chapter-6 case proceeds add a small line to the ledger. The reserve has gone up slightly. The structure persists *and* the firm has won and *and* the wages will not change.
- **Both:** Murrow finishes his entry, closes the ledger, places it in the drawer. Cula passes. The ledger is no longer visible. The system has absorbed the chapter-6 win without changing.

Recommended: **the third option** — Murrow closes the ledger as Cula passes. The closing is the visual difference. The structure persists *and* the moment ends. Phase 4 chapter-6 beat sheet to be updated.

### 18. Round 5 social-utility framing tension — Critical

**Failure mode:** cathartic-resolution drift in disguised form.

**Location:** `beats/chapter_5.md` Beat 13 Round 5 + canonical Pig sincere line ("we are not efficient. but people come here when efficient systems have already failed them.")

**Description.** Phase 4 says the Round 5 win is grounded on legal defects, not on social utility. Phase 4 also has Pig deliver his canonical sincere line as Round 5 testimony. The line *is* the social-utility argument. The writer has tried to have both — the win is procedural, but the player's emotional experience of Round 5 will be Pig's sincere line landing as the moment that wins the case. **This is the chapter-6 cathartic-resolution failure mode in its most subtle form.** If Pig's line lands as the moment that turned the court, the chapter has answered "yes, the firm's social utility justifies the firm" — exactly what the spine forbids.

**Remediation.** Two stage-level fixes required:
- **Make the judge's response to Pig's line dry and structurally minor.** The judge acknowledges Pig's testimony without validation. The Round 5 remedy is then announced separately, citing the legal defects from Rounds 1-4. The judge does not say "the firm's testimony moves me"; the judge says something procedurally precise. Phase 7 voice work for the judge is critical here.
- **Reduce the temporal weight of Pig's line in the round.** Phase 4 has Pig deliver the line; Swine deliver his sincere line; Whimsy give closing support. The round's load-bearing argument-content is in Whimsy's closing support and the procedural framing — Pig and Swine's testimonies are character moments inside the round, not the round's basis. Phase 4 chapter-6 beat sheet must explicitly de-emphasize Pig and Swine's testimonies as basis-of-decision and emphasize them as supportive.

This is the **Critical** hit because it sits at the chapter-6 ending and the spine's most-protected commitment. If Round 5 lands as social-utility wins, the spine collapses. Phase 4 chapter-6 beat sheet, `bibles/pig.md`, and `audit/chapter_5.md` to be updated with the explicit framing.

## Cross-cutting failure modes

### 19. Six recoloring layers may exceed the player's tracking budget — Significant

**Failure mode:** structural overload; the spine has accreted too many recoloring devices.

**Location:** across all phase-4 beat sheets and bibles.

**Description.** The phase-4 work added six distinct recoloring layers: the chapter-4 ledger inflection, the chapter-5 family-photo discovery, the firm-survival celebration ordering, Pig's "back in my day" lectures, the Pro-Bono Stub, Plotek's structural reach. Each layer is sound. The cumulative effect risks overwhelming the half-right portrait rather than supporting it — the player is being asked to track six accreting patterns simultaneously, plus the case mechanics, plus the running jokes (printer, coffee machine, postcards), plus the chapter-specific characters.

**Remediation.** Prioritize. The load-bearing layers for the spine are:
- **Critical (must work):** Pig's "we're broke" register → chapter-4 inflection. Family-photo discovery → chapter-5 Beat 0. Firm-survival celebration ordering → all chapters.
- **Significant (should work):** Pro-Bono Stub. The "back in my day" lectures.
- **Decorative (could shrink):** Plotek's structural reach (the chapter-4 manifestation specifically).

If phase-7 production constraints force shrinking, cut from Decorative first. Plotek's chapter-4 structural reach manifestation (Asia's audit-mention line) can be reduced to ambient — even removed — without breaking the spine. The chapter-2 first visit and the chapter-5 procedural win + the chapter-6 newspaper footnote carry enough.

Phase 4 to add a priority ranking explicitly. Phase 7 to budget against the ranking.
