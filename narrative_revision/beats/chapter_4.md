### Beat structure

3. **Mr. Pig in his corner.** Maritime metaphor at peak. Phase 7 voice work: a specific Pig line about toner costs ("the toner alone is taking on water; we are bailing with thimbles"). The line is sincere; he means it. The line will recolor in retrospect against what Cula is about to see.

5. **The glimpse.** Cula's eye catches the page for the moment it takes to walk past it. **What Cula sees:** an entry near the top of the visible page reading "Konto operacyjne — saldo bieżące: 285 400 PLN" (operating account — current balance: 285,400 PLN). The handwriting is Murrow's, careful and consistent across months. **The number contradicts what Mr. Pig has just said about toner.** The number is also approximately one full year of doubled junior salaries — a coincidence Cula does not consciously calculate but that the player may feel.

6. **The thought is shelved.** Cula does not stop. Cula does not look longer. Cula does not look at Mr. Pig. Cula takes the procedural notice from the case file cabinet and continues toward the team's working area. (Per Cula bible: the urgency of the filing pulls focus; the thought is shelved; the player feels it land.)

7. **Murrow returns from the call.** **(Phase-6 revision for Hit #10 + #20.)** He carries the courier-confirmation receipt. ### Phase-4 staging notes

- **No music sting.** The inflection has no audio cue. The scene's existing ambient tone (the night office) carries it.
- **No camera move.** The visual is the same as the rest of the office work scenes. The inflection happens in normal staging.
- **No flag-set notification.** A flag is set internally (`chapter4.glimpsed_ledger_anomaly = true`) but no UI surfaces it. The player notices because the player is paying attention.
- **The ledger entry remains visible after Murrow returns.** This is deliberate. Murrow does not close the ledger because closing it would be staging the moment. The ledger stays open through the rest of Beat 11 and Beat 11.5; the player can re-approach Murrow's desk and see it again if they choose. **Doing so does not produce additional dialogue.** The number is what is there.
- **Re-traversal note:** in chapter 5 retraversal beats, the player will re-encounter Murrow's ledger area. The chapter-4 inflection prepares this.

### Why this form

The ledger glimpse as a steady-reserve line (rather than a hidden windfall, a recent windfall, or a single mismatched receipt) makes the inflection about *Pig's framing pattern* rather than about *one specific concealment*. Mr. Pig is not hiding a recent score. He has been maintaining the wage-suppression frame against an ordinary, stable, unremarkable reserve. The inflection is structural, not transactional. This is exactly the half-right portrait the spine commits to: Mr. Pig's austerity is voluntary; the structural effect is independent of the sincerity; the ledger doesn't show a lie, it shows a pattern.

The "285,400 PLN" number is recommended because it is: large enough to make the wage suppression read as plainly unjustified (one full year of doubled junior salaries); small enough to read as substantial buffer rather than corporate windfall; specific enough to be cited if the player remembers it. The number is anchored to the salary math — at ~60,000 PLN gross/year per junior with three juniors plus Asia, the reserve covers a full year of the firm's entry-level wage burden at twice the current minimum-wage rates. Phase 4 may adjust the exact number; the *shape* of the entry (steady reserve scaling with the salary picture, not windfall) is committed.
