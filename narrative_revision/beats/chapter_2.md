## Beat 8.5 — Plotek detention visit (NEW BEAT)

Inserted between Beat 8 (Landlord's Office) and Beat 9 (Document Chase). Phase 4 specifies the scene in detail.

### Setting

- **Location**: detention facility (*areszt śledczy*) meeting room. Plain. A table; two chairs; a small interview window. Cutscene-shaped — no exploration.
- **Time**: late afternoon. Between the Landlord's Office investigation and the office return for Document Chase.
- **Personnel present**: Cula and **Murrow** (silent presence) and Plotek. **(Phase-6 revision for Hit #6.)** Murrow accompanies on the first visit — he is training Cula on the case. Murrow does not participate in the conversation; he is present at the table with case files. The lawyer-client privilege still operates; the meeting is privileged; both Cula and Murrow are counsel. **Murrow's archival neutrality buffers the scene.** Plotek's menace operates around the silent third party rather than against an alone protagonist. Cula goes alone in chapter 4 second visit, having earned the responsibility. This matches how junior lawyers actually handle high-stakes meetings.

### Beat structure

(N) The scene is approximately 3 minutes of in-game time.

1. **Cula enters.** Plotek is already seated. He stands politely as Cula enters. Phase 7 voice work: Plotek's first words are warm, courteous greeting in formal Polish. Phase 7 specifies exact text.

2. **Procedural review.** Cula opens the warrant motion paperwork. Phase 7 voice work for Cula: professional, brief, slightly clipped. He explains the motion's structure. Plotek follows, asking precise procedural questions. The conversation is genuinely about the warrant.

3. **The legal-landscape access.** **(Phase-6 revision for Hit #7.)** The chapter-2 first visit shows Plotek's *legal-landscape access* rather than his *biographical access*. Mid-conversation, Plotek demonstrates unusual depth of reading on the warrant motion's likely outcome — he cites a recent procedural decision the firm has not yet engaged with, or anticipates a counter-argument the prosecutor is likely to raise, with specificity that suggests he has been following the law more carefully than a typical defendant would. The depth is courteous, professional, and — to Cula and Murrow — unsettling. **The biographical mention (the mother at Saska Kępa) moves to chapter 4 second visit**, when Cula is alone and the menace can escalate.

   Phase 7 voice work for Plotek's legal-landscape demonstration: unfailingly courteous, embedded in procedural content. Recommended shape (phase 7 specifies exact text): a sentence that names a specific Polish-criminal-procedure decision (real or plausibly fictional) and frames the warrant motion's chances against it, in a way that suggests Plotek has been reading the firm's work product before it has been filed. The depth is the menace; no biographical references in chapter 2.

4. **Cula's response.** Cula registers the depth. Cula answers the procedural question. Murrow's silent presence at the table absorbs some of the weight. Phase 7 voice work: the answer is precise, slightly more formal than usual, no acknowledgment of the unsettling depth.

5. **(REMOVED — phase-6 revision for Hit #15.)** The original phase-4 plan included a three-option player-choice dialogue at this beat. Phase 6 flagged that menu-shaped player choices in emotionally-loaded scenes deflate the moment. The choice is now distributed across the chapter rather than menu-shaped. The Plotek detention visit plays without a player-choice menu; Cula's professional restraint is the writing-discipline. Phase 4 chapter-2 flag taxonomy adjusted: `chapter2.plątek_addressed_mention` is removed.

6. **The visit ends.** Cula leaves with the warrant motion paperwork ready. Phase 7 voice work: Plotek's exit line is warm, courteous, looks forward to the motion's filing. Cula's exit line is brief.

### Phase-4 staging notes

- **No music sting at the mention.** The conversational register holds throughout.

- **No structural reach manifestation in chapter 2.** Per Plotek bible: chapter 2 is conversational menace only. The reach comes in chapter 4.

### Why this form

The first detention visit is the principle-test in miniature. Cula does the work because the firm has accepted the case and the procedural arguments are real. Plotek's menace is conversational and structural — the player exits feeling something they cannot name, which is precisely the spine's intended texture. **The chapter's main arc (Waldek) resumes immediately after.** The Plotek thread is folded under, not foregrounded.
