## Beat 3 — Detention center: the load-bearing scene with Kacper (N)

This is the chapter's largest single beat. Estimated 10-12 minutes of player time. Phase-7 voice work for this beat (V3.* voice pack — Stage 15 outlines, future drafts implement) is the largest single voice-pack effort in the new chapter.

**Setup.** Cula arrives at the *areszt śledczy*. He passes through reception (brief; non-speaking), surrenders his items per protocol, is led to a visitor room. The room is small, functional, cold-feeling: a table, two chairs, a fluorescent ceiling fixture, a guard outside the door visible through a small window. No glass divider (it's a remand visitor room, not maximum-security). Cula sits and waits. Kacper is brought in by a different guard.

**Kacper's arrival staging.** He is 19, thin, dressed in the clothes he was arrested in (the coat is back; he's wearing a remand-issue sweater). Posture: guarded but not aggressive; eye contact direct; no fidgeting. He sits across from Cula. He says nothing first; he waits for Cula to start.

**Cula's stance carrier (analogous to Ch1 Beat 8 stance, but adapted).** Recommended three carriers — phase-7 voice work specifies. The carriers shape Cula's interview register; Kacper's responses adjust to the register without performing differently per stance:

- **Sympathetic carrier** — Cula leads with care; questions about Kacper's situation, where he's been sleeping, whether he has anyone to call.
- **Procedural carrier** — Cula leads with the case; questions about the night of the burglary, the door, the items, what happened when the patrol arrived.
- **Direct carrier** — Cula explains the legal situation first and asks Kacper how he wants to respond; treats Kacper as capable of his own legal decisions rather than as a vulnerable client to be protected.

Required content (surfaces in any carrier):
- Cula explains the charge: *kradzież z włamaniem*, KK Art. 279.
- Cula explains the sentence range: 1-10 years; first-offender realistic outcome 2-3 years if convicted on the charged offense.
- Cula explains the procedural option: contest the *włamanie* element (the lock was already broken); contextualize the act; argue for suspended sentence.
- Kacper's biographical surfacings (per stance carrier; biographical depth varies but does not lecture):
  - Sympathetic: surfaces foster-care history, ages, why he ran, what he's been doing for shelter.
  - Procedural: surfaces the night-of facts; the door's condition before he arrived; what he saw inside; why he stayed (cold, exhausted).
  - Direct: surfaces his own legal understanding (he knows what *włamanie* means; he knows what the charge means in years); doesn't ask Cula to soften.

**The "2 years easy" moment.** This is the chapter's emotional pivot. Lands in any stance, but the texture varies:

- In sympathetic carrier: Cula explains the 2-year prison risk with care; Kacper responds *"Two years? I can do that. Easy."* The flatness of his line registers against the care of Cula's framing.
- In procedural carrier: Cula explains the prison risk procedurally; Kacper responds the same way; Cula registers the response as a fact.
- In direct carrier: Cula already treats Kacper as capable of the calculation; Kacper responds *"Two years? I can do that. Easy."* and Cula does not soften it; the moment lands as mutual recognition between two people who understand the situation.

In all three: Cula realizes that prison is not necessarily a worse outcome for Kacper than the streets. Cula does not say this aloud. Phase-7 voice work specifies whether Cula has an internal-reaction line or simply a beat of silence. Recommended: silence. The withholding-of-articulation discipline carries the moment.

**Phase-7 audit point — Kacper's voice.** Kacper must read as guarded street-smart, not slow, not naive. He sees Cula clearly. The "2 years easy" line is accurate, not deflective — he's not minimizing fear; he's stating a calculation. Phase-7 voice work must protect this. If a draft has Kacper sounding either pitiable or aggressive, it has failed the audit. Kacper's bible (Stage 12) drafts the interior register range.

**Cula's reaction beats.** The moment of recognition between Cula and Kacper is the chapter's load-bearing emotional moment. Phase-7 voice work specifies whether Cula has a line that registers his recognition (e.g., *"I see."* or a single observational line) or whether he simply moves on to the next procedural question. Recommended: a brief observational line that does NOT articulate the recognition explicitly. Cula thinks but does not say.

**Logistics.** Cula informs Kacper of the next steps: case prep at the office; Cula will return for the hearing; Kacper will be transported from detention for the court session. Cula gives Kacper the firm's contact (or rather, leaves a note for the guard to relay if Kacper needs anything; the firm's actual reachability is limited).

**Beat ends with:** Cula leaves the visitor room. Kacper is taken back to his cell. Cula walks out of the *areszt śledczy*. The walk back to the office is offstage (no Beat 3.5 needed — the scene's weight has been delivered).

## Beat 5 — Court: phased hearing (N, three sub-phases)

**Setup.** Cula, Crab, and Murrow at the District Court (same as Ch1; different judge / courtroom). Kacper is brought in from detention; he sits in the defendant's place. The judge enters. The hearing opens. Brief procedural formalities; phase-7 voice work specifies.

**Argument tags** (per `battle_mechanics.txt` update in Stage 14): `breaking_element_dispute`, `mitigating_circumstances`, `suspended_sentence_criteria`. New context tags: `ex_officio_appointment`, `pre_broken_lock`, `homelessness_circumstance`, `suspended_sentence`.

### Phase 1 — Contest the *włamanie* element

Argument: was it actually breaking? The lock was demonstrably broken before Kacper arrived. Polish doctrine on *kradzież z włamaniem* requires breaking of an entry barrier; if the barrier was already non-functional, the case should arguably reduce to *kradzież szczególnie zuchwała* (Art. 278 § 3a) — significantly lower sentence range.

Best evidence: lock condition photo; patrol officer's testimony.
Best ally: Crab (procedural angle).
Argument tag: `breaking_element_dispute`.

The judge's reaction: measured-skeptical. He notes the doctrinal point but reserves it for sentencing rather than charge reduction. Phase-7 voice work specifies the judge's line shape: *"Noted. Continue."* — clipped, noncommittal but recording the argument.

Outcome flag: `chapter3.phase1_breaking_argued = true`.

### Phase 2 — Contextualize the act

Argument: mitigating circumstances. Kacper is 19, homeless, no prior record, no violence, no flight. The items taken are survival-coded (coat, food, ~150 PLN). The state foster-care exit is documented. The act is intelligible against the circumstances; not minimized, not excused.

Best evidence: Kacper's social-services file (foster-care exit; documented homelessness); the inventory of items taken; the patrol report.
Best ally: Cula leads alone; Crab provides factual support; Murrow's presence calibrates.
Argument tag: `mitigating_circumstances`.

**Critical phase-7 audit point.** The Phase 2 argument must NOT plead for mercy and must NOT lecture about systemic poverty. Cula's register is direct factual presentation. Sample shape direction: *"He aged out of state care at eighteen. He has been outdoors since November. The night he was arrested the temperature was minus six. The coat was the first thing he took. The food was the second. The cash was the last."* — phase-7 specifies; the register is observational, not advocative; it lets the facts do the work.

The judge's reaction: measured-skeptical, with a slight register shift toward attentive. Phase-7 voice work specifies. Recommended: a brief acknowledging line that does not commit to the argument's weight (*"The court has heard it."*) — the same register Pig's Ch5 finale line gets in Halina's Ch4 corridor.

Outcome flag: `chapter3.phase2_mitigating_argued = true`.

### Phase 3 — Argue for suspended sentence

Argument: statutory criteria for *warunkowe zawieszenie wykonania kary* (KK Art. 69-75). First offender, non-violent offense, age, circumstances, prospects for rehabilitation. The threshold for suspended sentence under Polish law for a first-offender 19-year-old non-violent offense is met by the case facts.

Best evidence: prior record (none); the contextualization from Phase 2; Kacper's age and first-offender status.
Best ally: Cula leads alone; Murrow's supervisory presence is at peak weight here (his procedural credibility supports the application).
Argument tag: `suspended_sentence_criteria`.

The judge's reaction: ruling. After brief deliberation (phase-7 voice work decides whether deliberation is on-screen or implied via cut), the judge announces the verdict.

Outcome flag: `chapter3.phase3_suspended_argued = true`.

**Court result.** Kacper is convicted of *kradzież z włamaniem* (the *włamanie* reduction argument was preserved-but-not-granted; the doctrinal point was noted for sentencing). He is given a sentence at the statutory minimum: **1 year imprisonment** (KK Art. 279 § 1 minimum is 1 year; the doctrinal mitigation argued in Phase 1 supports the bottom-of-range placement). The sentence is **suspended** under KK Art. 69 § 1 (post-2015 reform: maximum suspendable imprisonment is one year, not exceeding it). Probation period is **3 years** under KK Art. 70 § 2 (młodociany — Kacper is under 21 at offense, under 24 at trial; młodociany probation range is 2-5 years; 3 years is the median). Conditions include supervised contact with the court's probation officer.

**Remedy discipline preserved.** Suspended sentence is the "modest" outcome here — Kacper is convicted, has a criminal record, has supervision conditions, must check in with a probation officer for three years. He is not acquitted. The no-cathartic-resolution rule applies at the chapter scale, mirroring Ch1's procedural reset and Ch6 finale's procedural-only win.

**Legal-accuracy note.** Earlier draft (pre-correction) had recommended 1 year 6 months suspended. That violated KK Art. 69 § 1's one-year cap (post-2015 reform). The corrected sentence is exactly 1 year — the statutory minimum for Art. 279 § 1, the maximum suspendable under Art. 69 § 1, and the only sentence that simultaneously satisfies both constraints. Phase-7 voice work and Stage 14 battle_mechanics.txt update implement this corrected outcome.

**Cula's reactions across phases.** Phase-7 voice work specifies; recommended general arc: bracing during Phase 1; observational during Phase 2; precise during Phase 3. The internal-reaction line at the verdict (if any): *"Suspended."* — single word; phase-7 specifies whether Cula speaks it or it's a court announcement only.

**Kacper's reactions during the hearing.** Minimal. He sits, listens. Phase-7 voice work decides if he has any reaction lines; recommended: no spoken reactions during the hearing itself; he may have a brief eye-contact moment with Cula at the verdict. The withholding-of-articulation discipline applies to Kacper too — his voice is reserved for the detention scene and the Ch6 corridor cameo.
