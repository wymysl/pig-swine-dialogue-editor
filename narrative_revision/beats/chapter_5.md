**This is the recoloring chapter.** The chapter-4 inflection plants in the player; chapter 5 makes the office and the firm's people read differently to Cula (and the player) on each return. The chapter as written takes the player to new spaces (airport, embassy, penthouse) and returns to the office without explicitly using those returns as recoloring surfaces. **Phase 4 must add three retraversal beats** at Beats 0, 10, and 16, plus pin the Pro-Bono Stub specifics and the Plotek case's chapter-5 procedural resolution.

The chapter's spine integration hinges on the Pro-Bono Transfer Stub (Beat 8) as the partial-redemption-of-Swine artifact that *complicates without resolving* the chapter-4 inflection. Mr. Pig must be *genuinely surprised* by Swine's redirections — his austerity sincere, his blind spot intact regardless. The chapter does not redeem Mr. Pig; it adds layers to the firm's situation that Mr. Pig has not seen.

1. **The Pro-Bono Transfer Stub recipient list is diversified** (phase-6 revision for Hit #13). The original phase-4 plan named Stowarzyszenie Pomocy Lokatorom Warszawskim as the sole recipient. Phase 6 flagged that as too tidy — the redirections felt staged rather than organic. Phase-6 revision: **one primary recipient + several smaller secondaries across diverse causes**:
   - **Stowarzyszenie Pomocy Lokatorom Warszawskim** (fictional Warsaw Tenants' Aid Association) — primary. Thematically connects to Waldek's case-type but is not the only thread.
   - A refugee legal aid center (specific name TBD in phase 7 — recommended: a fictional but plausible Polish/Warsaw shape, e.g., *Centrum Pomocy Prawnej dla Uchodźców*).
   - A domestic violence shelter's legal fund (recommended: *Fundacja Schronienia*).
   - An environmental rights group (recommended: *Stowarzyszenie Obrony Środowiska Mazowsza* or similar).
   - One or two additional small recipients across years.

   The visible Stub at Beat 8 shows one specific transfer (3,400 PLN to Stowarzyszenie Pomocy Lokatorom Warszawskim, 18 months ago) — which lands the Waldek thematic connection — but Crab's Beat-10 Pro-Bono Ledger placement at chapter 6 reveals the diversified recipient list. Swine cared diffusely. The Waldek connection is one of several; the player feels it without the writer underlining it.
2. **Total redirected: approximately 95,000 PLN over 4 years.** Multi-year pattern across multiple smaller transfers. Material to the firm's reserves but not catastrophic. 3. **Mr. Pig did not know.** The redirections happened through Swine's discretionary expenses (the postcards, the travel, "consultancies"). Pig accepted the cover stories. His austerity is sincere; his blind spot intact.
4. **Plotek's case resolves in chapter 5** with a procedural win. The warrant motion granted; key evidence excluded; Plotek likely walks on the most serious charges. Resolution beat placed at chapter-5 Beat 10 retraversal.
5. **Three retraversal beats are inserted: Beat 0 (post-chapter-4 office state), Beat 10 (mid-chapter office return), Beat 16 (chapter-end office return).** Each has specific phase-4 framing. No editorialization from Cula; the player feels the layering.

7. **Swine's Beat 16 partial truth** — phase 4 specifies what he admits and what he withholds. Per Swine bible: cheerful-evasion-on-motivation; admit redirection, name the cause, withhold the why-not-told-Pig.
