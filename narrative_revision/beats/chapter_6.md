2. **Whimsy closing support follows Cula's opening.** Phase 7 voice work: theatrical-cynic flourish that locks the procedural argument in memorable rights language. The pairing rule from style_canon §2 — Whimsy is sharper paired with Murrow (structure) — applies. Whimsy's flourish reinforces *the procedural argument*, not the social-utility argument. Recommended register: a flourish that names the four legal defects together as a single proposition.

3. **Mr. Pig's witness line — the canonical sincere line.** *"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."* (style_canon §1 pass examples). **Phase 4 protects the line. Phase 4 also explicitly de-emphasizes its load-bearing weight in Round 5.** The line is staged as *character moment*, not as *basis of the remedy*. Specifically:
   - Pig is called to testify briefly. He is given perhaps thirty seconds.
   - He delivers the line.
   - **The judge's response is dry acknowledgment, not validation.** Recommended phase 7 shape (phase 7 specifies exact text): *"Counsel will note the testimony of Mr. Pig. The court has heard it."* The judge does not say the line is moving; the judge does not say the firm should be saved on its account; the judge files it.
   - The maritime register is absent (per Pig bible).
   - The line lands as Pig being Pig. The player feels its sincerity. The judge does not reward it.

4. **Mr. Swine's witness line — second sincere line of chapter 6.** *"I was not avoiding responsibility. I was avoiding accounting, which is similar but less noble."* (style_canon §1 pass examples). Phase 4 protects the line. Same de-emphasis as Pig's: Swine is called to testify briefly; he delivers the line; the judge's response is dry acknowledgment. The line is character, not basis. 5. **The judge announces the remedy** after both witness lines. **The remedy is grounded explicitly in the legal defects from Rounds 1-4.** Phase 7 voice work for the judge's remedy announcement is high-priority. Recommended shape (phase 7 specifies exact text): the judge cites the defective notice (Round 1), the lack of individual assessment (Round 2), the technical suppression (Round 3), and the conflict of interest (Round 4) as cumulative grounds, then orders the scheme set aside or amended accordingly. **The firm's social utility is not cited.** The judge's tone is dry surprise per style_canon §6 court design principles.

**Result of the revision:**
- Pig's sincere line still lands. The player feels its sincerity. The half-right portrait holds.
- The win is procedural. The chapter-6 closing-frame question stays posed.
- Pig's testimony is a character moment, not a magic key. The cathartic-resolution failure mode is averted.

**Phase 7 voice-work priority items for Round 5:**

- The judge's dry acknowledgment of Pig's testimony.
