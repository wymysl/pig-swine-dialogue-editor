## Beat 13 — Return to Pig & Swine — celebration + ledger + fee reference

### Beat 13 — opening (team returns from court; office at canonical chaos distance)

*The Pig & Swine open-office area is almost exactly as Cula left it, which makes the change in the room more noticeable. The case board still has Whimsy's third-clause note. Asia is at reception with two stacks of folders, sandwich labels, a calendar page, and the day's mail spread in a practical fan. Her hands keep moving. Murrow is at his desk, one drawer half-open, the ledger not yet visible. Crab is at his work table, appendix returned to its working pile. Whimsy has set the Rights Memo binder near the case board. The coffee machine makes a low sound and stops before anyone has to take a position on it.*

*Cula enters with Crab and Whimsy. Murrow looks up once. Asia slides a blue folder into a labelled stack without stopping.*

**ASIA:** Good work, Dr. Cula; the blue file is back on your desk.

**DR. A. CULA:** Thank you, Asia.

*Mr. Pig appears in his office doorway with a handkerchief in one hand and a file in the other. He sees the team, stops, and collects himself for a beat that almost holds.*

**MR. PIG:** You are back. Good. Good.

*He steps into the open-office area. The file under his arm slips; he catches it against his side and carries on as if that was the intended method.*

### Beat 13 — Mr. Pig's celebration (H4 firm-first / client-second / return-to-anxiety)

**MR. PIG:** We have come through another impossible thing. The client — yes, the client — also remains housed until the next date. The rent will breathe; the printer can wait; the 5,000 PLN Sikorska fee came through. Mr. Swine's retainer is still — somewhere. Sea of Japan. Presumably alive.

*Cula receives the celebration without comment. Asia's hands continue through the folder spines. Crab turns one page of the appendix. Whimsy looks from the case board to Pig as if he has just heard an overture tuning itself.*

### Beat 13 — reactions (Asia sorting; Crab indifferent; Whimsy proposes the musical)

*Asia tucks one sandwich label under the corner of the blue file and reaches for the next stack.*

**ASIA:** I'll mark the court folder returned before lunch.

*Crab closes the appendix, squares it with the edge of his table, and does not look pleased.*

**CRAB:** The countersignature carried the floor. The order does what the ground required.

*Whimsy lifts one finger toward the case board. He does not touch it.*

**WHIMSY:** I propose the matter as a chamber operetta. The bailiff sings the renumbering from the wrong doorway.

**DR. A. CULA:** Heavy.

*The coffee machine clicks once. Nobody gives it a line.*

### Beat 13 — Murrow's ledger update (visual; no dialogue)

*Murrow opens the drawer fully. The ledger comes out, dark cover first, then the blotter beneath it. He turns to a marked page. The page is angled away from Cula and unreadable from the floor. Murrow writes one short notation. He waits for the ink to settle, closes the ledger, returns it to the drawer, and closes the drawer with one hand.*

*No one speaks during the notation. Pig's handkerchief folds and unfolds in his hand. Asia sorts. Crab writes one short mark in the appendix margin. Whimsy's lifted finger lowers.*

### Beat 13 — Murrow's brief post-update line to Cula

*Murrow turns from the closed drawer to the next folder on his desk.*

**MURROW:** Cula. The Borowski file is on your desk for tomorrow.

**DR. A. CULA:** Mr. Murrow.

*Murrow has already opened the folder.*

### Beat 13 — Mr. Pig's exit

*Pig looks from Murrow's desk to Asia's folders, then toward his own office, as if the room has reminded him of several future disasters in strict order.*

**MR. PIG:** Yes. Yes, the next thing. Also the retainer. Possibly the same thing.

*He drifts back toward his office, still holding the handkerchief, the file now under his other arm. The office resumes its ordinary volume around him.*

## Beat 14 — Swine postcard stinger

### Beat 14 — postcard arrival (Asia announces and reads the address label)

*A short interval later. The celebration has dispersed back into work. Asia opens the last piece of mail in the fan, pauses only long enough to read the address label, and keeps the remaining envelopes moving with her other hand.*

**ASIA:** Mr. Pig. Postcard. The address says Sapporo.

*She sets the postcard on the near edge of Pig's desk as he emerges again, already reaching for it.*

### Beat 14 — Mr. Pig reads the postcard aloud

*Pig turns the postcard over. The front shows a snowy building and a sign too small to read at this distance. Pig reads the body aloud, sincerely and without understanding the timing.*

**MR. PIG:** "Greetings from Sapporo. A very serious gentleman in the lobby has proposed a venture involving conference centers and possibly fish, and I have given him my card on principle. Please keep the firm solvent until I clarify which principle. Yours, S."

*Asia's hands continue through the envelopes. Murrow does not look up from the Borowski folder. Crab reopens the appendix. Cula says nothing.*

**MR. PIG:** The Sea of Japan, then.

**WHIMSY:** Behold. The postcard.

*Pig lowers the postcard to the desk beside the handkerchief. For a moment the Sapporo stamp faces the room. Then the room keeps working.*

### Beat 14 — chapter close

*The postcard remains on Pig's desk. The ledger drawer is closed. Asia's mail stack is thinner. The case board still carries the third-clause note until someone erases it for the next matter. The office does not celebrate again.*

**BADGE:** Day-One Survivor.

**CHAPTER ROUTE UNLOCKS:** Residential, Business District, Court Plaza.
