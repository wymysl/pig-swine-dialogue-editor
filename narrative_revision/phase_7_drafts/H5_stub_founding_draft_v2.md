**Pack:** H5 (Tier 2 horizontal). **Status:** v2 after first hostile critique. 10 of 11 critique items accepted; item #3 (Whimsy "three of us against the silence") rejected on the grounds that §F bible and §B per-character notes explicitly canonicalise this phrasing as the heavier-than-expected sincere-as-theatrical line, which the deflationary surround ("smallest possible echo," napkin-scale, STUB abbreviation) is designed to hold up.

## PART 1 — Chapter 4, Beat 11.5: STUB founding

### Setting note

Pig & Swine office, coffee corner. ~30–45 minutes after Beat 11. Late evening, deeper than the inflection beat. The urgent filing has been couriered out; Cula is en route to the District Court for the next hearing. Mr. Pig is asleep at his desk, offstage. Murrow is at the courier dispatch overseeing the delivery, offstage. Asia is at reception finalizing courier paperwork, within earshot of the coffee corner. The mood is depleted relief.

No music sting. No scene-marking camera move.

---

### Step 1 — coffee corner; conditions stated

*Crab leans against the counter. Whimsy is rinsing a mug that has been rinsed before, possibly tonight, possibly never. The espresso machine is cold.*

CRAB: We are doing the lawyering. On no sleep.

WHIMSY: A small, comprehensive list.

CRAB: The printer is being sabotaged by a rival firm. We have been paid in postcards this month.

WHIMSY: From a man currently in Bali.

CRAB: Postcards do not clear payroll.

---

### Step 2 — the founding

WHIMSY: Then I propose we found the smallest possible echo of *Solidarność*. Three of us against the silence. It will fit on a napkin. Solidarity Trade Union of Barristers. STUB.

*Beat. Crab considers this with the equanimity of a man who has just been offered a free pen.*

CRAB: It costs nothing.

WHIMSY: A napkin.

CRAB: Fine. I can sign that.

---

### Step 3 — the napkin

*Whimsy produces a black ballpoint and a paper napkin from the stack by the espresso machine. He draws: a small pig in profile, holding a tiny gavel, with the letters S-T-U-B in block capitals beneath. He inspects the result, then makes a small correction to the gavel.*

WHIMSY: Behold.

CRAB: It is a pig with a gavel.

WHIMSY: It is *the* pig with the gavel.

---

### Step 4 — phone call to Cula

*Whimsy lifts the desk phone. Crab is already pulling the napkin to safer ground, away from a coffee ring.*

WHIMSY (into phone): Counsel. We are founding a union.

CULA (phone, distant): Now?

WHIMSY: Now. The napkin is still clean enough.

CULA: Understood.

CRAB (over Whimsy's shoulder, into the phone): It costs nothing.

CULA: I will sign in the morning.

*Click. Phone returned to cradle.*

---

### Step 5 — Asia at reception

*Crab carries the napkin to reception. Asia is at her desk, sorting courier slips into a folder marked DELIVERED. She is cheerful in the same way she has been cheerful for eleven hours.*

CRAB: We are forming a union. You are invited.

ASIA: Oh — is there paperwork?

CRAB: One napkin.

ASIA: Lovely.

*She finishes the slip she's on, sets the folder aside, and looks at the napkin Crab is holding. The look is not long.*

ASIA: Front-desk staff have negotiating power that lawyers cannot match — I have the password to the photocopier.

*She returns to the courier slips.*

---

### Step 6 — pin to cork board

*Crab walks the napkin back to the coffee corner. Whimsy takes it. The cork board carries: Mr. Pig's chamber-of-commerce calendar (two months expired), a notice about elevator maintenance, a Polaroid of a man Crab does not recognize. Whimsy pushes a thumbtack through the napkin's upper edge and pins it dead-centre, displacing the elevator notice by an inch. Crab watches the thumbtack hold.*

---

## PART 2 — Chapter 6 callback: Crab's grievance-#4 line

### The line

*Chapter 6, Beat 0 retraversal. The cork board is visible as Crab passes the case-files cabinet. He does not stop.*

CRAB (glancing at the board, walking past): Grievance #4. Still unresolved.

*Cula does not respond. Crab continues to the case-files cabinet.*

### Recommended placement

Beat 0 retraversal (preferred) — the cumulative office-state pass already foregrounds the cork board and the printer sticker; a passing Crab line during traversal lands as part of the visual pass rather than as plot. Beat 10 reveal is workable as a fallback but has higher emotional weight nearby and would risk the line registering as setup.

### Discipline notes

- ONE line. The "Grievance #4. Still unresolved." formulation uses Crab's two-sentence rhythm (number, then status); the implication (the pile of unresolved grievances) is left for the player.
- Grievance #4 is not specified. The number is the joke; the subject stays unstated.
- No setup-and-punchline structure. The line is delivered while walking; Cula does not respond; the player carries the recognition.
