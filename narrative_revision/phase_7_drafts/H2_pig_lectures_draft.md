# Phase 7 H2 — Mr. Pig's "back in my day" lectures (revised draft, post-§J)

Five lecture moments authored as a coherent set. Each is sincere as memory; the recoloring lands at the chapter-5 family-photo discovery and is paid off by the chapter-6 absence.

**Revision history.** First draft used Polish loan-words (aplikatura, wokanda, sąd rejonowy, pełnomocnictwo) and the name "Wilkanowicz," following the H2 pack's "Polish-aplikatura specificity" framing. The H2 pack predates the §1.5 language convention in `ai_voice_constraints.md` (committed 2026-05-05), which translates Polish legal vocabulary to English equivalents in the English version and renames Wilkanowicz → Borowski. This revision applies §1.5 throughout, plus the §J staging fixes. Open calls retained where the §J critique was rejected on grounds independent of localization.

## Lecture 1 — Chapter 1, Beat 8 (readiness check)

**Theme:** no readiness check, only fear.
**Maritime density:** low (none in the lecture itself; Pig's earlier kraken/printer-lease aside carries the maritime in the wider scene).
**Embedded in:** Murrow's readiness-check downtime, while she works through the binder index with Cula.

> **Murrow:** *(to Cula, opening the binder)* Powers of attorney, signed. Court fees, paid. Witness order, confirmed.
>
> **Mr. Pig:** *(hovering at the edge of the desk, half to Cula and half to the air)* At Master Borowski's no one ran a checklist. My first appearance was a substitution at district court, and I read the power of attorney on the tram. Murrow, do continue.
>
> **Murrow:** *(without looking up)* Service of the witness summons.

**Junior reactions:** silence. Cula does not respond; the line lands as Pig being Pig and the readiness check resumes.

## Lecture 2 — Chapter 2, around Beat 10 (office return)

**Theme:** no rota, only mutual cover.
**Maritime density:** moderate ("bailed" in the lecture; the surrounding scene carries more — the printer is making noises Pig is interpreting as nautical).
**Embedded in:** Pig wandering past the juniors' shared desk on his way to his coffee. Cula, Crab, Whimsy have just dumped their day's notes from Tenants' Row, Waldek's apartment, the Kowalski + Zielinska interviews, and the Landlord's Office.

> **Mr. Pig:** *(passing behind Cula's chair, not stopping, an empty cup in hand)* We had no rota. Four apprentices to one desk and we bailed each other's case lists without being asked; that was the solidarity. Who emptied the kettle —
>
> *(He wanders off toward the kitchenette. Cula keeps sorting the eviction file. Crab marks the top sheet. Whimsy keeps reading.)*

**Junior reactions:** silence.

## Lecture 3 — Chapter 4, Beat 11 (urgent filing prep)

**Theme:** toner-rationing — magenta as privilege.
**Maritime density:** high. The lecture sits inside the panic; the panic and the lecture share vocabulary.
**Embedded in:** Pig's printer-lease maritime crisis, mid-assembly of the urgent filing. Cula has, fifteen minutes earlier, glimpsed the 285,400 PLN reserve on Murrow's open ledger. The irony is the player's; Pig does not have it.

> **Mr. Pig:** *(at his desk corner, holding the lease quote at arm's length as though it might bite)* This lease will run us aground. We rationed magenta by the page at Borowski's, and counted yellow on Fridays against the logbook. A logbook for toner.
>
> *(Murrow steps out for the courier signature. Pig does not stop staring at the quote. The juniors continue assembling the filing.)*

**Junior reactions:** silence. Cula does not reply, does not glance toward the operating-account ledger, does not catch Crab's or Whimsy's eye. The reserve number sits in Cula's head; the line about the logbook sits in Pig's mouth; the two do not meet in the scene.

## Lecture 4 — Chapter 5, Beat 0 (retraversal, post-family-photo focal moment)

**Theme:** the forging.
**Maritime density:** low (Pig is subdued post-printer-apology and post-Swine-bill-arrival; the lecture itself has no nautical material; the maritime sits in the idle line about the new bill that immediately precedes the lecture).
**Embedded in:** the Beat 0 retraversal. Cula has just walked into the office. The framed family photo on Pig's desk is in the same beat — examined or not, depending on player choice. Pig's idle line about the new Swine bill has just been delivered.

> **Mr. Pig:** *(looking down at the desk, not at Cula; the Swine bill in one hand and a pen he is not using in the other)* The apprenticeship is supposed to forge you. We had three chairs for five apprentices and no one petitioned the master about the seating; we considered it part of the forging. I am forged, Cula.
>
> *(He sets the bill down, picks it up again, sets it down. The courier's footsteps are not yet audible in the corridor.)*

**Junior reactions:** silence. Cula does not respond to the line; if Cula has examined the photo, Cula has new context but does not show it; if Cula has not examined the photo, the lecture is sincere and the recoloring waits. Either way, no comment.

## Lecture 5 — Chapter 6, Beat 14 (final speech: lecture **absent**)

**Theme:** none. The pattern is absent. Pig tries to speak plain, finds nothing to fall back on, and is interrupted before he can recover.
**Maritime density:** none. (Per Pig bible's chapter-6 sincere-mode rule.)
**Embedded in:** the Beat 14 closing sequence. Returning NPCs have spoken; Grzyb's beige card has been read; Szpon's headline and Plotek's headline have appeared; the printer's final message has printed and been handed around. Pig stands.

> **Mr. Pig:** *(stands, both hands on the back of a chair)* We have done the work. The firm — the firm —
>
> *(He looks at the room, loses the sentence, and does not rebuild it.)*
>
> **Mr. Swine:** *(reading aloud from the printer's last postcard, gentle and unaware)* "Greetings from Pig & Swine, Warsaw…"
>
> *(Pig lets the interruption stand.)*

**Junior reactions:** silence. None of them fills the gap; none of them visibly registers what Pig was reaching for. The half-line is left where it falls.

## Set-level notes

- Themes: readiness, solidarity-as-cover, rationing, forging, absence. No two share. ✓
- Three sentences max each lecture. ✓ (Ch1: 3. Ch2: 3 + trailing fragment. Ch4: 3. Ch5: 3. Ch6: half-line.)
- Sincere on first pass: each. ✓
- Master Borowski named in Ch1 and Ch4; Ch2 and Ch5 use "we" / "the master." Two named anchors give the player a memory to carry into the photo recoloring.
- Generic uplifting endings: none. Ch1 deflects to Murrow. Ch2 trails on the kettle. Ch4 ends on the rhetorical fragment "A logbook for toner." Ch5 ends on Pig's self-presentation "I am forged, Cula" — character-specific, not generic moral.
- Em-dashes per lecture-paragraph: Ch1: 0. Ch2: 1 (trailing kettle). Ch4: 0. Ch5: 0. Ch6: 1 (prescribed in the half-line).
- Family wealth: not referenced. Borowski named without relation disclosed.
- Self-aware meta-references: none. Pig does not know he is giving a lecture.
- Comparison made explicit: never. Pig describes only his own conditions.
- Junior reactions: silence across all five.
- Framing-around-firm: Ch1 about training (the firm's apprenticeship); Ch2 about cover within the work; Ch4 about firm resources (toner); Ch5 about the apprenticeship forging the lawyer; Ch6 about "the firm." None turn outward to clients' welfare. ✓

## Open calls retained for a second pass

1. **Ch5 ending: "I am forged, Cula."** §J recommended cutting to "We called that training." Rejected: "we called that" introduces self-ironic distance Pig doesn't have. The bible explicitly samples "Look at me — I am forged" as canonical (`bibles/pig.md` theme list). Held as is. If a second read flags the line as still too declarative, the de-risking move is to drop the vocative ("I am forged.") rather than reach for the §J replacement.

2. **Ch2 trailing em-dash on "Who emptied the kettle —".** §J recommended a hard period for irritability. Held as em-dash because Pig's pattern is to wander off mid-thought and the trailing dash is the audible cue for that wandering. If a second read finds the dash too rhythmic-AI, swap to period.

3. **Borowski name distribution.** Ch1 + Ch4 only. Ch5 holds the "master" reference without surname so the chairs and the "I am forged" landing carry the specificity. If a second read wants the surname reinforced before the photo, add "at Borowski's" to Ch5's middle sentence.

4. **Junior silence.** No flourishes. If a later pass finds any lecture too dry, Ch2 is the natural slot for one Whimsy aside in cynic register, after Pig has wandered out of earshot. Holding the discipline by default.
