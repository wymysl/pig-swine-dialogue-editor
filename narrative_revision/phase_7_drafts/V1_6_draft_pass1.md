## Beat 12 — First court battle

### Beat 12 — opening (court is in session; petitioner in the gallery; counsel at table)

*Courtroom four. The room is panelled in the same dust-colour wood as the embankment trees outside. The District Court Judge sits on the bench beneath a fresh bronze state seal. The clerk's desk is below the bench to its left; the witness stand is to the right and unoccupied. Counsel's table is set in two halves. Cula stands at the petitioner's half. Crab and Whimsy are seated to his right and left respectively. The Procedural Binder is open under Cula's hand. The case folder is squared on the table at his elbow. The Rights Memo binder, coffee-ring margin visible, is at Whimsy's elbow. Crab's service-and-knowledge appendix is on the table between them. The respondent's table is opposite. Landlord's counsel is a man in his fifties in dark grey, a thin folder open in front of him; he has the blandness of a practitioner who has done this twice this week. The gallery row directly behind petitioner's counsel holds Murrow, the Procedural Binder closed against his knee. Halina Sikorska is seated two seats further along the same gallery row, alone. Her folder is squared in her lap. Her handbag is on the seat beside her. Her posture is upright. Her eyes are on the bench.*

*The clerk has read the matter on the cause list. The judge has the file open on the bench. Counsel for both sides have indicated they are ready. The judge looks once at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel for the petitioner.

*Cula remains on his feet. He inclines his head.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The petitioner moves to set aside the hearing of the eighth on the ground of invalid service. Mr. Crab will present the service-and-knowledge submission.

*Cula sits. Crab rises.*

### Beat 12 — Round 1: defective service (Crab carries; three stance variants)

#### Round 1 — sympathetic carrier (Wójcik bonus)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. He does not move from behind the table. His hands rest flat on the appendix.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice was sent to number seven. The petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 sets the consequence of failure. The notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises with a thin folder.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the petitioner tenders an affidavit of the second-floor neighbour, Mrs. Wójcik. The petitioner has been continuously resident at number twelve since nineteen eighty-seven. The affidavit is for the file.

*Cula crosses to the clerk, hands up the affidavit, returns to the table. The judge does not yet acknowledge it. Landlord's counsel rises.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was received at the address by an adult occupant of the premises. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file briefly, then at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel — the renumbering and the affidavit of the second-floor neighbour. The court will note the witness. The service question is sufficient on the documents alone. The respondent will be heard on the third clause if there is anything to hear.

*Halina's eyes do not move from the bench. Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

#### Round 1 — blunt-procedural carrier (return-to-sender slip bonus)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. The slip is a separate small page on top of the appendix; the rest of the appendix is squared beneath it.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice was sent to number seven. The petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen. The landlord sent a prior letter to number seven six months ago. The post returned it. The slip is in his file; we requested it on Monday and it arrived yesterday. The renewal of two thousand and nineteen carries the corrected address and was countersigned by the landlord's office. Knowledge is in the slip and on the document; not in inference. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 sets the consequence of failure. The notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises briefly.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the slip and the renewal are tendered for the file.

*Cula hands the documents up to the clerk and returns to the table. Landlord's counsel rises. He pauses fractionally before he speaks; the slip has shortened the room he had.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was received at the address by an adult occupant of the premises. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file longer than in the prior variant. He turns one page, then back.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel — the renumbering, the prior return, and the countersignature. The court is satisfied that knowledge is not in serious dispute. The respondent's submission on the adult occupant is held over to the third clause if there is anything to hear.

*Halina's eyes do not move from the bench. Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

#### Round 1 — technical carrier (countersignature only; lease + inheritance held for Round 3)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. The renewal is on the appendix's top sheet; the lease and the inheritance papers remain in the case folder under Cula's hand.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice was sent to number seven. The petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen. The renewal of two thousand and nineteen carries the corrected address and was countersigned by the landlord's office. Knowledge is on the document. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 sets the consequence of failure. The notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises briefly.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the renewal is tendered for the file.

*Cula hands the renewal up to the clerk and returns to the table. Landlord's counsel rises.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was received at the address by an adult occupant of the premises. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file briefly, then at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel — the renumbering and the countersignature. The court is satisfied that knowledge is not in serious dispute. The respondent will be heard on the third clause if there is anything to hear.

*Halina's eyes do not move from the bench. Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

### Beat 12 — Round 2: fair hearing (Whimsy carries; Rights Memo with Coffee Ring; third-clause non-cure preemption)

*The judge looks at the petitioner's table. Cula remains seated and inclines toward Whimsy. Whimsy rises with the Rights Memo binder. The coffee-ring stain on the margin is briefly visible as he sets the binder open on the table. He leaves it flat. He does not gesture with it. He addresses the bench in a register he keeps a half-step beneath theatre.*

**WHIMSY:** Your Honour. A summons addressed to a door that is not the addressee's door is a summons sent into another person's house. The petitioner was invited to a hearing she could not have attended; the invitation reached a door that was not hers and a hand that was not hers. The respondent will say, with the law's permission, that an adult occupant of number seven accepted the notice on the petitioner's behalf. Article 135-bis § 2, third clause, addresses this circumstance. A third-party acceptance at a wrong address does not cure invalid service. The third clause was written for the case in which a notice arrives at the wrong door and is taken in by the person standing nearest. Verily — the petitioner has not been heard.

*Whimsy's hand rests on the binder. He does not sit.*

*Landlord's counsel does not rise; he has been answered. The judge looks at the binder a moment, at Whimsy a moment, at the bench file.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The metaphor is registered. The point on third-party non-cure is registered. The respondent's earlier submission on the adult occupant is answered by the third clause. The court will not hear the respondent further on the service question. Counsel for the petitioner will proceed to remedy.

*Whimsy inclines his head. He sits. Cula closes the case folder; he opens the page tabbed for the motion. Crab adjusts the appendix to make room. Halina's eyes do not move from the bench.*

---

### Beat 12 — Round 3: remedy (Cula carries; three stance variants; technical bonus surfaces here for the technical variant)

#### Round 3 — sympathetic carrier

*Cula rises. The case folder is open in front of him at the motion page. He does not lift it. His hands rest on the table. The opening register is short; the law-arguing register lengthens as he proceeds.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The court is asked to set aside the hearing of the eighth on the ground of invalid service and to reschedule on properly served notice. The petitioner has been continuously resident in the building since nineteen eighty-seven. The motion does not ask the court to determine the underlying tenancy; it asks only that the petitioner be heard.

*Cula remains on his feet. He does not say anything further. The judge looks at the bench file. He turns one page.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service per Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The matter is rescheduled. The new hearing date will be assigned by the clerk of the court within the statutory window. Counsel for the respondent will be served the new date in the ordinary course.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap. Her eyes do not move.*

---

#### Round 3 — blunt-procedural carrier

*Cula rises. The case folder is open at the motion page. His hands rest on the table.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The court is asked to set aside the hearing on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The motion does not reach the merits. The motion asks for the rescheduled hearing on properly served notice.

*Cula remains on his feet. The judge looks at the bench file. He turns one page.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service per Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The matter is rescheduled. The new hearing date will be assigned by the clerk of the court within the statutory window. Counsel for the respondent will be served the new date in the ordinary course.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap. Her eyes do not move.*

---

#### Round 3 — technical carrier (lease + inheritance bonus surfaces)

*Cula rises. The case folder is open at the motion page; the lease and inheritance papers are in his hand. He sets them on the table beside the motion.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The chain of tenancy runs from nineteen sixty-two; the inheritance paperwork is from nineteen eighty-seven; the renewal is from two thousand and nineteen. The court is asked to set aside the hearing of the eighth on the ground of invalid service and to reschedule on properly served notice. The underlying tenancy is not at issue today.

*Cula remains on his feet. He does not say anything further. The judge looks at the bench file longer than in the prior variants. He turns two pages.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service per Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The matter is rescheduled. The new hearing date will be assigned by the clerk of the court within the statutory window. Counsel for the respondent will be served the new date in the ordinary course. The court notes that the underlying tenancy is not before it on this motion.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap. Her eyes do not move.*

### Beat 12 — closing (the court rises; the gallery rises; the team gathers documents; transition to V1.7)

*The judge closes the bench file. The clerk rises. The judge rises. The bailiff calls the court to its feet. Counsel and gallery rise.*

*Halina rises with the gallery. She does not weep. She does not turn toward the respondent's table. She holds the folder against her hip; her handbag is over her shoulder. Her posture is upright. The slight stiffness she carries is the same stiffness she carried into the courtroom.*

*The judge exits through the door at the rear of the bench. The clerk follows. The state seal stays where it is.*

*Cula closes the case folder. Crab stacks the appendix and the slip and the renewal in the order they were received. Whimsy closes the Rights Memo binder; the coffee-ring margin disappears. Landlord's counsel collects his thin folder, inclines briefly toward the petitioner's table without making eye contact with the petitioner, and leaves the room. Murrow lifts the Procedural Binder from his knee and stands; he meets Cula's eye across the rail; he does not speak.*

*The team files out of counsel's table — Cula first with the case folder, Crab and Whimsy behind. Halina falls in beside them at the courtroom door. Murrow brings up the rear. None of them speak in the corridor. The doors close behind the last of them.*
