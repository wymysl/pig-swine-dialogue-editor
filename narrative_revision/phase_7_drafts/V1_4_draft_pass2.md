### Beat 9 — Murrow's clause-by-clause narration

#### First clause — invalid-service definition

**MURROW:** Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The first clause defines invalid service: the address used must have been altered, renumbered, or otherwise made non-current to the knowledge of the serving party. The renumbering on Mrs. Sikorska's building was in two thousand and fifteen; the notice was sent to the pre-renumbering address eleven years later. The post returned a previous letter to the landlord at the same wrong address six months ago; the slip is producible from his file by request. Knowledge is not in serious dispute.

#### Second clause — motion plus the fourteen-day window

**MURROW:** The second clause sets the motion procedure. A hearing held upon invalid service shall, on motion of the party not properly served, be set aside and rescheduled, provided the motion is filed within fourteen days of actual notice. Mrs. Sikorska received actual notice on the twenty-eighth of April; the fourteenth day is the eleventh of May. The fourteen-day window is the floor. We file Thursday.

#### Third clause — third-party non-cure

**MURROW:** The third clause preempts the obvious defence. The appearance of a third party at the served address shall not be considered as curing service unless that party held the express authority of the served party. The young man at number seven brought the envelope down because it was on his floormat; he was not authorised. The non-cure clause closes the landlord's defence before he reaches it.

*A small pause. Cula closes the binder a quarter-inch and opens it again to keep his place.*

**DR. A. CULA:** All three.

**MURROW:** All three. The motion writes itself.

#### Option B — "Have you used this clause before?"
