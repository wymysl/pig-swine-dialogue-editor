**Pass-1 critique disposition.**

Critical fixes applied:

1. **First court citation corrected.** Pass 1 first cited `Article 135-bis § 2` in Crab's Round-1 line without the full "of the Code of Civil Procedure" form. Pass 2 makes the first citation in each Round-1 branch full-form.
2. **Crab's bench rhythm compressed.** Pass 1 let Crab sprawl into several short courtroom sentences, especially in the blunt-procedural branch. Pass 2 restores the salutation plus two argument sentences: fact stack, consequence.
3. **Crab's "Knowledge is in the slip..." antithesis cut.** The pass-1 line was useful but too close to stylised contrastive proof-language. Pass 2 replaces it with documentary fact inside the first argument sentence.
4. **Cula Round-3 remedy asks scrubbed of closing-speech pressure.** Pass 1 used "does not ask... asks only..." and related negative/positive framing. Pass 2 makes each ask a narrow procedural request and stops.
5. **Whimsy Round-2 flourish simplified.** Pass 1's "with the law's permission," "third clause was written for," and "Verily" were ornamental or overclaiming. Pass 2 keeps the wrong-door/nearest-hand metaphor and ties it directly to third-party non-cure.
6. **Judge Round-2 response sharpened.** Pass 1 repeated "registered" twice and risked becoming mechanical rather than dry-surprised. Pass 2 gives one dry lift, then rules on the adult-occupant submission.
7. **Wójcik handling clarified.** Pass 1's judge line "The court will note the witness" made a document sound like live testimony. Pass 2 says the affidavit is noted.
8. **Blunt-procedural staging cooled.** Pass 1's "the slip has shortened the room he had" was authorial metaphor in a procedural scene. Pass 2 leaves only the pause.
9. **Closing symbolism cut.** Pass 1's "The state seal stays where it is" leaned toward thematic stage whisper. Pass 2 removes it and keeps the procedural exit.

Marginals applied:

- Halina's repeated "eyes do not move from the bench" beats were thinned. Her silence and posture still carry the dignity discipline without becoming a refrain.
- The landlord's counsel line was tightened slightly so it remains plausible across all three Round-1 variants.
- The remedy announcements were shortened after the first corrected citation, reducing courtroom boilerplate without changing the order.

Marginals held as defensible:

- **Courtroom four / cause-list continuity.** Held from V1.5 pass 2.
- **Dust-colour courtroom wood.** Borderline stylised, but it ties the courtroom to Beat 11's embankment approach and does not intrude into dialogue.
- **Halina's age-stiffness in closing.** Held as V1.4/V1.5 dignity-continuity staging, not a new biographical aside.

## Beat 12 — First court battle

### Beat 12 — opening (court is in session; petitioner in the gallery; counsel at table)

*Courtroom four. The room is panelled in the same dust-colour wood as the embankment trees outside. The District Court Judge sits on the bench beneath a fresh bronze state seal. The clerk's desk is below the bench to its left; the witness stand is to the right and unoccupied. Counsel's table is set in two halves. Cula stands at the petitioner's half. Crab and Whimsy are seated to his right and left respectively. The Procedural Binder is open under Cula's hand. The case folder is squared on the table at his elbow. The Rights Memo binder, coffee-ring margin visible, is at Whimsy's elbow. Crab's service-and-knowledge appendix is on the table between them. The respondent's table is opposite. Landlord's counsel is a man in his fifties in dark grey, a thin folder open in front of him; he has the blandness of a practitioner who has done this twice this week. The gallery row directly behind petitioner's counsel holds Murrow, the Procedural Binder closed against his knee. Halina Sikorska is seated two seats further along the same gallery row, alone. Her folder is squared in her lap. Her handbag is on the seat beside her. Her posture is upright. Her eyes are on the bench.*

*The clerk has read the matter on the cause list. The judge has the file open on the bench. Counsel for both sides have indicated they are ready. The judge looks once at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel for the petitioner.

*Cula remains on his feet. He inclines his head.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The petitioner moves to set aside the hearing of the eighth on the ground of invalid service. Mr. Crab will present the service-and-knowledge submission.

*Cula sits. Crab rises.*

---

### Beat 12 — Round 1: defective service (Crab carries; three stance variants)

#### Round 1 — sympathetic carrier (Wójcik bonus)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. He does not move from behind the table. His hands rest flat on the appendix.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice went to number seven; the petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure sets the consequence of failed service; the notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises with a thin folder.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the petitioner tenders Mrs. Wójcik's affidavit. It confirms continuous residence at number twelve since nineteen eighty-seven.

*Cula crosses to the clerk, hands up the affidavit, and returns to the table. The judge does not yet acknowledge it. Landlord's counsel rises.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was accepted by an adult occupant at the address used for service. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file briefly, then at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The affidavit is noted. The renumbering is enough for the service question on the documents. The respondent will be heard on the third clause if there is anything to hear.

*Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

#### Round 1 — blunt-procedural carrier (return-to-sender slip bonus)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. The slip is a separate small page on top of the appendix; the rest of the appendix is squared beneath it.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice went to number seven; the petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen; a prior letter to number seven came back undelivered, and the two thousand and nineteen renewal carries the corrected address with a countersignature from the landlord's office. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure sets the consequence of failed service; on these documents, service is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises briefly.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the slip and the renewal are tendered for the file.

*Cula hands the documents up to the clerk and returns to the table. Landlord's counsel rises. He pauses before he speaks.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was accepted by an adult occupant at the address used for service. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file longer than in the prior variant. He turns one page, then back.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The renumbering, the prior return, and the countersignature are before the court. Knowledge is not in serious dispute. The respondent's adult-occupant point is held over to the third clause if there is anything to hear.

*Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

#### Round 1 — technical carrier (countersignature only; lease + inheritance held for Round 3)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. The renewal is on the appendix's top sheet; the lease and the inheritance papers remain in the case folder under Cula's hand.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice went to number seven; the petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen; the two thousand and nineteen renewal carries the corrected address with a countersignature from the landlord's office. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure sets the consequence of failed service; the notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises briefly.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the renewal is tendered for the file.

*Cula hands the renewal up to the clerk and returns to the table. Landlord's counsel rises.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was accepted by an adult occupant at the address used for service. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file briefly, then at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The renumbering and the countersignature are before the court. Knowledge is not in serious dispute. The respondent will be heard on the third clause if there is anything to hear.

*Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

### Beat 12 — Round 2: fair hearing (Whimsy carries; Rights Memo with Coffee Ring; third-clause non-cure preemption)

*The judge looks at the petitioner's table. Cula remains seated and inclines toward Whimsy. Whimsy rises with the Rights Memo binder. The coffee-ring stain on the margin is briefly visible as he sets the binder open on the table. He leaves it flat. He does not gesture with it. He addresses the bench in a register he keeps a half-step beneath theatre.*

**WHIMSY:** Your Honour. A summons sent to the wrong door invites the wrong household. The respondent's answer is that an adult at number seven accepted the paper. Article 135-bis § 2's third clause answers that circumstance: acceptance at the wrong address does not cure invalid service. The law does not appoint the nearest hand as an usher. The petitioner was not heard.

*Whimsy's hand rests on the binder. He does not sit.*

*Landlord's counsel does not rise; he has been answered. The judge looks at the binder a moment, at Whimsy a moment, at the bench file.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The metaphor is noted. The third-clause point is clearer than its costume suggested. The respondent's adult-occupant submission is answered by Article 135-bis § 2. The court will proceed to remedy.

*Whimsy inclines his head. He sits. Cula opens the case folder to the page tabbed for the motion. Crab adjusts the appendix to make room.*

---

### Beat 12 — Round 3: remedy (Cula carries; three stance variants; technical bonus surfaces here for the technical variant)

#### Round 3 — sympathetic carrier

*Cula rises. The case folder is open in front of him at the motion page. He does not lift it. His hands rest on the table. The opening register is short; the law-arguing register lengthens as he proceeds.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The requested order is narrow: set aside the hearing of the eighth and assign a new date on properly served notice. The petitioner has been continuously resident in the building since nineteen eighty-seven. The merits can be heard after proper service.

*Cula remains on his feet. He does not say anything further. The judge looks at the bench file. He turns one page.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2. The matter is rescheduled. The clerk will assign a new hearing date within the statutory window. Notice will be served in the ordinary course.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap.*

---

#### Round 3 — blunt-procedural carrier

*Cula rises. The case folder is open at the motion page. His hands rest on the table.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The court is asked to set aside the hearing under Article 135-bis § 2 and assign a new date on properly served notice. The requested order is a procedural reset.

*Cula remains on his feet. The judge looks at the bench file. He turns one page.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2. The matter is rescheduled. The clerk will assign a new hearing date within the statutory window. Notice will be served in the ordinary course.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap.*

---

#### Round 3 — technical carrier (lease + inheritance bonus surfaces)

*Cula rises. The case folder is open at the motion page; the lease and inheritance papers are in his hand. He sets them on the table beside the motion.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The chain of tenancy runs from nineteen sixty-two; the inheritance paperwork is from nineteen eighty-seven; the renewal is from two thousand and nineteen. Those documents support the procedural reset requested today. The court is asked to set aside the hearing of the eighth and assign a new date on properly served notice.

*Cula remains on his feet. He does not say anything further. The judge looks at the bench file longer than in the prior variants. He turns two pages.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2. The matter is rescheduled. The clerk will assign a new hearing date within the statutory window. Notice will be served in the ordinary course. The underlying tenancy is not before the court on this motion.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap.*

---

### Beat 12 — closing (the court rises; the gallery rises; the team gathers documents; transition to V1.7)

*The judge closes the bench file. The clerk rises. The judge rises. The bailiff calls the court to its feet. Counsel and gallery rise.*

*Halina rises with the gallery. She does not weep. She does not turn toward the respondent's table. She holds the folder against her hip; her handbag is over her shoulder. Her posture is upright. The slight stiffness she carries is the same stiffness she carried into the courtroom.*

*The judge exits through the door at the rear of the bench. The clerk follows.*

*Cula closes the case folder. Crab stacks the appendix, the slip, and the renewal in the order they were received. Whimsy closes the Rights Memo binder; the coffee-ring margin disappears. Landlord's counsel collects his thin folder, inclines briefly toward the petitioner's table without making eye contact with the petitioner, and leaves the room. Murrow lifts the Procedural Binder from his knee and stands; he meets Cula's eye across the rail; he does not speak.*

*The team files out from counsel's table: Cula first with the case folder, Crab and Whimsy behind. Halina falls in beside them at the courtroom door. Murrow brings up the rear. None of them speak in the corridor. The doors close behind the last of them.*
