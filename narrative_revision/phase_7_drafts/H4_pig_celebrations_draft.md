## Self-check against §G cross-chapter coherence

1. **Firm-first / client-second ordering, all four expressions.**
   - Ch 1: "We have come through" → "the client also" → "the firm sails." Firm framing first via "we"; explicit "firm" in punchline.
   - Ch 2: "Another impossible thing survived" → "Waldek too" → "the firm stays afloat." Same shape.
   - Ch 4: "Another improbable thing survived" → "the arbitration too" → "the firm has weathered." Same shape.
   - Ch 5: "We have done the work. The firm — the firm —" carries the framing-around-firm pattern; arrives unfinished.

2. **No two share exact phrasing.** "Come through" / "survived" / "improbable thing survived" / "done the work" — recognizably-related, distinct lexical choices in each opening.

3. **Brief.** Ch 1: 5 sentences (3 celebration + 2 missing-retainer aside). Ch 2: 2 sentences. Ch 4: 2 sentences (the second cut by Murrow). Ch 5: half-line.

4. **Maritime density curve held.** Ch 1 moderate ("come through," "sails," "Sea of Japan"). Ch 2 light ("stays afloat"). Ch 4 moderate-to-low ("weathered"). Ch 5 absent.

5. **Return-to-anxiety beat in every full celebration except Ch 5.**
   - Ch 1: missing-retainer aside.
   - Ch 2: electric overdue.
   - Ch 4: cut off mid-rent — the off-center quality lands through interruption rather than analytic sentence-construction.
   - Ch 5: interrupted by Swine before he can return.

6. **No reference to Pig's family wealth.** ✓
7. **No diagnostic line about firm structure.** Pig does not say "the firm comes first." He frames it that way without articulating the framing. ✓
8. **Read-aloud test.** The four expressions form a sequence; the pattern lands as habit; Ch 4 is the clearest pattern moment because it is now four iterations old, lands subdued, and is cut off by the unaddressed thing — precisely the cumulative+destabilization combination the chapter-6 retraversal will recolor.

**Accepted in modified form:**
- §J #2 (em-dash count, Ch 1): dropped "is still — somewhere" em-dash; brought Ch 1 paragraph from 3 em-dashes to 2 (both inside the canonical "yes, the client" interruption — character-voice, not AI tic). Did not flatten the doubled interruption to commas because the em-dash interruption rhythm is the pack §H canonical.
- §J #4 (solidaristic, Ch 2): "Whimsy will later argue was solidaristic" → "clicks once and does not continue." Removes Whimsy preview and writerly flourish without sterilizing. STUB priming via the canonical "coffee machine 'may be solidarity'" cue can land elsewhere; H4 setting note does not have to carry it.
- §J #9 (postcard, Ch 5): explicit placeholder marker added; H6 deferred. Holding text revised to "Local prospects favorable by reputation" — closer to Swine's cosmopolitan-evasion register than critic's "Business opportunity ongoing," which read telegram-flat.

**Rejected (with reasoning):**
- §J #1 (Ch 1 firm-arrival-third): the pack's own §H/§D canonical recommendation has "firm" as the third grammatical noun ("We have survived" / "the client" / "the firm"). The pattern commitment is firm-survival *framing*, not "firm" as the literal first word. The "we" framing situates Pig inside the firm — which is his blind spot and the reason the pattern recolors at chapter-5 retraversal. Forcing "The firm has come through" would make Pig externalize the firm and weaken the character work. Authority hierarchy: pack canonical > critic. Held.
- §J #6 (Ch 4 weaker than Ch 1-2): critic's fix ("The firm has weathered another improbable thing") makes Ch 4 break the pattern from Ch 1-2 rather than culminate it. If "firm" must be the grammatical subject in Ch 4, the rule applies retroactively to Ch 1-2 — and that contradicts the pack canonical. The pattern is implied-firm-via-"we" / explicit-"firm" in punchline, consistent across all four. Ch 4 instead lands harder via the return-to-anxiety failure (critic's #7, accepted), not by switching grammatical structure. Held.
