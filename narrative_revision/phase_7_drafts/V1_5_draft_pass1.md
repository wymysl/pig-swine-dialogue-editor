## Beat 10 — Court readiness check at the office

### Beat 10 — opening (team has returned from Archive Room; Murrow runs the readiness check)

*The Pig & Swine open-office area. Cula, Crab, and Whimsy have just returned from the Archive Room. The case folder is closed under Cula's arm; the Procedural Binder is back on its shelf. Murrow is at his desk, list in front of him, a pencil resting on the page. The case board behind him still carries the Service / Fair hearing / Remedy notation in Whimsy's hand. Asia is at the reception desk, head down over the morning's calendar. A telephone is ringing somewhere beyond the corridor and is not being answered yet.*

*Cula crosses to Murrow's desk. Crab sets his laptop on his work table; opens it a quarter; sets it aside. Whimsy lays his binder — the Rights Memo with the coffee-ring margin — on the chair behind Cula's borrowed desk.*

**MURROW:** Cula.

**DR. A. CULA:** Murrow.

*Murrow does not look up. He reads items off the list in his head.*

**MURROW:** Motion filed. Hearing on the cause list at fourteen hundred. Courtroom four.

**CRAB:** Service-and-knowledge appendix is in.

**WHIMSY:** Third-party non-cure preemption is drafted.

*Murrow's pencil ticks once against the list. He does not nod; the tick is the acknowledgment.*

---

### Beat 10 — Pig's printer-lease aside (first visit)

*Mr. Pig drifts past Murrow's desk, blazer half-buttoned, a sheaf of paper under one arm. He stops a short pace from Murrow. Speaks toward the room rather than to anyone in particular. The maritime metaphor lands without invitation; he is in panic register.*

**MR. PIG:** The printer lease has come back as a kraken. We renew next month, presumably with bait.

*Pig keeps walking, papers shifting under his arm. Murrow does not look up. Cula does not respond. The room moves on.*

---

### Beat 10 — Pig's first lecture (second visit)

*A short interval. Murrow has flipped his list to a second page. Pig drifts back the other way, slower now, a coffee cup in one hand. He stops near Cula but does not look at him. The lecture comes out unsolicited; he gives it to the air. Sincere as memory. Three sentences; he wanders out before a fourth could land.*

**MR. PIG:** When I was an apprentice, we had no readiness check. Only fear. We navigated by panic alone.

*Pig drifts toward his own desk, coffee cup loose in his hand. Cula does not respond. Crab does not look up. Whimsy does not flourish. The lecture lands and the room continues.*

---

### Beat 10 — closing (Cula's stance-aligned bonus-evidence confirm; Murrow's transition cue)

*Murrow looks up briefly. The case folder under Cula's arm is the day's instrument; Murrow's eye registers it.*

**MURROW:** Cula. What we tender.

*Cula opens the case folder a quarter-inch and reads the order, top to bottom. The bonus-evidence reference varies by stance carrier from Beat 8 — character-dwell texture, not branching. Three short variants of one acknowledgment.*

**(if sympathetic carrier)**

**DR. A. CULA:** The motion. The Wójcik affidavit if the court asks for character witness.

**(if blunt-procedural carrier)**

**DR. A. CULA:** The motion. The return-to-sender slip on knowledge.

**(if technical carrier)**

**DR. A. CULA:** The motion. The countersignature on the renewal.

*Murrow's pencil ticks once more. He closes the list. Sets the pencil on its cover.*

**MURROW:** We walk at a quarter past one.

*Crab closes his laptop and lifts the service-and-knowledge appendix from his work table. Whimsy collects the Rights Memo binder. Cula tucks the case folder under his arm. Murrow rises and takes the Procedural Binder from his desk.*

## Beat 11 — Walk to the District Court

### Beat 11 — opening (team leaves the office; brief stage directions; Warsaw daytime register established)

*Office Street. The team — Cula in the lead with the case folder; Crab a half-pace behind on his right; Whimsy a half-pace behind on his left; Murrow in the rear with the Procedural Binder under one arm — turns out of the firm's doorway onto the pavement. The sky is a Warsaw daytime grey, neither bright nor dim. A tram bell sounds two blocks east. A public-works truck idles outside a renovation; the workman in the cab is reading a folded-up newspaper. Two clerks in suits cross the opposite pavement carrying folders bigger than their forearms. The bureaucratic-public-noisy register is operative; the team's pace is steady.*

*No dialogue. The team walks.*

---

### Beat 11 — walk

*East two blocks, then north along the courts' embankment. The river is on the left, low and brown; the embankment trees are in the dust-colour of late spring. A pigeon lifts from the bronze head of a statesman whose name has been re-cast on his pedestal three times in the last fifty years. Two more pigeons hold their ground on the embankment railing as the team passes. The pigeons' eyes do not move. The courthouse pediment becomes visible at the far end of the embankment, sandstone over the trees. Whimsy registers the pigeons in his peripheral and flourishes once — observational, brief.*

**WHIMSY:** Judgmental pigeons. The chorus is in already.

*Cula's mouth does the not-quite-smile. Crab does not look up. Murrow's pace does not change. The line lands and the walk continues.*

---

### Beat 11 — arrival (the courthouse becomes visible; Halina meets the team at the steps)

*The District Court Plaza opens out at the end of the embankment. The courthouse is a long sandstone building with a pediment that has held three different state seals across the century; the current one is in fresh bronze. The plaza is half-populated — a clerk on a phone, a woman with a folder under one arm, a man in a black suit smoking near the railing. Halina Sikorska is at the foot of the steps. She has travelled separately by tram. She is in the same grey wool jacket from the meeting; her folder is squared under one arm; her handbag on the other shoulder. Her posture is upright; the slight age-stiffness has not become a stoop. She sees the team approach and steps a quarter-pace forward to meet them at the foot of the steps.*

*The team reaches her.*

**HALINA:** Dr. A. Cula. Mr. Murrow. Mr. Crab. Mr. Whimsy.

**DR. A. CULA:** Mrs. Sikorska.

**CRAB:** Mrs. Sikorska.

**WHIMSY:** Mrs. Sikorska — a brief courtesy.

*Murrow inclines his head a quarter-inch. The acknowledgment is given in posture, not voice; the files-outweigh-the-people register holds. Halina returns the incline.*

**HALINA:** I came on the tram. I am here in time.

*A small pause. The dignity discipline holds; she has not apologised, has not asked, has not explained. Murrow's eye is on the courthouse doors.*

**MURROW:** Courtroom four. Inside.

*Halina turns and falls in beside Cula. Crab and Whimsy fall in behind. Murrow brings up the rear, the Procedural Binder under his arm. The team crosses the plaza and ascends the courthouse steps. The sandstone is warm under the daytime grey. The doors are open.*
