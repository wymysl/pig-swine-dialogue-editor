# Phase 7 H3 — Cula-Murrow staging, Beat 11 + Beat 14 (post-critique draft)

Two silent visual specifications written at Godot-scene-script resolution. No dialogue authored.

**Revision history.** First draft passed §F drafting prompt cleanly but failed the §G hostile critique on multiple substantive items: glimpse semantics (overlay persistence converted a glimpse into player-driven inspection), inflection robustness (fixed-time return could miss the ledger trigger), trigger convention (Beat 14 used the reused desk-proximity zone instead of the canonical camera-frame entry), object-state contradiction (notation completion timing), and unresolved deferred items in §A. Eighteen of nineteen critique items applied here; one (Beat 14 §E "only conveyance" wording) revised rather than replaced — the load-bearing-element framing is preserved but scoped to the shared-spatial acknowledgment and supplemented with explicit no-cue constraints.

## Scene 1 — Chapter 4 Beat 11 (the inflection)

### A. Setting

Time: late evening, post-business-hours nocturnal beat per `style_canon.txt §8`. Location: Pig & Swine Office, work area framed on Murrow's desk. Murrow exits and re-enters through `office_reception_exit_node`; the node is bound to the final floor-plan asset and resolves the screen direction at runtime. Lighting: desk lamps on at Murrow's desk, the Crab/Whimsy shared bench, and reception. Coffee machine unplugged (visible state per `style_canon.txt §3`). Ambient audio throughout: Bajtek's keyboard at low density (loop), occasional paper-shuffle SFX, Mr. Pig's maritime mutterings on a low-frequency loop from his corner. No music change at any point in the scene. Personnel visible in frame at scene start: Murrow at his desk; Crab and Whimsy at the shared work bench (ordinary assemble-exhibits work animation); Bajtek at his terminal (idle-typing); Mr. Pig at the edge of the frame in idle-pacing. Asia is at reception, off-camera throughout.

### B. Character positions / blocking

Murrow starts seated at his desk in the standard idle-at-desk-with-ledger pose. On phone-ring trigger, his sprite plays the standard stand-from-desk animation, then standard NPC walk cycle (32×48, 4-direction) from desk to `office_reception_exit_node`, exiting frame. Off-screen for the duration determined by §D step 4 (event-gated, minimum 45s). Re-enters from the same node; standard walk cycle to his desk; on arrival, plays the generic reusable `npc_return_glance_down` animation — if the engine does not yet have this animation, create it as a reusable NPC animation available to all desk-returning NPCs and do not author a Murrow-specific reaction animation. Plays the standard stamp-pickup-and-place-back tic; returns to seated idle-at-desk-with-ledger pose. Cula (player-controlled) starts at the team work area near Crab and Whimsy. Player walks Cula past Murrow's desk to the case file cabinet, then back past Murrow's desk to the team area. Cula's sprite uses the standard walk cycle throughout. Crab, Whimsy, Bajtek, and Mr. Pig run their ordinary ambient animation loops; none reacts to the phone, the glimpse, Murrow's exit, or Murrow's return. No sprite in the scene plays a reaction-shot or noticed-something animation at any point.

### C. Object states

Murrow's ledger: open at scene start; remains open throughout; state at scene end is still open, exactly as Murrow left it. Visible entry on the open page: rendered from `chapter4.ledger_operating_balance_text`, whose current beat-sheet value renders as "Konto operacyjne — saldo bieżące: 285 400 PLN", positioned near the top of the visible page. The chapter-4 beat sheet remains the single source of truth for the figure. Rendering: a high-resolution diegetic sub-asset of the page anchored to the ledger sprite's world position, fading in when Cula's collision box enters the proximity zone and fading out on exit per §D step 6. No UI chrome, no tooltip framing, no notification badge. Recommended optional accessibility caption: "operating account — current balance: 285,400 PLN" rendered in plain text adjacent to the readable overlay, sized to match in-world signage typography (not framed as UI). Phone (at reception, off-camera): rings (ambient SFX); no phone sprite in the work-area frame. Desk stamp: visible on Murrow's desk at scene start; handled briefly on Murrow's return (pickup, place-back); state otherwise unchanged. Coffee machine: unplugged (visible). Case file cabinet: closed at scene start; player-interaction draws the procedural notice; returns to closed state.

### D. Beat sequence

1. T+0s. Scene opens. State per §A and §C. Murrow at desk in idle-at-desk-with-ledger pose.
2. T+~5s. Phone rings (ambient SFX, originating from the reception direction). Murrow's sprite plays stand-from-desk animation (~1s). If the surrounding Beat 11 dialogue script has already queued Murrow's canonical phone-acknowledgment line, that line plays before this silent staging block begins. This spec does not define or alter that dialogue.
3. T+~6s. Murrow walks toward `office_reception_exit_node` via standard walk cycle. Exits the camera frame at approximately T+~10s.
4. From T+~10s onward, Murrow is off-screen. Murrow's return is gated by `chapter4.glimpsed_ledger_anomaly == true` and `chapter4.procedural_notice_collected == true`, with a minimum absence of 45 seconds. If the player delays, the off-screen call ambience continues until both conditions are satisfied. Player retains control of Cula throughout. Ambient audio: muffled reception phone-call ambience continues off-screen; no intelligible voice is heard. No music change. Crab, Whimsy, Bajtek, Mr. Pig continue ordinary animations.
5. The glimpse trigger. When Cula's collision box enters Murrow's `desk_proximity` zone (a rectangular floor area adjacent to Murrow's desk, sized so that Cula's natural traversal path to the case file cabinet passes through it), set `chapter4.glimpsed_ledger_anomaly = true`. Flag set is silent: no UI notification, no SFX, no camera change.
6. Concurrent with step 5: the readable overlay appears once per pass for 1.5–2.0 seconds and fades out whether Cula continues walking or idles. Re-entry can replay the same capped reveal, but no persistent inspection state is created. The cap is enforced by the overlay's own timer, independent of player input.
7. Cula's sprite during the glimpse: standard walk cycle (or standard idle if the player has stopped him). No pause animation. No noticed-something pose. No sprite expression change. The player chooses pacing; the engine signals nothing.
8. Cula reaches the case file cabinet (collision box enters cabinet interaction zone). Standard cabinet-open interaction; Cula draws the procedural notice. Set `chapter4.procedural_notice_collected = true` (existing inventory-add per `chapter_3.md` Beat 11.6).
9. Cula returns toward the team work area. Path takes him back past Murrow's desk; collision box re-enters the proximity zone; the capped overlay replays per the same parameters as step 6. Both flags are already set; re-entry does not re-fire any event.
10. After the minimum 45-second absence has elapsed and `chapter4.procedural_notice_collected == true`, the phone-call ambience ends (handset-replace SFX from the reception direction) and Murrow re-enters from `office_reception_exit_node` via standard walk cycle.
11. Murrow walks from the node to his desk (~3-5s). On arrival, his sprite plays `npc_return_glance_down`. Frame held for the engine's ordinary duration (~0.3s). No emphasis, no held beat, no special framing. The animation is generic, not Murrow-specific.
12. Murrow's sprite plays the ordinary stamp-pickup / stamp-placeback tic (~1s). Resumes seated idle-at-desk-with-ledger pose. Ledger remains open.
13. Scene continues into the filing-assembly beat (handled in Pack H2; not part of this spec).
14. No additional triggers fire on Murrow's return. No flag references Murrow's "registration" of the glimpse. The visual sequence at step 11 is the only conveyance.

### E. Explicit constraints (negative space)

No internal narration, thought bubble, or text overlay for Cula at any point. Cula's sprite carries no special expression, no special pose, and no pause animation. No eye contact between Cula and Murrow at any point. Murrow's sprite at step 11 orients toward his own desk; at no point does Murrow's sprite orient toward Cula. Cula's sprite at no point orients toward Murrow on Murrow's return. No music sting, music change, or audio cue marks the phone-ring response, the glimpse, the flag set, or Murrow's return. No camera move; the camera follows Cula per default behavior throughout. No reaction shot (no camera close-up on either character). No theatrical pause or held beat anywhere; Murrow's glance at step 11 uses the engine's ordinary duration for `npc_return_glance_down`. No portrait of either character surfaces in this scene. No UI notification or achievement-style popup on either flag set. No book-close SFX, since the ledger does not close. No facial expression conveyance.

### F. Engine notes

Camera: default player-following per project convention; no camera state change in this scene. Trigger: rectangular collision-box proximity zone adjacent to Murrow's desk, sized to lie on Cula's natural path to the case file cabinet, dimensions set against the actual desk asset placement. Flags: `chapter4.glimpsed_ledger_anomaly` (bool), set true on first collision-box entry during the open-ledger window, re-entry does not re-fire; `chapter4.procedural_notice_collected` (bool), set on cabinet interaction. Murrow's return event is gated on both flags being true and a minimum 45-second absence having elapsed; failure of either condition extends the ambience loop. Ledger readability: a high-resolution sub-asset of the open page anchored to the ledger sprite's world position, layered above world tiles and below any UI, fading in/out per the timing in §D step 6 with a hard 2.0s upper-bound on visibility per pass. Begins with phone-ring SFX, transitions to a muffled-reception ambient loop with no intelligible voice, ends with handset-replace SFX gated on the return conditions in §D step 10. Murrow's exit/return path: scripted NPC traversal at standard walk speed via `office_reception_exit_node`; no acceleration, no special timing. `npc_return_glance_down`: generic reusable animation; create as reusable if not already in the engine's animation set. Other NPCs (Crab, Whimsy, Bajtek, Mr. Pig): independent ambient animation loops; no scripted reactions. Music: per `world.txt`, the room's music continues unchanged; no crossfade authored for this scene.

## Scene 2 — Chapter 6 Beat 14 (the final image)

### A. Setting

Time-of-day inherits `chapter6.ending_sequence_time_of_day`; this spec does not override lighting. Location: Pig & Swine Office, work area, framed on Murrow's desk and immediate surroundings as Cula traverses. Lighting: ordinary office-lamp setup as configured by the inherited time-of-day; no lighting change for this beat. Ambient audio: low office ambient — Bajtek's keyboard at lower density than chapter 4 (the urgency is gone), occasional paper-shuffle, ordinary footstep SFX as Cula traverses. Optionally, distant Asia voice from reception at low volume. No music change for this beat. Personnel in frame: Murrow at his desk; Cula approaching/passing. No other NPC enters the Murrow-desk camera frame during the notation-close-store sequence; any ending-sequence NPCs remain outside this frame or continue ambient loops without facing Murrow's desk.

### B. Character positions / blocking

Murrow starts seated at his desk, mid-notation: pen in hand, ledger open in front of him, the standard writing-at-desk animation already running. Cula's traversal during this beat is scripted, not player-controlled: Cula follows the ending-sequence walk path at standard walk speed. Player input is restored after Cula exits the Murrow-desk frame. Cula's sprite uses standard walk cycle throughout the beat; no pause animation; no special pose. No reaction-shot or noticed-something animation on either sprite. The blocking is ordinary office traversal; Murrow is doing ordinary work; Cula is passing.

### C. Object states

Ledger: open at scene start; the chapter-6 proceeds entry is in progress. The final short stroke is not placed until §D step 3. The reserve total has incremented from its chapter-4 value (specific value per `chapter_5.md` beat sheet; the increment is small and the line is short). No readable overlay is used in Beat 14. The notation stroke and close-ledger action use a high-resolution diegetic sub-asset anchored to the ledger sprite, matching Beat 11's layering but without readable text or caption. Pen: in Murrow's hand at scene start; placed on the desk after the closing-stroke animation completes. Drawer: closed at scene start; opens during the close-and-store sequence; closes after the ledger is inside. Desk: ordinary state; no special props introduced. Other room props: ordinary chapter-6 ending state.

### D. Beat sequence

1. T-3s (before Cula enters frame). Murrow already mid-notation; the writing-at-desk animation has been running for several seconds; player encounters the scene mid-action.
2. T+0s. Cula's path enters `chapter5_murrow_frame_entry_zone`, a trigger volume on the ending-sequence walk path that guarantees Murrow's desk is already in frame. Set `chapter6.murrow_close_sequence_started = true`. Flag set is silent.
3. T+0s to T+~1s. Notation animation visibly continues for ~0.5–1.0s into Cula's frame-presence: the final short stroke is placed on the page while Cula is in the camera frame. Cula's scripted path keeps him visible from his position so that the line being made is in view.
4. T+~1s. Notation completes (pen lifts and is placed on the desk). Close-ledger animation begins: hands move ledger cover from open to closed over approximately 1.0 second. Engine's ordinary pacing for object manipulation; no slow-motion, no held beat. When the close-ledger animation begins with both Murrow's desk and Cula within the camera viewport, set `chapter6.murrow_closes_ledger_seen = true`.
5. T+~2s. Ledger fully closed. Drawer-open animation begins (~0.4s), then place-ledger-in-drawer (~0.4s), then drawer-close (~0.4s). Total drawer phase ~1.5s. Set `chapter6.ledger_stored_in_drawer = true` on drawer-close completion.
6. T+~3.5s. Drawer closed. Murrow picks up the adjacent routine document and resumes ordinary desk work. The structure persists; the system absorbs the chapter-6 win and continues.
7. Cula remains in frame through the drawer-close animation. The ending walk path is long enough, at standard speed, to keep Cula visible until `ledger_stored_in_drawer == true`. Player input is restored after Cula exits the Murrow-desk frame.
8. Cula's sprite continues across the frame and exits on the far side at standard walk speed.
9. Scene continues into the rest of the ending sequence (handled elsewhere). No additional triggers fire from this beat. The flags remain set.

The only SFX associated with the closing sequence are the engine's ordinary book-close SFX (a single soft thud layered with paper-rustle) and the ordinary drawer-open / drawer-close SFX (standard wooden-drawer ambient), all at default diegetic volume. No camera move; camera follows Cula per default behavior, and Murrow's desk is incidentally framed by Cula's path. No reaction shot. No theatrical pause or held beat at any point in the close-and-store sequence; engine pacing is ordinary throughout. No portrait of either character surfaces. No UI notification on any flag set.
