## BEAT 3 — Find Murrow

*Murrow's desk, file area. He is annotating the Sikorska case file with a pencil. The desk is orderly. A closed ledger sits beside the blotter as ordinary office furniture. Pig's panic continues at audible distance from the other side of the office. The case file open in front of Murrow has tabs flagged in two colours.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Mr. Murrow. I was told you'd have the Sikorska file.

*Murrow looks up. Sets the pencil down. A brief look — taking Cula in.*

**MURROW:** Doctor Cula. Welcome to Pig & Swine. You've picked a noisy day to begin.

*He turns the open case file ninety degrees so Cula can read it.*

**MURROW:** The case is the Sikorska eviction defense. Halina Sikorska, at number twelve; the notice was served to old number seven.

**MURROW:** The building was renumbered in two thousand and fifteen. The two thousand and nineteen renewal carries the landlord's countersignature. He served the old door anyway.

**MURROW:** Hearing already ran ex parte. The current resident at number seven brought the notice down three weeks late. Actual notice reached her on the twenty-eighth of April.

**MURROW:** We file a motion to set aside. Fourteen days from actual notice. We are inside the window.

**MURROW:** Procedural binder, second shelf, blue spine. Rights memo clipped inside the front cover. Motion hearing Friday at fourteen hundred, courtroom four.

**MURROW:** She has not formally retained yet. She is coming in this afternoon.

**DR. A. CULA:** The resident at number seven. Does his accepting the notice cure service?

**MURROW:** No. Express authority is required. He had none.

**DR. A. CULA:** Thank you, Mr. Murrow.

**MURROW:** It is Murrow, to friends. The 'Mister' I keep for invoices.

**DR. A. CULA:** Then it's Cula.

**MURROW:** I am still deciding whether reception staff qualify as friends or as workplace constellations.

**DR. A. CULA:** She'll be relieved either way.

*A beat. Murrow's mouth moves in something that is not quite a smile. Pig's voice carries briefly across the office. Murrow's pencil taps once against the desk.*

**MURROW:** Binder first. Memo second. Find me before fourteen hundred if anything in the binder reads wrong.

## BEAT 5 — Crab recruitment

*Crab is at a work table near the window. Laptop open, three stacks of paper sorted by size. He is reading something on screen. He does not look up immediately as Cula approaches.*

### First approach (binder not yet collected)

**DR. A. CULA:** Crab. I'm Cula. I could use a second pair of eyes on the Sikorska file before fourteen hundred.

*Crab finishes the line he's reading. Looks up. Closes the laptop halfway.*

**CRAB:** Cula. Heard.

*He glances at the spread of paper on his table, then back.*

**CRAB:** The binder is not in the room. I cannot check service without the binder.

**DR. A. CULA:** Right.

*Cula turns toward the archive.*

### Second approach (binder secured)

*Cula returns. Sets the binder on the corner of Crab's table.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Here.

*Crab glances at the spine. Pulls the binder toward him. Opens to the tabbed section.*

**CRAB:** The binder cites Article 135-bis § 2. The notice mentions service. I can check that.

**DR. A. CULA:** Good.

*Crab is already reading. Cula moves to the next quest beat.*
