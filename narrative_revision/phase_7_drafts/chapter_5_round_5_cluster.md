# Chapter 6, Round 5 — Four-line cluster (final)

*The Supreme Court chamber. Cula at the lectern. Whimsy seated at counsel table behind him. Pig and Swine in the witness rows. The bench is the judge's bench.*

*Cula does not consult notes. He speaks directly to the bench.*

**Cula:**
"The remedy follows the defects. The notice was defective. The assessment was absent. The objections were suppressed. The benefit was private. On these grounds, the court need not reach the firm's social utility to set aside the scheme."

*A pause. The judge does not interrupt. Whimsy rises.*

**Whimsy:**
"With the court's leave. I propose the court read the scheme's four faults as a single instrument: a notice that announced nothing, an assessment that examined no one, objections processed by archive, and a private benefit dressed for public audit. The remedy answers the procedure."

*Whimsy sits. The judge makes a note. She addresses the witness rows.*

**Judge:**
"Mr. Pig, briefly."

*Pig stands. He clears his throat.*

**Pig:**
"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."

*Pig sits.*

**Judge:**
"Counsel will note the testimony of Mr. Pig. The court has heard it. Mr. Swine."

*Swine stands. His hand rests on the rail.*

**Swine:**
"I was not avoiding responsibility. I was avoiding accounting, which is similar but less noble."

*Swine sits.*

**Judge:**
"Counsel will note the testimony of Mr. Swine. The court has heard it."

*The judge consults the bench papers. Looks up.*

**Judge:**
"Against several expectations, the submission is legally coherent. The notice fell short of statutory requirement. No individual assessment was conducted. The objection mechanism functioned as suppression. The conflict of interest is, on the record, not denied. The scheme is set aside in the form presented, without prejudice to a properly noticed and individually assessed reformulation. The matter is remitted to the relevant authority for reconsideration in conformity with this order."

*The court rises.*
