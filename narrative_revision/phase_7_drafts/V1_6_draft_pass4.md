**Pass-3 critique disposition.**

Critical fixes applied:

1. **Landlord's counsel characterization cut.** Pass 3 described him as having *"the blandness of a practitioner who has done this twice this week."* The pack says he is functional and not characterised. Pass 4 leaves his age, suit, and folder only.
2. **Crab blunt-procedural branch compressed.** Pass 3's blunt-procedural turn had eight sentences and became a document inventory rather than Crab's bench rhythm. Pass 4 restores the salutation plus two argument sentences: fact stack, consequence.
3. **Crab technical branch compressed.** Pass 3's technical turn also overextended the knowledge proof. Pass 4 folds the countersignature into the fact stack and keeps the consequence sentence clean.
4. **Judge Round-1 boilerplate reduced.** Pass 3 repeated *"if there is anything to hear"* across all three variants. Pass 4 keeps the adult-occupant issue procedurally alive without the phrase becoming a bench tic.
5. **Cula sympathetic remedy ask defused.** Pass 3's *"The petitioner has been continuously resident..."* still risked making residence history feel like emotional support for the order. Pass 4 reframes the residence evidence as address-fixing support for proper service.
6. **Cula blunt-procedural self-label cut.** Pass 3's *"The requested order is a procedural reset"* described the pack discipline from inside the dialogue. Pass 4 lets the requested order carry the procedural character.
7. **Cula technical self-label cooled.** Pass 3's *"procedural reset requested today"* similarly leaked the modest-remedy label. Pass 4 changes it to *"the limited order requested today."*
8. **Whimsy's em-dash climax removed.** Pass 3's *"Verily — the petitioner was not heard"* was permitted by budget but made the final sentence land too theatrically. Pass 4 keeps the one archaic beat as a sentence, not a stage flourish.

## Beat 12 — First court battle

### Beat 12 — opening (court is in session; petitioner in the gallery; counsel at table)

*Courtroom four. The room is panelled in the same dust-colour wood as the embankment trees outside. The District Court Judge sits on the bench beneath a fresh bronze state seal. The clerk's desk is below the bench to its left; the witness stand is to the right and unoccupied. Counsel's table is set in two halves. Cula stands at the petitioner's half. Crab and Whimsy are seated to his right and left respectively. The Procedural Binder is open under Cula's hand. The case folder is squared on the table at his elbow. The Rights Memo binder, coffee-ring margin visible, is at Whimsy's elbow. Crab's service-and-knowledge appendix is on the table between them. The respondent's table is opposite. Landlord's counsel is a man in his fifties in dark grey, a thin folder open in front of him. The gallery row directly behind petitioner's counsel holds Murrow, the Procedural Binder closed against his knee. Halina Sikorska is seated two seats further along the same gallery row, alone. Her folder is squared in her lap. Her handbag is on the seat beside her. Her posture is upright. Her eyes are on the bench.*

*The clerk has read the matter on the cause list. The judge has the file open on the bench. Counsel for both sides have indicated they are ready. The judge looks once at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel for the petitioner.

*Cula remains on his feet. He inclines his head.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The petitioner moves to set aside the hearing of the eighth on the ground of invalid service. Mr. Crab will present the service-and-knowledge submission.

*Cula sits. Crab rises.*

---

### Beat 12 — Round 1: defective service (Crab carries; three stance variants)

#### Round 1 — sympathetic carrier (Wójcik bonus)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. He does not move from behind the table. His hands rest flat on the appendix.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice went to number seven. The petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure sets the consequence of failed service. The notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises with a thin folder.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the petitioner tenders Mrs. Wójcik's affidavit. It confirms continuous residence at number twelve since nineteen eighty-seven.

*Cula crosses to the clerk, hands up the affidavit, and returns to the table. The judge does not yet acknowledge it. Landlord's counsel rises.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was accepted by an adult occupant at the address used for service. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file briefly, then at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The affidavit is noted. The renumbering is enough for the service question on the documents. The respondent may address the third clause.

*Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

#### Round 1 — blunt-procedural carrier (return-to-sender slip bonus)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. The slip is a separate small page on top of the appendix; the rest of the appendix is squared beneath it.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice went to number seven; the petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen; the landlord's own file contains a returned letter to number seven and a countersigned renewal at number twelve. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure sets the consequence of failed service; the notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises briefly.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the slip and the renewal are tendered for the file.

*Cula hands the documents up to the clerk and returns to the table. Landlord's counsel rises. He pauses before he speaks.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was accepted by an adult occupant at the address used for service. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file longer than in the prior variant. He turns one page, then back.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The renumbering, the prior return, and the countersignature are before the court. Knowledge is supported by the documents. The adult-occupant point is preserved for the third clause.

*Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

#### Round 1 — technical carrier (countersignature only; lease + inheritance held for Round 3)

*Crab on his feet at counsel's table. The renewal is on the appendix's top sheet; the lease and the inheritance papers remain in the case folder under Cula's hand.*

**CRAB:** Your Honour. The notice went to number seven; the petitioner's address has been number twelve since the renumbering of two thousand and fifteen; the two thousand and nineteen renewal carries the corrected address with a countersignature from the landlord's office. Article 132 sets the service requirement; Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure sets the consequence of failed service; the notice is invalid.

*Crab sits. Cula rises briefly.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour, the renewal is tendered for the file.

*Cula hands the renewal up to the clerk and returns to the table. Landlord's counsel rises.*

**LANDLORD'S COUNSEL:** Your Honour. The respondent submits that the notice was accepted by an adult occupant at the address used for service. Service was therefore effective.

*Landlord's counsel sits. The judge looks at the bench file briefly, then at the petitioner's table.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The renumbering and the countersignature are before the court. Knowledge is not in serious dispute. The respondent may address the third clause.

*Crab returns the appendix to the table.*

---

### Beat 12 — Round 2: fair hearing (Whimsy carries; Rights Memo with Coffee Ring; third-clause non-cure preemption)

*The judge looks at the petitioner's table. Cula remains seated and inclines toward Whimsy. Whimsy rises with the Rights Memo binder. The coffee-ring stain on the margin is briefly visible as he sets the binder open on the table. He leaves it flat. He does not gesture with it. He addresses the bench in a register he keeps a half-step beneath theatre.*

**WHIMSY:** Your Honour. A summons sent to the wrong door invites the wrong household. The respondent's answer is that an adult at number seven accepted the paper. Article 135-bis § 2's third clause answers that circumstance: acceptance at the wrong address does not cure invalid service. The law does not appoint the nearest hand as an usher. Verily. The petitioner was not heard.

*Whimsy's hand rests on the binder. He does not sit.*

*Landlord's counsel does not rise; he has been answered. The judge looks at the binder a moment, at Whimsy a moment, at the bench file.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. The metaphor is noted. The third-clause point is clear enough. The respondent's adult-occupant submission is answered by Article 135-bis § 2. The court will proceed to remedy.

*Whimsy inclines his head. He sits. Cula opens the case folder to the page tabbed for the motion. Crab adjusts the appendix to make room.*

---

### Beat 12 — Round 3: remedy (Cula carries; three stance variants; technical bonus surfaces here for the technical variant)

#### Round 3 — sympathetic carrier

*Cula rises. The case folder is open in front of him at the motion page. He does not lift it. His hands rest on the table. The opening register is short; the law-arguing register lengthens as he proceeds.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The court can set aside the hearing of the eighth and assign a new date on properly served notice. The residence evidence fixes number twelve as the address for service. The merits can be heard after proper service.

*Cula remains on his feet. He does not say anything further. The judge looks at the bench file. He turns one page.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The matter is rescheduled. The clerk will assign a new hearing date within the statutory window. Notice will be served in the ordinary course.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap.*

---

#### Round 3 — blunt-procedural carrier

*Cula rises. The case folder is open at the motion page. His hands rest on the table.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The court is asked to set aside the hearing under Article 135-bis § 2 and assign a new date on properly served notice.

*Cula remains on his feet. The judge looks at the bench file. He turns one page.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The matter is rescheduled. The clerk will assign a new hearing date within the statutory window. Notice will be served in the ordinary course.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap.*

---

#### Round 3 — technical carrier (lease + inheritance bonus surfaces)

*Cula rises. The case folder is open at the motion page; the lease and inheritance papers are in his hand. He sets them on the table beside the motion.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Your Honour. The chain of tenancy runs from nineteen sixty-two; the inheritance paperwork is from nineteen eighty-seven; the renewal is from two thousand and nineteen. Those documents support the limited order requested today. The court is asked to set aside the hearing of the eighth and assign a new date on properly served notice.

*Cula remains on his feet. He does not say anything further. The judge looks at the bench file longer than in the prior variants. He turns two pages.*

**JUDGE:** Counsel. On the motion of the petitioner, the hearing of the eighth is set aside on the ground of invalid service under Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure. The matter is rescheduled. The clerk will assign a new hearing date within the statutory window. Notice will be served in the ordinary course. The underlying tenancy is not before the court on this motion.

*Cula inclines his head. He sits. Halina's hand rests on the folder in her lap.*

---

### Beat 12 — closing (the court rises; the gallery rises; the team gathers documents; transition to V1.7)

*The judge closes the bench file. The clerk rises. The judge rises. The bailiff calls the court to its feet. Counsel and gallery rise.*

*Halina rises with the gallery. She does not weep. She does not turn toward the respondent's table. She holds the folder against her hip; her handbag is over her shoulder. Her posture is upright.*

*The judge exits through the door at the rear of the bench. The clerk follows.*

*Cula closes the case folder. Crab stacks the appendix, the slip, and the renewal in the order they were received. Whimsy closes the Rights Memo binder; the coffee-ring margin disappears. Landlord's counsel collects his thin folder, inclines briefly toward the petitioner's table without making eye contact with the petitioner, and leaves the room. Murrow lifts the Procedural Binder from his knee and stands; he meets Cula's eye across the rail; he does not speak.*

*The team files out from counsel's table: Cula first with the case folder, Crab and Whimsy behind. Halina falls in beside them at the courtroom door. Murrow brings up the rear. None of them speak in the corridor. The doors close behind the last of them.*

### Modest-remedy discipline

The order sets aside the hearing of the eighth and reschedules. It does not rule on the underlying tenancy. The renumbering is not cured. The landlord is not punished. The case is not finally decided. The firm's social utility is not cited. The petitioner's circumstances are not cited as the basis of relief. The matter remains live.

No catharsis in the courtroom. Closing is procedural exit.
