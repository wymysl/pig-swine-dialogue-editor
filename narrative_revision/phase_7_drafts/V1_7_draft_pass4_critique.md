> **Failure:** *"Pig's eyes drift toward the corridor that leads out toward the post slot, then away. The relief has thinned. He addresses no one in particular; if the line is to anyone it is to Murrow at the ledger."*
> **Mode:** Beat-14 structural-comedy discipline / §A stage-direction discipline / Murrow-ledger staging. The post-slot glance overflags the postcard payoff before it arrives. The "if the line is to anyone" construction is authorial uncertainty, not staging. "Murrow at the ledger" is also premature: the ledger has not been brought out yet, and the pack protects ledger activity as visible staging later in the beat.
> **Fix:** Keep only observable, non-telegraphing behavior: *"Pig's eyes drift toward Murrow's desk, then away. The relief has thinned. He addresses no one in particular."*

> **Failure:** *"Cula's mouth does the not-quite-smile. He does not articulate."*
> **Mode:** §B.6 Cula / §A prompt-language leak. This narrates the withholding discipline instead of staging it. "He does not articulate" is a rule sentence wearing scene clothes. The "mouth does the not-quite-smile" phrasing is also writerly and has already been corrected in nearby phase-7 work.

> **Fix:** Narrow the claim: *"No procedural detail surfaces in Pig's celebration; Crab and Whimsy may name the renumbering in their reaction lines per §B.4/§B.5."*

> **Marginal:** **ASIA:** *"Good work, Dr. Cula; the blue file is back on your desk."*
