**Theme:** we did not have unions; we had each other and the work.
**Maritime density:** moderate ("bailed" in the lecture; the surrounding scene carries more — the printer is making noises Pig is interpreting as nautical).

**Junior reactions:** silence. The STUB founding is still a chapter away; none of them know the lecture is one chapter early on its own irony.

## Lecture 3 — Chapter 4, Beat 11 (urgent filing prep)

**Theme:** toner-rationing — magenta as privilege.
**Maritime density:** high. The lecture sits inside the panic; the panic and the lecture share vocabulary.
**Embedded in:** Pig's printer-lease maritime crisis, mid-assembly of the urgent filing. Cula has, fifteen minutes earlier, glimpsed the 285,400 PLN reserve on Murrow's open ledger. The irony is the player's; Pig does not have it.

> **Mr. Pig:** *(at his desk corner, holding the lease quote at arm's length as though it might bite)* This lease will run us aground. We rationed magenta by the page at Borowski's, and counted yellow on Fridays against the logbook. A logbook for toner, Cula.
>
> *(Murrow steps out for the courier signature. Pig does not stop staring at the quote. The juniors continue assembling the filing.)*

**Junior reactions:** silence. Cula does not reply, does not glance toward the operating-account ledger, does not catch Crab's or Whimsy's eye. The reserve number sits in Cula's head; the line about the logbook sits in Pig's mouth; the two do not meet in the scene.

## Lecture 4 — Chapter 5, Beat 0 (retraversal, post-family-photo focal moment)

**Theme:** the forging.
**Maritime density:** low (Pig is subdued post-printer-apology and post-Swine-bill-arrival; the lecture itself has no nautical material; the maritime sits in the idle line about the new bill that immediately precedes the lecture).
**Embedded in:** the Beat 0 retraversal. Cula has just walked into the office. The framed family photo on Pig's desk is in the same beat — examined or not, depending on player choice. Pig's idle line about the new Swine bill has just been delivered.

> **Mr. Pig:** *(looking down at the desk, not at Cula; the Swine bill in one hand and a pen he is not using in the other)* The apprenticeship is supposed to forge you. We had three chairs for five apprentices and no one petitioned the master about the seating; we considered it part of the forging. I am forged, Cula.
>
> *(He sets the bill down, picks it up again, sets it down. The courier's footsteps are not yet audible in the corridor.)*

**Junior reactions:** silence. Cula does not respond to the line; if Cula has examined the photo, Cula has new context but does not show it; if Cula has not examined the photo, the lecture is sincere and the recoloring waits. Either way, no comment.

## Open calls for the §J pass (carried from the original draft, still relevant)

1. **Ch5 ending: "I am forged, Cula."** This is the highest-risk line in the set. It must read as Pig sincerely owning his self-narrative, not as authorial irony pointing the player at the photo. If §J flags it as too obviously the moral, candidates for replacement: deflect to the bill ("The bill — where did I put the bill"), or trail off ("I am — well."). Current preference: keep "I am forged, Cula" because the photo recoloring is the lever, and the line needs to be recolorable.

2. **Ch4 "A logbook for toner, Cula."** The vocative might be one too many. Pig uses Cula's name in Ch1, Ch4, and Ch5. Three vocatives across four lectures is a tic; if §J flags it, drop the Ch4 vocative and let "A logbook for toner." stand alone.

3. **Ch2 trailing em-dash on "Who emptied the kettle —".** If §J reads this as an AI tic (the ambiguous trail-off), substitute a hard period: "Who emptied the kettle." — declarative, irritable, no rhythm cue.

4. **Borowski name distribution.** Ch1 + Ch4 only. If §J wants reinforcement (so the player carries the name into Ch5's photo), add it to Ch5 ("at Borowski's" before "we considered it part of the forging"). Currently held back to keep Ch5's specificity on the chairs and the "I am forged" landing.

5. **Junior silence.** No flourishes. If §J finds any of the lectures too dry without one, the natural slot is Ch2 (Whimsy, after Pig has wandered out of earshot, in cynic register, not articulating the comparison). Default candidate if needed: Whimsy mutters, half-attention, "...the kettle." — picking up Pig's last word as a small private joke.

## New open call (added by localization pass)

6. **"Master" register check.** The English-version translation of *patron* is "master" in the apprentice-sense. Used in Ch5: "no one petitioned the master about the seating." If a phase-7 reader finds "master" reads as old-fashioned or off-tone in modern legal English, candidates for replacement: "the senior" (works but flatter), "the partner-in-charge" (too institutional), or "old Borowski" (more colloquial; loses the apprenticeship register). Current preference: keep "master" — the master/apprentice register is comprehensible and culturally distinctive, which is the point.
