## Beat 10 — Court readiness check at the office

### Beat 10 — opening (team has returned from Archive Room; Murrow runs the readiness check)

*The Pig & Swine open-office area. Cula, Crab, and Whimsy have just returned from the Archive Room. The case folder is closed under Cula's arm. Murrow is at his desk, the Procedural Binder squared on his blotter, a list in front of him, a pencil resting on the page. The case board behind him still carries the Service / Fair hearing / Remedy notation in Whimsy's hand. Asia is at the reception desk, head down over the morning's calendar. A telephone is ringing somewhere beyond the corridor and is not being answered yet.*

*Cula crosses to Murrow's desk. Crab sets his laptop on his work table and leaves it closed. Whimsy lays the Rights Memo binder, coffee-ring margin visible, on the chair behind Cula's borrowed desk.*

**MURROW:** Cula.

**DR. A. CULA:** Murrow.

*Murrow does not look up. He reads items off the list in his head.*

**MURROW:** Motion filed. Hearing on the cause list at fourteen hundred. Courtroom four.

**CRAB:** Service-and-knowledge appendix is in.

**WHIMSY:** Third-party non-cure is in the binder. The trapdoor has a label.

*Murrow's pencil ticks once against the list. He does not nod; the tick is the acknowledgment.*

---

### Beat 10 — Pig's printer-lease aside (first visit)

*Mr. Pig drifts past Murrow's desk, blazer half-buttoned, a sheaf of paper under one arm. He slows past Murrow's desk and speaks toward the room.*

**MR. PIG:** The printer lease has come back as a kraken. We renew next month, presumably with bait.

*Pig keeps walking, papers shifting under his arm. Murrow does not look up. Cula does not respond. Murrow's pencil moves to the next line.*

---

### Beat 10 — Pig's first lecture (second visit)

*A short interval. Murrow has flipped his list to a second page. Pig drifts back the other way, slower now, a coffee cup in one hand. He stops near Cula but does not look at him.*

**MR. PIG:** When I was an apprentice, we had no readiness check. Only fear. We navigated by panic alone.

*Pig drifts back toward the corridor, coffee cup loose in his hand. Cula does not respond. Murrow turns the page.*

---

### Beat 10 — closing (Cula's stance-aligned bonus-evidence confirm; Murrow's transition cue)

*Murrow looks briefly at the folder under Cula's arm.*

**MURROW:** Cula. What we tender.

*Cula opens the folder and checks the top sheet.*

**(if sympathetic carrier)**

**DR. A. CULA:** The motion. The Wójcik affidavit if the court asks for character witness.

**(if blunt-procedural carrier)**

**DR. A. CULA:** The motion. The return-to-sender slip on knowledge.

**(if technical carrier)**

**DR. A. CULA:** The motion. The countersignature on the renewal.

*Murrow's pencil ticks once more. He closes the list. Sets the pencil on its cover.*

**MURROW:** We walk at a quarter past one.

*Crab lifts the service-and-knowledge appendix from his work table. Whimsy collects the Rights Memo binder. Cula tucks the case folder under his arm. Murrow rises and lifts the Procedural Binder from his desk.*

## Beat 11 — Walk to the District Court

### Beat 11 — opening (team leaves the office; brief stage directions; Warsaw daytime register established)

*Office Street. The team turns out of the firm's doorway onto the pavement. Cula leads with the case folder. Crab keeps to his right. Whimsy keeps to his left. Murrow brings up the rear with the Procedural Binder under one arm. The sky is a flat Warsaw grey. A tram bell sounds two blocks east. A public-works truck idles outside a renovation; the workman in the cab is reading a folded-up newspaper. Two clerks in suits cross the opposite pavement carrying folders bigger than their forearms.*

*No dialogue. The team walks.*

---

### Beat 11 — walk

*East two blocks, then north along the courts' embankment. The river is on the left, low and brown; the embankment trees are in the dust-colour of late spring. A pigeon lifts from the bronze head of a statesman. Two more pigeons hold their ground on the embankment railing as the team passes. The pigeons' eyes do not move. The courthouse pediment becomes visible at the far end of the embankment, sandstone over the trees. Whimsy glances at the railing.*

**WHIMSY:** Judgmental pigeons. The chorus is in already.

*Cula does not quite smile. Murrow's pace does not change.*

---

### Beat 11 — arrival (the courthouse becomes visible; Halina meets the team at the steps)

*The District Court Plaza opens out at the end of the embankment. The courthouse is a long sandstone building with a pediment that has held three different state seals across the century; the current one is in fresh bronze. The plaza is sparse: a clerk on a phone, a woman with a folder under one arm, a man in a black suit smoking near the railing. Halina Sikorska is at the foot of the steps. She is in the same grey wool jacket from the meeting; her folder is squared under one arm; her handbag on the other shoulder. Her posture is upright, with the same slight stiffness she had at the meeting. She sees the team approach and steps a quarter-pace forward to meet them at the foot of the steps.*

*The team reaches her.*

**HALINA:** Dr. A. Cula. Mr. Murrow. Mr. Crab. Mr. Whimsy.

**DR. A. CULA:** Mrs. Sikorska.

**CRAB:** Mrs. Sikorska.

**WHIMSY:** Mrs. Sikorska — a brief courtesy.

*Murrow inclines his head and looks back to the courthouse doors. Halina returns the incline.*

**HALINA:** I came on the tram. I am here in time.

**MURROW:** Courtroom four. Inside.

*Halina turns and falls in beside Cula. Crab and Whimsy fall in behind. Murrow brings up the rear, the Procedural Binder under his arm. The team crosses the plaza and ascends the courthouse steps. The doors are open.*
