## BEAT 1 — Reception desk, first morning

*Pig & Swine office interior, early morning. Asia is at the reception desk. Three file piles to her left, an overflowing wall calendar above her, the desk phone ringing. Mr. Pig paces a short loop near the case board, gesturing at no one in particular. Mr. Murrow is partly visible in the file area behind a shelf — his back, a sleeve, the corner of a binder. He does not look up. The coffee machine on the side wall makes a noise that sounds admissible only under seal.*

*Asia picks up the phone, says "One moment, please," sets it gently back in the cradle, and turns to Cula.*

**ASIA:** Dr. A. Cula, good morning. We've been expecting you.

**ASIA:** Your blue folder is on the second pile from the left. The archive is through that door, and Mr. Murrow is in there with the case file whenever you're ready for him.

*The phone starts ringing again. Asia notes it without alarm, slides a sandwich-labelled envelope into the third pile, and continues.*

**ASIA:** Mr. Pig is at the case board. He'll want to say hello first.

*Mr. Pig has been audibly muttering throughout. As Cula steps forward, he half-turns mid-stride, sees Cula, and the sentence collapses.*

**MR. PIG:** It would appear that we are...

*He stops. Sets a hand briefly against the case board for balance.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Mr. Pig. Good morning.

## BEAT 2 — Mr. Pig reveals the rent crisis

*Mr. Pig recovers his composure for exactly one sentence. Then it goes. The framed family photo sits on the corner of his desk; the diploma hangs above; a casebook leans on the shelf behind him. None of these are remarked upon. They are office decoration.*

**MR. PIG:** Dr. A. Cula. Welcome. You arrive during a moment.

**MR. PIG:** The firm is not bankrupt. Bankruptcy requires paperwork, and our paperwork is currently underwater along with the rest of us.

**MR. PIG:** The rent is six weeks overdue.

**MR. PIG:** Six weeks under the surface. The landlord knocks on the hull. Politely. The polite knock is somehow worse than a loud one.

**MR. PIG:** Can a firm sail while six weeks underwater? I have asked myself this. The honest answer appears to be yes, on momentum.

**MR. PIG:** So. We float. In the way that boats with leaks float, which is to say, less every morning.

*Cula does not interrupt. He waits until Pig stops, then speaks once.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Six weeks. Understood.

*Mr. Pig looks toward the archive door as though the question itself has briefed him. He starts a sentence twice and abandons each one.*

**MR. PIG:** Mr. Murrow has the folder. Mr. Murrow knows the— ask Mr. Murrow.

*Cula gives a short nod. He does not press Pig further.*

**DR. A. CULA:** I'll find him.

**QUEST BOX:** Save Pig & Swine. Ask Murrow what the case is actually about.
