## BEAT 3 - Find Murrow

*Murrow's desk, file area. He is annotating a stack of case files with a pencil. The desk is orderly. A closed ledger sits beside the blotter as ordinary office furniture. Pig's panic continues at audible distance from the other side of the office. The case file open in front of Murrow has tabs flagged in three colours.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Mr. Murrow. I was told you'd have the Borowski file.

*Murrow looks up. Sets the pencil down. A brief look - taking Cula in.*

**MURROW:** Doctor Cula. Welcome to Pig & Swine. You've picked a noisy day to begin.

*He turns the open case file ninety degrees so Cula can read it.*

**MURROW:** Three procedural defects in the plaintiff's complaint. The first is service. The notice was sent to an address the client has not occupied for two years.

**MURROW:** The second is the response window. Filed inside the statutory limit if you count properly.

**MURROW:** The third is the joinder. They have stacked a payment claim onto a tenancy dispute. Article 191 does not allow it the way they have used it.

**MURROW:** The procedural binder is on the second shelf, blue spine. The rights memo is clipped inside the front cover. Hearing is at fourteen hundred, courtroom 4.

**DR. A. CULA:** The client's current address. Is it on file with the court?

**MURROW:** It is. Filed eighteen months ago in another matter. Same panel.

**DR. A. CULA:** Thank you, Mr. Murrow.

**MURROW:** It is Murrow, to friends. The 'Mister' I keep for invoices.

**DR. A. CULA:** Then it's Cula.

**MURROW:** I am still deciding whether reception staff qualify as friends or as workplace constellations.

**DR. A. CULA:** She'll be relieved either way.

*A beat. Murrow's mouth moves in something that is not quite a smile. Pig's voice carries briefly across the office. Murrow's pencil taps once against the desk.*

**MURROW:** Binder first. Memo second. The defects matter in that order. Find me before fourteen hundred if anything in the binder reads wrong.

**ITEM DESCRIPTION:** "a binder so dense it has its own weather system."

**DR. A. CULA:** Heavy.

## BEAT 5 - Crab recruitment

*Crab is at a work table near the window. Laptop open, three stacks of paper sorted by size. He is reading something on screen. He does not look up immediately as Cula approaches.*

### First approach (binder not yet collected)

**DR. A. CULA:** Crab. I'm Cula. I could use a second pair of eyes on the Borowski file before fourteen hundred.

*Crab finishes the line he's reading. Looks up. Closes the laptop halfway.*

**CRAB:** Cula. Heard.

*He glances at the spread of paper on his table, then back.*

**CRAB:** The binder is not in the room. I cannot check service without the binder.

**DR. A. CULA:** Right.

*Cula turns toward the archive.*

### Second approach (binder secured)

*Cula returns. Sets the binder on the corner of Crab's table.*

**DR. A. CULA:** Here.

*Crab glances at the spine. Pulls the binder toward him. Opens to the tabbed section.*

**CRAB:** The binder cites Article 132. The notice mentions service. I can check that.

**DR. A. CULA:** Good.

*Crab is already reading. Cula moves to the next quest beat.*

**Uncertain lines flagged for arbitration:**

3. Murrow's closing - "Find me before fourteen hundred if anything in the binder reads wrong." Borderline-reassurance; defensible as availability arrangement, the binder being the subject rather than Cula's wellbeing.

6. Cula's clarifying question - sentence-fragment-then-question form. Alternative: smoother continuous sentence form. Arbitrate against read-aloud test.
