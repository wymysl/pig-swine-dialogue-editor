## BEAT 7 — Recruit Whimsy at Café Paragraf

*[Interior. Whimsy at a corner table, eggplant velvet blazer, notebook open, a half-finished espresso at his elbow. Cula approaches.]*

**CULA:** Mr. Whimsy. Cula, Pig & Swine. We need a second voice in court tomorrow.

**WHIMSY:** Cula. Sit. Have you anything beyond procedural vibes, or am I to be recruited on atmosphere?

**CULA:** Notice went to an address the client left two years ago. The objection is in the papers; the record does not show it. Hearing at fourteen hundred.

**WHIMSY:** Ah. The fair-hearing doctrine, asked to do its gymnastics again. Notice to an address without a client. An objection present in paper and absent from the record. I have heard worse music.

*[Whimsy reaches into his bag, produces a folder, slides it across the table. The top page is the Rights Memo. A coffee ring darkens the upper margin.]*

**CULA:** The Rights Memo.

**WHIMSY:** Marginalia in espresso. Half the argument is already mine.

*[Beat.]*

**WHIMSY:** I propose. The fair-hearing argument can sing. The service defect gives it a throat. I will lend my voice tomorrow at fourteen hundred.
