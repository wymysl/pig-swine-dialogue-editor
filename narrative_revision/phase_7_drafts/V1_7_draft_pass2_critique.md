> **Failure:** *"The coffee machine makes a low sound and stops before anyone has to take a position on it."*
> **Mode:** §C.4 coffee-machine discipline / §A social-context discipline. V1.7 allows the coffee machine as ambient stage direction only. This line makes the machine a legal-comic participant ("take a position") and foregrounds the gag at exactly the moment the office return needs clean spatial setup.
> **Fix:** Replace with a flat ambient beat: *"The coffee machine clicks once and stops."*

> **Failure:** **MR. PIG:** *"We have come through another impossible thing. The client — yes, the client — also remains housed until the next date. The rent will breathe; the printer can wait; the 5,000 PLN Sikorska fee came through. Mr. Swine's retainer is still — somewhere. Sea of Japan. Presumably alive."*
> **Mode:** §B.1 Pig / H4 sentence-budget and return-to-anxiety structure. The line contains six spoken sentences in one paragraph. The three-sentence celebration proper is present, but the missing-retainer aside is fused into the same paragraph, which blurs the undercut and makes the return-to-anxiety feel like one more item in the list rather than the canonical third financial claim landing after relief. The draft self-check calls it a paragraph; a hostile reader will count it as overfull.
> **Fix:** Split the celebration and the retainer aside into two Pig beats:
> **MR. PIG:** *"We have come through another impossible thing. The client — yes, the client — also has a new date. The rent will breathe; the printer can wait; the 5,000 PLN Sikorska fee came through."*
> **MR. PIG:** *"Mr. Swine's retainer is still — somewhere. Sea of Japan. Presumably alive."*

> **Failure:** **MR. PIG:** *"Yes. Yes, the next thing. Also the retainer. Possibly the same thing."*
> **Mode:** §B.1 Pig exit beat / third-financial-claim structure. The distractable-anxious exit is permitted, but repeating the retainer immediately after the canonical aside hammers the setup and weakens the Beat-14 postcard's structural joke. The retainer should land once, then be paid off by the Sapporo card.
> **Fix:** Keep the exit anxious but move it away from the retainer: *"Yes. Yes, the next thing. Before the next thing finds us."*

> **Failure:** *"Pig reads the body aloud, sincerely and without understanding the timing."*
> **Mode:** §B.7 Swine / §B.1 Pig / Beat-14 structural-comedy discipline. The stage direction explains the joke to the reader and explicitly diagnoses Pig's awareness. The pack says Pig is not in on the joke and the player carries the structural comedy; the draft should not narrate that gap.
> **Fix:** Flatten to observable staging: *"Pig reads the body aloud."*

> **Marginal:** *"The Pig & Swine open-office area is almost exactly as Cula left it, which makes the change in the room more noticeable."*
> **Concern:** This is writerly explanation rather than staging. It tells the reader how to read the room instead of letting the unchanged props and altered tempo do the work.
> **Suggestion:** Replace with concrete room state only: *"The Pig & Swine open-office area still has the case board, the reception stacks, and Murrow's closed drawer in their morning positions."*

> **Marginal:** *"Whimsy looks from the case board to Pig as if he has just heard an overture tuning itself."*
> **Concern:** This pre-scores Whimsy's musical proposal before he speaks. It is a neat writerly flourish, and V1.7 asks for the musical image to land in Whimsy's line, not in narration around him.
> **Suggestion:** Cut or simplify: *"Whimsy looks from the case board to Pig."*

> **Marginal:** **CRAB:** *"The countersignature carried the floor. The order does what the ground required."*
> **Concern:** The two-sentence rhythm is right, but "floor" and "ground" create a little metaphor system. Crab's office indifference is better when it is flatter. The sample direction permits "carried the floor," but pairing it with "ground required" makes it feel composed.
> **Suggestion:** *"The countersignature was on the document. The hearing is reset."*

> **Marginal:** **MR. PIG:** *"Greetings from Sapporo. A very serious gentleman in the lobby has proposed a venture involving conference centers and possibly fish, and I have given him my card on principle. Please keep the firm solvent until I clarify which principle. Yours, S."*
> **Concern:** The H6 draft defends "possibly fish," but in V1.7 isolation it still risks reading as random absurdity rather than vague-and-slightly-wrong business opportunity. The postcard should snag, not dissolve into word salad. Also, because the signature is read in the same quoted block, a hostile sentence-count reader may count four sentences despite the three-sentence-plus-signature convention.
> **Suggestion:** Either keep as H6-authorized and format the signature separately, or roughen the opportunity into a clearer bad deal: *"A very serious gentleman in the lobby has proposed a venture involving conference centers and a fisheries contact, and I have given him my card on principle."*

> **Marginal:** **MR. PIG:** *"The Sea of Japan, then."*
> **Concern:** This line is canonically permitted, but in this pass it arrives after the retainer has already been repeated in the exit line. With both lines present, the continuity is overflagged.
> **Suggestion:** Leave this line only if the Beat-13 exit no longer mentions the retainer. Otherwise cut it and let the postcard sit.

> **Marginal:** *"The office does not celebrate again."*
> **Concern:** This is the no-catharsis rule stated as prose. It is correct as discipline but weak as scene text.
> **Suggestion:** Delete the sentence. The preceding object-state lines already close the chapter without triumph.
