## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut. AI overuses em-dashes for rhythm.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern. **Pig's lecture especially vulnerable** ("we did not have unions — we had each other" — the lecture is the place AI most reliably reaches for this shape; categorically cut).
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions. Use only if a character would.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would. **Pig's lecture especially vulnerable** ("the work, the discipline, the hours" — categorically cut). **Crab's two-sentence rhythm is two sentences, not three.**
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough" / "and so we continue."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."
- **Pig-lecture-specific tells:** AI will pad the three sentences into a paragraph. Hold three. AI will reach for the contrastive "we did not have X — we had Y" pattern. AI will reach for triplet lists of past-hardship items. Hold the chapter_1.md sample shape: one declarative + two supporting sentences; sincere; brief; he leaves.
- **Murrow-readiness-specific tells:** AI will pad the readiness check into a pep talk. Murrow does not pep. The check is mechanical inventory. Three or four short declaratives; he is reading items off a list; the list is in his head; he does not editorialise.

**Stylistic disciplines:**

- Concrete nouns over abstractions. English-equivalent legal specifics over generic abstractions. See §A.5.
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical. Pig's panic register has snags by nature; Murrow's terse register has snags by truncation; Cula's bracing register has snags by short-sentence interruption. The walk is the easiest place to drift into unsnagged smoothness — resist.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite. Particularly the lecture; particularly the pigeons line.
- One canonical sincere line per character per chapter. **Sincerity is rationed. Chapter 1 has no fully sincere line for any speaker.** Pig's Beat 10 lecture is sincere-as-memory and reads sincere on first pass — this is character, not a sincere break. **No character has a sincere break in Beat 10 or 11.**
