# Phase 7 Pack H6 — Mr. Swine's postcards

**Goal:** author the four canonical postcards from Mr. Swine that punctuate chapters 1-5: chapter 1 final stinger (first postcard, sets up the formula); chapter 2 final stinger (second postcard, hint of escalating chaos); chapter 4 chapter-5 hook (postcard with attached bill, "small misunderstanding with a ski resort"); chapter 6 Beat 14 (postcard read aloud while Swine is physically present — the formula's comic-deflationary closure).

**Note on count:** the manifest references "five postcards" but per the bibles and beat sheets there are **four postcards proper** plus one related Swine in-person line at chapter 5 Beat 16 ("he should be in the office tomorrow"). The Beat 16 line is in-person dialogue — covered separately in V4.8 chapter-vertical pack — not a postcard. H6 authors the four postcards.

**Why this pack:** the postcard formula is one of the project's cleanest comic running gags. The "Greetings from [place], [business opportunity], stay solvent" structure repeats across chapters 1-3 with variations, then reaches its comic-deflationary closure in chapter 6 when Swine reads a postcard aloud despite being physically present in the office. By chapter 6 the player learns the postcards were partly cover for Swine's pro-bono redirections (per style_canon §3 + chapter-6 Beat 10 reveal); the formula recolors retroactively.

**Estimated session length:** 1-2 hours. Four short postcards + one address-label flavor line per chapter for Asia. The discipline is consistency of the formula across chapters with deliberate variation.

**How to use this pack:** open a new Claude conversation. Upload this pack. Issue the §H drafting prompt at the bottom. Hand the result to a second model with §I for hostile critique.

## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern.
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would.
- Generic uplifting endings.
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."

**Stylistic disciplines:**
- Concrete nouns over abstractions. Use English-language legal specifics where applicable; postcards do NOT need legal vocabulary.
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical.
- Read-aloud test. If a postcard reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as something written and posted, rewrite. Postcards are short, slightly chaotic, and have the awkward rhythm of someone writing in transit.

## §A.5 — Language convention (English version)

**The English version reads smoothly for an English-speaking player with no Polish-language training.** Postcards have less Polish-vocabulary risk than most pack contents because they're traveling-Swine register, not Polish-legal register.

**Acceptable in postcard text:**
- Proper place names: Sapporo, Hokkaido, Bali, Tokyo, Kraków (without diacritic — "Krakow"), Reykjavik, etc.
- Polish proper names of people if mentioned (rare in postcards).
- PLN currency.
- Cosmopolitan name-drops (per Swine voice rules — he name-drops cities).

**Translate / localize:** none of the postcards should require Polish-legal vocabulary. If a postcard mentions a Polish institution (e.g., a tax inspectorate), translate.

**Pronunciation rule for invented place names:** if any invented place names appear (e.g., a fictional ski resort), apply the easy-pronunciation rule from `ai_voice_constraints.md §1.5`. Real-world place names (Sapporo, Hokkaido, Bali) are fine.

## §B — Per-character constraints

### Mr. Swine (the postcard author)

**Voice essence:** cheerful evasion. Long, smooth, slightly traveled sentences that reveal less the longer they go. **Postcards are Swine's voice in compressed form** — they show his evasion register at its most distilled.

**Canonical samples:**
- *"I signed many things. Most were menus. One may have been laminated."*
- *"This sentence is not a contract. It is politeness wearing a very expensive hat."*

**Syntax disciplines:**
- Cosmopolitan vocabulary, name-drops cities.
- Says "personally" and "informally" too often.
- Uses "we" when he means "I" and "I" when he means "the firm."
- Sentences that get longer the more evasive he is.
- Notices first: an opportunity, usually a bad one.

**Will not say (in postcards):**
- The direct truth about what he's actually doing (the pro-bono redirections). Postcards are *partly cover* — the player learns this at chapter 6 Beat 10.
- Anything that resolves why he is traveling.
- Anything venal about clients.
- Anything cruel to Pig.
- Generic "I'm sorry" or "I hope you're well" register.
- Anything that breaks the postcard formula (greeting + business opportunity + stay-solvent).

**AI tells most vulnerable to (specific to postcards):**
- **Generic touristy postcard register** ("Wish you were here!" / "The weather is lovely!"). Cut. Swine's postcards are cosmopolitan-evasive, not generic-touristy. Each postcard is specifically about a vague business opportunity in a specific place.
- **Symmetric formula execution.** AI will follow the formula too rigidly: greeting → opportunity → stay-solvent in clean three-beat order. Real postcards from Swine snag — sentences trail, the opportunity is half-explained, the stay-solvent line lands in different positions. Phase 7 should produce variation within the formula.
- **Direct truth slipping through.** AI may write Swine being clear about what he's doing. Cut. Until Beat 10 chapter 6, Swine is *partly cover*; the postcards must be cheerful-evasive.
- **Excessive name-dropping.** Swine name-drops, but each postcard is short — one or two name-drops per postcard, not five.
- **The chapter-6 Beat 14 postcard read with too much self-awareness.** The joke at chapter 6 Beat 14 is that Swine reads the postcard aloud despite being physically present. **Swine is NOT in on the joke.** He reads it sincerely; the comedy is the player's. Phase 7 voice work must hold this — no winking.

**Quick-reference:** hidden idealist with cheerful evasion as armor. Postcards are his armor in compressed form. Greeting from place + business opportunity + stay-solvent. Each postcard slightly varies the formula. By chapter 6 the postcards recolor as partly cover for the pro-bono redirections; the chapter-6 Beat 14 postcard's place-twist ("Greetings from Pig & Swine, Warsaw") is the formula's comic-deflationary closure.

### Asia (delivers postcards to Mr. Pig; reads address labels)

**Voice essence:** warm, dry, practical. Cheerful in tone, exhausted in content.

**Asia's role with postcards:** in chapter 1 (canonical from story.txt), "Asia reads the address label and says: [line]." Asia delivers each postcard to Mr. Pig and may comment on the address or the place. Brief warm-practical lines.

**Will not say:**
- Legal opinion in her own voice.
- Anything that breaks her warm-practical register.
- Anything cruel about Mr. Swine or his postcards.

**AI tells most vulnerable to (postcards-specific):**
- **Sharp commentary on the postcards.** Per Asia bible: her single sharp moment is the STUB decline (Pack H5). Everything else stays warm-practical. **Asia's postcard-related lines are warm-practical only.** No quips about Swine's travel patterns, no observations about the postcards' implausibility.
- **Editorial commentary.** Asia delivers; she does not editorialize.

**For postcard delivery:** Asia announces the postcard's arrival, possibly notes the address (place name), hands it over. Brief. One sentence per chapter at most.

## §C — The postcard formula

**Canonical structure (per style_canon §3 + Swine bible):**

1. **Greeting from a place.** "Greetings from [place]." The place is geographically implausible enough to be funny but not punchline-y on its own.
2. **A business opportunity.** Always announces one. Vague, slightly wrong, possibly self-undermining.
3. **A request to stay solvent.** Asks Pig & Swine to remain financially viable. Direct or indirect.

**Variation discipline:**
- The formula's three elements are present in each postcard but in **different proportions and orders**.
- Postcard 1 (Ch 1) establishes the formula clearly.
- Postcard 2 (Ch 2) varies the proportions slightly; opportunity is more chaotic.
- Postcard 3 (Ch 3, with bill) breaks the formula — the bill makes the "stay solvent" line ironic; the opportunity element disappears or is undermined by the bill.
- Postcard 4 (Ch 5 Beat 14) is the formula's comic-deflationary closure — read aloud while Swine is present, with a place-twist that names the office itself as the place.

**Length:** each postcard is 2-4 sentences. No paragraphs. Postcards are short by definition.

**Tonal commitment:** postcards are *cheerful-evasive*, not touristy. The cheerfulness is Swine's armor; the evasion is the substance.

**Recoloring:** by chapter 6, the player learns "the postcards were partly cover" (style_canon §3, Swine bible). All four postcards recolor retroactively — the cheerful-evasion was hiding the pro-bono redirections; the business opportunities were partly real, partly fabricated to justify travel.

## §G — Cross-postcard coherence rules

**The four postcards must work as a set.** Phase 7 voice work must hold:

1. **The formula's three elements appear in every postcard** (with the chapter-6 closure being the formula's deliberate self-collapse).
2. **The proportions vary.** Each postcard emphasizes a different element. Postcard 1 establishes; postcard 2 escalates the opportunity; postcard 3 disrupts (the bill); postcard 4 closes (the place-twist).
3. **Each postcard is short.** 2-4 sentences. No paragraphs.
4. **Cheerful-evasive tonal register throughout.** No touristy generic. No direct truth.
5. **No two postcards use the same place.** Postcard 1 / 2 / 3 places vary; postcard 4 is the place-twist (the office itself).
6. **The Sapporo/Hokkaido/ski-resort thread is canonical** — the chapter-1 postcard sets up Japan; the chapter-4 postcard concerns a ski resort there; the chapter-5 arbitration is about that ski resort. Phase 7 may iterate specific locations within this thread.
7. **Asia's address-label/delivery line is brief and warm-practical** in every chapter — one sentence per chapter at most.
8. **The chapter-6 Beat 14 postcard is read sincerely by Swine.** He is not winking. The comedy is structural, not character-aware.
