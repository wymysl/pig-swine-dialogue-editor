# Phase 7 Pack H1 — Round 5 four-line cluster

**Goal:** draft the four-line cluster at chapter 6 Round 5 (Cula's procedural-remedy opening + Whimsy's closing-support flourish + Pig's canonical sincere line + the judge's remedy announcement, plus Swine's testimony and the judge's response to it).

**Why this is the first session:** this is the spine's Critical-hit phase-7 expression (per pressure_test.md Hit #18). If any one of these lines lands as the round's emotional climax, the cathartic-resolution failure mode appears and the spine slips. Author the lines together, sit on the draft, re-read with hostile eyes, then commit.

**Estimated session length:** 2-3 hours. Expect the first draft to take 45 minutes; the critique-and-revision loop another 60-90 minutes; the read-aloud and final scrub another 30-45 minutes.

**How to use this pack:** open a new Claude conversation. Paste this file as your first message (or upload it). Then issue the drafting instruction at the bottom. After the first draft, hand the result to a second model (GPT-5 or another Claude session) for hostile critique, using the same pack as context. Arbitrate disagreements yourself. Commit the four lines once you're satisfied.

## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern.
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions. Use only if a character would.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would.
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."

**Stylistic disciplines:**
- Concrete nouns over abstractions. Use English-language legal specifics ("Article 132 of the Code of Civil Procedure," "the apprenticeship," "the case file") over generic abstractions, AND over Polish-borrowed terms (see Language convention §A.5 below).
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite.
- One canonical sincere line per character per chapter. **Sincerity is rationed.**

## §A.5 — Language convention (English version)

**The English version reads smoothly for an English-speaking player with no Polish-language training.** The bibles and beat sheets use Polish-legal vocabulary internally for precision; **dialogue does not**.

**Acceptable in English dialogue:**
- Proper names of people: Wilkanowicz, Plotek, Kowalski, etc.
- Warsaw place names where flagship: the Vistula, Marszałkowska, Saska Kępa.
- PLN currency.
- Zero-to-one Polish flavor word per scene as deliberate texture, glossed inline first time. **Do not exceed one per scene.**

**Translate to English** (do NOT borrow into dialogue):
- *sąd rejonowy / okręgowy / najwyższy* → "district court" / "regional court" / "Supreme Court"
- *pełnomocnictwo* → "power of attorney"
- *wokanda* → "docket" / "cause list"
- *aplikatura* → "the apprenticeship"
- *aplikant* → "apprentice"
- *patron* → "master" (apprentice-sense)
- *kancelaria* → "the firm" / "the practice"
- KPC / KK / KKS → "the Code of Civil Procedure" / "the Penal Code" / "the Fiscal Penal Code" — or "the Code" when clear.

**Critical for this pack (Round 5):** Cula's procedural-remedy opening cites "the four legal defects." In the English version, name them in English: "defective notice, lack of individual assessment, technical suppression, conflict of interest." Do not borrow Polish technical vocabulary. The judge's remedy announcement also names the four defects in English. The Polish version (when it exists) will use the original terminology.

**Test:** if a non-Polish English-speaking reader needs a dictionary, translate the term.

## §B — Per-character constraints (only the speaking characters in Round 5)

### Mr. Pig

**Voice essence:** formal-while-falling-apart. Sincere panic. Maritime metaphors under stress.

**Canonical samples:**
- *"The firm is not bankrupt. Bankruptcy has paperwork. We are currently in the pre-paperwork screaming phase."*
- *"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."* (Ch 5 sincere line — this one is the Round 5 line we are authoring around.)

**Syntax disciplines:**
- Sentences start composed and escalate.
- Maritime metaphor density ~1 in 4 lines, denser in panic, **absent in calm/sincere moments**. **In Round 5: maritime register absent.**
- Stock openers: "We are currently in...", "It would appear that...", "If anything, this confirms..."
- Frequent rhetorical questions answered by himself.
- Dramatic re-framing of bad news as good news.

**Will not say:**
- Anything that diagnoses the firm's structure as unjust to the juniors.
- Anything that admits his austerity is voluntary while theirs isn't.
- Anything that frames himself as having missed something significant about the firm.
- Anything cynical about the firm's mission.
- Anything venal about clients.
- "Heart of" abstractions about the firm.
- Self-aware lines that diagnose his own situation.

**AI tells most vulnerable to in Round 5:**
- Generic uplifting / cathartic speeches in sincere mode. **Especially here.** The canonical line ("we are not efficient...") is the half-right portrait at full intensity. Hold the line: he is sincere AND not articulate.
- "Heart of" / "essence of" abstractions about the firm's purpose.
- Maritime metaphor overshoot. **In Round 5: maritime register absent. Strict.**
- Self-aware lines that diagnose his own situation. He doesn't see it.

**Quick-reference:** sincere idealist with a class blind spot. The canonical Round 5 line is character, not basis of the remedy. **The line lands; the player feels its sincerity; the judge does not reward it.**

**Will not say:**

**AI tells most vulnerable to in Round 5:**

- Generic "I was wrong" redemptive lines.

**Quick-reference:** hidden idealist with cheerful evasion as armor. By Round 5 his portrait grows more sincere — same face, less evasive mouth. The canonical line is character, not basis. **The judge's response is dry acknowledgment.**

- **CRITICAL FOR ROUND 5 (phase-6 second-pass residual N1):** Whimsy's flourish in Round 5 must NOT be the round's emotional climax. The flourish reinforces the procedural argument; recommended framing: a flourish that names the four legal defects together as a single proposition, bitter accuracy theatricalized rather than new emotional content. **If Whimsy's line lands as the round's emotional peak, the spine slips by routing the cathartic-resolution failure through him instead of Pig.**

**Quick-reference:** the theatrical cynic. His Round 5 flourish is the cynic's bitter accuracy theatricalized — locking the procedural argument in memorable rights language. NOT new emotional content. NOT the climax.

**Round structure (style_canon §6):**

- Five rounds for the final court in Chapter 6.
- Round 5 question: remedy and social utility. **Per phase-6 revision: the load-bearing argument is procedural; social utility is supportive, not basis.**

**Wrong-answer templates** (for reference; not used in Round 5 directly but characteristic of the register):
- "The client was spiritually present."
- "The envelope looked guilty."
- "The deadline was emotionally unreasonable."
- "Counsel relies on ambient fairness."
- "The relevant document is morally on file."

**Judicial reactions — dry surprise.** Correct judicial reactions are dry surprise, never explicit praise. The judge sounds like they were not expecting to be persuaded and now have to be.

**Remedy discipline.** The remedy is modest and procedurally plausible. The player wins enough to save the client, not everything. **Total victories are not on the table; procedural reprieves, fair-hearing resets, narrowed orders, and remitted matters are.** This applies at chapter scale; chapter 6 Round 5 follows the same discipline at game scale.

## §D — Beat sheet excerpt: chapter 6 Round 5 (post-phase-6 revision)

**(From `narrative_revision/beats/chapter_5.md`. This is the canonical specification.)**

### Round 5 — Remedy and social utility — CRITICAL SPINE BEAT

**Phase-6 commits an explicit revision of Round 5's structure to prevent the cathartic-resolution failure mode.**

Round 5 opens with Cula's procedural-remedy argument as the round's load-bearing argument-content. Pig's and Swine's canonical sincere lines are character moments inside the round, not the basis of the decision. The judge's responses to their testimonies are dry acknowledgment. The judge's remedy announcement cites the four legal defects from rounds 1-4 (defective notice, lack of individual assessment, technical suppression, conflict of interest), not the firm's social utility.

#### Round structure

1. **Cula opens the round** with the procedural-remedy argument. Recommended shape: *"The remedy follows the defects. The notice was defective. The assessment was absent. The objections were suppressed. The benefit was private. The court need not reach the firm's social utility to set aside the scheme."* The line explicitly tells the court that social utility is not the basis. The judge can rule on legal grounds without rejecting the social-utility framing entirely.

2. **Whimsy closing support.** Theatrical-cynic flourish that locks the procedural argument in memorable rights language. **Phase-6 N1: must NOT be the round's emotional climax.** Recommended framing: a flourish that names the four legal defects together as a single proposition, bitter accuracy theatricalized rather than new emotional content.

3. **Mr. Pig's witness line — the canonical sincere line.** *"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."* Phase-4 protects the line. Phase-6 explicitly de-emphasizes its load-bearing weight in Round 5. Staged as character moment, not basis.
   - Pig is called to testify briefly. Perhaps thirty seconds.
   - He delivers the line.
   - **The judge's response is dry acknowledgment, not validation.** Recommended: *"Counsel will note the testimony of Mr. Pig. The court has heard it."*
   - Maritime register absent.

4. **Mr. Swine's witness line — second sincere line of chapter 6.** *"I was not avoiding responsibility. I was avoiding accounting, which is similar but less noble."* Same de-emphasis as Pig's: testimony is brief; the judge's response is dry acknowledgment. Per Swine bible's rationed-sincerity rule.

5. **The judge announces the remedy** after both witness lines. **The remedy is grounded explicitly in the legal defects from Rounds 1-4.** The judge cites: defective notice (Round 1), lack of individual assessment (Round 2), technical suppression (Round 3), conflict of interest (Round 4) as cumulative grounds. The judge orders the scheme set aside or amended accordingly. **The firm's social utility is not cited.**
