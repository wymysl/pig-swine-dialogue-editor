## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern.
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would.
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."

**Stylistic disciplines:**
- Concrete nouns over abstractions. Use English-language legal specifics over generic abstractions (see Language convention below).
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite.

## §A.5 — Language convention (English version)

**The English version reads smoothly for an English-speaking player with no Polish-language training.** The bibles and beat sheets use Polish-legal vocabulary internally for precision; **dialogue does not**.

**Acceptable in English dialogue:**
- Proper names of people: Wilkanowicz, Plotek, Kowalski, etc.
- Warsaw place names where flagship: the Vistula, Marszałkowska, Saska Kępa, the Old Town.
- *Solidarność* (only in the deliberate STUB callback context).
- PLN currency.
- Zero-to-one Polish flavor word per scene as deliberate texture, glossed inline first time: *"the wokanda — the day's cause list"*. **Do not exceed one per scene.**

**Translate to English** (do NOT borrow into dialogue):
- *sąd rejonowy* → "district court"
- *pełnomocnictwo* → "power of attorney" (or "POA" in informal lawyer talk)
- *wokanda* / *wokandy* → "docket" / "cause list" / "case list"
- *aplikatura* → "the apprenticeship" / "the training"
- *aplikant* / *aplikanci* → "apprentice" / "apprentices"
- *patron* → "master" (apprentice-sense)
- *kancelaria* → "the firm" / "the practice"
- *areszt śledczy* → "pre-trial detention" / "detention facility"
- *postanowienie* → "order" / "ruling"
- *akta sprawy* → "case file"
- KPC / KK / KKS → "the Code of Civil Procedure" / "the Penal Code" / "the Fiscal Penal Code" — or "the Code" when clear.

**Master/apprentice register: use "master" and "apprentice" in English.** "Master Wilkanowicz was strict about witness summons" reads fine; "*Patron* Wilkanowicz" doesn't.

**Test:** if a non-Polish English-speaking reader needs a dictionary, translate the term.

**Critical for this pack (lectures):** Mr. Pig's lectures are about his apprenticeship. The temptation to use *aplikatura*, *aplikants*, *patron*, *wokanda*, etc., is high because these are the actual Polish terms. **Use English equivalents in dialogue.** "When I was an apprentice..." / "Master Wilkanowicz never ran a checklist..." / "we covered each other's case lists..." / "the apprenticeship is supposed to forge you..." All of these read as Pig being Pig in formal-legal English with the Wilkanowicz name as a Polish anchor; none require a reader to know Polish.

## §B — Per-character constraints

### Mr. Pig (the lecturer)

**Voice essence:** formal-while-falling-apart. Sincere panic. Maritime metaphors under stress. **The "back in my day" lectures are sincere as memory and inaccurate as comparison.** Pig believes them. He does not see that his "begging for opportunity" was within a family network that promised eventual payoff.

**Canonical samples:**
- *"The firm is not bankrupt. Bankruptcy has paperwork. We are currently in the pre-paperwork screaming phase."*
- *"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."* (Ch 5 sincere line — note: this line is in Round 5, not Beat 14; do not confuse.)

**Syntax disciplines:**
- Sentences start composed and escalate.
- Maritime metaphor density ~1 in 4 lines, denser in panic, **absent in calm/sincere moments**.
- Stock openers: "We are currently in...", "It would appear that...", "If anything, this confirms..."
- Frequent rhetorical questions answered by himself.
- **Formal-legal English register** with Polish proper-name anchors (Wilkanowicz, etc.). The bibles' "formal Polish legal register" rule translates to "formal-legal English register, with proper-name Polish anchors used sparingly" for the English version. See §A.5 above.

**Will not say:**
- Anything genuinely hopeful that is not immediately undermined by the next sentence (until Ch 5 sincere-line moments).
- Anything that diagnoses the firm's structure as unjust to the juniors.
- Anything that admits his austerity is voluntary while theirs isn't.
- Anything that frames himself as having missed something significant.
- Anything cynical about the firm's mission.
- Anything venal about clients.
- Any reference to his own family wealth or background. **He doesn't tell that story; the player discovers it via the family photo.**
- Any direct ask about how the juniors are doing materially.
- Generic "uplifting" lecture endings ("and that is what made us strong" type).
- "Heart of" / "essence of" abstractions about the firm's purpose.
- Self-aware lines that diagnose his own situation.

**AI tells most vulnerable to (specific to lectures):**
- **Generic "back in my day" cliché register.** AI loves this trope and will produce phrases like "When I was your age..." with no Polish-legal specificity. **Each lecture must cite specific Polish aplikatura material** — actual conditions, actual masters, actual procedural memories. Use specifics: paper-stock rationing, the master's name in passing, the specific kancelaria room he was made to clean, the Polish-bar exam date he was preparing for. The specificity is what makes the lecture sincere; the cliché register is what marks it as AI.
- **Wisdom-monologuing.** AI will give Pig long, structured lectures with a thesis and a moral. **Pig's lectures are short — three sentences maximum.** They land sideways, embedded in his other panic or his other dialogue. They are not standalone monologues.
- **Generic uplifting endings.** "And that is what built me." "And it made me who I am." "And so I learned." **Cut categorically.** Pig's lectures end mid-thought, on a fragment, on a deflection, or with a return to the surrounding panic — not on a moral.
- **Maritime metaphor overshoot inside the lectures.** The lecture register can BLEED into maritime metaphor (e.g., "when I was an aplikant, we navigated by panic alone") but should not be saturated with maritime imagery. The maritime is the panic-around-the-lecture, not the lecture itself.
- **Self-aware references to "back in my day" being a trope.** Pig is not aware that he's giving a lecture; he's just talking. AI sometimes makes characters meta-reference their own clichés. **Pig doesn't know.**
- **Comparison made explicit.** Pig's lectures are sincere as memory; the comparison to the juniors' conditions is implicit. AI will sometimes write Pig saying "you have it easier than I did" — **cut.** Pig doesn't articulate the comparison; the lecture is the comparison, by being a lecture in the first place.

**Quick-reference:** sincere idealist with a class blind spot. Lectures the juniors comparing his "begging for opportunity" to their conditions, sincere as memory and inaccurate as comparison. Three sentences maximum. Embedded in surrounding scene activity (readiness checks, panic, retraversal), not standalone. The juniors receive without comment. **The chapter-5 family-photo discovery recolors all four lectures retrospectively.** The chapter-6 absence is the recoloring's payoff.

### Junior reactions (Cula, Crab, Whimsy)

**The juniors' reactions to Pig's lectures are deliberately silent or minimal.** Phase 4 commits: Cula receives the lecture without comment (per his restraint discipline). Crab files it without engagement (opportunist; treats it as data about the boss). Whimsy may have an internal flourish but does not say it out loud during the lecture — possibly a one-line deflective comment afterward in his cynic register.

**Critical writing rule:** the juniors do NOT visibly resent the lectures, do NOT articulate the comparison, do NOT call attention to the lecture as a lecture. **Silence is the writing-discipline.** AI will want to give the juniors reaction shots that diagnose the lecture's tone-deafness; this breaks the spine's commitment that the diagnosis is for the player, not for any character.

**Will not say (juniors during lectures):**
- "I don't think it was that hard for you."
- "We have it harder, actually."
- "Things have changed since then."
- Any line that treats the lecture as comparison-content rather than as Pig being Pig.

**AI tells most vulnerable to:**
- Reaction shots in eye-rolls or sighs that the player reads as the writing pointing at the lecture's failure mode. The lectures are written with the failure mode invisible to the characters.
- Whispered junior asides making fun of Pig.
- Cula visibly registering the comparison.

## §E — Bible excerpt: Mr. Pig — preamble + per-chapter lecture notes

**(From `narrative_revision/bibles/pig.md`.)**

### Preamble — the contradiction he carries

Mr. Pig is not a hypocrite. He genuinely believes the firm matters; he genuinely refuses lucrative corporate retainers; he keeps his own salary low; he cries when wrong about the printer. His commitment is real and the player must feel it.

His blind spot is structural and biographical. His austerity is voluntary while the juniors' is structural — he can stop being austere any time, they can't.

Underneath this is a deeper biographical blind spot: **Mr. Pig comes from a wealthy, multi-generational family of Warsaw advocates.** His father was an established advocate; the family has held a kancelaria for at least three generations; Pig went to expensive private schools that the family paid for as a matter of course; his aplikatura was in his uncle's firm under his uncle's tutelage. Pig founded Pig & Swine partly as a rebellion — he wanted rights work, not corporate work, and the family kancelaria did corporate work — but the family safety net was always there. He has never been one bad month away from anything. He does not know what that fear feels like.

He has nonetheless built a self-narrative in which he overcame hardship to found Pig & Swine. He talks about "begging for the opportunity to learn." He claims he worked 18 hours a day. He says the wage was a sandwich and the chance to be in the room. **These claims are not lies in his head.** The hours were real; the tutelage was real; the willingness to refuse easier paths was real. What he cannot see is that all of it took place inside a family network that promised eventual payoff. His "begging" was within a system that always meant to grant him the favor. His "sandwich" was eaten in a kancelaria where his future was guaranteed.

### Voice rules — the "back in my day" lectures

The lectures are a recurring Pig speech pattern — at least one per chapter, more in chapters where the juniors are visibly tired or the firm is under pressure. The lectures vary around themes:

- "I worked eighteen hours a day for two years before I was given my first independent matter."
- "I had to beg my masters for the opportunity to learn. Beg. With my dignity in a paper cup."
- "When I was your age, the wage was a sandwich and the chance to be in the room."
- "We did not have unions. We had each other and the work."
- "The aplikatura is supposed to be hard. It is supposed to forge you. It forged me. Look at me — I am forged."

Phase 7 voice work writes specific lines per chapter using the canonical template variations. **The lectures must read sincere on first pass.** Pig believes them. The recoloring lands across chapters as the player accumulates evidence that Pig's "begging" was within a family system.

### Per-chapter lecture context

**Chapter 1 (Beat 8 readiness check):** First lecture; plants the pattern. Pig delivers it as encouragement-disguised-as-comparison to the new aplikant Cula. The lecture is short, sincere, lands as Pig being Pig. Cula receives it without comment.

**Chapter 2 (around Beat 10 office return):** Second lecture; pattern is established. Pig delivers it when the juniors return tired from the eviction investigation, framed as a pep talk that accidentally compares his hours to theirs.

**Chapter 4 (Beat 11 urgent filing prep):** Third lecture; embedded in his maritime-crisis register about toner costs. The lecture lands ironically given that Cula has just seen the ledger; the player feels the irony, Pig does not.

**Chapter 5 (Beat 0 retraversal):** Fourth lecture; the chapter-5 family-photo discovery is the focal recoloring moment. After the player encounters the photo, all subsequent Pig lectures recolor — Pig has not changed; the player has more information.

**Chapter 6 (Beat 14 absence):** The lecture pattern's absence from the final speech is the recoloring's payoff. Pig tries to speak directly without the lecture frame and gets a half-line out before Swine interrupts.

## §G — Cross-chapter coherence rules

**The five lectures must work as a set.** Phase 7 voice work must hold:

1. **No two lectures use the same theme.** Each chapter picks a different variation from the bible's theme list.
2. **No lecture is longer than three sentences.** Brevity is the discipline.
3. **Each lecture must read sincere on first pass.** Pig believes them. The recoloring is the player's, retrospectively.
4. **The chapter-4 lecture has the highest maritime-metaphor density** (because Pig is in peak panic). The chapter-1 and chapter-2 lectures have moderate density. The chapter-5 lecture has lower density (Pig has been somewhat subdued by the chapter-4-end printer apology and the new chapter-5 Swine-bill arrival). The chapter-6 lecture absence is the discipline's culmination.
5. **The chapter-6 absence must read as Pig trying to speak plain and failing.** Not as Pig successfully transcending his pattern. The half-line is sincere and unfinished; the lecture pattern's gravitational pull is felt by its absence.
6. **The juniors' silence holds across all five.** Cula receives without comment; Crab files; Whimsy may have one cynic-flourish aside in one of the four chapters but no more.
7. **No lecture references Pig's family directly.** No "my father said..." or "in my family's firm..." references. The family is hidden from his self-narrative; the player discovers it via the photo, not via Pig's words.
8. **The "framing-around-firm" pattern is intact across all five.** Pig's lectures and his Beat-14 half-line all frame the work as the firm's work, not the clients' welfare. The pattern carries through to the final half-line: "the firm is — the firm is —"
