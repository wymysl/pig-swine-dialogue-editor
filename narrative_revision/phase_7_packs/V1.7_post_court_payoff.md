## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut. AI overuses em-dashes for rhythm. **Pig's celebration especially vulnerable** — the H4 canonical sample has two em-dashes flanking the *"yes, the client"* afterthought beat; that exact construction is the canonical pattern's signature and is permitted within Pig's celebration paragraph; outside that one slot, the ≤1-per-paragraph rule applies. The retainer aside is allowed one em-dash within the canonical sample shape (*"is still — somewhere"*). **Whimsy's musical-proposal especially vulnerable** (the theatrical register tempts em-dash bursts). **Murrow's post-update line vulnerable** (AI will pad his single sentence with em-dash interruptions; cut). **Asia vulnerable** (AI will give her em-dash-spliced cheerful asides; cut to one or zero).
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** **Pig's celebration vulnerable** (the temptation to say "the firm did not win — the firm survived" — categorically cut; the H4 pattern is firm-first ordering, not contrastive antithesis). **Whimsy's musical proposal vulnerable** (the temptation to say "not a courtroom — a stage"). **Crab's professional-indifference vulnerable** (the temptation to say "not impressive — expected"; cut to flat declarative).
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection." **"Heart of" especially vulnerable in Pig's celebration** ("the heart of the firm" — categorically cut; he doesn't talk about the firm that way). **"Essence of" vulnerable in Whimsy's musical proposal** ("the essence of justice in three acts" — categorically cut).
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions. Use only if a character would. **Whimsy may deploy archaic register** ("Verily," "Behold," "I propose") in the musical proposal but should not pile transitions; the proposal is a one-beat flourish, not an aria.
