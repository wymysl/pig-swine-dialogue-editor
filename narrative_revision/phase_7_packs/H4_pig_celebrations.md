## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern.
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would.
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."

**Stylistic disciplines:**
- Concrete nouns over abstractions. Use English-language legal specifics over generic abstractions (see Language convention below).
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite.

## §B — Per-character constraints

### Mr. Pig (the only speaking character authored in this pack)

**Voice essence:** formal-while-falling-apart. Sincere relief at celebrations. Maritime metaphors at moderate density (chapter-end celebrations are not peak-panic moments; the maritime register is present but not saturated).

**Canonical samples:**
- *"The firm is not bankrupt. Bankruptcy has paperwork. We are currently in the pre-paperwork screaming phase."*
- *"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."* (Ch 5 Round 5 sincere line — note: this is in Round 5, NOT in Beat 14. Do not confuse the chapter-6 sincere line with the Beat 14 half-line; they are different beats.)

**Syntax disciplines:**
- Sentences start composed and escalate.
- Maritime metaphor density at chapter-end celebrations: moderate. The relief is real; the panic has temporarily receded; the metaphors are present but spread thin.
- Stock openers: "We are currently in...", "It would appear that...", "If anything, this confirms..."
- Frequent rhetorical questions answered by himself.
- **Formal-legal English register** with Polish proper-name anchors (Borowski, etc.) used sparingly.

**Will not say:**
- Anything that diagnoses the firm's structure as unjust to the juniors.
- Anything that admits his austerity is voluntary while theirs isn't.
- Anything that frames himself as having missed something significant about the firm.
- Anything cynical about the firm's mission.
- Anything venal about clients.
- Any reference to his own family wealth or background.
- Any direct ask about how the juniors are doing materially.
- Generic "uplifting" celebration endings.
- "Heart of" / "essence of" abstractions about the firm's purpose.

**AI tells most vulnerable to (specific to celebrations):**
- **Mentioning the client first.** This is the single Critical-tier failure mode for this pack. AI will write Pig saying "Waldek is safe" or "the client got their hearing" first, then mention the firm. **Reverse the order.** Pig says the firm survived first; the client comes second or third. The ordering is the pattern; the ordering is what recolors at chapter 6.
- **Generic uplifting endings.** "And that is what we do." "And so we continue." **Cut categorically.** Pig's celebrations end on a return-to-anxiety beat (the next month's bills, the next case, the next thing he's worried about) — not on a triumphal coda.
- **Symmetric paired phrases.** AI loves "the firm survived AND the client survived." The pattern wants a hierarchy: firm first, then client mentioned secondarily — possibly even with a "the client — yes, the client — also survived" register that suggests Pig is remembering the client as an afterthought.
- **Maritime metaphor saturation.** Chapter-end celebrations are not peak-panic; metaphor density should be moderate. If the celebration line is wall-to-wall maritime, dial back.
- **Rule-of-three lists in celebration mode.** "We won the case, we kept the firm, we live to fight another day." AI loves this rhythm. Pig's celebrations should snag — fragments, asides, returns to specific worries — not roll out in symmetric trios.

**Quick-reference:** Pig celebrates the firm's continued survival across all chapters with a celebration. The firm-first / client-second pattern is the spine commitment. Cumulative across chapters; lands hardest in chapter 5 Beat 16 (because chapter 6 retraversal recolors it); carries through to chapter 6 Beat 14 half-line in plain register.

## §C — The pattern: firm-first / client-second ordering

**The pattern's structure:**

1. Pig opens with relief framed around the firm's survival.
2. The client's win (Waldek / Szpon procedural reprieve / arbitration / consolidation-scheme set-aside) is mentioned secondarily — possibly with a "the client — yes, the client — also survived" rhythm that signals Pig is remembering the client as a secondary thought.
3. Pig pivots back to the firm's next worry (next month's bills / next case / new pressure) — preventing the celebration from reading as triumphal.

**What this pattern is NOT:**
- An ordering test the player should consciously notice on first read. The pattern is invisible-but-present in chapters 1-3 and becomes legible to the player only on chapter-5 retraversal (per the spine's funny-in-hindsight commitment).
- A diagnostic line that Pig delivers about the firm's structure. Pig does NOT say "the firm comes first." He just frames it that way without articulating the framing.
- A cathartic moment. The celebration is brief; Pig pivots back to anxiety; the player feels the pattern accumulating but is not asked to interpret it.

**What this pattern IS:**
- A linguistic ordering tic that recolors when the player learns about the operating-account reserve (chapter-4 inflection) and the family-photo background (chapter-5 Beat 0 retraversal).
- A character signature that lands as Pig being Pig. The firm-survival framing is sincere from his perspective; the player's reading shifts as evidence accumulates.

**Cross-chapter coherence:** the celebration pattern reads as a habit by chapter 5. By chapter 6 the half-line "the firm — the firm —" carries through one final time, then breaks (Pig is interrupted; the framing doesn't get completed; the pattern's gravitational pull is felt by its absence-of-completion).

## §G — Cross-chapter coherence rules

**The four celebrations (chapters 1, 2, 4) plus the chapter-6 half-line must work as a set.** Phase 7 voice work must hold:

1. **Firm-first / client-second ordering across all four expressions.** No exceptions. The pattern is the spine commitment.

Chapter 1's "we live to fight another month" / Chapter 2's "the firm walks another month" / Chapter 5's specific phrase / Chapter 6's "the firm — the firm —" are recognizably-related but not identical.
