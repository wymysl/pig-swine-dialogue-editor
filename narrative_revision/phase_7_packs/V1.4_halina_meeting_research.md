## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut. AI overuses em-dashes for rhythm. **Halina especially vulnerable** (the formal register tempts em-dash punctuation).
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern. **Whimsy especially vulnerable**; **Halina also vulnerable** (the teacher diction tempts contrastive pairs).
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions. Use only if a character would. **Halina may use "indeed" sparingly** in lightly-archival register; **Whimsy may deploy archaic register** ("verily," "behold," "I propose") but should not pile on transitions.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would. **Whimsy may use rule-of-three for theatrical effect**, sparingly.
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."
- **Halina-specific tells:** AI will reach for "It has been a difficult time" / "I have lost everything" / "I don't know what to do" — categorically cut. Halina knows what she is doing; she is asking a lawyer for help with a procedural defect. She is not in crisis-narrative register.
- **Kacper-precaution (forward-looking):** if drafting any forward reference to Kacper in this pack — there shouldn't be — Kacper's voice rules apply (no plea, no aggression, no sentimentality).

**Stylistic disciplines:**

- Concrete nouns over abstractions. English-equivalent legal specifics over generic abstractions. See §A.5.
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical. Halina's prose has measured rhythm with brief snags (a teacher's pause where she chooses precision over flow); Cula's prose is observational with short sentences when stressed; Crab's two-sentence rhythm; Whimsy's flourishes wrap legal content; Murrow's narration is short declaratives.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite.
- One canonical sincere line per character per chapter. **Sincerity is rationed.** **Chapter 1 has no fully sincere line for any speaker.** This includes Halina (she does not have a sincere break in this scene — her dignity is the discipline; the cardiologist plant is *not* a sincere break, it lands neutrally); Pig (panic register throughout chapter 1; sincere moments accrue toward chapter 4 printer apology / chapter 6 Round 5); Whimsy (cynic stance is the position); Cula (observational restraint); Crab (opportunist register); Murrow (archival neutrality). If a V1.4 draft includes a "sincere" beat for any speaker, it's over-budget — re-shape it as register-appropriate.

## §A.6 — Scene social context (per ai_voice_constraints §1.6)

V1.4 covers Beats 8 and 9 of chapter 1. Two distinct social contexts; each imposes its own context-awareness requirements.

**Beat 8 — Pig & Swine office meeting room (formal client interview).**

The meeting room is a separate interior sub-area within the office, with a big table, chairs arranged formally, the door open to the open office (so Pig's intrusion is plausible but unwelcome). Cula, Crab, Whimsy enter the meeting room as a team when Asia announces Halina. Murrow is already at the table. Halina is seated opposite Cula's seat, with her folder of documents in front of her. The room registers visually as professional and contained — the chaotic open office is NOT visible from this scene's framing.

- **Context-awareness for Cula:** he is leading a client meeting on his second day at the firm. He carries a stance carrier (sympathetic / blunt-procedural / technical) chosen by the player — not by him narratively. He is professionally calm. He is not nervous about the client; he is calibrated about the case. He uses Halina's surname-form throughout (Mrs. Sikorska / Mrs. Sikorska — the bible recommends Mrs. Sikorska through Ch1).
- **Context-awareness for Halina:** she has just entered a formal meeting where four young lawyers are seated waiting for her. She greets the room formally, sits when invited, places her folder on the table, and waits for Cula to begin. She is not impressed by their youth; she is not impressed by their formality; she is here to discuss a procedural defect that affects her home. The dignity is the discipline. Across the meeting her assessment of the team upgrades modestly: these lawyers are competent; this is going to be efficient. The upgrade does not register as relief.
- **Context-awareness for Crab:** he is in a client meeting, professionally indifferent to client morale but professionally engaged with case features. He sits at the table; he registers Halina's documents with brief professional interest; he speaks when the case's structural features call for his angle (notice/service analysis). He does not engage Halina socially. He addresses Cula or the table when speaking.
- **Context-awareness for Whimsy:** he is in a client meeting, performing detachment from earnest urgency but in close enough proximity to a sympathetic person that the protective-distancing register applies. The flourish addresses the case, not the person. Halina herself receives a brief courteous acknowledgment in his lower register. **Critical: do not let his cynic-register punch down at her age, her situation, or her hopes.** If V1.4 produces a Whimsy line that does, the pack has failed.
- **Context-awareness for Murrow:** he is the supervising senior at a junior-led client meeting. He intervenes only when needed (a stance line goes somewhere unproductive; a client question requires a senior-bar nuance). His default mode is silent-and-watching. Sample intervention shape: *"The notice."* (Returns the conversation to the document.) Phase-7 specifies.
- **Context-awareness for Mr. Pig (brief intrusion):** he is in panic register, has wandered into the meeting room because something has gone wrong elsewhere in the office. **He does not directly engage Halina.** His lines are addressed to Murrow or to nobody in particular. He is in the room briefly (two lines max), then Murrow shepherds him out gently — a brief courteous line from Murrow ("Mr. Pig, give us a moment") suffices. Halina watches without comment.
- **Context-awareness for Asia (announce-only):** she is at her reception desk; she steps to the meeting-room doorway to announce Halina's arrival. One brief line. She does not enter the meeting room. She does not speak again in this beat.

**Beat 9 — Pig & Swine office Archive Room (Cula + Murrow only).**

The Archive Room is a contained interior — file cabinets, shelves of binders, dust silence, archive shelf creaks. Cula and Murrow have walked here from the meeting room (Halina has left). Murrow opens the Procedural Binder to the Article 135-bis section. The scene's pace is procedural and observational; Halina's emotional weight from Beat 8 has receded; the work is now applying the rule to the facts.

- **Context-awareness for Cula:** he has just finished his first formal client meeting; he is processing what he heard but does not narrate the processing. He is here to learn the legal hook from Murrow. He asks precise questions; he listens; he can use brief character-dwell options to surface archival depth without breaking Murrow's archive-rule. The withholding-of-articulation discipline applies — he does not editorialize about Halina.
- **Context-awareness for Murrow:** he is in his element. The Archive Room is his domain. His narration is precise, clause-by-clause, applied. He is faintly amused by Cula's competence (this junior is going to do well) but does not editorialize that either. He answers precise questions precisely; vague questions get nothing (archive rule).

**The two-test discipline (from ai_voice_constraints §1.6):** each line passes both (1) the will-not-say list AND (2) social-context awareness. A line that passes (1) but fails (2) is functional but flat — fix it with one beat of acknowledgment in the character's existing register, not by adding new content.

### B.1 — Halina Sikorska (NEW; first appearance in V1.4)

**Source:** `narrative_revision/bibles/halina_sikorska.md` (full bible).

**Stance:** wronged client; dignified; tired-but-not-broken.

Does not dramatize. Does not plead. May make literary references in passing.

**Interior register range (four facets):**

- **Formal-precise** (default). Self-introduction, document presentation, logistical statements, fee discussion. Sample shape: *"Good morning. I am Halina Sikorska. I have been told you can help."* Sample shape: *"The notice was dated the eighth of April. I did not see it until the twenty-eighth."*
- **Quietly-warm.** Used when speaking about her past — father, late husband, son in Wrocław, her career. Not nostalgic; matter-of-fact about what mattered. - Anything in modern register (no internet voice, no clipped slang, no contemporary idioms).
- Anything that mocks or condescends to the lawyers.
- Anything that overclaims her own legal understanding.
- Anything that begs or pleads.
- Anything melodramatic about her health, situation, family, or apartment.

**Physical presentation in scene:** modest, considered, unfashionable in a dignified way. Carries a folder of documents. Posture upright, slight age-stiffness. Does not gesture much. Eye contact direct but not intense. Voice clear, measured.
