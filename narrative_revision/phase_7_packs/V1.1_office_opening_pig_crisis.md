## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut. AI overuses em-dashes for rhythm.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern.
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions. Use only if a character would.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis ("the rent, the toner, the deadline") unless a specific character would. **Pig's Beat 2 specifically wants ONE item, not three.**
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."

**Stylistic disciplines:**

- Concrete nouns over abstractions. English-equivalent legal specifics ("the rent," "the lease," "the retainer") over generic abstractions ("the financial situation," "our liquidity"). See §A.5.
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical. Pig's panic specifically wants *interrupted* sentences — fragments, asides, returns to the same worry.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite.
- One canonical sincere line per character per chapter. **Sincerity is rationed.** For chapter 1 specifically: Pig probably has no fully sincere line (he is in panic mode throughout the chapter); Asia's warmth is *not* a sincere-line ration spend (warmth is her register, not a sincere event); Cula has no sincere line (he is observational/restrained).

## §A.5 — Language convention (English version)

**The English version reads smoothly for an English-speaking player with no Polish-language training.** The bibles and beat sheets use Polish-legal vocabulary internally for precision; **dialogue does not**.

**Acceptable in English dialogue:**

- Proper names of people: Borowski, Plotek, Kowalski, Zielinska, etc.
- Warsaw place names where flagship: the Vistula, Marszałkowska, Saska Kępa, the Old Town.
- PLN currency.
- Zero-to-one Polish flavor word per scene as deliberate texture, glossed inline first time. **Do not exceed one per scene.**

**Translate to English** (do NOT borrow into dialogue):

- *sąd rejonowy* → "district court"
- *pełnomocnictwo* → "power of attorney"
- *wokanda* → "docket" / "cause list"
- *aplikatura* → "the apprenticeship"
- *aplikant* → "apprentice"
- *patron* → "master" (apprentice-sense)
- *kancelaria* → "the firm" / "the practice"
- KPC / KK / KKS → "the Code of Civil Procedure" / "the Penal Code" / "the Fiscal Penal Code"

**For this pack specifically:** the dialogue is light on legal vocabulary (Beat 2 is panic, not procedural). The temptation to borrow Polish is low; the discipline still applies. Asia's hint register may use *the blue file* / *the cause list* / *the binder* — translate any Polish working-vocabulary words she would otherwise use.

**Test:** if a non-Polish English-speaking reader would need a dictionary, the term should be translated.

## §B — Per-character constraints (only the speaking characters in V1.1)

### Mr. Pig (Beat 2 lead)

**Voice essence:** formal-while-falling-apart. Sincere panic. Maritime metaphors under stress.

**Canonical samples:**

- *"The firm is not bankrupt. Bankruptcy has paperwork. We are currently in the pre-paperwork screaming phase."*
- *"We are not efficient. But people come here when efficient systems have already failed them."* (Ch 5 sincere line — NOT for use in chapter 1.)

**Syntax disciplines:**

- Sentences start composed and escalate. Beat 2 is the chapter's panic peak — escalation should run higher than chapters 2–4.
- Maritime metaphor density: **HIGH in Beat 2**, denser than anywhere else in chapter 1. Pig is at peak panic. Sample shape (phase-4 recommended): *"The rent is taking on water. We are six weeks under the surface."*
- Stock openers: *"We are currently in..."*, *"It would appear that..."*, *"If anything, this confirms..."*
- Frequent rhetorical questions answered by himself.
- Dramatic re-framing of bad news as good news *attempted but failing* — Beat 2 is where the re-framing breaks down because the panic exceeds the optimism reflex.

**Will not say (chapter 1):**

- Anything genuinely hopeful that is not immediately undermined by the next sentence.
- Anything that diagnoses the firm's structure as unjust to the juniors.
- Anything that admits his austerity is voluntary while theirs isn't.
- Anything that frames himself as having missed something significant about the firm.
- Anything cynical about the firm's mission.
- Anything venal about clients.
- Any reference to his own family wealth or background. **Especially load-bearing in chapter 1**, where the family photo / diploma / casebook are visible as ordinary office decoration.
- Any direct ask about how the juniors are doing materially.
- The case explanation. **He must not explain the case properly.** His panic precludes it. Cula must go to Murrow.

**AI tells most vulnerable to (specific to Beat 2):**

- **Citing all three financial claims.** AI will write "the rent, the printer lease, the missing retainer" because rule-of-three rhythm is hardwired. **Cut categorically.** Beat 2 is one item: the rent. The printer lease arrives in V1.5 (Beat 10). The retainer arrives in V1.7 (Beat 13). Phase-6 Hit #2 distribution is locked.
- **Pivoting into a "back in my day" lecture mid-panic.** AI will reach for the "when I was an apprentice…" register because it sounds like character. **Cut categorically in this beat.** The first lecture moves to V1.5.
- **Explaining the case.** AI will give Pig a clarifying paragraph about what the client is being sued for. **Cut.** Pig is too panicked. Cula seeks Murrow.
- **Generic uplifting closer.** AI will end Pig's panic with a "but we will prevail" beat. **Cut.** Beat 2 ends with Pig deferring to Cula and pointing him toward Murrow, not with reassurance.
- **Maritime metaphor saturation.** Beat 2 wants high density, but not wall-to-wall. The metaphors snag against specific objects (the rent, the firm, the desk) — they do not coat every line.

**Quick-reference:** sincere idealist with a class blind spot. The crisis-talk in Beat 2 is *sincere* — there is no inner cynic. He really does experience the firm as on the edge of collapse. The recoloring lands in chapter 4 (when Cula glimpses the ledger) and chapter 5 (when the family photo becomes legible). Beat 2 is the plant.
