## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. If more than one per paragraph, cut. AI overuses em-dashes for rhythm.
- Contrastive antithesis: "not X but Y" / "it's not just X — it's Y." **Categorically cut.** Project owner's preference forbids this pattern.
- "Delve," "tapestry," "myriad," "vibrant," "navigate" (as metaphor), "showcase," "essence of," "heart of," "rich" (as adjective), "deeply," "robust," "intricate," "weave," "intersection."
- "Indeed," "moreover," "furthermore" — buttery transitions. Use only if a character would.
- "Let me be clear," "I won't pretend X," "to be honest" — hedge-preambles.
- "Not only X but Y" / "X, yet Y" symmetric paired phrases.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis unless a specific character would.
- Generic uplifting endings: "and that, in the end, is what mattered" / "and that is enough."
- "There is something to be said for X" / "one might argue."

**Stylistic disciplines:**

- Concrete nouns over abstractions. English-equivalent legal specifics ("the procedural binder," "Article 135-bis § 2 of the Code of Civil Procedure," "the case file") over generic abstractions. See §A.5.
- Sentences with snags. AI prose is too smooth and too symmetrical. Murrow's voice specifically wants short declaratives without snag-ornament; Crab's voice wants two-sentence rhythm; Cula's voice wants short observational lines.
- Read-aloud test. If a line reads aloud as a paragraph rather than as a person speaking, rewrite.
- One canonical sincere line per character per chapter. **Sincerity is rationed.** For chapter 1 specifically: Murrow has no sincere line in chapter 1 (his sincere moments are filed activities, not speech); Crab has no sincere line (the opportunist stance is the position); Cula has no sincere line.

## §A.6 — Scene social context (per ai_voice_constraints §1.6)

V1.2 covers Beats 3–5 of chapter 1: **the new-associate-meets-existing-colleagues scene on day one, against the backdrop of the Sikorska eviction defense the firm is preparing for a Friday motion hearing.** The client is not yet on-site; the firm has the case file, has identified the procedural target (motion to set aside an ex parte hearing under Article 135-bis § 2), and is bringing Cula up to speed. The social context is load-bearing — the will-not-say lists in §B govern *stance and commitment*, not basic acknowledgment that a new colleague has joined the firm. Each character's context-awareness for this scene:

- **Murrow:** a new colleague has arrived at his desk during the morning's panic. He has been at the firm for years; this is not his first new-hire greeting. **Context-awareness:** register Cula's arrival (recognition + brief welcome + observational beat about the day) before launching procedural framing. The acknowledgment is faintly amused via brevity, not warm-language. See `bibles/murrow.md` Social-register samples for the canonical shape (*"Doctor Cula. Welcome to Pig & Swine. You've picked a noisy day to begin."*).
- **Crab:** a new colleague has approached his work table during focused work. He is professionally indifferent to morale, not socially absent. **Context-awareness:** a glance, a two-word acknowledgment (*"Cula. Heard."*), a half-laptop-close stage direction — show Crab seeing Cula before the canonical refusal lands. The professional indifference is the register, not the manners. See `bibles/crab.md` Social-register samples.
- **Cula:** he is on his first day; he is meeting these colleagues for the first time. **Context-awareness:** he introduces himself professionally (name + concrete request), asks after files using passive forms that avoid gossiping about sources, reciprocates address-form invitations briefly without fanfare, closes brief exchanges with one-word acknowledgments. Restraint is the discipline; cardboard is the failure mode. See `bibles/cula.md` Social-register samples.

**The two-test discipline (from ai_voice_constraints §1.6):** each line passes both (1) the will-not-say list AND (2) social-context awareness. A line that passes (1) but fails (2) is functional but flat — fix it with one beat of acknowledgment in the character's existing register, not by adding new content. Adding tragic backstories or extra dimensions is scope creep; letting an existing voice register an existing context is execution depth.

**Anti-pattern check:** if the draft has a character launching directly into procedural cue / stance-coded refusal / observational fragment with zero acknowledgment that another character has just arrived in their physical space, the draft is in scrub-mode failure. Fix before §J critique.
