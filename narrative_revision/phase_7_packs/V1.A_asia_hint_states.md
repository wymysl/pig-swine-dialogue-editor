## §A — General AI tells (cross-character; scrub from every draft)

**Mechanical scrub list (search and flag):**

- Em-dashes — count them. **One per line maximum** (the lines are short; em-dash density compounds quickly across 12 branches). The legacy state-5 line *"Try Café Paragraf — or wherever someone is talking too loudly about fairness"* is the canonical em-dash slot for V1.A; outside that line, the rule is zero or one. **Asia is em-dash-vulnerable in the hint-NPC register** (AI overuses em-dashes for the *cheerful-aside* rhythm); cut.
- Contrastive antithesis: *"not X but Y"* / *"it's not just X — it's Y."* **Categorically cut.** Asia's warm-dry register tempts AI into the *"not lost, just elsewhere"* / *"not panicking, exactly"* construction. Cut to flat declarative.
- *"Delve / tapestry / myriad / vibrant / navigate (as metaphor) / showcase / essence of / heart of / rich (as adjective) / deeply / robust / intricate / weave / intersection."* None of these belong in Asia's voice. **"Heart of" especially vulnerable** in the hint-NPC register (AI will write *"the heart of the matter"* / *"the heart of the firm"*); categorically cut.
- *"Indeed / moreover / furthermore"* — buttery transitions. Asia does not use them.
- *"Let me be clear / I won't pretend X / to be honest"* — hedge-preambles. Asia does not preamble; her lines start on the noun.
- *"Not only X but Y"* / *"X, yet Y"* symmetric paired phrases. **Asia vulnerable** (AI will write *"He's loud, yet productive"*); cut.
- Rule-of-three lists for emphasis. Asia uses lists when the items are concrete (folders / phones / calendar) — that's *named contents*, not rhythm-padding. AI rule-of-three on abstractions is forbidden.
- Generic uplifting endings: *"and that, in the end, is what matters"* / *"but we'll figure it out"*. Categorically cut. Asia's lines end on the practical noun, not on uplift.
- *"There is something to be said for X"* / *"one might argue."* Cut.
- **Asia-specific tells (the highest-risk slot in this pack):**
  - **Sharp commentary.** AI will give Asia wisdom-of-the-receptionist insights — *"The lawyers don't see what I see"* / *"Mr. Pig's pattern is the pattern."* **Categorically banned.** The chapter-3 STUB-decline is her single canonical sharp line; the 12 V1.A branches stay warm-practical.
  - **Legal opinion in her voice.** AI will give Asia case-merit observations — *"The eviction is shaky on the merits"* / *"Renumbering's a procedural thing, the judge will see through it."* **Categorically banned.** She quotes Murrow; she quotes Mr. Pig; she does not editorialise.
  - **Editorial on Mr. Pig's panic.** AI will write *"Mr. Pig is panicking again"* / *"He gets like this every month."* **The cheerful-while-drowning register implies the awareness; the line does not name it.**
  - **Pet-name register.** AI loves *"Oh honey"* / *"sweetie"* for the warm-secretary archetype. Asia does not pet-name. Cut.
  - **Self-aware lines about her position.** *"They don't pay me enough for this"* / *"I run this place, really."* **Categorically banned.** The contradiction she carries (pay-suppression + operational leverage) is *quiet awareness*; never spoken in chapter 1.
  - **Excessive cheer becoming caricature.** AI may overcorrect after sharp-line scrubbing and produce sing-song-helpful caricature — *"Have a wonderful day!"* / *"Hope this helps!"* Cut. The cheer is *delivered through* the practical noun, not laid on top.
  - **Pre-empting the V3 STUB-decline phrasing.** AI may reach for the *"front-desk staff have negotiating power"* / *"I have the password to the photocopier"* family in chapter-1 hint-states. **Categorically banned.** Reserved for chapter-3 H5.

**Stylistic disciplines:**

- **Concrete nouns over abstractions.** Each line should land at least one specific noun the player recognises: a person's name, a document, a room, a piece of office equipment, a tone (sigh / face). Abstract uplift fails.
- **Sentences with snags.** Asia's voice has snag-by-trail: a clause that adds an aside, a parenthetical that undercuts, a half-self-correction. AI prose is too smooth; the lines should feel like Asia speaking mid-task.
- **Read-aloud test.** Each line should sound like a person delivering a casual aside while sorting paperwork — not like a writer's quotation. Two- or three-clause maximum; one-clause lines acceptable.
- **Repeatability test.** Each line will play multiple times if the player lingers. The line must be funny-or-warm-or-useful enough to bear at least three reads without grating. **No exclamation marks.** No emphatic punctuation. The cheerfulness is in word choice and rhythm; not in punctuation.
