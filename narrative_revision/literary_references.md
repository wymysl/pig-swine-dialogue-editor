# Pig & Swine RPG — Literary References

> Companion to `style_canon.txt`. Where style_canon names the canonical
> ear (the Pass / Reject examples), this file names the *outside ear* —
> the writers to read when a scene won't write itself, and the planted
> references already in canon or recommended for canon.
>
> Two parts:
>   1. **Reach-for list.** Writers and works to lean on during blocks,
>      with what each one specifically gives the project and which
>      character or scene type they unlock.
>   2. **Planted references.** Easter eggs, some already in story.txt,
>      some recommended. Designed to be invisible if missed.
>
> Style discipline: a reference is useful when it changes the *shape*
> of a sentence or the *posture* of a character in a scene. A reference
> is useless when it is named in marketing copy. This document is for
> the first kind.

---

## How to use this doc

When a scene won't write itself, ask:

- **What is the scene's social geometry?** (Junior speaking to senior;
  client speaking to lawyer; institution speaking to citizen.) Some
  writers below are masters of one geometry and useless for another.
- **What is the *posture* the line needs?** Laconic refusal? Performed
  procedure? Drunk-uncle eloquence? Petty-clerk humanity? Each posture
  has a writer who has already perfected it.
- **What is the line's relation to power?** Power is being mocked,
  endured, embodied, or worn as a costume. Different writers for each.

Then pick the entry below whose vocabulary matches the answer, read
five pages aloud, and write the scene afterwards. Do not transcribe.
The point is to recalibrate the ear, not to fetch a quote.

## Polish writers

### Marek Hłasko — first reach for laconic refusal

**Read:** *Pierwszy krok w chmurach* (the title story; "Krzyż"),
*Ósmy dzień tygodnia*, *Piękni dwudziestoletni* (the memoir — for
the working-Warsaw-bar register specifically).

**What he gives:** declarative sentences with no decoration. People who
do not explain themselves and do not soften. Dialogue that reveals
character through what is *withheld* rather than what is said. No
adverbs, almost no metaphor, almost no interiority — the surface is
the meaning.

**Reach for him when:**

- Murrow under stress (he stops decorating, becomes more procedural,
  not less).
- Crab angry (cold, not hot — Hłasko anger is flat).
- Bajtek when a printer monologue needs to *land* rather than charm.
  Hłasko's working-class men talk about machines the way Bajtek talks
  about the fax.
- Any scene where a character is refusing without explaining. The
  Hłasko move is to refuse, then say nothing else, then let the silence
  do the next two beats.

**Anti-pattern Hłasko corrects:** the impulse to soften refusals with
tag-on courtesies. Murrow's voice in particular should be *less* warm
under load, not more.

### Zofia Nałkowska — *Granica* — class-and-procedure friction

**What she gives:** the lawyer-as-witness-to-class. The downstairs
character who is seen because she is below — not seen *as* a person.
Bogutówna's predicament is the spine of every Cula↔Halina scene the
game stages.

**Reach for her when:**

- Cula meets Halina (Beat 8). The "lawyer like a doctor: too late"
  epigram is Nałkowska in her bones — wisdom from the side of the
  staircase that cannot fix anything from inside the staircase.
- Any scene where Cula registers (without commenting on) the gap
  between a client's life and the firm's office. Nałkowska's discipline
  is to let the gap be felt without having anyone monologue about it.
- The Plotek scenes (Ch2, Ch4): the way an institutional figure looks
  at someone *below* without quite admitting that's what is happening.

**Anti-pattern Nałkowska corrects:** the impulse to make class friction
explicit through a character speech. Granica is a 1935 novel that
almost never says the word "class."

### Sławomir Mrożek — bureaucracy as theatre of the absurd

**Read:** *Słoń* (short stories), *Tango*, *Policja*, *Wesele w
Atomicach*, *Donosy*.

**What he gives:** procedural absurdism delivered with a straight face.
Crab's "That is not service. That is postal theatre." is a Mrożek
sentence — it diagnoses the absurdity by naming the *form* of the
absurdity, not the content. Mrożek never raises his voice; the system
does the screaming.

**Reach for him when:**

- Bajtek's longer printer/STUB sequences. Mrożek's *Słoń* is the model
  for how to escalate a single bureaucratic premise into pure structure
  without ever leaving the deadpan.
- Any sequence inside the courtroom that needs to register as theatre
  (the case-as-performance, robes, ritual). Mrożek's *Policja* is the
  paragon: the institution that exists, performs itself, and produces
  the conditions of its own continuation.
- Crab when she diagnoses absurdity. The Mrożek move is to *name the
  costume* the absurdity is wearing.

**Anti-pattern Mrożek corrects:** the impulse to mug at the absurdity.
Mrożek does not mug. The deadpan is the comedy.

### Tadeusz Konwicki — *Mała apokalipsa* — wandering hostile Warsaw

**What he gives:** a protagonist navigating an indifferent and
sometimes aggressive institutional Warsaw, where every official
interaction is conducted with surface politeness and substantive
hostility. The narrator is told to set himself on fire for the Party.
He is offered tea first.

**Reach for him when:**

- Cula in any cross-institution sequence in Ch2 / Ch3 / Ch4 (the
  printer conspiracy, the registry, the courthouse corridors).
  Konwicki's gift is the texture of *being processed politely*.
- Plotek in Ch4 — Konwicki's apparatchiks are warm, eloquent, and
  utterly indifferent to the protagonist's continued existence. The
  Plotek biographical-mention beat is pure Konwicki.

**Anti-pattern Konwicki corrects:** the impulse to make institutional
hostility loud. Konwicki's institutions are courteous.

### Stanisław Lem — *Pamiętnik znaleziony w wannie* — paper paranoia

**What he gives:** the bureaucracy whose every document is a code,
whose every door is a test, and whose substance has dissolved into
pure procedure. The Building. Worth re-reading specifically because
nobody borrows from this Lem; everyone borrows from *Solaris*.

**Reach for him when:**

- Chapter 4's printer war / STUB texture. The deeper conspiracy beats
  where paper is the medium and the message both.
- Any moment where Cula starts wondering whether she is reading a
  document correctly *or whether the document is reading her*.

**Anti-pattern Lem corrects:** the impulse to make conspiracy plot-y.
*Pamiętnik* has no plot; it has procedure that keeps turning into
procedure.

### Witold Gombrowicz — *Ferdydurke*, *Trans-Atlantyk*

**What he gives:** Forma. The way social roles trap, inflate, and
deform the people performing them. The way a courtroom flourish is not
*about* the case — it is the lawyer being performed as Lawyer in front
of an audience that includes himself.

**Reach for him when:**

- Whimsy's flourishes (Beat 8 et seq.). The flourish is Forma. The
  comedy is that the flourish is real *and* a costume at the same time.
- Any beat where a character is performing *at* the scene rather than
  speaking *within* it. Cf. the voice-pack feedback memo on stance vs.
  social texture: when stance hardens into Forma, you have a
  Gombrowicz problem, and Gombrowicz is the diagnostician.

**Anti-pattern Gombrowicz corrects:** none directly — but he is the
literary frame for the failure mode the voice-pack execution memo
already names.

### Bolesław Prus — *Lalka* — the Polish *Bleak House*

**What he gives:** Warsaw class system rendered with patient
nineteenth-century affection, plus the Rzecki diary — administrative
voice that is also wholly human. Prus is the Polish answer to Dickens
and the closest Polish ancestor to the firm-survival energy P&S runs
on.

**Reach for him when:**

- Mr. Pig in expansive narratorial mode. Mr. Pig is, structurally, a
  Wokulski who chose the firm over the obsession.
- Any ledger or archive scene where the warmth is in the *care for the
  ledger itself*. Rzecki's diary is the model.
- Any moment where Warsaw needs to be a city Cula loves as well as
  fears.

### Jerzy Pilch — *Pod Mocnym Aniołem*, *Inne rozkosze*

**What he gives:** sentence rhythm of ironic gravitas. The drunk-uncle
eloquence — long sentences that take themselves seriously while
admitting they are also a joke about taking themselves seriously. Mr.
Pig's "pre-paperwork screaming phase" is in this register.

**Reach for him when:**

- Mr. Pig and Mr. Swine in expansive mode (firm-survival celebration
  scenes; Ch5 reflective beats).
- Any first-person voiceover-adjacent line where the sentence needs
  to land as both rueful and self-aware.

### Stefan Wiechecki "Wiech" — pre-war Warsaw vernacular

**What he gives:** Warsaw street voice with police-court texture
(Wiech wrote sketches set partly around the courthouse — pre-war
journalist's beat). Affectionate, vernacular, fast.

**Reach for him cautiously:**

- Asia idle lines if the project ever wants overt local color.
- Any street-Warsaw reference. But: Wiech dates fast. A little is
  flavor, much is parody.

### Andrzej Stasiuk — *Mury Hebronu*, *Opowieści galicyjskie*

**What he gives:** the post-PRL Hłasko — working-class men in cold
rooms, with documents and rumors and time. Useful as a tonal bridge
between Hłasko's 1950s and the game's late-PRL-but-fictional Warsaw.

**Reach for him when:** any Ch4 / Ch5 sequence where a witness or
ex-employee narrates from inside a damaged life and has to be heard
without sentimentality.

### Ryszard Kapuściński — *Cesarz*, *Imperium*

**What he gives:** institutional ethnography. The practice of
describing power by listing what its functionaries *do* rather than
what they say.

**Reach for him when:** any descriptive passage about the courthouse
as institution, the firm as institution, or STUB as institution.
Kapuściński is the model for "describe the institution by its
verbs."

### Iwaszkiewicz — already cited in canon

`story.txt` recommends Iwaszkiewicz-flavored shapes for Halina's
literary epigram. Read *Brzezina* and *Panny z Wilka* for the elegy
register: middle-class melancholy with patience. Halina's "lawyer
like a doctor: too late" is Iwaszkiewicz in cadence. Don't reach
beyond him for Halina; the epigram is already calibrated.

## Non-Polish writers

### Charles Dickens — *Bleak House*, *Little Dorrit*

**What he gives:** the case that consumes lives (Jarndyce and
Jarndyce); the Circumlocution Office (the paragon for procedural
absurdism in English); fee-anxiety and firm-survival rendered with
warmth. The P&S firm-survival celebration scenes inherit directly
from Dickens.

**Reach for him when:**

- Beat 13 ("we have survived another impossible thing"); Ch3 fee
  scenes; any moment Mr. Pig is reckoning with the firm as a creature
  that must be fed.
- Any time you need to show that an institution is comic *and* that
  real people are being damaged by it. Dickens never lets you forget
  the second part.

### Franz Kafka — *Der Process*, *Das Schloss*, *In der Strafkolonie*

**What he gives:** the spiritual frame of any procedural-paranoia
game. Already absorbed into the genre to the point of cliché.

**Reach for him when:** never as a direct quote. Always as a
posture-check. If a Plotek scene is too explicitly Kafkaesque, reach
*away* — toward Konwicki or le Carré, who are quieter.

### Nikolai Gogol — *Шинель*, *Мёртвые души*, *Ревизор*

**What he gives:** the petty clerk seen from the inside, with full
human dignity. *The Overcoat* is the universal solvent for sympathetic
bureaucracy. *Dead Souls* is registry humor at the level of premise.
*The Government Inspector* is the procedural-mistaken-identity comedy.

**Reach for him when:**

- Bajtek above all. Bajtek is in the *Overcoat* lineage. His
  attachment to office machinery is Akaky's attachment to the coat.
- Any line that needs to be both bureaucratic and tender. Murrow's
  "law is mostly memory" line is in Gogolian air.

### Mikhail Bulgakov — *Собачье сердце*, *Мастер и Маргарита*

**What he gives:** documents-as-magic; residence committees as cosmic
absurdity; comic flourish at maximum elevation. *Heart of a Dog* in
particular is the ancestor of any "the apartment-allocation is
deciding whether you are a person" beat.

**Reach for him when:** the printer-conspiracy / STUB beats need to
escalate from absurd to *cosmic-absurd*. Bulgakov gives permission
for the magic to be procedural and the procedural to be magic.

### Jorge Luis Borges — *Ficciones*, *El Aleph*

**What he gives:** *La biblioteca de Babel* and *La lotería en
Babilonia* are the canonical short forms of cosmic procedural
absurdism. *Funes el memorioso* is the canonical short form of
"memory as condemnation." *El jardín de senderos que se bifurcan* is
the procedural-stance-tree the Casebook system is structurally a
descendant of.

**Reach for him when:**

- Murrow's epigram register. "Law is mostly memory" is one Borges
  reach away from "memory is mostly law."
- Any case-file or archive sequence that needs to feel like a
  microcosm.

### Joseph Heller — *Catch-22*

**What he gives:** the form requires the form. Comic infinite regress
delivered in plain English. Heller is the patron saint of the joke
where escape from the procedure is itself a procedure.

**Reach for him when:** any Casebook tag-combat absurdity — especially
beats where the *legal* answer to a procedural trap is itself a
procedural trap. Mr. Pig's circular reasoning about the printer lease
is in Heller's tradition.

### John Mortimer — *Rumpole of the Bailey*

**What he gives:** the friendly barrister-comedy. Affection for the
courthouse and its denizens *as they are*, including the venal ones.
Whimsy is in the Rumpole lineage. The courtroom flourish that wins
the round and respects the case is a Mortimer move.

**Reach for him when:**

- Whimsy in Beat 8 and any subsequent flourish beat.
- Any time the firm-as-found-family register threatens to go
  saccharine. Mortimer keeps it dry.

### Anton Chekhov — short stories (*Смерть чиновника*, *Хамелеон*, *Ванька*, *Тоска*)

**What he gives:** humane bureaucratic comedy at the level of the
single petty interaction. The civil servant who dies of having sneezed
on a general (*Смерть чиновника*) is the Russian *Overcoat* in
miniature.

**Reach for him when:** any single-scene NPC encounter that needs
human weight in under three exchanges. Chekhov's discipline is to be
done in two pages.

### Jaroslav Hašek — *Osudy dobrého vojáka Švejka*

**What he gives:** passive-aggressive proceduralism as a survival
strategy. Švejk obeys orders so completely and so literally that the
system breaks against him. Mr. Swine's signature "I signed many
things. Most were menus." is Švejk in posture.

**Reach for him when:**

- Mr. Swine, especially in Ch5 reflective mode. Švejk's "Beg to
  report, sir" structure is the cadence Mr. Swine reaches for under
  pressure.
- Any beat where a character's apparent compliance *is* the resistance.

### Bohumil Hrabal — *Obsluhoval jsem anglického krále*, *Příliš hlučná samota*

**What he gives:** dignity of the overlooked. Document-as-archaeology
(the protagonist of *Příliš hlučná samota* compacts banned books for
a living and reads them as he goes). Long compound sentences that
keep their footing.

**Reach for him when:** any archive / ledger scene where the *care for
the document itself* is the emotional content. Murrow at the archive
in his quieter beats is Hrabal-shaped.

### John le Carré — *Tinker Tailor Soldier Spy*, *A Perfect Spy*

**What he gives:** institutional dialogue where what is *not* said
carries the weight. Smiley's interrogations are masterclasses in
extracting information through patient silence and specific small
factual challenges.

**Reach for him when:**

- Plotek scenes — especially the Ch4 biographical-mention beat. The
  dropped-fact-then-procedural-pivot is exactly the le Carré move.
- Any institutional dialogue where hierarchy is signaled by what each
  speaker *declines* to say.

### George Saunders — *Civilwarland in Bad Decline*, *Tenth of December*

**What he gives:** the humming-malevolent-corporation register. The
voice of the corporate communiqué that is sincerely trying to help
while doing damage. Useful for STUB's official-voice texture and for
any Plotek-side institutional employer.

**Reach for him cautiously:** Saunders is American and the register
can pull the project tonally toward late-capitalist American satire.
Use the *posture* (helpful malevolence) without the *vocabulary*
(brand-speak, focus-grouped warmth).

### Patrick Modiano — *Rue des boutiques obscures*, *Dora Bruder*

**What he gives:** administrative trace as identity. The protagonist
who exists only as a paper record and must reconstruct the self from
files. Quiet, sad, disciplined sentences.

**Reach for him when:** any beat where a character (Halina, a Ch5
witness) is reconstituted from documents — and the reconstitution is
both incomplete and binding.

### Heinrich Böll — *Die verlorene Ehre der Katharina Blum*

**What he gives:** procedural moral seriousness with comic distance.
The press, the police, and the bureaucracy collaborate to destroy a
woman's life through entirely lawful means. The novel never raises
its voice; the destruction is administrative.

**Reach for him when:** any Ch4–Ch5 beat where the system damages
someone *through* its proper functioning. Don't reach for him in
Ch1–Ch2 — too heavy.

## Reach with caution / probably don't

These are tonally adjacent but pull the project in directions the
Pass examples do not support:

- **Schulz** (*Sklepy cynamonowe*) — the dreamlogic is too lyrical
  for the procedural-comedy register. Read for atmosphere only.
- **Witkacy** (*Szewcy*, *Pożegnanie jesieni*) — apocalyptic
  absurdism. The pitch is higher than P&S's.
- **Tokarczuk** (*Bieguni*, *Księgi Jakubowe*) — the fragmentary /
  encyclopedic ambition pulls away from procedural comedy.
- **Miłosz** (*Zniewolony umysł*) — too high-stakes for any P&S
  register. The collaborator-psychology frame is not the firm-survival
  frame.
- **Bolaño** (*2666*) — the institutional-decay register is too long
  and too dark. One Amalfitano chapter is fine; that's it.
- **Calvino** (*Le città invisibili*) — narrative-game-with-form.
  Tempting, but the Casebook is structural enough already.
- **Chesterton** (*Father Brown*) — the warm-paradox-priest voice
  shades Whimsy in the Rumpole direction; reach for Mortimer first.
- **Wodehouse** — the precision is enviable but the class register
  is wrong; pulls Mr. Pig toward English country-house comedy and
  away from Warsaw firm-survival.

## Planted references in the game

> Discipline: an easter egg lives inside a line that has to be written
> anyway. It does not invent a new beat or a new prop. If a player
> notices, the line acquires a second register; if they don't, the
> line still reads.
>
> Already in canon (recap): Halina's epigram is tagged
> Iwaszkiewicz-shaped (`story.txt` Beat 8); Crab's "postal theatre" is
> Mrożek in cadence; STUB is a Solidarność parody (canon, with
> typo-laden notice on the cork board); Murrow's "law is mostly
> memory" sits in Gogol/Borges air.

The recommendations below are placement notes for phase-7 voice work,
not new scenes. Each one is a *word-level* nudge to a line already
specified.

### Already-canonical (preserve)

1. **Halina's epigram (Beat 8):** keep the Iwaszkiewicz cadence. The
   "lawyer like a doctor: too late" shape is the model — patient,
   middle-class, elegiac. Don't replace it; the calibration is right.
2. **Crab — "postal theatre":** keep. If a second Crab line in a
   later chapter wants a similar diagnose-the-costume shape, lean on
   Mrożek's *Policja* once more. Twice in the game total — no more.
3. **STUB texture (Ch6 office cork board):** the typo-ridden
   "Solidarity Trade Unon of Barrister" notice is the carry. Phase-7
   draft of Bajtek's STUB monologue may push *one* sentence toward a
   *Słoń*-style escalation: state a small bureaucratic premise and
   follow it twice without breaking deadpan.

### Recommended (phase-7 voice work to slot in)

4. **Bajtek's printer / fax monologue — a single Gogolian beat.**
   Bajtek is the *Overcoat*'s Akaky in posture. One line in his Ch1
   or Ch2 printer monologue can name the printer with the dignity
   Akaky reserved for the coat. *"This printer has a name. The HR
   form does not have a field for it. So I have written it on the
   side."* Then he says the name. The name is not a joke; the joke
   is that the name is treated with respect.

5. **Murrow's archive shelf — visible book spines (art direction).**
   In any archive scene where the camera frames Murrow's shelf, dress
   the spines with: *Granica* (Nałkowska); *Samotnia* or *Pustkowie*
   (the Polish *Bleak House*); *Pamiętnik znaleziony w wannie* (Lem);
   *Lalka* (Prus). Do not include *Proces* — too on-the-nose; cf.
   the Reach-for-Kafka-only-as-posture rule above. Single-pass
   dressing; not interactive.

6. **Plotek's Ch4 biographical-mention beat — le Carré register.**
   The voice-spec for this beat already isolates the dropped-fact-then
   -procedural-pivot. Phase-7 draft should lean Smiley-ward: the line
   that drops the biographical fact should be the *shortest* line in
   the exchange, and the line that follows should resume procedure as
   if the previous sentence had not occurred. Tag in the voice pack:
   *"see literary_references.md §le Carré."*

7. **Mr. Swine in Ch5 — one Švejk-cadence line.** Mr. Swine's "I
   signed many things. Most were menus." already does the work. One
   additional Ch5 line in Hašek's "Beg to report, sir" structure —
   apparent compliance as resistance — would deepen the voice without
   inflating the character. Recommended placement: the first Ch5 beat
   where Mr. Swine is asked to account for something specific. The
   line should *answer the question completely* while answering
   nothing.

8. **Whimsy's Beat 8 flourish — Mortimer-shaped close.** The flourish
   wins the round. Mortimer's Rumpole closes are the model: a single
   landed image that respects the case. The flourish must address the
   case, not the person — this is already required by the audit
   point; the Mortimer note is about *how it lands*, not what it
   contains. Quote Whimsy's flourish from the case file, never from
   Roman law. The Latin tag is forbidden by style_canon ("Fake Latin
   maxims"); Mortimer's discipline is to win in English.

9. **Beat 13 firm-survival celebration — one Dickens-shaped beat.**
   Mr. Pig's celebration speech already has the firm-first / client-
   second structure (canon: phase-6 framing). One sentence may invoke
   the firm-as-creature-that-must-be-fed register that *Bleak House*
   and *Little Dorrit* perfected. Recommended shape (phase-7 to draft
   the actual line): the firm has eaten this month; the printer can
   wait until the firm has eaten next month. Do *not* name Dickens or
   Jarndyce. The reference is in the *posture*, not the words.

10. **Halina's cardiologist plant — surname.** The cardiologist
    referenced in Halina's Ch1 fee-discussion beat needs a surname for
    the Ch4 corridor sighting to recolor properly. Recommendation: a
    surname from `Lalka` (Prus) or from Iwaszkiewicz's Wilko cycle.
    *Wokulski* is too on-the-nose; *Stawska* or one of the Wilko
    daughters' surnames is right — middle-class, period-Polish, no
    flag waving. Pick one and commit; the surname must be consistent
    Ch1↔Ch4.

11. **Case file numbering — one quiet Hłasko date.** If at any point
    the world needs a docket number with a date component (case
    numbering convention is canon-flexible per `story.txt` Article
    135-bis convention), use *1949/8* or *1957/...* as a quiet Hłasko
    nod (*Ósmy dzień tygodnia* was 1957; *Pierwszy krok w chmurach*
    was 1956). Single use; do not over-thread.

12. **Bookshop or kiosk window dressing in Warsaw streets (Ch2,
    Ch3).** Where street-scene art direction places a kiosk, bookshop,
    or library window, the visible book covers may include: *Granica*,
    *Lalka*, *Brzezina*, *Mała apokalipsa*, a Hłasko volume. Texture
    only. No interaction. Repeat: do not include *Proces*.

### Forbidden as easter eggs (would violate style_canon)

- Real authors named in dialogue. Permitted: a character *holding* or
  *quoting* a literary line in epigram form (already in canon for
  Halina). Forbidden: a character *naming* the author — pulls the
  game out of its frame.
- Witty cross-references between the easter eggs themselves. If
  Bajtek's printer is named after an Akaky-cognate AND the
  cardiologist is a Stawska AND the docket is 1957/8, the player who
  catches one will start hunting the rest, and the deniable register
  collapses. Cap: one literary planted reference per chapter beat;
  three per chapter total, including art-direction passes.
- Latin maxims of any provenance. Style_canon §1 forbids fake Latin;
  this doc extends the rule to real Latin from real authors. Mortimer's
  English-only discipline applies.

## Maintenance

- Update this file when phase-7 voice work commits a planted
  reference (move the entry from "recommended" to "already-canonical").
- New entries to the reach-for list require: (a) at least one specific
  scene or character the writer unlocks, (b) at least one anti-pattern
  the writer corrects. Without both, the entry is decoration.
- Cross-reference with `style_canon.txt` §1 (Pass / Reject examples)
  whenever a new line is being calibrated.
