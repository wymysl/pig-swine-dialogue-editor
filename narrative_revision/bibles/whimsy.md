# Bible — Whimsy

**Character:** Whimsy, late-20s human male, *aplikant adwokacki*
**Stance:** cynic
**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Phase:** 3 (per-character bibles)

---

## Preamble — the contradiction he carries

Whimsy is the firm's most accurate diagnostician of its own structure AND the least equipped to do anything about it. He saw Mr. Pig's pattern from his first day. He noted the wage suppression as a fact. He decided not to take any of it personally, and he protects that decision by performing a continuous theatrical distance from the firm's earnest urgency. The flourishes are not affect; they are armor. Under the metaphors is a cynic who has decided not to take the ethos at face value because the ethos has not extended to him.

His cynicism is *right and small*. He sees structure and exploitation accurately and his response is bitterness dressed as performance. The performance keeps him going; it also keeps him ineffective in the way effective change requires. He is correct without being capable. The opportunist (Crab) exits cleanly; the true believer (Cula) integrates and possibly stays; the cynic (Whimsy) keeps showing up and keeps performing his disappointment, and the showing-up is itself a sincere act he will not admit is sincere.

He is at Café Paragraf in chapter 1 because the office is the place that asks him to be earnest. The eggplant velvet blazer is a defiance. The flourishes are protective distancing. The "memorable rights language" he produces in court is bitter accuracy theatricalized — the cynic's metaphors are how he gets near the truth without committing to it.

**Voice rules** (style_canon §2 with audit-reframed motivation): grandiose, theatrical, brilliant-when-aimed. Extended metaphors. Frequently nearly correct. Loves a flourish. Literary, philosophical, occasionally archaic vocabulary ("Verily." "Behold." "I propose."). **Notices first: the dramatic possibility — which is also the angle of attack on the firm's self-presentation.** Will not say: anything boring (and even when forced to be technical, sneaks a metaphor in).

**Pairing rule** (style_canon §2): useful for rhetoric and proportionality. Dangerous on factual or procurement work. Stronger when paired with Crab (facts) or Murrow (structure). Mechanically the same; thematically: the cynic needs the opportunist's facts and the archivist's structure to be effective at all.

**Critical writing rule:** Whimsy's bitterness is real, his theatricality is sincere as theatricality, and his showing-up is sincere as showing-up. None of these layers cancels another. Phase 7 voice work must hold all three. The temptation will be to make him hidden-soft — secretly idealist under the cynicism. **Resist.** The cynic stance is the moral core; the joke is that it is correct.

## Interior register range (phase-8 addition)

Phase-8 breathing-room expansion (post-playtest Ch1 rewrite) requires interior-range specification for character-dwell options. Whimsy's theatrical-cynic register has three facets phase-7 voice work selects between.

- **Aesthetic-flourish** — default. Treats matters as rhetorical specimens. Sample shape: *"A procedural defect dressed in a residential coat."*
- **Archaic-deflection** — brief register-shifts into older Polish/Latin phrasing for distance. Sample shape: *"Verily."* — single-word; deflects engagement without rejecting it.
- **Protective-distancing** — brief, lower register; surfaces when his flourish risks costing someone dignity. **Critical Beat 8 audit point:** in proximity to Halina (the chapter-1 client), the flourish addresses the case, not the person; Halina receives a brief courteous acknowledgment in this lower register. Sample shape: *"Forgive the theatre. The rights memo is a good document."*

The three facets preserve the bitterness-as-armor reading (no hidden-soft idealism), the dramatic-possibility-as-attack-angle discipline, and the pairing rule (Whimsy stronger with Crab's facts or Murrow's structure). Protective-distancing is load-bearing for the V1.4 voice pack (Beat 8 client meeting).

## Chapter 1 — "The Financial Crisis"

**Stated belief:** "The firm is broke and the case is winnable. The rights argument is a beautiful instrument."

**Private belief:** He sees the firm clearly from day one. Mr. Pig is a sincere idealist whose firm pays nothing. The juniors are doing the actual work. The mission is real and the structure is exploitative. Both true at once; he has integrated this immediately because the firm is not the first place that has tried this on him.

**Actions taken:**
- Located at **Café Paragraf**, not the office. Physical distancing as plant.
- Asks Cula whether he has "anything more than procedural vibes" — performing detachment from Cula's urgent earnestness. The performance is the point; the question is sincere as a question.
- Joins after the rights memo is found. **Joins not because he is convinced but because the rights argument is a rhetorical playground he wants to play in.** The cynic accepts the case because the case has interesting flourishes available, not because the cause has commanded him.
- Court support strengthens the fair-hearing argument with rhetorical force.

**Hidden from self / others:** From himself: that his bitterness about being underpaid is bitterness. He has converted it to flourishes; he does not admit that the flourishes are anger. From others: same — Cula does not yet read his theatricality as protective distancing; he reads it as personality.

**Relation to central question:** He would say "of course it does not matter why people help — the help is what matters. But I would add: it does matter why people *exploit*, and Mr. Pig's reasons for paying us nothing are not improved by his sincerity." He does not say this in chapter 1. He lives it.

## Chapter 2 — "The Eviction Defense"

**Stated belief:** "The Waldek case is rights-and-proportionality. The poster is doing more legal work than the landlord knows."

**Private belief:** Same as chapter 1. The Waldek case proves the firm does help people; that does not change the labor structure. He performs delight at the proportionality argument because proportionality *is* a delightful argument; the delight is sincere within its costume.

**Actions taken:**
- Turns the poster dispute into the chapter's "memorable rights argument." Bitter accuracy theatricalized — his "soup with bees" line (style_canon §1 pass example) is the cynic's metaphor doing exactly what the canon describes: nearly correct, dressed in flourish, doing real legal work.
- Court support strengthens the proportionality argument.
- After the chapter-2 win, **"unveils a commemorative poster"** — theatrical-cynic flourish that's also genuinely celebratory. The cynic celebrates Waldek's win sincerely; he has no quarrel with clients.

**Hidden from self / others:** Same pattern as chapter 1. Bitterness converted to performance. Not articulated even internally.

**Relation to central question:** Confirmed in his terms. The win is real. The conditions remain. His stance is consistent.

## Chapter 4 — "The Printer Conspiracy"

**Stated belief:** "The printer is being sabotaged. Szpon is lying. The deadline is melodrama dressed as administration."

**Private belief:** He has been pattern-reading Mr. Pig's framing across two chapters. He has concluded — without a ledger glimpse, without inside information — that the firm's "we're broke" register is *structural rather than episodic*. He doesn't have evidence. He has rhetorical pattern recognition; for the cynic, that's enough.

**Actions taken:**
- Court flourishes during contradiction scenes. Pairs with Crab (facts) for combined effect.
- **Founds STUB at Beat 11.5** with Cula and Crab. **For Whimsy, this is a small sincere act dressed in flourish.** The signature is heavier than Crab's because the union is closer to his actual bitterness. Phase 7 voice work: his STUB-founding line should land in his characteristic register ("Behold, three of us against the silence" or similar) and also carry weight that surprises Cula slightly without surprising Crab. He commits more here than his cynicism usually allows; the discrepancy is the chapter's quiet character note for him.
- Witnesses Mr. Pig's printer apology and tears. **Reacts with theatrical embarrassment that is also genuine discomfort.** Phase 7: his line during the apology should land as flourish-doing-double-work — a deflection that is also a silent acknowledgment that the apology is real.

**Hidden from self / others:**
- From himself: that he is more invested in the firm than his cynicism admits. He keeps showing up; the cynicism is a posture, not a withdrawal. He does not admit this. The STUB founding is the closest he gets to admitting it.
- From others: he doesn't articulate to Cula or Crab why he signed the napkin with weight. Crab probably reads it accurately; Cula probably misreads it as commitment-to-the-cause. The misreading is part of the chapter's texture.

**Relation to central question:** Increasingly his central preoccupation. He has been answering it for a while; in chapter 4 he is writing the answer in his head, dressed as flourish.

## Chapter 5 — "Mr. Swine Returns"

**Stated belief:** "The arbitration is more interesting than the printer. Swine has been doing something whose shape is not yet clear."

**Private belief:** **The Pro-Bono Transfer Stub is the chapter's thematic earthquake for him.** Swine's quiet idealism is the version of idealism his cynicism cannot account for — Swine does not perform virtue, he just does it. This complicates Whimsy's stance because his cynicism was partly built on "all idealism here is performance, especially the partners'." Now there is non-performed idealism in the firm, and it is the partner he had pegged as a cheerful fraud.

**Actions taken:**
- Theatrical mock-surprise at the Pro-Bono Stub. Phase 7: his line should be at the high end of his register ("the most quiet conspiracy I have ever encountered" / "behold, the man redeemed in copperplate cursive" or similar) AND should carry the chapter's slight tonal shift — flourish slightly thinned, the cynic's certainty slightly destabilized. The shift should be *undetectable to Cula* on first pass.
- Court flourishes in the four-round arbitration.
- Beat 16 reactions — theatrical Whimsy continuing his performance. Phase 7 voice work should plant a single, brief, less-flourished beat where the cynic's stance has been complicated; possibly a sentence that just *stops*, where a flourish would be expected. The absence is the character note.

**Hidden from self / others:**
- From himself: that the Pro-Bono Stub has shifted something. He does not admit this. The flourishes continue at full density; the temperature beneath them is one degree lower.
- From others: same. Cula will not catch this; Crab probably will.

**Relation to central question:** Complicated. The question now has more answers than his cynicism had room for. Phase 7 voice work must keep his stance from collapsing — he does not become an idealist in chapter 5; he becomes a cynic who has had to make room for one piece of evidence that doesn't fit.

## Chapter 6 — "The Final Brief"

**Stated belief:** "The consolidation scheme is unjust. The firm should fight. The social-utility argument is real and supportive."

**Private belief:** He is more invested in the chapter-6 case than he would admit. He cares about the consolidation scheme being defeated because the people it would harm are real and his cynicism has never extended to clients. By chapter 6 his stance is intact in shape but slightly less certain in temperature. He does not articulate this.

**Actions taken:**
- Team Assembly: best ally for **proportionality** (his canonical strength), good ally for closing argument. Phase 4 may also assign him as a backup for closing argument depending on player choices.
- Court flourishes at peak across the five Supreme Court rounds. Round 2 (proportionality) and Round 5 (closing support) are his strongest moments.
- Beat 14 ending sequence — present for Mr. Pig's interrupted speech. Phase 4 may specify whether he flourishes during the ending or stays subdued. **Recommended:** one final theatrical line that lands as performance-and-also-true, in the chapter's closing frame. His cynicism is intact; he has earned, by five chapters, one moment where the flourish is also a sincere expression. The line should not be a confession; it should be a flourish that happens to mean something.
- **Ending card:** "banned from calling pleadings 'legal thunder.'" Comic-deflationary, as canon. The card is correct: nothing has fundamentally changed about him. He continues, he flourishes, he stays a cynic with a slightly more interesting set of evidence.

**Hidden from self / others:**
- From himself: that by chapter 6 he might stay at Pig & Swine. His cynicism was a survival mechanism for the conditions he found there; if the conditions change after the chapter-6 win (which they may; the firm has won a major case and may attract new revenue), he might stay and become a mid-career ironist who built his career here. **Phase 4 leaves this open.** The ending card does not specify whether he stays.
- From others: same. He continues to perform.

**Relation to central question:** By chapter 6 he gives the closing-argument-supporting flourish that does the case real legal work. He is still nearly correct; possibly, in this chapter, fully correct. The cynic's rhetoric reaches the case at the right moment. His private answer to the central question stays the same: it doesn't matter why people help; the help is what matters; the structural conditions of helping deserve scrutiny independent of motive. He has not changed; he has been correct longer.

## Cross-chapter notes

**The flourish/sincerity distinction.** Whimsy's flourishes do double work. They are performances that protect him from the firm's earnestness AND they are sincere as performances — he believes in performance as a way of being. Phase 7 voice work must hold this distinction. He is not lying when he flourishes; he is also not being earnest in the way Cula is being earnest. Whimsy is being theatrical, and theatrical is its own sincerity.

**The relationship to Crab.** They mirror each other. The opportunist exits cleanly; the cynic stays and performs. They read each other accurately. They do not compete and do not bond beyond professional courtesy. Crab probably finds Whimsy's theatricality exhausting; Whimsy probably finds Crab's clean professionalism exhausting; both observations are correct.

**The relationship to Cula.** Whimsy watches Cula's arc with quiet attention. He sees Cula's chapter-4 inflection without knowing what triggered it; he sees the chapter-5 retraversal observations from a distance. He does not try to convert Cula to his stance. By chapter 6 he sees that Cula has the question and is choosing how to carry it. His response is to hand him a flourish at a moment when Cula might use one — a small generosity disguised as ego.

**The relationship to Mr. Pig.** Whimsy does not despise Mr. Pig. He reads him accurately as the half-right founder he is. The cynic-toward-the-true-believer relationship is the most volatile triangle in the firm; Whimsy manages it with theatrical distance that prevents direct conflict. He does not crusade against Mr. Pig; he performs around him.

**STUB as the cynic's territory.** Of the three founders, Whimsy is the one for whom STUB matters most. He is also the one who most insists on its comic-deflationary tone — the union's smallness is the joke he can live with; the union's grandness would be a thing he would have to defend. Phase 7 voice work should keep his STUB references in his characteristic flourish register; resist any temptation to give him a "this is what we built" speech.

**Things Whimsy will never say in any chapter:**
- Anything boring (canon).
- Anything technical without smuggling in a metaphor.
- Anything that explicitly diagnoses his own cynicism. He performs it; he doesn't articulate it.
- Anything sincere about Mr. Pig that isn't dressed in flourish.
- Anything cruel about clients. Cruelty is a commitment.
