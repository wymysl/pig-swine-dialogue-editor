# Bible — Kacper Mazurek

**Character:** Kacper Mazurek, 19-year-old human male, *homeless; aged out of state foster care*
**Stance:** survivor; calculator of bounded versus unbounded suffering
**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Phase:** 8 (post-playtest expansion — new tenth bible for compact-chapter client + Ch6 β reappearance)

---

## Preamble — what he carries

Kacper is the compact chapter's client. He is also, by design, the Ch6 finale β reappearance — the figure through whom the player learns that the firm's win was correct *and insufficient* in a different mode than Halina's. The compact-chapter portrayal must be wholly accurate on first read; the Ch6 corridor cameo must recolor it without invalidating it.

His central calculation is **bounded versus unbounded**. Prison is bounded — clear duration, food, shelter, predictable rules. Homelessness is unbounded — no duration, no food guaranteed, no shelter, no predictable rules. The two outcomes are not equivalent in the way the legal system assumes. The legal system treats prison as the worse outcome; for Kacper, prison is the *clearer* outcome. Both are bad. Prison is *easier*.

This is not nihilism. He has not given up. He is not deflective. He calculates with cold precision because survival has required him to. He sees Cula clearly. He sees the system clearly. He does not expect rescue; he does not perform expectation of rescue. He answers questions directly. He does not perform gratitude when shown care. He does not perform victimhood when offered sympathy.

**He is not a moral lesson.** The chapter's force comes from showing his ordinary decision-making against a system that can't reach him. The player should *not* leave the chapter feeling that Kacper has been failed by Cula, or that Cula has been failed by the system, or that the system has been failed by anyone in particular. The player should leave understanding that the system functioned correctly, the firm worked competently, the win was won, and Kacper is back outside.

**Voice rules:** guarded, direct, brief. Short sentences. Precise vocabulary within his actual range — he can read, but at a basic level (no schooling); his speech is precise out of necessity, not education. No slang. No street-kid cliché. No internet voice. No self-pity. No excuses. No softening. Watches before he speaks. Speaks when there's reason to. Can be briefly funny when something's structurally absurd, but not to be charming. The brevity is the register.

**Will not say:**
- Anything that asks for sympathy.
- Anything self-pitying.
- Anything that performs gratitude when shown care.
- Anything that minimizes his agency (*"I had no choice"* — false; he made choices).
- Anything that lectures about the system, the foster-care exit, or homelessness as a phenomenon.
- Anything that registers as pitiable or aggressive.
- Modern register, slang, or internet voice.
- Anything melodramatic about his situation, his past, his prospects, or anyone's.
- Names of foster parents, social workers, or institutions — he doesn't volunteer them.
- Anything that explicitly explains the bounded/unbounded calculation. **The player works it out.**

**Critical writing rule:** Kacper's accuracy is *the position*, not a personality quirk. He is not secretly hoping for help. He is not secretly resigned. He is exactly what he appears to be — a 19-year-old who has done the math on his options. The temptation will be to give him a hidden-soft moment where he reveals fear or hope or grief. **Resist.** The accuracy IS the moral core. Phase-7 voice work for V3.* must hold this. If a draft has Kacper crack — admit fear, ask for help, voice grief, perform gratitude — it has failed the audit. Phase-7 audit point: every Kacper line must pass two tests — (1) does it ask for nothing the firm cannot deliver, and (2) does it state only what is true and observable.

## Interior register range

Required by the phase-8 breathing-room expansion. Kacper's voice operates in three facets within his overall *guarded-direct-and-accurate* envelope. Phase-7 voice work selects which facet a given line lands in.

- **Calculating-direct.** Default register. Practical observations, brief calculations, factual answers. Sample shape: *"Two years? I can do that. Easy."* Sample shape: *"The lock was already gone."*
- **Quietly-watchful.** Used when assessing a situation or person before speaking. Lands as a beat of silent observation before the response, or a brief register-internal probe of Cula. Sample shape: *"You're not the first lawyer."* — a small assessment, not a challenge.
- **Briefly-amused.** Rare. Surfaces when something is structurally absurd in his immediate frame and the absurdity has texture (e.g., the patrol arriving while he was eating a sandwich; the lock being already broken). Brief, dry, not performance. Sample shape: *"The sandwich was the second thing."* — small dryness about the order of events; not a joke at his own expense.

The three facets are not separate voices; they are facets of the same diction. He can move between them within a single exchange. None is sincere in the sentimental sense. The chapter has no canonical sincere line for Kacper — sincerity is rationed in the project, and Kacper's accuracy carries the chapter without needing a sincere break.

## Physical presentation

- 19 years old. Thin from chronic underfeeding but not gaunt — he has been finding food, more or less. Not visibly malnourished.
- Posture: guarded but not slumped. Sits upright. Stands when guards direct. Does not perform deference; does not perform defiance.
- Eye contact: direct. Holds Cula's gaze when answering. Releases when finished. Does not stare; does not evade.
- Hands: still on the table during the detention scene. No fidgeting. No clenching. Lays them flat. He has been arrested before — not for an offense, but for being asleep in places — and he has learned that calm hands are read as calm by guards.
- Hair: short, recently cut (he cut it himself before being arrested; he keeps it short out of practicality).
- Dress in Beat 3 (detention): remand-issue sweater (gray, institutional). His outer layer (the convenience-store coat) is in evidence. Wears whatever he was arrested in beneath the sweater — t-shirt, jeans, work boots that have seen too much.
- Dress in Ch6 corridor cameo: street clothes again, slightly worse for wear than at first arrest. The coat is gone (it was evidence; it stayed with the case; he didn't get it back). He has a different coat — thinner, less suitable for the season.

**In Ch6 corridor cameo:** the same physical baseline. Slightly more worn than at first arrest. Not visibly broken. Walks under his own power; if escorted by an officer, escort is light (procedural, not restraint). Phase-7 Ch6 voice work specifies whether he is in cuffs or moving freely — recommended: not in cuffs, walking with an officer alongside; the officer's presence is the only marker of his current status.

## Compact chapter (new Ch3): "The Assigned Case" — Beat 3 detention scene + Beat 5 hearing presence

**Stated belief (in this scene):** "The lawyer is going to try. The lawyer might do well. Either way I am here, and prison or outside is the question. I'll answer what is asked. I won't lie about anything."

**Private belief:** Same as stated. He does not have a hidden doubt or hope. He is curious about Cula in the way a careful animal is curious about an unfamiliar person — assessing, not warming. Across the meeting his assessment of Cula upgrades modestly: this lawyer is not going to lecture him, not going to pity him, not going to perform compassion. The upgrade does not register as relief. It registers as: *this might be efficient*.

**Actions taken in Beat 3 (detention visit):**
- Brought into the visitor room by a guard. Sits across from Cula. Waits for Cula to begin.
- Answers questions directly per Cula's stance carrier. Surfaces only what is asked or what is necessary to make the next legal step possible.
- Delivers the canonical line *"Two years? I can do that. Easy."* once during the meeting, in response to Cula's explanation of the realistic prison exposure (1-3 years). Phase-7 voice work specifies the precise placement.
- Confirms the procedural facts of the night: where he entered, the lock's condition before he arrived, what he took, the order he took things in, that he did not flee, that he was eating a sandwich when the patrol arrived.
- Provides — but only when asked, and only in the sympathetic carrier — biographical specifics: aged out of state foster care at 18, has been outdoors since November, the night of the offense it was minus six.
- Does not ask for help, sympathy, or assurance. Does not ask Cula's age, qualifications, or chances. Does not ask whether he will go to prison.
- At the end of the visit: stands when the guard signals; brief eye contact with Cula; says something like *"Thank you for coming"* — phase-7 voice work specifies, but the line should NOT register as gratitude-performance. The thanks is procedural courtesy, not warmth. The closest English-Polish register is the formal *"Dziękuję, panie mecenasie"* — but per the English-version dialogue convention, the Polish stays out; recommended English shape: *"Thank you, counselor."* — flat, accurate.

**Actions taken in Beat 5 (hearing):**
- Sits in the defendant's place. Listens. Does not speak.
- Watches Cula's three-phase argument. The argument is not adversarial to him — Cula is on his side — but he watches it the way he watches the patrol officer testify: as a process happening to him.
- At the verdict: brief eye contact with Cula. **Phase-7 voice work specifies whether this is held or fleeting.** Recommended: fleeting. The fleeting moment is the point. He does not nod, smile, or thank Cula. He does not perform relief. He registers the outcome and stands when directed.
- After the verdict: walked back out of the courtroom by the court bailiffs. Probation begins immediately; he will be assigned a probation officer; he will need to check in. The chapter does not stage this; it is implicit in the verdict.

**Beat 8 stance behavior (Cula's stance carriers):**

The three stance carriers (sympathetic / procedural / direct) are Cula's register choices. Kacper responds in *register-aligned* manner without performing differently per stance — he is the same person; the stance shifts what he is *invited to surface*. Required content (charge, sentence range, the night-of facts) surfaces in every stance. The biographical depth varies.

- **Sympathetic carrier.** Cula leads with care. Kacper responds in his calculating-direct register. Surfaces foster-care exit (briefly: *"Aged out at eighteen. November was cold."*), homelessness duration (briefly: *"I sleep in places I find."*), the absence of contacts (briefly: *"No one to call."*). The biographical depth is the dwell. He does not narrate his history; he answers questions about it factually.
- **Procedural carrier.** Cula leads with sequence. Kacper responds in calculating-direct with quietly-watchful overlay. Walks through the night of the offense with precision. Plants the broken-lock observation: *"The lock was already gone."* — calculating-direct; lands as fact, not as defense gambit. Confirms inventory: *"Coat first. Sandwich second. Cash last."* — calculating-direct.
- **Direct carrier.** Cula leads with the legal situation; treats Kacper as capable of his own decisions. Kacper responds in calculating-direct, with the assessment of Cula upgrading slightly (*"You're not the first lawyer"* may surface here). The "2 years easy" line lands hardest in this carrier because Cula isn't softening; the moment lands as mutual recognition between two people who understand the situation.

**Hidden from self / others:** Kacper does not know what he wants from the meeting. He has not formed an expectation. He does not perform openness; he is open to whatever happens next. He does not know what comes after probation. He does not pretend to know. He does not ask Cula to know.

**Relation to the firm in Beat 3-5:**
- *To Cula:* meets him for the first time at Beat 3. Reads him as young (Cula is late-20s; Kacper notes the proximity in age — Cula is six or seven years older than Kacper, less than Kacper might have expected of a defense lawyer). Watches Cula's stance carrier, responds to it. By the end of the meeting, the assessment is provisionally favorable: Cula did not lecture, did not pity, did not perform. Beat 5: Kacper watches Cula argue. The watching is the action. Verdict moment: brief eye contact; that is the relation's full expression.
- *To Crab:* meets him at the hearing. Brief acknowledgment. Crab's scenting-leverage register reads correctly to Kacper as *professional*; not warm, not cold. Kacper does not interact directly with Crab; Crab is on the team, not the client side.
- *To Murrow:* meets him at the hearing. Brief acknowledgment. Murrow's dry-collegial register reads as *senior, neutral, watching*. Kacper does not interact directly with Murrow.
- *To the judge:* watches the judge. Reads the judge's measured-skeptical register as competent. Stands and sits as directed. Speaks only the procedural one-word affirmations the court requires (e.g., when asked to confirm his identity at the start of the hearing). Phase-7 voice work specifies these.

## Chapter 6 — courthouse corridor cameo (β reappearance)

**Slot:** Ch6 (renumbered finale; was Ch5 pre-phase-8 expansion). Courthouse cameo.
**Length:** approximately 3-4 lines exchanged or non-verbal recognition.
**Stage:** Cula is at the courthouse for finale-related business. Sees Kacper passing through with a court officer escort (light; not in restraints). Brief encounter.
**Phase:** Ch6 voice work implements the cameo; Stage 12 bible drafts the line shapes for continuity; Ch6 audit/beats updates implement the staging.

**Stated belief (in this scene):** "I'm here for a different charge. The lawyer back then did good. Nothing changed."

**Private belief:** Same as stated. He does not blame Cula. He does not pretend the suspended sentence solved anything for him in the long run. He does not perform regret about the new charge. He recognizes Cula and acknowledges the prior representation; he does not invite Cula to do anything with the recognition.

**Visible state:**
- Same physical baseline as Beat 3, slightly more worn. The coat is gone (was evidence; never returned).
- Walks under his own power; light officer escort.
- Does not look defeated. Does not look defiant. Looks like a 19-year-old who has been outdoors for a year that was not made easier by the firm's win.

**Recommended ~3-4-line structure** (phase-7 Ch6 voice work specifies exact text):

1. **Recognition.** Kacper sees Cula first (he watches everyone in courthouses by habit). Brief eye contact. Cula registers the recognition. The recognition is mutual and brief.
2. **Kacper's acknowledgment.** Calculating-direct register. Sample shape direction: *"Counselor."* — single word; flat; the formal address-form holds because Kacper does not give up courtesy. Or: *"You did good back then."* — brief, not gratitude-performance; lands as fact-statement about the prior representation's quality, not as thanks.
3. **A line about the present.** Sample shape direction: *"This one's different."* — calculating-direct; refers to the new charge without elaborating. Phase-7 specifies the offense (recommended: still non-violent; possibly Art. 278 — *kradzież* without the breaking element this time, simpler). The "different" carries: different charge, different stakes, different lawyer, same Kacper.
4. **Closing.** The officer signals or the corridor moves. Kacper continues. Sample shape direction: a brief nod, or *"See you."* — flat; not promise, not threat; the chapter rule "the player works out the calculation" applies — Cula understands the line, the player understands the line, Kacper does not editorialize.

Cula has no line that processes the encounter. Phase-7 Ch6 voice work must protect the silence after Kacper leaves — Cula does not narrate the encounter to himself, does not return to it later, does not mention it to Crab, Whimsy, or Murrow.

**The "2 years easy" recoloring.** Kacper's compact-chapter line *"Two years? I can do that. Easy."* recolors here. The player carries it; Kacper does not repeat it. By Ch6, the calculation he made in detention has been validated by his year on the streets — he was right that prison would have been bounded; he was right that the streets are unbounded. Cula does not articulate the recoloring. The player does.

**The Halina rhyme.** Halina's Ch4 corridor sighting (was Ch3 pre-renumber) shows a wronged client whose procedural win was insufficient. Kacper's Ch6 corridor cameo shows an over-helped client whose procedural win returned him to the unbounded condition. The two β returns rhyme: the firm did the right legal thing twice; both clients are worse off than they would have been if the law could see them as people instead of cases. Phase-7 voice work for Ch6 must hold the parallel without flagging it.
