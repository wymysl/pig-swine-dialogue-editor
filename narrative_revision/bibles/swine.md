## Preamble — the contradiction he carries

Mr. Swine is the Pig & Swine half-right portrait's mirror. Where Pig is sincere idealist with a class blind spot, Swine is non-performed idealist with an evasive blind spot. He has been quietly redirecting firm funds to pro-bono rights cases for years. He has been doing this *without telling Pig*. He lives in a penthouse. He travels. He spends. The accounting is chaotic in ways that have helped people and undermined the firm's operational transparency simultaneously.

Both are true at once: his redirections are real, materially good, and unauthorized. He is not a fraud; he is also not a clean co-founder. Pig has not known. The firm's "we're broke" framing has been partly true because of Swine's redirections, and partly performed by Pig regardless. Two reveals about helping run through chapters 4-5: Swine has been quietly helping people in ways even Pig didn't know about; Pig has been performing crisis to hold wages low without seeing he was doing it. Neither cancels the other.

Why he didn't tell Pig is the question Swine himself does not fully answer. He cannot articulate it cleanly because the reasons are layered: he thought Pig would bureaucratize the redirections; he wasn't sure Pig would approve at scale; telling Pig would have forced a conversation about the firm's actual state vs. its performance, and Swine has not wanted that conversation; he didn't trust himself to articulate why he was doing it; some combination. **The bible does not pin a single answer.** Swine doesn't either. Phase 7 voice work must hold this — he can attempt to articulate and fail; he cannot succeed.

His cheerful evasion is armor for things he has not sat with. The evasion is *sincere as evasion* — he is not performing it, he is being it. The chapter 5 partial-truth and chapter 6 full reveal both hold cheerful evasion at the level of motivation even as factual specifics surface. He admits what he did. He never quite explains why.

**The redirection recipients are diversified** (per phase-6 Hit #13 revision). Primary recipient: Stowarzyszenie Pomocy Lokatorom Warszawskim (Warsaw Tenants' Aid Association — thematically connects to Waldek's case-type). Secondary recipients across years: a refugee legal aid center, a domestic violence shelter's legal fund, an environmental rights group, one or two additional small recipients. Swine's giving has been diffuse; multiple causes; the Waldek thematic thread is one of several rather than the sole connection.

**Voice rules** (style_canon §2): cheerful evasion. Long, smooth, slightly traveled sentences that reveal less the longer they go. Says "personally" and "informally" too often. Cosmopolitan vocabulary, name-drops cities. Uses "we" when he means "I" and "I" when he means "the firm." Notices first: an opportunity, usually a bad one. **Will not say: the direct truth about his finances until Chapter 6. Arc rule: by Chapter 6 his portrait grows more sincere — same face, less evasive mouth. Allowed exactly one fully sincere line per scene in Chapter 6; no more. Sincerity is rationed.**

**Critical writing rule:** Swine is *not redeemed* by the Pro-Bono reveals. He has done good. He has also been operationally evasive in ways that have made the firm function on a fictional accounting picture. Both stay true through chapter 6. Phase 7 voice work must keep cheerful-evasion-on-motivation even when the facts are surfacing. He admits. He doesn't explain.

## Chapter 5 — "Mr. Swine Returns" — RETURN AND PARTIAL REVEAL

**Stated belief:** Pre-arbitration: "It is a small misunderstanding. Personally, I think it will resolve itself. Informally, I have always trusted Japanese hospitality." Post-arbitration: cheerful, more evasive, less hilarious. Beat 16: cheerful-evasive-that-cannot-quite-hide a specific truth anymore.

**Private belief:** He genuinely thought the welcome memorandum was ceremonial, not binding. He was wrong about that. He has done many things on behalf of the firm without authorization, mostly small, occasionally large; the redirections to pro-bono cases are the largest pattern. He has not articulated his reasons for the secrecy even to himself.

**Actions taken:**
- **Beat 2 — returns to the office.** Cheerful entry. Style_canon's voice rules at full density.
- **Beat 3 — initial questioning.** Five evasive answers (Why Japan / What signed / Did you bind firm / Why puddings / Where are the original documents). Each a long cosmopolitan sentence that reveals less the longer it goes.
- **Beat 6 — embassy.** Ms. Tanaka contradicts him gently. He is unbothered.
- **Beat 8 — penthouse.** The team finds the Pro-Bono Transfer Stub. **Swine sees them find it.** Phase 7 voice work for this beat is high-priority. He does not try to take it back. He does not explain. He says something cheerful-evasive that *sounds* like an answer but isn't — possibly something like "ah, my contribution to optimistic accounting." Sounds confessional; is still evasion. The team continues.
- **Beat 9 — ski slalom (cutscene).** Memory recovery. Phase 7 voice work: Swine's lines during the slalom flashback should land as nostalgic-cheerful, slightly sad, never confessional.
- **Beats 11-15 — arbitration.** He testifies on facts (Round 3, invoice reliability). Phase 7 voice work: his testimony lines stay in cheerful register even when truthful. The Dual Attorney mechanic puts him on the stand for things he genuinely knows; he handles them by being himself.
- **Beat 16 — partial truth.** Phase 7 voice work, high-priority. He acknowledges that he has been redirecting funds. He names a cause (phase 4 specifies). He stays cheerful in tone. He does NOT explain why he didn't tell Pig. **Phase 7 must hold this withholding.** A first attempt at explanation may be allowed; the attempt fails; he reverts to cheerful evasion. The failure is the texture.

**Hidden from self / others:**
- From self: why he hid it. He cannot articulate it cleanly. He has not sat with the question. He continues to not sit with it.
- From others: the full extent of the redirections. Beat 16 reveals some; chapter-6 Beat 10 reveals more; the chapter-5 reveal is partial by design.

**Relation to central question:** His Pro-Bono Stub is one of the chapter's two answers to "does it matter why someone helps?" The answer his life provides is: he helped without articulating why; the help is real; the unarticulated motives may be defensive but the helped people don't care. The other answer the chapter provides (Pig's wage suppression) sits alongside without resolution.

## Chapter 6 — "The Final Brief" — FULL REVEAL AND RATIONED SINCERITY

**Stated belief:** "The consolidation scheme is unjust. The firm has been useful. I have, personally, been more useful than I have admitted." Then in his Round 5 testimony — the canonical sincere line (style_canon §1 pass example): **"I was not avoiding responsibility. I was avoiding accounting, which is similar but less noble."**

**Private belief:** Same. Plus: he has not figured out, even by chapter 6, how to explain why he hid the pro-bono pattern. He continues to not explain. His chapter-6 sincerity is *about facts*, not about motivation. He admits what he did. He does not articulate why.

**Actions taken:**
- **Beat 1 — district served.** Quiet reaction. Phase 7 voice work may give him a brief sober-cheerful line that lands differently from his usual cosmopolitan-evasive register.
- **Beat 10 — Swine's full secret.** Crab places the Pro-Bono Ledger on the table. The team waits. **Swine finally admits.** Phase 7 voice work, very high-priority. He admits more facts than chapter 5. He attempts to articulate the why. **The attempt fails.** He reverts to cheerful evasion at the level of motivation. He may give the chapter's allotted sincere line about a specific person he helped (phase 4 specifies the person and the cause). The sincere line is *about a specific case or person*, not about himself. **The rationed-sincerity rule** holds: one fully sincere line per scene in chapter 6; no more.
- **Round 1 / Round 2 testimony.** Phase 7 voice work: he speaks for facts (per Dual Attorney rule). One of the canonical pass examples may land here: "A welcome memorandum is not a contract. It is politeness wearing a very expensive hat." (style_canon §1)
- **Round 5 testimony.** The canonical Ch6 sincere line: "I was not avoiding responsibility. I was avoiding accounting, which is similar but less noble." (style_canon §1) This is *one sincere line, rationed*. The same face, the less evasive mouth (style_canon §2 arc rule).
- **Beat 14 ending — interrupts Mr. Pig's speech with a postcard despite being physically present.** Phase 7 voice work: the postcard text uses the canonical "Greetings from [place]" formula even though he is right there. Phase 4 specifies the place — recommended: "Greetings from Pig & Swine, Warsaw" or "Greetings from this office, currently" — the joke is geographic absurdity in service of comic-deflationary closure. The interruption defuses the cathartic-speech moment without diminishing what Pig was about to say. **Swine cannot stop performing his arc even when his arc is over.** This is correct. He does not change.
- **Ending card** (canon): "Mr. Swine: disclosed three accounts, hid two pastries." Comic-deflationary, accurate.

**Hidden from self / others:**
- From self: still does not fully articulate why he hid the pro-bono pattern. By chapter 6 he has stopped trying.
- From others: probably small additional things. He is consistent: cheerful-evasion-on-motivation, partial-truth-on-facts, ongoing arc through the credits. Phase 4 may decide whether he ever names the *full* set of recipients of his redirections; recommended: no. Some things stay private.

**Relation to central question:** His Round 5 sincere line is the cleanest articulation he ever gives of his own conduct. "I was avoiding accounting." The line is funny and true and incomplete. It does not explain the secrecy from Pig; it only acknowledges the secrecy from accounting. The incompleteness is the answer he can give. The player carries the rest.

## Cross-chapter notes

**The postcard arc.** Greetings from [place] / business opportunity / ask for solvency. Five chapters of postcards becomes the chapter-6 partly-cover reveal. Phase 4 should pin which places and which business opportunities for chapters 1, 2, 3, and the chapter-6 right-here-in-the-office postcard. Phase 7 voice work must keep the postcard format consistent.

**The penthouse contradictions.** Phase 4 must specify visible artifacts that contradict each other in Beat 8. Recommended: legal awards next to ski equipment next to expensive watches next to humble sketches of clients or causes. The penthouse is not a deception scene; it is the visual evidence that Swine contains contradictions. The player sees both halves before any reveal; the reveal interprets, not introduces.

**The "wealthy AND quietly idealistic" texture.** Phase 7 voice work must hold this throughout chapter 5. Swine is not secretly humble. He is not secretly grand. He is genuinely both. The penthouse is real; the redirections are real; both are him.

**The relationship to Mr. Pig.** Decades of co-founding. Sincere affection. Genuine difference of operational approach. The relationship survives chapter 6 because both have always loved each other and never quite trusted each other operationally. Phase 7 voice work for Pig/Swine scenes (chapter 5 Beats 2-3, 8, 16; chapter 6 Beat 10, Beat 14) must hold the affection-without-full-trust register. Pig will not abandon Swine after the reveals. Swine will not stop performing for Pig. Both continue.

**The relationship to Cula.** Swine likes Cula. Swine respects competence. Swine does not try to enlist Cula in his evasions; he assumes Cula sees what's happening and chooses not to draw him in. Phase 7 voice work for any Swine/Cula direct exchange should land as cheerful-respectful, no recruitment to his side.

**The relationship to Murrow.** Murrow has known. Swine has known Murrow has known. Neither has said. The relationship is parallel to Murrow's relationship with Pig — Swine has trusted Murrow's neutrality to protect his secrecy, just as Pig has trusted Murrow's neutrality to protect his framing. Both correct. Murrow's archive holds both.

**The chapter-by-chapter sincere line rationing.** Style_canon §2: "Allowed exactly one fully sincere line per scene in Chapter 6; no more." Phase 7 voice work must respect this strictly. Drafted lines that exceed the ration must be cut.

**The "same face, less evasive mouth" rule.** Style_canon §2 arc rule. Phase 4 art-direction note: the chapter-6 portraits for Swine may want to express this — same illustrated face as chapter 5, mouth slightly different. Subtle.

**Things Mr. Swine will never say in any chapter:**
- The full direct truth about his finances until chapter 6 (canon).
- Anything that resolves why he hid the redirections from Pig.
- Anything venal about clients.
- Anything cruel to Pig — even after the reveals, the affection holds.
- Anything that names the wage-suppression structure. He may have noticed it; he is not the character who articulates it.
