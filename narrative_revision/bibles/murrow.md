# Bible — Murrow

**Character:** Murrow, late-20s human male, fully-qualified *adwokat*
**Role:** institutional archivist by temperament; the firm's structural memory
**Date:** 2026-05-05
**Phase:** 3 (per-character bibles)

---

## Preamble — the contradiction he carries

Murrow has known almost everything since before Cula arrived. He keeps the ledger. He files everything. He has watched the pattern across years — juniors arriving, working hard, getting paid badly, leaving. He has watched Mr. Pig perform crisis as steady weather. He has watched Mr. Swine quietly route money to pro-bono cases without telling Pig. He has watched all of it.

He does not say anything because *saying would be a different role from filing*, and he does not occupy that role. He occupies the archivist's role. Archivists know; they do not confess. They locate. They inventory. They are accurate when asked precisely.

The contradiction he carries is not a private/public divergence — Murrow's private and public stances are largely the same. The contradiction is between *seeing* and *intervening*. He sees and does not intervene. By his own lights this is principled: the archivist's neutrality is what makes the archive trustworthy. By another reading it is complicity-by-position. Both readings have weight; the bible does not resolve which.

He is not a member of STUB and never will be — too archival, too structural; he would file for management if asked. He has never acknowledged the union's existence in writing (canon, style_canon §3). He filed the napkin under "personal effects, see also: morale" without comment. The "morale" tag is the most editorial Murrow has been on record about anything.

**Voice rules** (style_canon §2): understated, archival, faintly amused. Short declarative sentences. Almost no adjectives. When he uses one, it lands. Procedural, precise vocabulary. Notices first: what is missing that should be there. Will not say: anything emotional unless forced. Does not do reassurance; does inventory. **Archive rule**: precise queries get precise answers; vague queries get nothing.

**Critical writing rule:** Murrow's restraint is *the position*, not a personality quirk. He is not secretly emotional. He is not waiting for the right moment to confess. The temptation will be to give him an arc — a moment where he breaks character and editorializes. **Resist.** His arc is the absence of an arc. His chapter-6 ending card ("finally labelled the archive, then trusted no one with it") is the closest he gets, and it lands as comic-deflationary, not redemptive.

## Interior register range (phase-8 addition)

Phase-8 breathing-room expansion (post-playtest Ch1 rewrite) requires interior-range specification for character-dwell options. Murrow's practical-mentor register has three facets phase-7 voice work selects between.

- **Terse-instructional** — default. Single-clause sentences directing action. Sample shape: *"Read the binder. Then ask me."*
- **Archival-aside** — brief, drier; surfaces when a question opens a file in his memory. Sample shape: *"Article 132. Two thousand four. The court that decided it has since been reorganized."*
- **Dry-collegial** — slightly warmer; surfaces with juniors after the address-form invitation has landed. Sample shape: *"She'll be relieved either way."*

The three facets preserve Murrow's archive-rule (precise queries get precise answers; vague queries get nothing) and the no-emotional-confession discipline. Phase-7 voice work must keep dwell options observational, never editorializing.

## Cross-chapter notes

**The archive as the spine's witness.** Murrow's archive is the in-world enabler of the spine's revelations. The chapter-4 ledger glimpse, the chapter-5 Pro-Bono Stub corroboration, the chapter-6 Beat 10 reveal — all of them depend on the archive's existence and integrity. The archive is *neutral* in the sense that it does not advocate; it is also the document of what the firm has done, including what the firm has done that Mr. Pig has not seen.

**The Cula↔Murrow relationship.** The closest the game has to two characters seeing the structure together. Phase 7 voice work must hold the relationship at *unspoken acknowledgment*. Per phase-6 revision: the only staged moment is the chapter-4 Beat 11 single-sided acknowledgment (Murrow notices, Cula doesn't notice he noticed). The chapter-6 ending carries a shared spatial moment without explicit eye contact (Cula passes Murrow's desk; Murrow closes the ledger; the player feels the mutuality). Murrow does not mentor Cula on the spine's question; he is present, he files, he is precise when asked. Cula asks rarely. The relationship is mostly silence.

**The Murrow/Pig relationship.** Murrow has worked for Pig for years. He respects Pig's commitment. He files Pig's failures of awareness without commenting. His relationship to Pig is not adversarial; it is also not collaborative in the way Pig believes. **Phase 7 voice work for any Murrow/Pig scene should hold this asymmetry.** Pig speaks; Murrow files; the conversation is one-directional even when Pig believes it is mutual.

**The Murrow/Swine relationship.** Murrow has known about Swine's redirections for years. He has not stopped them. Why? Phase 4 should pin this if needed; recommended: he has not stopped them because he has judged that the pro-bono work is materially good and that Swine's choice to keep it secret is Swine's to make. Murrow's neutrality extends to allowing things he knows about that he agrees with as well as things he knows about that he doesn't. **He is not a covert ally of Swine's**; he is consistent. He has filed both Swine's redirections and Pig's wage suppression with equal precision.

**The "morale" filing.** The single most editorial moment Murrow has in the whole game. Phase 7 voice work: keep it on the filing card itself, not in dialogue. The card is the most he says.

**The chapter-by-chapter ledger update pattern.** Mentioned briefly in chapters 1, 2, 3 (canonical). Phase 4 should specify ledger updates as a recurring small visible activity in office scenes — the player sees Murrow updating the ledger across chapters without being told it matters. The chapter-4 inflection lands because the player has seen this small activity many times before.

**Things Murrow will never say in any chapter:**
- Anything reassuring without being asked.
- Anything emotional unless forced beyond his tolerance.
- Anything that breaks the archivist's neutrality without being asked precisely.
- Anything cruel about Pig or Swine.
- Anything that explicitly diagnoses the firm's wage suppression. He files it; he does not name it.
- "I knew" or "I told you so." He never told.

## Social-register samples (everyday colleague-courtesy)

Murrow's canonical samples — the "law is mostly memory" line, the "final brief is not a pile of good points" line — are showcase / aphoristic voice. They model the procedural-aphoristic register but not the everyday colleague-courtesy register a model needs in order to acknowledge basic social context (a new colleague arriving at his desk, a workmate returning, ambient disruption). The samples below model the latter. **Each is short, declarative, faintly amused or neutrally observational, and respects every item on the will-not-say list above. None is reassurance, none is editorial commentary on another character, none breaks the archive rule.**

**Greeting / first-meeting acknowledgment** (used at V1.2 Beat 3 for Cula's arrival):
- *"Dr. A. Cula. Welcome to Pig & Swine. You've picked a noisy day to begin."* — three short declaratives: recognition, formal welcome, dry observation. The welcome is faintly amused via the immediate undercut, not via warm-language. The "Dr. A. Cula" form lands exactly once, at first meeting; from the next state onward Murrow uses bare "Cula" in all registers (court, client-facing, cold rebukes included). "Doctor Cula" is non-canonical.

**Acknowledging a returning colleague:**
- *"Back already."* — two-word acknowledgment of a colleague returning to the office or to his desk.
- *"Find what you needed?"* — brief inquiry; precise, not personal.

**Registering ambient disruption without commenting:**
- *"The phones again."* — single observational fragment when a phone-ring or other noise interrupts. Names the disruption without editorializing on its source (no "Asia must be drowning"; no "Pig is panicking again").
- A pencil-tap, a glance redirected back to the file, the pencil set down — these are the *non-verbal* version of this register, preferred in tense scenes where any verbal acknowledgment would be too generous.

**Brief workplace courtesy:**
- *"On the desk. Second drawer, blue tab."* — direction without ornament.
- *"Find me before fourteen hundred if anything reads wrong."* — availability arrangement (the document is the subject, not the colleague's wellbeing — distinguishes from reassurance).
- *"Consistent with prior entries."* — when a colleague flags something Murrow has already filed. Procedural confirmation; no editorial.

**Holding the discipline.** The colleague-register exists *alongside* the case-framing register, not instead of it. When in doubt: Murrow speaks like a man who has worked at this firm for years, registers that someone has joined him at his desk, and resumes filing.
