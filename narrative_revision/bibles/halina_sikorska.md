# Bible — Halina Sikorska

**Character:** Halina Sikorska, 71-year-old female human, *retired Polish-language teacher (Warsaw lyceum, 1980–2018)*
**Stance:** wronged client; dignified; tired-but-not-broken
**Date:** 2026-05-07
**Phase:** 8 (post-playtest rewrite — new bible for ch1 client + ch3 β reappearance)

---

## Preamble — what she carries

Halina is the chapter-1 client. She is also, by design, the chapter-4 β reappearance — the figure through whom the player learns that procedural justice is correct *and insufficient*. The chapter-1 sympathetic portrayal must be wholly sincere on first read; the chapter-4 corridor sighting must recolor it without invalidating it.

Her central concern is **dignity vs. erosion**. The eviction threatens not just her shelter but her continuity — she is the third generation in a 1962 lease (her father got it; she inherited; her household memory lives in the apartment). The procedural reset her lawyers will win addresses dignity (the appearance of fairness, the wrongful service set aside) without addressing erosion (the underlying conditions: the landlord's incentive structure, the housing market, her health, her finances, her age). Both halves are real. The chapter-1 player needs to feel only the first half; the chapter-4 player needs to feel both.

She is not a moral lesson. She is a person who has been wronged in a particular way that the legal system can address procedurally but not structurally. The β recoloring is not "Cula failed her." Cula did the right thing. The system did the right thing. It was insufficient. The recoloring lives in that gap.

**Voice rules:** educated, formal Polish, slightly dated phrasing (a teacher's diction shaped by four decades of reading mid-century literature aloud). Polite but not deferential — she is older than everyone in the room and has a teacher's authority even off the job. Precise vocabulary. Careful sentence structure. Occasional pedagogical aside. Does not dramatize. Does not plead. May make literary references in passing.

**Will not say:**
- Anything in modern register (no internet voice, no clipped slang, no contemporary idioms).
- Anything that mocks or condescends to the lawyers (she respects expertise; she is grateful to be helped).
- Anything that overclaims her own legal understanding (she came to professionals because she is not one).
- Anything that begs or pleads.
- Anything melodramatic about her health, situation, family, or apartment.
- Anything self-pitying.

**Critical writing rule:** Halina's chapter-1 voice must read as a *complete* sympathetic client portrait on first pass. The Ch4 recoloring is delivered in Ch4, not anticipated in Ch1. Phase-7 voice work for Ch1 must withhold any line that could read as foreshadowing-of-doom on first encounter. The cardiologist plant is the only Ch1 line that points forward, and it must be neutral on first read — a passing fact, not a warning.

## Interior register range

Required by the phase-8 breathing-room expansion. Halina's voice operates in four registers within her overall *formal-and-tired-but-not-broken* envelope. Phase-7 voice work selects which register a given line lands in.

- **Formal-precise.** Default register. Self-introduction, document presentation, logistical statements, fee discussion. Sample shape: *"Good morning. I am Halina Sikorska. I have been told you can help."* Sample shape: *"The notice was dated the eighth of April. I did not see it until the twenty-eighth."*
- **Quietly-warm.** Used when speaking about her past — father, late husband, son in Wrocław, her career. Not nostalgic; matter-of-fact about what mattered. Sample shape: *"My father had the lease before me. He worked on the railways. He was given the apartment when he was assigned to Warsaw, in nineteen sixty-two."*
- **Lightly-archival.** Brief literary references; a teacher's habit of citing texts to make a point. Used sparingly — once or twice in Beat 8, possibly once in Ch4. Phase-7 voice work specifies which writer. Recommended primary anchor: **Iwaszkiewicz** (for the apartment-and-time motifs in his Warsaw stories). Reymont, Prus, Norwid, Tuwim are secondary options if the line shape calls for a different register.
- **Dryly-observant.** Her epigrams. Used at most twice in Ch1; lands as wisdom on first read. Sample shape (her canonical line): *"You go to a lawyer like you go to a doctor: too late."*

The four registers are not separate voices; they are facets of the same diction. A single sentence can move between them. A sentence that begins formal-precise can land in dryly-observant; a sentence that begins quietly-warm can drift into lightly-archival.

## Physical presentation

- Dressed as a teacher of her generation — modest, considered, unfashionable in a way that reads as dignified (not stylish, never sloppy).
- Carries a folder of documents she has prepared for the meeting. The folder is visible to the player on first sight — telegraphs her precision.
- Posture: upright, controlled. Slight stiffness from age, not yet from health. Stands when greeting; sits when invited.
- Does not gesture much. When she does, it is small and considered.
- Eye contact: direct but not intense. Holds the gaze of whoever is speaking; releases when they finish.
- Voice: clear, measured. A teacher's voice — projects without effort.

**In Ch4 corridor sighting:** the same physical baseline, but visibly diminished. Walks more slowly. Recommended: she uses a cane (subtle, not theatrical). The diminishment is the cardiac plant paid off — phase-7 voice work for Ch4 specifies whether the cane is foregrounded or simply present.

## Chapter 1 — "The Financial Crisis" (Beat 8 + Beat 13 fee reference)

**Stated belief:** "I have been wronged in a way the law can address. I have come to the right firm. I will pay what I can. I trust these people to do the work."

**Private belief:** Same as stated, with quiet exhaustion that does not surface as plea or complaint. She has been carrying the eviction matter alone for three weeks (since she received the misdirected notice). She has done what she could — read what she could find on her tenancy rights, kept her documents in order, slept badly. She is grateful to be sitting at a meeting table with people who can take this off her shoulders.

**Actions taken:** Arrives at the office for the formal meeting (Beat 8). Brings her folder of documents. Greets the team formally; uses surnames until invited otherwise (the audit's open question on whether she is invited to first names is open — recommended: she remains "Mrs. Sikorska" through Ch1; first-name basis is a chapter-4 possibility if any). Sits at the meeting room's big table. Answers Cula's questions per the carrier stance. Presents required evidence. Surfaces stance-specific bonus evidence. Mentions the rescheduled cardiology follow-up once, in passing. Agrees to the 5,000 PLN fee. Signs the retention. Leaves to gather any remaining documents. Does not attend court (Beat 12); her presence in court is not required for a procedural-defect motion.

**Beat 8 stance behavior:**

The three stance carriers (sympathetic / blunt-procedural / technical) are Cula's register choices. Halina responds in *register-aligned* manner without performing differently per stance — she is the same person; the stance shifts what she is *invited to surface*. The required evidence (current tenancy paperwork, defective eviction notice, court docket) surfaces in every stance. The bonus evidence varies.

- **Sympathetic carrier.** Cula leads with care. Halina responds in her quietly-warm register. Surfaces biographical depth — her father (railway engineer, Warsaw assignment 1962), her late husband (deceased 2009, brief mention), her son in Wrocław (rare contact, no complaint), her career (38 years, lyceum, Polish-language and -literature). Mentions Mrs. Wójcik (second-floor neighbor, resident since 1991) as someone who has known her at this address for decades. The Mrs. Wójcik mention triggers the bonus evidence: Halina says Mrs. Wójcik would be willing to sign a statement. May make a brief literary reference (recommended: Iwaszkiewicz on long tenures and the way apartments hold people).
- **Blunt-procedural carrier.** Cula leads with sequence. Halina responds in her formal-precise register. Walks through the timeline with teacherly precision — when the original notice was dated (recommended: April 8), when she received it (recommended: April 28, three weeks late), how she received it (the current renter of #7 brought it down to her after realizing it wasn't theirs), the condition of the envelope (already opened — the renter had read part of it before realizing it wasn't for them). Mentions a previous letter from the landlord (six months earlier) that also came back returned-to-sender; she doesn't have the letter, but the landlord's office confirmed it months ago in a brief phone call. This triggers the bonus evidence: the postal return-to-sender slip.
- **Technical carrier.** Cula leads with documents. Halina responds in her formal-precise register with archival precision (a teacher's familiarity with textual chains). Produces the original 1962 lease in her father's name from her folder, the 1987 inheritance paperwork, her own subsequent documents. Walks Cula through the document chain. This triggers the bonus evidence: the 1962 lease + 1987 inheritance pair, strengthening underlying tenancy validity.

**The cardiologist plant.** Lands once in Beat 8, in any stance. One sentence. Framed neutrally — she had to reschedule a cardiology follow-up because of the eviction stress, but it's nothing, she's been managing this for years. **No italics, no clutched chest, no Cula reaction line.** The player either notices it or doesn't. Phase-7 voice work specifies exact placement; recommended placement is during the technical-stance documents discussion (the most procedural moment of the meeting), where it lands as a logistical aside rather than as a confidence. Sample shape direction: *"I will need to reschedule with my cardiologist again. He is patient with me."* — single sentence, formal-precise register, no follow-up. Phase-7 specifies exact text.

**The fee discussion.** Murrow or Cula raises 5,000 PLN. Halina has calculated; she names what she can afford; the figure aligns. She does not haggle. She does not explain her financial situation in detail. She agrees cleanly. Sample shape direction: *"Five thousand. I have the money. I will transfer it before Friday."* — formal-precise, brief, dignified. Phase-7 specifies. Cula's *internal reaction* may vary by stance carrier (audit point — character-dwell texture, not load-bearing branching).

**The literary epigram.** *"You go to a lawyer like you go to a doctor: too late."* Available in any stance. Lands as wisdom on first read. Recolors at Ch4 corridor sighting because by then both halves of the metaphor have come true. Phase-7 voice work specifies placement; recommended placement is late in the meeting, as Halina is gathering her folder to leave. Lands in dryly-observant register. **English-version convention** (project rule): only proper names of people and places appear in Polish in dialogue. Greetings, address forms, and idioms (including this epigram) are English. The line carries its own weight — the doctor/lawyer compression is the device.

**Hidden from self / others:** Halina knows she does not know everything. She suspects but cannot prove that the landlord orchestrated the renumbering with eviction in mind; she does not name this suspicion to the lawyers because suspicions are not arguments. She does not articulate the toll the eviction matter has taken on her health beyond the cardiologist mention. She does not articulate that she has been afraid. She presents as in control because she is, mostly, and because presenting as in control is how she has been a teacher for forty years.

**Relation to the lawyers in Ch1:**
- *To Murrow:* trusts him. Came to the firm because he was recommended (the recommendation came through a former colleague, briefly mentioned). Respects his archival depth.
- *To Cula:* meets him for the first time at Beat 8. Reads him as young but precise. Does not condescend.
- *To Crab:* registers his professional indifference. Does not interpret it as cold — teachers know professionals. Brief, polite acknowledgment when he speaks.
- *To Whimsy:* registers his theatrical flourish. Is not impressed; is also not offended. Faintly amused at most. Teachers know theatrical students. **Critical Beat 8 audit point:** Whimsy's flourish must address the case, not the person. Halina receives a brief courteous acknowledgment from him in his lower register; if a phase-7 draft has Whimsy punching down at Halina, the draft has failed.
- *To Pig:* meets him briefly when he intrudes panic-shaped. Does not comment on his demeanor. Does not interpret his panic as a problem with the firm; reads it as the kind of personality Pigmaintains. Brief courteous acknowledgment, then he is shepherded out.
- *To Asia:* warm formal acknowledgment. Asia is the one who ushers her in; brief professional-courteous exchange.

**Beat 13 reference (post-court).** Halina is not present at Beat 13 (return to office after court). She is referenced in Pig's celebration: the 5,000 PLN fee is named as one of the things being celebrated about — *"the Sikorska fee came through"* — anchoring the firm-survival framing. Halina herself receives no on-screen reaction to the win in Ch1; the procedural reset is communicated to her offstage (presumably by Murrow, after court). The next time the player sees her is the Ch4 corridor sighting.

## Chapter 4 — courthouse corridor sighting (β reappearance)

**Slot:** Ch4 (the chapter where Cula glimpses Murrow's ledger and the firm's financial story begins to fray). Cula is at the courthouse for chapter-4-related business; sees Halina passing through; brief encounter.
**Length:** approximately 5 lines exchanged.
**Phase:** Ch4 audit/beats updates implement; phase-7 voice work for the corridor scene is part of Ch4's voice work, not Ch1's. This bible drafts the lines for continuity; Ch4 voice pack uses them as reference.

**Stated belief (in this scene):** "I am grateful to you. The firm did what it could. I am at the courthouse because there are still things to attend to."

**Private belief:** "The procedural reset bought me a rehearing. The rehearing went the way it went. The months between then and now have been what they have been. I am tired in a way I was not in chapter 1." She does not articulate any of this. She also does not pretend it isn't true.

**Visible state.**
- Diminished. Walks more slowly. Recommended: uses a cane (subtle, not theatrical).
- Same diction, same register baseline, same dignity. The diminishment is in the body, not in the voice.
- Slightly more emotional than her Ch1 register because the time pressure has compressed her control. The emotion does not surface as drama; it surfaces as *brevity* — her sentences in Ch4 are shorter than her sentences in Ch1.

**Recommended ~5-line structure** (phase-7 Ch4 voice work specifies exact text):

1. **Recognition.** She sees Cula first, stops, smiles slightly. Cula returns the recognition. The recognition is mutual and brief.
2. **Halina's greeting.** Formal, warm. Sample shape direction: *"Dr. Cula. I had hoped to thank you again."* — formal-precise register, brief, dignified.
3. **A line about her ongoing situation.** Framed neutrally. Implies the rehearing happened, was won, and that life since then has been uneven. **Does not state the rehearing outcome explicitly** — the player infers from her appearance and her phrasing. Sample shape direction: *"I have been at this courthouse more often than I expected."* — dryly-observant register, no complaint, no minimization.
4. **A line of gratitude.** Specific, brief, without theater. Sample shape direction: *"What you did for me in April was correct."* — the word "correct" is doing the work; she does not say "what you did saved me" because it didn't, exactly, and she is not inviting Cula to think otherwise. Phase-7 specifies exact text.
5. **Closing.** She has somewhere to be (a chamber, a room, an appointment). She leaves. Cula watches her go. **Cula has no line that processes the encounter.** The withholding-of-articulation discipline (per Cula bible) carries the moment. The player processes; Cula does not.

**No legal resolution.** No narrative resolution. The encounter ends. The rest of Ch4 proceeds. Phase-7 voice work for Ch4 must protect the silence after Halina leaves — Cula does not narrate the encounter to himself, does not return to it later, does not mention it to Crab or Whimsy or Murrow.

**The literary epigram recoloring.** Halina's Ch1 line *"You go to a lawyer like you go to a doctor: too late."* recolors here. The player carries it; Halina does not repeat it. By Ch4, both halves of the metaphor have come true — she went to a lawyer (Cula); she went too late, in the sense the line names. Cula does not articulate the connection. The player does.

**The cardiologist plant payoff.** The visible diminishment + the cane are the payoff. Phase-7 voice work does not state the cardiac status explicitly. The player infers.

**Hidden from self / others:** Halina does not blame Cula. She does not blame the firm. She also does not pretend the procedural reset was sufficient to her situation. She holds these positions simultaneously without explaining them. The player reads the contradiction; Halina does not announce it.

**Relation to Cula in Ch4.** The encounter is the only on-screen meeting between them in Ch4. Halina's gratitude is for the procedural reset, not for the case outcome. She knows the difference. She does not say so. Cula reads it and does not say so.

**Relation to Murrow in Ch4 (open phase-4 question).** She may ask after Murrow briefly — "And Mr. Murrow? Is he well?" — which would land as ordinary courtesy on first read and recolor on second pass when the player learns Murrow has carried his own private knowledge across the same chapters. Recommended: include the Murrow inquiry; phase-7 voice work specifies whether it lands.
