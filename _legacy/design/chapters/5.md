# Chapter 5 — "The Final Brief"

**Sprint 4 input.** Final convergence chapter. Every mechanic returns. Every major NPC matters.

**Source-of-truth reference:** `implementation_plan.md` lines 3804 onward (Chapter 5 spec runs through ~line 5393).

---

## Goal

The final synthesis. Chapters 1–4 each taught a separate skill. Chapter 5 has the player **using the whole toolbox**: investigation, evidence preparation, contradiction spotting, dual-perspective advocacy, and now team strategy. Reveals why Pig & Swine, despite being absurd, deserves to survive.

## Core loop

crisis erupts → citywide investigation → assemble allies → expose procedural scheme → survive Final Printer attack → Supreme Court hearing → reveal Swine's secret → save the district.

## Legal spine

> A mass legal process cannot extinguish homes, practices, claims, or procedural rights through automated notices, manipulated filings, vague public-interest language, and inaccessible remedies.

## Comedy spine

> The final villain is not one person. It is bureaucracy, opportunism, and an industrial printer with excellent paper velocity.

## New mechanics this chapter

### Team Assembly

Pre-trial preparation screen. Player assigns each ally to one of five argument tracks:

- `procedural_irregularity` (defective service, automated notices)
- `proportionality_and_dignity`
- `factual_causation` (procurement / conflict of interest)
- `social_utility` (P&S deserves to exist; Swine's pro-bono)
- `technical_suppression` (Objection Filter Log; Bajtek territory)

Each NPC has best, neutral, and bad fits. Best fit: argument lands strongest. Neutral fit: passes. Bad fit: a comic failure that still produces some progress (e.g. Wymysl on procurement).

Scoring affects the five-round trial weight.

### Final Printer Boss Battle

The Industrial Printer fights back. Five phases:

1. **Notice Spray** — dodge mass-printed closure notices.
2. **Duplicate Waves** — destroy duplicate-flagged objections.
3. **Red Tape Protocol** — beam attacks; collect Suppressed Objection pages.
4. **Toner Crisis** — environmental hazard; clean shutdown vs. messy shutdown affects evidence.
5. **Deadline Spiral** — final time-pressure phase.

Outcome variants:

- **Clean shutdown**: full Objection Filter Log + Master Notice Run.
- **Messy shutdown**: partial logs, additional procedural complications in court.
- **Emergency shutdown**: minimum logs, harder court.

Self-contained module under `src/minigames/final_printer_battle.js`. See `implementation_plan.md` lines ~24977 onward for full boss spec.

## Quest steps

1. **Opening crisis.** Notices arrive. Quest: "Prepare the final brief and save the Warsaw legal district."
2. **City Hall investigation.** Obtain Redevelopment Plan, Notice Template Batch, Procurement Link.
3. **Protest Square.** Collect Impact Statements; recruit returning NPCs.
4. **Industrial Printer Room.** Final Printer Boss Battle. Obtain Objection Filter Log + Master Notice Run.
5. **Team Assembly.** Assign allies to argument tracks.
6. **Supreme Court — five rounds.**
7. **Reveal scene.** Mr. Swine reveals he has been funding P&S's pro-bono work from Japan. The reveal is procedurally relevant (social utility argument), not just emotional.
8. **Ending.** Variant based on Team Assembly + court performance + boss outcome.
9. **Credits.**

## Court structure (5 rounds)

| Round | Question | Best argument track |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Were the notices procedurally valid? | procedural_irregularity |
| 2 | Were the consequences proportionate? | proportionality_and_dignity |
| 3 | Were objections suppressed by technical means? | technical_suppression (Bajtek + Objection Filter Log) |
| 4 | Is there a conflict of interest in the procurement? | factual_causation (Rak + Procurement Link) |
| 5 | Does Pig & Swine — and the district — have continuing social utility? | social_utility (Mr. Pig + Mr. Swine reveal + Pro-Bono Stub) |

Round 5 is the emotional climax. Mr. Swine's reveal lands here. Mr. Pig's earned sincere line lands here.

## Ending variants

- **Strong**: clean shutdown, optimal Team Assembly, all five rounds won → district saved, P&S thrives modestly, judge's understated approval, returning NPCs visible in epilogue.
- **Standard**: mixed outcomes → district saved, P&S survives, some allies regretful but loyal.
- **Weak**: rough boss outcome + suboptimal team → district saved by narrowest margin, P&S barely survives, sequel-tease ending.

All endings save the district. The variation is in how *well*, not whether. Even the weak ending is a win. (See design_bible §9: the player wins enough to save the client, not everything.)
