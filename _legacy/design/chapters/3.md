# Chapter 3 — "The Printer Conspiracy"

**Sprint 2 input.** Office slapstick that ends in real procedural-sabotage litigation.

**Source-of-truth reference:** `implementation_plan.md` lines 1479–2572.

---

## Goal

Teach **contradiction spotting** and **time pressure**. Chapter 1 taught the basic loop. Chapter 2 taught evidence preparation. Chapter 3 layers cross-examination and deadline urgency on top.

## Core loop

office malfunction → technical investigation → witness contradictions → urgent filing race → cross-examination → expose sabotage → save the printer.

## Legal spine

> A party's procedural rights are undermined where filings disappear, deadlines are manipulated, or access to the court is obstructed through deliberate interference.

## Comedy spine

> Everyone thinks the printer is sentient because that is emotionally easier than admitting a rival law firm is committing procedural sabotage.

## Trigger / preconditions

```js
chapter2.complete === true && chapter3.started === false
```

## Items / evidence

Required:

1. **Deadline Notice** — establishes urgency.
2. **Printer Log Fragment** (Server Room) — "Shows print jobs being redirected at impossible hours. The printer may be innocent, which is devastating for office morale."
3. **Repair Invoice** (Print Shop) — "A rival firm paid for 'emergency printer maintenance' on a machine it does not own. Even the invoice looks embarrassed." First explicit rival-firm link.
4. **Hidden Reroute Device** (Basement) — "A small device redirecting print jobs. It has one blinking light and the moral character of a tax haven." Hard proof.
5. **Bajtek Technical Statement** — court-friendly explanation.

Optional:

6. **Misprinted Filing** — shows actual harm.
7. **Rival Firm Leaflet** — comic; weak but suspicious.
8. **Backup Drive** — strong version of printer logs.

## New mechanics this chapter

### Contradiction Spotting (cross-examination)

During court rounds, Szpon makes statements. Player presents evidence that contradicts a statement. Pattern:

```js
contradictionEvidence: "repair_invoice",
successFlag: "caughtSzponInvoiceLie"
```

Wrong evidence: comic deflection from Szpon, no progress.
Right evidence: contradiction caught, argument advances.

### Filing Race (time pressure)

Before Round 1, a timed sequence: get the deadline filing through despite the sabotage. Failure produces a weaker procedural posture but does not hard-block.

## Mini-game (new this chapter)

**Scooter Racing through Warsaw streets** to the Print Shop. Triggered when Bajtek directs the player there urgently. Self-contained module under `src/minigames/scooter_race.js`.

- Win: arrive on time → full Repair Invoice + Print Shop NPC dialogue.
- Lose: arrive late → partial invoice info, rival firm has tipped off the print shop owner.

## Quest steps

1. **Opening crisis.** Printer malfunction. Quest unlocked: "Find out why Pig & Swine's filings keep vanishing."
2. **Meet Bajtek.** He requests Server Room access.
3. **Server Room investigation.** Obtain Printer Log Fragment.
4. **Scooter Race** to Print Shop.
5. **Print Shop.** Obtain Repair Invoice. First Szpon encounter.
6. **Basement.** Obtain Hidden Reroute Device.
7. **Filing Race** (time-pressure sequence).
8. **Court — three rounds with cross-examination.**
9. **Payoff.** Office ceremonially apologises to the printer.

## Court structure (3 rounds with cross-examination)

| Round | Question | Mechanic |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Were P&S's filings missed by negligence or interference? | Present Printer Log Fragment + Bajtek statement |
| 2 | Cross-examine Szpon: did the rival firm interfere? | Contradiction spotting using Repair Invoice |
| 3 | What is the appropriate remedy? | Argument: relief from procedural default + sanctions for sabotage |
