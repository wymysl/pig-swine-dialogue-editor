#### Chapter 1: "The Financial Crisis"

Chapter 1 is the starter-town chapter.

It should feel like the player has just arrived in a small, strange legal world where everyone already knows there is a disaster, except **Dr. A. Cula**, who has just walked into it.

**(Phase-8 post-playtest rewrite — see `narrative_revision/audit/chapter_1_v2.md` and `narrative_revision/beats/chapter_1.md`.)** Chapter 1 has been restructured into a 14-beat spine (up from canonical 12). Two new beats added: Beat 8 (client meeting at office) and Beat 9 (Archive Room research). All existing beats expanded with character-dwell dialogue options for breathing-room. **Halina Sikorska** is the chapter-1 client; the case is a wrongful eviction grounded in a landlord-orchestrated address renumbering. The English-version dialogue convention applies (only proper names of people and places appear in Polish; greetings, address forms, and idioms are English). Phase-7 voice work for the new beats lives in V1.4 voice pack (Stage 7 of the rewrite).

The core loop is:

```text
arrive on Office Street
→ enter Pig & Swine, meet Asia
→ Mr. Pig reveals firm-broke + Halina-eviction-Friday dual stakes
→ find Murrow, receive eviction briefing, collect Procedural Binder
→ recruit Crab (pitch is the eviction case)
→ walk to Café Paragraf
→ recruit Whimsy (Rights Memo + coffee tutorial as gate)
→ client meeting at office with Halina (three-stance dialogue, bonus evidence, fee, cardiologist plant)
→ Archive Room research (Murrow narrates Article 135-bis § 2 KPC)
→ court readiness check
→ walk to District Court
→ win first legal battle (procedural reprieve, three rounds)
→ return to office (firm-survival celebration, ledger update, fee referenced as collected)
→ receive Swine postcard
```

The chapter should teach:

```text
movement
interacting with signs and objects
talking to NPCs
repeatable hint dialogue
quest updates
evidence collection (required + bonus tier)
NPC recruitment
client meeting with stance-driven dialogue choices
case theory crystallization through legal research
court choices with stance-aware argument options
chapter completion
```

The legal spine is:

> A defective procedural step prevented the client from being properly heard. The remedy is not total victory, but a fair procedural reset.

The comedy spine is:

> Pig & Swine is on the edge of collapse, but somehow this ridiculous firm is the only thing standing between a client and procedural doom.

The emotional arc is:

```text
arrival → confusion → panic → investigation → client meeting → research → teamwork → court competence → fragile survival
```

**(Phase-8 polish — Beat 2 first-encounter UI rules.)** Two implementation rules captured from Chapter 1 playtest:

1. **Interact-prompt z-order rule (project-wide).** The `[E]` interact prompt must NOT occlude NPC sprites. Acceptable z-orders: (a) prompt rendered above the NPC's head with a small fixed offset; (b) prompt rendered at the player's feet (below the NPC's hitbox). Recommended: option (a) — anchored to the NPC's sprite top with a 4-8 pixel upward offset, rendered on a dedicated UI layer above all world sprites but below modal dialogue overlays. The desk furniture in particular must NEVER be the rendering anchor for the prompt; the NPC sprite is the anchor. Phase-5 implementation (Godot side) specifies the exact prompt node hierarchy and z-index assignment.

2. **Asia behind-counter behavior.** If the player crosses behind Asia's reception counter without having pressed E on Asia first, Asia automatically initiates dialogue with a brief "this area is for staff" line, then immediately recognizes Cula and bridges into the standard first-meeting dialogue *as if E had been pressed*. The brief preamble protects the office's professional surface (a real receptionist would not let a stranger walk into the staff area silently); the recognition transition keeps the first-meeting dialogue intact regardless of how the player approached.

   Sample preamble + recognition shape (phase-7 V1.1 voice work specifies exact text):

   > *"I apologise, only employees are allowed in this — oh. Of course, Dr. Cula, please —"*

   The first half is warm-direct register (apology + boundary). The second half (after the dash) bridges into the standard first-meeting dialogue tree without skipping any lines. Phase-7 V1.1 voice pack must add this line to Asia's first-encounter set; the dialogue tree must support both entry paths (E-press front-of-counter and behind-counter trigger) flowing into the same first-meeting branch.

   Quest flag `chapter1.met_asia` is set the same way regardless of entry path.

Asia should be a permanent office NPC and one of the most useful support characters in the game.

She is not a lawyer and not a recruitable court ally. She is the firm's massively overworked secretary: warm, practical, endlessly helpful, and somehow the only person in the office who still understands where things are.

### Beat 3 — Mr. Pig panic — firm-broke + eviction-Friday dual stakes

After Asia points the player toward him, Mr. Pig explains the crisis. **(Phase-8 expansion.)** Beat 3 now establishes *both* stakes: the firm's financial precarity (the rent claim) AND Halina Sikorska's eviction-Friday deadline. **(Phase-8 polish — Mr. Pig idle proximity trigger.)** Beat 3's first-encounter trigger has two paths:

1. **Standard E-press path.** Player presses E on Mr. Pig; first-meeting dialogue begins.
2. **Idle proximity path.** If the player stands within Mr. Pig's interaction range for **3 seconds** without pressing E, Mr. Pig automatically initiates the first-meeting dialogue — his panic register doesn't tolerate silent proximity from a junior. **This trigger is active ONLY before the first meeting** (i.e., before `chapter1.pig_revealed_crisis` is set). After the first meeting, the trigger is disabled; subsequent approaches require an explicit E-press.

Phase-7 V1.1 voice work decides whether the auto-trigger uses the same opening line as the E-press path or includes a brief preamble acknowledging that the player didn't initiate. **Recommended: the same opening line.** Pig's panic register is already self-initiating; he wouldn't notice he wasn't asked. A small "Cula!" or "There you are!" preamble is acceptable but optional — phase-7 V1.1 specifies.

The 3-second timer resets if the player walks away from Mr. Pig's interaction range; it does NOT carry over across re-approaches.

Quest flag `chapter1.pig_revealed_crisis` is set the same way regardless of entry path.

### Beat 4 — Find Murrow — eviction briefing + Procedural Binder pickup

Murrow functions as the first legal tutorial mentor.

He should explain the case in practical terms, not legal lecture terms.

**(Phase-8 expansion — substantive eviction briefing.)** The original spec had Murrow give an abstract case summary. The post-playtest rewrite has Murrow lay out specifics so the player can feel the case before recruiting allies. Substantive content additions:

- The client is named: **Halina Sikorska**, 71-year-old retired Polish-language teacher, tenant in a pre-war apartment building since 1987.
- The wrong-address mechanism is named: the 2015 landlord-orchestrated renumbering (Halina's flat went from #7 to #5); the eviction notice was served to the *current* #7 (a different renter); Halina got it three weeks late, after the ex parte hearing.
- The 14-day window is named: actual-notice clock starts from when Halina received the misdirected notice, not from original service.
- The motion-to-set-aside is named as the procedural target.
- **The fact that Halina has not yet formally retained the firm is named** — sets up Beat 8 client meeting where the formal retention happens.

He tells Dr. A. Cula:

```text
The client (Halina Sikorska) was affected by defective service.
Her landlord renumbered the building in 2015. Her flat went from #7 to #5.
The eviction notice was served to the current occupant of old #7 — not to her.
She got the notice three weeks late, after the ex parte hearing.
The motion-to-set-aside window is fourteen days from her actual notice. We are inside it, but barely.
The court hearing for the motion is today.
The firm needs two things before court:
1. the procedural binder;
2. the rights memo.
The client herself comes to the office later for the formal meeting (Beat 8).
```

**Canonical address-form beat (mandatory; see godot/AGENTS.md §Address forms in dialogue):** during this first conversation, Cula opens with "Mr. Murrow" — he is a new junior and uses the honorific. Mid-conversation, Murrow explicitly invites informality with a line of the shape *"It is Murrow, to friends. The 'Mister' I keep for invoices."* From this beat forward, Cula says "Murrow". Asia is *not* extended the same invitation in the same scene — Murrow hedges, with a line of the shape *"I am still deciding whether reception staff qualify as friends or as workplace constellations."* Asia keeps "Mr. Murrow" thereafter and may reference the unresolved status as a running joke. This beat is required: it bootstraps the Cula↔Murrow first-name terms that the rest of the game depends on, and it seeds an Asia↔Murrow comic dynamic that recurs into Chapter 6.

### Beat 8 — Client meeting at office (NEW; phase-8 addition)

After Whimsy is recruited (and the optional coffee tutorial), the team returns to Pig & Swine for the formal client meeting with **Halina Sikorska**. This is the chapter's largest single beat — estimated 8–12 minutes of player time.

**Setup.** Asia greets Cula, Crab, and Whimsy on their return and announces that Halina has arrived. The meeting takes place in the office's dedicated **meeting room** — a separate interior space with a big table that seats the team and the client. Cula, Crab, and Whimsy enter the meeting room when Halina is announced; the entry is its own brief staging beat (the contrast between the chaotic open office and the formal meeting room registers visually, without commentary). Murrow is present at the table and may briefly intervene. Mr. Pig is briefly present (two lines, panic-register, inappropriate to the room but not catastrophically so), then withdraws — Murrow shepherds him out gently.

**Halina's arrival staging.** She enters with a folder of documents she has prepared (the player should see this folder; it telegraphs her precision). She is dressed as a teacher of her generation — modest, considered, unfashionable in a way that reads as dignified. She greets the team formally; uses "Mr." / "Ms." until invited otherwise. Recommended: she remains "Mrs. Sikorska" through Ch1; first-name basis is a Ch4 possibility if any.

**Three load-bearing dialogue stances** (Cula's interview register). Each stance is a *carrier* — Cula's questions throughout the meeting take on the stance's register. The stance is a tone, not a script.

```text
sympathetic     — Cula leads with care
blunt-procedural — Cula leads with sequence
technical       — Cula leads with documents
```

**Required evidence (surfaces regardless of stance):**

```text
Halina's current tenancy paperwork
the defective eviction notice (addressed to old #7)
the court docket showing the ex parte hearing
```

**Bonus evidence by stance:**

```text
Sympathetic stance:
  Mrs. Wójcik's witness statement
  (second-floor neighbor, resident since 1991, will testify Halina has been a continuous resident).

Blunt-procedural stance:
  the postal return-to-sender slip
  (from a previous landlord letter six months earlier that also went to old #7;
   proves the landlord knew the address was bad).

Technical stance:
  the 1962 original lease + the 1987 inheritance paperwork
  (lease in Halina's father's name; strengthens underlying tenancy validity).
```

Required evidence is collected during the meeting regardless of stance. Bonus evidence requires the matching stance carrier; in court (Beat 12), bonus evidence surfaces additional argument options.

**Cardiologist plant.** Lands once during the meeting, in any stance. One sentence. Framed neutrally — Halina mentions she had to reschedule a cardiology follow-up because of the eviction stress, but it's nothing, she's been managing this for years. **No italics, no clutched chest, no Cula reaction line.** The plant pays off in the Ch4 corridor sighting (β reappearance per `bibles/halina_sikorska.md`). Recommended placement: during the technical-stance documents discussion, where it lands as a logistical aside rather than as a confidence. Phase-7 V1.4 voice work specifies exact text and placement.

**Fee discussion.** Murrow or Cula raises the fee. Halina has calculated; she names what she can afford (5,000 PLN); the firm accepts. **Not a player choice** (would open haggling questions that complicate the spine). Cula's *internal reaction* may vary by stance (character-dwell texture, not load-bearing branching).

**Halina's literary epigram (available in any stance):** *"You go to a lawyer like you go to a doctor: too late."* Lands as wisdom on first read. Recolors at Ch4 corridor sighting because by then both halves of the metaphor have come true. Phase-7 voice work specifies placement; recommended placement is late in the meeting, as Halina is gathering her folder to leave.

**Crab's reaction lines during Beat 8.** Scenting-leverage-professional register through Halina's presence. He registers her case as professionally tractable — not sympathy, not coldness, just professional interest in the case's structural features. One or two lines tied to specific evidence she presents.

**Whimsy's reaction lines during Beat 8.** Theatrical-cynic register through Halina's presence. He engages with the rhetorical possibilities of her case. **Critical phase-7 audit point:** Whimsy's flourish must address the **case**, not the **person**. Halina herself receives a brief courteous acknowledgment in his lower (protective-distancing) register. If a phase-7 draft of Whimsy's Beat 8 line punches down at Halina, it has failed the audit.

**Murrow's intervention lines.** Dry-collegial register. If a stance line goes somewhere unproductive, Murrow redirects with a single line. Sample shape: *"The notice."* (returns the conversation to the document).

**Character-dwell options for Cula with Halina (within stance carrier).** Recommended option set:

  — past tense; she taught Polish language and literature for 38 years;

### Beat 9 — Archive Room research (NEW; phase-8 addition)

After Halina leaves, Cula and Murrow walk to the Archive Room. Murrow opens the Procedural Binder to the relevant section. The Archive Room visit is functional — they are here to apply the rule to Halina's specific facts.

**Article 135-bis § 2 KPC (fabricated; project-canonical text):**

> **Art. 135-bis § 2 KPC.** Service is invalid when the address used has been altered, renumbered, or otherwise made non-current to the knowledge of the serving party. A hearing held upon invalid service shall, on motion of the party not properly served, be set aside and rescheduled, provided the motion is filed within fourteen days of actual notice. The appearance of a third party at the served address shall not be considered as curing service unless that party held the express authority of the served party.

**Project convention on article numbering.** Real KPC article numbers may be cited where their real content fits the case (e.g., Crab's Article 132 reference in V1.3 stands). Fabricated numbers use the -bis suffix and sit plausibly near real KPC topical neighbors. Article 135-bis § 2 sits next to real KPC Art. 135 (residence service). This convention applies forward to Ch2–5 legal texture.

# Chapter 2: "The Eviction Defense"

Chapter 2 is the first proper case chapter.

Chapter 1 was about saving Pig & Swine. Chapter 2 is about saving someone's home.

It should feel like the world has opened up: the player is no longer just walking between the office, café, archive, and court. They now leave the starter-town corridor and enter **Tenants' Row / the Residential District**.

The core loop is:

```text
start in Pig & Swine Office
→ Waldek arrives with eviction papers
→ Asia points player toward next step
→ Murrow frames the legal issues
→ visit Waldek's Apartment
→ inspect poster and eviction notice
→ interview Kowalski
→ interview Zielinska
→ visit Landlord's Office
→ obtain Building Rules Extract
→ play or survive Document Chase
→ return to office
→ prepare case on Evidence Board
→ walk to Housing Court
→ defeat Attorney Grzyb
→ save Waldek's home
→ return to office / apartment payoff
→ Chapter 4 hook appears
```

The chapter should teach:

```text
field investigation
client intake
witness interviews
multi-location quest progression
evidence classification
Evidence Board preparation
soft-fail mini-game outcomes
adversarial court argument
proportionality reasoning
victory grades
persistent world changes
```

The legal spine is:

> Eviction is not a mechanical response to a rule breach. Where someone's home, expression, notice rights, and individual circumstances are at stake, the decision must be procedurally fair and proportionate.

The comedy spine is:

> The landlord believes a poster is the first stage of constitutional collapse.

The emotional arc is:

```text
office recovery → client panic → home vulnerability → factual doubt → targeted enforcement → structured preparation → courtroom pressure → relief → public ripple
```

Chapter 2 should be longer than Chapter 1 but still clean.

Target length:

```text
First playthrough: 40–55 minutes
Replay: 22–30 minutes
```

No grinding. No random encounters. No hidden-pixel evidence. Every required clue should be signposted by Asia, Murrow, the quest log, or an NPC.

### Plotek Case File

> A retainer engagement and supporting documents from a Mr. Plotek, currently in pre-trial detention (areszt śledczy). Charges: tax fraud (Art. 56 KKS), money laundering (Art. 299 KK), influence peddling (Art. 230 KK). The retainer is for a procedural-defense scope only — specifically, the firm is engaged to draft and file a warrant-suppression motion. The search warrant that authorized the seizure of Plotek's offices was issued under expedited protocol with a vague scope clause and signed by a duty judge who did not have the case file. The procedural defects are real.

Mr. Pig's framing line — Phase 7 voice work writes the principle-acceptance line. **(Phase-6 priority: the framing must read as sincere and abstract — Pig genuinely believes in the principle and has not thought about what working the case will feel like.)** Recommended shape: *"The warrant is the warrant. Procedural protections do not check whom they protect. We file the motion."*

The juniors react briefly:
- Cula: silent acknowledgment. Receives the assignment. Per the white-collar-client bible writing rule (professionalism through discomfort).
- Crab: opportunist register. Recommended phase-7 shape: a brief observation about the warrant's defects being real and the case being legally interesting. He sees the case as good portfolio work. **(This professional engagement will make Cula uncomfortable on retraversal.)**
- Whimsy: cynic register. Recommended phase-7 shape: a theatrical flourish about *"defending the indefensible on principle, which is what principles are for."* Bitter accuracy.

Mr. Pig assigns the warrant motion to Cula and Murrow. Cula will visit Plotek in detention for the procedural intake (Beat 8.5); Murrow will draft the motion.

The chapter's main arc resumes immediately. Mr. Pig pivots back to Waldek.

# Beat 8.5 — Plotek first detention visit (PHASE-6 NEW BEAT)

**(Phase-6 addition; Hits #6 + #7 staging applied.)** Inserted between Beat 8 (Landlord's Office) and Beat 9 (Document Chase). A short cutscene-shaped scene; no new mechanics. ~3-4 minutes of in-game time.

### Setting

- **Location:** detention facility (*areszt śledczy*) meeting room. Plain. A table; two chairs across from a third; a small interview window. Cutscene-shaped — no exploration.
- **Time:** late afternoon, between the Landlord's Office investigation and the office return for Document Chase.
- **Personnel present:** Cula, **Murrow** (silent training presence — phase-6 Hit #6 revision), and Plotek. Murrow accompanies Cula on this first visit; he does not participate in the conversation. Plotek's chapter-3 second visit (see chapter 4) Cula goes alone, having earned the responsibility.

### Beat structure

Approximately 3 minutes of in-game time.

1. **Cula and Murrow enter.** Plotek is already seated. He stands politely as they enter. Phase 7 voice work: Plotek's first words are warm, courteous greeting in formal Polish.

2. **Procedural review.** Cula opens the warrant motion paperwork. Murrow places case files on the table beside Cula's stack but does not speak. Phase 7 voice work for Cula: professional, brief, slightly clipped. He explains the motion's structure. Plotek follows, asking precise procedural questions. The conversation is genuinely about the warrant.

3. **Legal-landscape access.** **(Phase-6 Hit #7 revision: chapter 2 shows legal-landscape access only; the biographical mention moves to chapter 4 second visit.)** Mid-conversation, Plotek demonstrates unusual depth of reading on the warrant motion's likely outcome. He cites a recent procedural decision the firm has not yet engaged with, or anticipates a counter-argument the prosecutor is likely to raise, with specificity that suggests he has been following the law more carefully than a typical defendant would. The depth is courteous, professional, and — to Cula and Murrow — unsettling.

4. **Cula's response.** Cula registers the depth. Cula answers the procedural question. Murrow's silent presence at the table absorbs some of the weight. Phase 7 voice work: the answer is precise, slightly more formal than usual, no acknowledgment of the unsettling depth.

5. **The visit ends.** Cula and Murrow leave with the warrant motion paperwork ready. Phase 7 voice work: Plotek's exit line is warm, courteous, looks forward to the motion's filing. Cula's exit line is brief.

# Chapter 3: "The Assigned Case"

**(Phase-8 post-playtest expansion — see `narrative_revision/audit/chapter_3.md` and `narrative_revision/beats/chapter_3.md`.)** Chapter 3 is the compact criminal-defense chapter, inserted between Chapter 2 (Waldek's eviction defense — the firm's clean win) and Chapter 4 (the printer conspiracy / inflection chapter). The chapter introduces criminal-defense register to the spine via an ex officio appointment forced on the firm. The client is **Kacper Mazurek**, a homeless 19-year-old facing burglary charges (*kradzież z włamaniem*, KK Art. 279). The firm wins a suspended sentence (1 year imprisonment, suspended for 3 years' probation under KK Art. 70 § 2 *młodociany*). The win is partially Pyrrhic — keeping Kacper out of prison sends him back to the homelessness he was trying to escape. The chapter extends the β technique introduced in Chapter 1 (Halina's Ch4 corridor sighting) into a different legal mode; Kacper reappears in Chapter 6 finale as a corridor cameo on a new charge.

**Compact discipline:** target 25-30 minutes runtime; six beats; single new interior (detention center visitor room); no world expansion at the city level.

The core loop is:

```text
office anger (Mr. Pig fury at ex officio assignment + Asia operational briefing)
→ walk to detention center
→ meet Kacper at detention; the "2 years easy" pivot
→ return to office; case prep with Crab; Whimsy absent (working Plotek's case)
→ court hearing (three-phase mechanic)
→ office return; ambiguous aftermath; no celebration
→ printer-stinger (sets up Chapter 4)
```

The chapter should teach:

```text
ex officio appointment mechanics
detention visitation
criminal-defense register
phased criminal hearing (three sub-phases as Casebook adaptation)
suspended sentence as outcome
ambiguous-win pacing (the player notices the win is partial)
character development in negative space (Whimsy's absence)
```

The legal spine is:

> The win that keeps the client out of prison sends him back to the place he committed the crime to escape.

The comedy spine is restrained. Mr. Pig's fury at the office is funny. Asia's deadpan delivery of the rate structure is funny. The detention visit and hearing are not comic territory. The chapter's primary register is *measured-and-grim* with comic relief at the office bookends — opposite to Chapter 2's register (warmth + bureaucratic absurdism in roughly equal measure).

The emotional arc is:

```text
office anger → bureaucratic resignation → uneasy approach → encounter (the "2 years easy" pivot)
→ procedural prep → court pressure → procedural win → ambiguous aftermath
```

This inverts Chapter 2's arc (which climbs from client panic to public ripple). Chapter 3 descends from office anger to ambiguous aftermath. The descent prepares the Chapter 4 inflection.

**English-version dialogue convention applies.** Polish only for proper names of people and places (Kacper, Sikorska, Plotek, etc.) and for PLN currency. Greetings, address forms, and idioms are English. Per the convention established in Chapter 1's audit_v2.

Three load-bearing dialogue stances (Cula's interview register — analogous to Chapter 1 Beat 8 stance carriers, adapted for criminal defense):

- In sympathetic carrier: the flatness of Kacper's line registers against the care of Cula's framing.
- In procedural carrier: Kacper responds the same way; Cula registers the response as a fact.

## Phase 1 — Contest the *włamanie* element

Argument: was it actually breaking? The lock was demonstrably broken before Kacper arrived. Polish doctrine on *kradzież z włamaniem* requires breaking of an entry barrier; if the barrier was already non-functional, the case should arguably reduce to *kradzież szczególnie zuchwała* (Art. 278 § 3a) — significantly lower sentence range.

```text
Best evidence: lock condition photo + patrol officer testimony
Best ally: Crab (procedural angle)
Argument tag: breaking_element_dispute
```

The judge's reaction: **measured-skeptical**. He notes the doctrinal point but reserves it for sentencing rather than charge reduction. Sample judge line shape (phase-7 specifies):

## Phase 3 — Argue for suspended sentence

Argument: statutory criteria for *warunkowe zawieszenie wykonania kary*.

**Polish criminal-law specifics (post-2015 reform):**

```text
KK Art. 69 § 1 — caps suspendable imprisonment at one year (not exceeding)
KK Art. 70 § 2 — for młodociany (offender under 21 at offense, under 24 at trial), probation period 2-5 years
KK Art. 279 § 1 — kradzież z włamaniem statutory range: 1-10 years
```

The two constraints converge: the statutory minimum for Art. 279 § 1 (1 year) is also the maximum suspendable under Art. 69 § 1 (1 year). The recommended outcome is exactly 1 year imprisonment, suspended for 3 years' probation under Art. 70 § 2 *młodociany* (Kacper at 19 fits).

```text
Best evidence: prior record (none) + Phase 2 contextualization + Kacper's age + first-offender status
Best ally: Cula leads alone; Murrow's supervisory presence at peak weight (procedural credibility supports application)
Argument tag: suspended_sentence_criteria
```

The judge's reaction: ruling. After brief deliberation (phase-7 specifies whether on-screen or implied via cut), the judge announces:

```text
Verdict:
Conviction for kradzież z włamaniem (KK Art. 279 § 1).
Sentence: 1 year imprisonment (statutory minimum for Art. 279 § 1; also maximum suspendable under Art. 69 § 1).
Suspension: 3 years' probation (KK Art. 70 § 2, młodociany).
Conditions: supervised contact with court probation officer.
```

# Beat 11 — Urgent filing preparation — INFLECTION BEAT (PHASE-4 + PHASE-6 REVISION)

Back upstairs in Pig & Swine Office, the team prepares the filing.

This should be short, tense, and funny — and contains the chapter-3 inflection moment, the spine's load-bearing pivot.

### Setting

- **Time:** late evening. One of the canonical nocturnal beats per `style_canon.txt §8`.
- **Location:** Pig & Swine Office, work area near Murrow's desk.
- **Personnel present:** Cula, Crab, Whimsy, Murrow (briefly absent — see step 2 below), Mr. Pig (in his corner having a maritime crisis), Asia (on the phone with the courier service), Bajtek (assembling the technical statement).
- **Mood:** urgency under fatigue.
- **Lighting:** lamps on. Coffee machine has been unplugged (Asia's second unplugging this year — `style_canon.txt §3`).

### Beat structure (the inflection)

The filing-prep scene runs ~2-3 minutes of in-game time:

1. **Setup.** Murrow announces what's needed for the filing: case file, technical statement, contradicting-Szpon evidence, plus a stamp from reception. Cula assigned to retrieve a specific procedural notice from the case file cabinet. Murrow himself is working from the ledger because he needs to confirm the firm can pay Bajtek's invoice on time — the technical statement requires a formal engagement record. Mr. Pig delivers his **third "back in my day" lecture** here (per Pig bible). Recommended phase-7 shape: a maritime-tinged invocation about how at Cula's age the toner was a privilege ("we rationed magenta by the page"). Cula receives the lecture without comment. The lecture lands ironically after the inflection but Cula doesn't speak to it.

2. **Murrow is interrupted mid-update** (Phase-6 Hit #8 revision — replaces original "Murrow steps out for a stamp"). The phone rings — Asia is on the other line transferring a courier callback that needs Murrow's signature confirmation. Murrow says something brief and short-declarative: *"One moment."* He steps out of the work area to take the call at reception. **He does not close the ledger because he intends to return immediately.** The interruption is in-character — Murrow responds to procedural needs.

3. **Mr. Pig in his corner.** Maritime metaphor at peak. Phase 7 voice work writes a specific Pig line about toner costs. Recommended shape: *"The toner alone is taking on water; we are bailing with thimbles."* The line is sincere; the line will recolor against what Cula is about to see.

4. **Cula moves to the case file cabinet.** Crab and Whimsy are on the other side of the work area, assembling the technical exhibits. Cula crosses near Murrow's desk to reach the cabinet. **The ledger is open in Cula's path of natural movement.** The page is visible.

5. **THE GLIMPSE.** Cula's eye catches the page for the moment it takes to walk past it. **What Cula sees:** an entry near the top of the visible page reading **"Konto operacyjne — saldo bieżące: 285 400 PLN"** (operating account — current balance: 285,400 PLN). Below it, the line has been carrying steady values for the previous several months (small fluctuations within tens of thousands; no recent inflow that would explain it; the reserve has been there). The handwriting is Murrow's, careful and consistent across months. **The number contradicts what Mr. Pig has just said about toner.** The number is also approximately one full year of doubled junior salaries (the juniors are at ~60,000 PLN minimum-wage; four staff including Asia ≈ 240k annual entry-level wage burden; 285,400 PLN = ~five years of Bajtek-invoice-scale expenses sitting unused, or one full year of doubled junior salaries). Cula does not consciously calculate this; the player may feel it.

6. **The thought is shelved.** Cula does not stop. Cula does not look longer. Cula does not look at Mr. Pig. Cula takes the procedural notice from the case file cabinet and continues toward the team's working area. The urgent filing pulls focus; the thought is shelved; the player feels it land.

7. **Murrow returns from the call** (Phase-6 Hit #10 + #20 revision — single-sided acknowledgment). He carries the courier-confirmation receipt. He sees Cula at the cabinet. **He glances at his own desk and registers that the ledger is still open — a half-second observation, internal to him.** He does not look at Cula; Cula does not look at him. The moment is single-sided: Murrow notices that someone has been past his desk while the ledger was visible. He files the observation. He picks up the stamp from his desk, places it back, and resumes his work. Cula returns to the team.

8. **Filing assembly.** The team finishes assembling. Phase 7 voice work specifies short urgent-filing-prep dialogue for all four (Cula, Crab, Whimsy, Murrow). The dialogue is about the case, not about what just happened.

### Phase-4 staging notes

- **No music sting.** The inflection has no audio cue.
- **No camera move.** The visual is normal staging.
- **No flag-set notification.** A flag is set internally (`chapter4.glimpsed_ledger_anomaly = true`) but no UI surfaces it.
- **The ledger entry remains visible after Murrow returns.** Murrow does not close it. Re-approaching Murrow's desk produces no additional dialogue. The number is what is there.

- **Personnel:** Crab and Whimsy in the office. **Mr. Pig is asleep at his desk.** Murrow is at the courier dispatch overseeing the delivery. Asia is at reception finalizing courier paperwork.
- **Mood:** depleted relief.

### Revised beat content

Crab and Whimsy at the coffee corner. The conditions are stated plainly: they are doing all the actual lawyering, on no sleep, with the firm's printer literally being sabotaged by a rival, and the only payment so far this month has been a postcard from Mr. Swine. **The framing carries; the inflection has happened earlier but does not feature in this conversation.** The juniors found STUB because they are tired and unpaid this month. **Phase 7 voice work must not have any character reference the chapter-3 inflection in this scene.**

Cula joins via phone — a brief call from Crab or Whimsy. Phase 7 voice work writes the call: short, dry, an invitation to sign the napkin tomorrow morning when Cula returns from the courthouse. Cula agrees. The call ends. Cula's signature happens off-screen and the napkin is signed for him later.

In the resulting scene at the office coffee corner, Crab and Whimsy found **STUB** — the **S**olidarity **T**rade **U**nion of **B**arristers — with Cula as a remote third founder. The acronym is deliberate: the union is tiny, like a stub, and the name nods to *Solidarność* in the way the entire game's Polish-legal flavor nods to real institutions. Murrow is not invited; he is the institutional spine and would file for management if asked. Asia is at reception within earshot, is offered membership, and declines on grounds that "front-desk staff have negotiating power that lawyers cannot match — I have the password to the photocopier."

The scene is brief — two or three exchanges between Crab and Whimsy, one phone call to Cula, a hand-drawn STUB logo on a coffee napkin (small drawn pig with tiny gavel, S-T-U-B beneath; drawn by Whimsy) that gets pinned to the office cork board. No mechanical effect this chapter; STUB is a comic-canonical fact from this point on. Murrow finds the napkin in Beat 15 and files it under "personal effects, see also: morale" without comment.

Polish translation note: in the Polish-language version, STUB is rendered as **PUTKA** — *Pakt Uboczny Trzech Krytycznych Adwokatów*. "Putka" in Polish is a small box / kennel; the diminutive joke survives the translation.

- A formal STUB notice pinned to the office cork board with typo-ridden expansion of the acronym.

Do not over-mine the joke. Two or three callbacks across the rest of the game; not a recurring punchline.

### Beat structure (phase-4 specified)

1. **Initial relief.** Brief celebration of the arbitration win. Phase 4 follows the chapter-1 Beat 11 firm-survival pattern: Mr. Pig celebrates the firm's continued survival; the win for the firm-as-cause is mentioned secondarily. **Pattern is now four chapters strong.**

2. **The unaddressed thing.** The Pro-Bono Stub from Beat 8 has not been spoken about during the arbitration prep or the hearing. The team has been focused on the arbitration. Now, with the win secured, the unaddressed thing surfaces.

   - **Swine attempts to explain why he didn't tell Mr. Pig. The attempt FAILS** (per Swine bible). He gets close, loses it, reverts to cheerful evasion. The failure is the texture. Recommended shape: a sentence that begins to articulate ("I thought, personally, that — ") and then trails into a cosmopolitan name-drop or a deflection ("well. The Sapporo light is very different in winter. You would not believe it."). The deflection is specific to Swine; the question of why-not-told sits in the silence.
   - Mr. Pig's reaction. **Phase 7 voice work, high-priority.** Per Pig bible: genuine emotion (admiration, conflict, possibly resentment), no self-redemption, no diagnosis-of-the-firm. The line might be something simple, sincere, half-finished — the same way Swine's attempt failed, Pig's response also doesn't quite land. Both founders unable to articulate.

5. **Cula's behavior** (phase-6 Hit #15 revision: NO menu choice). Phase 4 originally placed a three-option dialogue menu here; phase 6 removed it. **Cula does not speak unless his accumulated player-flag profile triggers it (per the chapter-5 branch resolution).** Cula's restraint is the writing-discipline; the player's accumulated behavior across the chapter shapes the resolution.
