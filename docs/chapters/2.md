# Chapter 2 — "The Eviction Defense"

**Sprint 1 input.** First real case chapter. Less tutorial, more investigation, evidence management, and adversarial litigation.

**Source-of-truth reference:** `implementation_plan.md` lines 523–1478.

## Goal

Teach the player that Pig & Swine is becoming a strange but serious human-rights law firm. Chapter 1 was about saving the firm. Chapter 2 is about saving someone's home.

## Core loop

client intake → field investigation → witness interviews → evidence board → mini-game recovery → housing court → victory with comic aftershock.

## Legal spine

> An eviction cannot be treated as a mechanical property dispute where the tenant's expressive conduct, proportionality, notice, and home-life consequences are ignored.

## Comedy spine

> The landlord believes posters are the first stage of constitutional collapse.

## Items / evidence

Required (3 core items):

1. **Eviction Notice** — found in Nowak's apartment. "Formal, severe, and suspiciously enthusiastic. Alleges repeated disturbance but gives no concrete dates." Unlocks `notice_problem` argument slot.
2. **Building Rules Extract** — found in Landlord's Office. "Forbids 'visual agitation, façade disharmony, and other disturbances as determined by management.' Legally, this sentence is wearing a fake moustache." Unlocks `rule_problem` slot.
3. **Neighbor Statement: No Disturbance** OR **Recovered Complaint Page** (mini-game outcome) — Unlocks `factual_problem` slot.

Optional (depth):

4. **Poster Photo** — strengthens `proportionality_problem` slot. "Shows poster is harmless / expressive."
5. **Neighbor Statement: Targeted Enforcement** — strengthens proportionality / arbitrariness angle.

## Evidence Board mechanic (new system this chapter)

Pre-court preparation screen. Player assigns evidence cards to argument slots.

Argument slots: `notice_problem`, `rule_problem`, `factual_problem`, `proportionality_problem`.

Scoring:

- 0–1 correct slots: shaky case, court warnings, recoverable
- 2–3 correct slots: normal path
- 4 correct slots: strong path, judge praise variant, badge variant

Interaction: select-evidence → select-slot. Keyboard/controller-friendly. Drag-and-drop is later visual polish.

## Mini-game (new this chapter)

**Document Chase: Stampede of Evidence.** Triggered when player learns from Zielińska that a missing complaint page may exist. Papers fly out a window during inspection.

- Move crosshair, stamp flying papers, avoid junk papers.
- Win: stamp ≥10 of 15 relevant pages in 30s → **Recovered Complaint Page** (full).
- Fail: <10 → **Damaged Complaint Page** (partial; medium argument).
- No page at all: forces reliance on neighbor testimony only.

Failure does not block progress. Court effect is graded.

## Quest steps

1. **Client intake** in office. Receive Eviction Notice, Nowak apartment key.
2. **Visit Nowak's Apartment.** Inspect poster, window, letters.
3. **Interview Kowalski.** He complains about everything; eventually gives the photo-repeatedly clue.
4. **Interview Zielińska.** She gives targeted-enforcement testimony and mentions missing papers.
5. **Visit Landlord's Office.** Obtain Building Rules Extract.
6. **Document Chase mini-game** triggered during landlord visit or follow-up.
7. **Evidence Board preparation** in office.
8. **Housing Court — three rounds.**
9. **Payoff** with comic aftershock.

## Court structure (3 rounds)

| Round | Question | Correct argument |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Was there a valid basis for eviction? | The basis (vague rule + fabricated complaint record) is unsound |
| 2 | Was there actual disturbance or harm? | The factual record is unreliable; no genuine disturbance |
| 3 | Was eviction proportionate? | Even if some technical breach existed, eviction is disproportionate to a poster |

Three victory variants:

- **Strong** (4 correct slots + complete evidence): judge praise, "Poster Defender" badge variant.
- **Standard** (2–3 slots): normal victory.
- **Weak** (0–1 slots, damaged page): court reset/adjournment, not full vindication, but Nowak keeps her home.
