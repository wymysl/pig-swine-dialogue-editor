## Goal

Chapter 1 is the polished playable prologue. Teach the whole basic game loop in 20–30 minutes:

> explore → talk → collect evidence → recruit help → prepare argument → go to court → win a ridiculous but legally coherent case.

By the end, the player should understand: who Dr. A. Kula is, why Pig & Swine matters, how legal investigation works, why the comedy has a legal spine.

## Core loop

confusion → panic → investigation → improvised teamwork → courtroom competence → fragile survival.

## Legal spine

> A defective procedural step (defective service) undermined the client's right to a fair hearing. The remedy is procedural reset, not substantive judgment.

## Comedy spine

> Pig & Swine is broke, panicked, and somehow the right people for the job.

## Trigger / preconditions

New game start. No prior state.

## Quest steps

1. **Cold open** — outside office. Objective: "First day. New suit. Old debt. Enter Pig & Swine."
2. **Mr. Pig reveals crisis.** Receive main quest: "Save Pig & Swine from immediate financial extinction."
3. **Find Muraś.** Receive sub-quest: "Find the law binder and the rights memo."
4. **Collect law binder.** Unlocks Defective service argument.
5. **Recruit Rak** (show binder or clue). Stronger Defective service argument.
6. **Find rights memo.** Unlocks Fair hearing argument.
7. **Recruit Wymysl** (show memo or clue). Stronger Fair hearing argument.
8. **Optional coffee mini-game.** Coffee buff. Failure is comic only.
9. **Court preparation checkpoint** with Muraś or Mr. Pig.
10. **Court — three rounds.**
11. **Payoff scene + Swine postcard.** Badge: Day-One Survivor.

## Court structure (3 rounds)

| Round | Question | Correct answer | Required support | Bad-but-funny options |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Why did the client fail to appear/respond? | Defective service | law binder + Rak | "Spiritually present" / "Envelope looked guilty" / "Deadline was emotionally unreasonable" |
| 2 | Why does that matter legally? | Defective service undermined fair hearing | rights memo + Wymysl | (write 3 funny-wrong; do not signpost) |
| 3 | What should the court do? | Adjourn / reopen / allow proper participation | (consequence of 1+2) | (write 3 funny-wrong) |

Judge final line: "Against several expectations, counsel has made a legally coherent submission."

## Payoff scene

Back at office:

- Mr. Pig celebrates too early.
- Muraś quietly updates the ledger.
- Rak pretends not to be impressed.
- Wymysl proposes turning the judgment into a musical.
- The coffee machine makes an ominous sound.
- Mr. Swine's postcard arrives:
  > Greetings from Japan. Excellent news: I have discovered a business opportunity involving ski resorts, arbitration, and no immediate downside. Please keep the firm solvent until I return. Yours evasively, Swine.

Badge: **Day-One Survivor**. Set `chapter1.complete = true` and `currentChapter = 2`.

## Acceptance criteria

A new player can start a new game and complete the full loop without external explanation. Every blocked step has an in-game hint. The player exits Chapter 1 understanding the four pillars (Kula, the firm, investigation mechanics, the legal-spine joke).
