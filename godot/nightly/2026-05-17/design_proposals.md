## Proposal 2: Author Beat 9 — Murrow archive narration under the motion-packet model

**Type:** DIALOGUE
**Addresses:** `story.txt` §Beat 9 — Archive Room research; the unset
`chapter1.archive_research_complete` flag; the unset
`chapter1.article_135bis_understood` flag referenced at `story.txt` §Beat 12
**PROPOSALS.md status:** not mentioned as a standalone entry. Proposed in
2026-05-15 nightly (Proposal 1) but the proposal predates the 2026-05-16
motion-packet rewrite (Sessions 43–45). The architecture changed under it;
the proposal needs to be re-cast before promotion.

### Gap

`chapter1.archive_research_complete` is declared in `state.gd` (line 146)
and `save.gd` (line 185) and used as a trigger in five live dialogue states
(`whimsy::after_recruitment_court_ready`,
`crab::crab_post_halina_incapacity_offer`,
`crab::hint_needs_archive`, `crab::crab_hint_court_ready`,
`murrow::murrow_archive_walkthrough_decoy_merits`,
plus `asia_hint_states_ch1::hint_archive_research_complete` and
`hint_court_ready`). It is never *set* by any dialogue state or system
script. Beat 10 (Murrow's readiness check) is structurally unreachable from
Beat 8 (Halina meeting) without it.

The 2026-05-15 proposal had Murrow narrate Article 135-bis § 2 KPC
clause-by-clause in the archive. Under the post-2026-05-16 motion-packet
rewrite, Crab is the article-naming voice — see
`crab.json::crab_post_packet_names_article` ("Article one-thirty-five-bis,
paragraph two of the Code of Civil Procedure — translated..."), the *only*
place in the live catalogue where the article is cited. Re-citing the
article in Murrow's archive narration would (a) duplicate Crab's beat,
(b) put the doctrine in Murrow's mouth before the player has assembled the
four packet elements, breaking the v3 motion-packet contract that the
article gets named only once the packet is complete, and (c) collide with
`crab::crab_post_packet_names_article`'s `once: true` semantics if a player
arrives at the archive before assembling the packet.

The Beat-9 role under the motion-packet model is no longer doctrine
narration. It is fact-application: Murrow walks Cula through Halina's
specific facts against the packet elements he has already surfaced
(notice timeline, third-clause authority requirement) and produces the
`archive_research_complete` flag plus the Phase-1 fact-flags PROPOSALS.md
§10 carries forward to Phase 2 closing.

### Proposed addition

**File to edit:** `godot/data/dialogues/murrow.json`

Insert one new state between `murrow_archive_walkthrough_decoy_merits`
(currently at line 111) and `murrow_post_packet_motion_vehicle` (line 128):

- **`murrow_archive_research`**
  - Trigger:
    `chapter1.met_murrow == true && chapter1.halina_met == true &&
    chapter1.recruited_crab == true && !chapter1.archive_research_complete &&
    !chapter1.entered_court`
  - `_comment`: Beat 9. Murrow walks Cula through how the four packet
    elements (already surfaced via Crab's binder read-aloud and Murrow's
    own briefing) apply to Halina's specific facts. NOT a doctrine recitation
    — Crab will cite the article post-packet. The point of this beat is the
    fact-arithmetic: the 2015 renumbering against the April envelope; the
    countersignature on the 2019 renewal vs landlord knowledge; the
    fourteen-day clock against the 28-April actual-notice receipt; the
    third-clause requirement against the unauthorised resident at #7.
    Address form bare "Cula" (post-friend-invitation, private firm
    context). Three to five lines. No adjectives.
  - `on_dismiss`:
    - `chapter1.archive_research_complete = true`
    - `chapter1.article_135bis_understood = true` (gates Beat-12 strongest
      arguments per `story.txt` §Beat 9)
  - `once: true`

**Voice rule reminder.** Murrow does not name the article in this state.
The narration register is fact-arithmetic ("the envelope is 8 April; her
actual notice is the 28th; we have nine days remaining of fourteen"),
not citation. The article gets named one beat later by Crab once the
packet is complete.

**Code dependency:** Declare `chapter1.article_135bis_understood` (bool,
default `false`) in `state.gd::reset_state()`, add to
`chapter1.json::new_state_flags`, bump `SAVE_VERSION` and add the
migration step.

**Optional: dwell options.** `story.txt` §Beat 9 specifies three
character-dwell options ("How did you find this article?" / "Have you
used this clause before?" / "What do we need next?"). These are not
required for the engine to advance — the state above closes Beat 9 with
or without them. The dwell options would add archival texture for the
player and re-use the existing `options.write_path` + chain pattern
visible in `asia.json::cula_approach`. Recommend deferring the dwell
options to a separate session: they are a refinement on top of a working
beat, not a precondition for shipping the beat.

## Proposal 3: Author locked-route flavor lines in `data/doors.json`

**Type:** DATA
**Addresses:** `story.txt` §Beat 1 ("a small street with [...] several
NPCs blocking other roads", "blocked residential route", "blocked business
route"); `world.txt` lines 534, 574, 609, 643 (per-district locked-route
flavor); `PROPOSALS.md` §2 KEEP decision on locked-route discipline
**PROPOSALS.md status:** §2 marked DONE for the *decision* (locked routes
remain visible via signpost convention) and DONE for `world.txt` cleanup.
The data files implementing the decision are still empty.

### Gap

`data/doors.json` is the engine's single source for door transitions and
their `locked_text` flavor. Every one of the six declared doors carries
`"locked_text": ""` and `"required_flag": ""`. None of the visible-but-
blocked routes named in `world.txt` (Residential, Business District, City
Hall, Airport Road, Supreme Court stairs) has a door entry at all.

`AGENTS.md` §File ownership table assigns Design ownership of `locked_text`
in `data/doors.json`. PLAN.md §Vertical slice plan §"Skeleton" step
explicitly calls for "Locked routes (Residential, Business, City Hall,
Airport, Supreme Court) visible with `route_blocker.gd` flavor lines from
`story.txt`." The flavor is written in `world.txt`/`story.txt` in
draft-ready form ("Airport Road blocked: *You have no professional reason
to flee yet.*" — `world.txt` line 643) but never lifted into the runtime
data file.

### Proposed addition

**File to edit:** `godot/data/doors.json`

Add five new door entries for the visible-but-blocked Chapter-1 routes.
Each entry carries `locked_text` (Design-owned) and a `required_flag`
(Code-owned — Design proposes the flag name; Code declares it in
`state.gd` and wires the route_blocker actor to read it). Each
`required_flag` is a flag whose default is `false` in Chapter 1 and which
flips per the chapter-state table in `world.txt` lines 530–643.

  - `id: "office_street_to_residential"`, `required_flag:
    "chapter2.tenants_row_unlocked"`,
    `locked_text: "[Residential District. A handwritten sign on the gate:
    \"tenant meeting in progress — please return tomorrow with documented
    grievance.\"]"`
  - `id: "office_street_to_business"`, `required_flag:
    "chapter4.business_district_unlocked"`,
    `locked_text: "[A security guard at the entrance. He glances at the
    Pig & Swine letterhead and shakes his head. \"Glass Street is closed
    to walk-ins. Try after the audit.\"]"`
  - `id: "office_street_to_city_hall"`, `required_flag:
    "chapter6.city_hall_unlocked"`,
    `locked_text: "[The queue stretches around the building. A printed
    sign at the front: \"present queue position no. 814. Estimated wait:
    indeterminate.\"]"`
  - `id: "office_street_to_airport"`, `required_flag:
    "chapter5.airport_unlocked"`,
    `locked_text: "[The bus to the airport. You have no professional reason
    to flee yet.]"`
  - `id: "office_street_to_supreme_court"`, `required_flag:
    "chapter6.supreme_court_unlocked"`,
    `locked_text: "[The steps to the Supreme Court. A notice taped to the
    column: \"this entrance is for advocates of record. Apprentice access
    via the rear administrative door (closed for renovation).\"]"`

Each `locked_text` is bracketed because it reads as environmental
observation, not NPC dialogue (the route_blocker is the door object
itself; speaker omitted is the runner convention for this surface — verify
against `route_blocker.gd` reader at promotion). Each line passes the
Taste Standard:

  - *Funny:* the queue-number 814 line, the audit deferral, the apprentice-
    access-via-the-closed-rear-door.
  - *Clever:* every line names a real Polish-bureaucratic institutional
    referent (handwritten community notice, post-Soviet building-access
    triage, queue-number tickets at urzędy, the "rear administrative door"
    as the canonical advocate-of-record carve-out).
  - *Alive:* every line is in the voice of the place rather than the
    voice of an NPC; signpost-as-character.
  - *Clear:* each line tells the player exactly which chapter unlocks
    the route (the institutional gate names the unlock condition —
    "after the audit", "advocates of record only").
  - *Future-proof:* the per-chapter state escalation in `world.txt`
    (Beat 14 lines about Ms. Nowak appearing at the residential gate,
    rival-firm NPC at the business gate) can be added later by swapping
    `locked_text` per chapter without rewriting the joke; the locked-text
    field can be promoted to an object `{ ch1: "...", ch2: "..." }` when
    that data shape is needed.

**Code dependency:** Declare the five flag names in `state.gd` chapter-N
blocks per the chapter ownership above. None are Chapter-1 state changes
— all five remain false through Chapter 1.
