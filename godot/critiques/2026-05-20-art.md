# Pixel Art & Visual Direction — Hostile Critique, 2026-05-20

## Verdict
Three competing "canonical" sprite sizes are simultaneously documented and shipped, and the cast is the casualty. Mr. Pig and Mr. Swine are mutually unreadable, Cula and Crab collapse into the same dark-suited silhouette, and interior rooms outside the office are flat-color rectangles with no environmental art. The PROMPT.txt corpus still anchors on a style descriptor the project has rejected, so every "pending regeneration" is targeting the wrong brief.

## Findings

### F1 — Pick one sprite size and ship it
**Flaw:** `art_dims.txt` records 92×92 for every main-cast sprite on disk (Cula, Mr. Pig, Murrow, Crab, Whimsy, Asia, Halina). `godot/art/sprites/mr_swine/*` ships at 144×144. `godot/CONVENTIONS.md` line 10 claims canonical 124×124, then §Current divergences line 439 says canonical is 64×64. `godot/art/sprites/cula/PROMPT.txt` line 8 says `Size: 64×64` with a 128→64 downscale workflow.
**Why this fails:** Four numbers, zero ground truth. Y-sort offsets in `CONVENTIONS.md §Y-sort` assume 64×64 (`offset.y = -32`), but a 92×92 sprite at that offset stands 14 pixels through the floor. Every regeneration pass aims at a different target because nobody can cite a single source. The aesthetic itself depends on size — Świdziński-minimal is a 64×64 brief; the work on disk is not 64×64 and was not produced from that brief.
**Remediation:** Decide canonical size in one line at the top of `CONVENTIONS.md`. Delete the contradicting lines elsewhere. Update every PROMPT.txt to match. Add `tests/test_sprite_dims.gd` that asserts every PNG under `art/sprites/<character>/` matches the declared size. Treat the next regeneration sprint as a hard cutover: regenerate all main cast in one Pixellab session at the chosen size, commit, delete prior frames.
**Severity:** S1

### F2 — Mr. Swine is 1.57× the size of Mr. Pig
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/mr_swine/mr_swine_idle_front.png` is 144×144; `godot/art/sprites/mr_pig/mr_pig_idle_front.png` is 92×92.
**Why this fails:** Same species, similar archetype, on screen together in `pig_swine_office.tscn`. At a 1280×720 viewport Swine renders as a head taller than Pig and significantly bulkier. The reading is that Swine is *physically* the bigger founder, which is not the canon — both are adult pigs, and "smooth well-groomed" vs "round heavyset" is a posture/profile contrast, not a stature contrast. This sabotages the founders-as-mirror joke.
**Remediation:** Regenerate Swine at the same canonical size as Pig once F1 is resolved. Until then, the 144×144 asset must be downscaled in import to match the 92×92 cast, not in code at runtime. Bake the size; don't ask the scene to fix it.
**Severity:** S1

### F3 — Pig and Swine are the same silhouette
**Flaw:** Compare `godot/art/sprites/mr_pig/mr_pig_idle_front.png` and `godot/art/sprites/mr_swine/mr_swine_idle_front.png`. Both: pink head, navy suit, red tie, upright stance, hands at sides. Cover them in pure black and you cannot tell which founder is which.
**Why this fails:** The character contrast in `style_canon.txt` §Mr. Pig vs §Mr. Swine is *barrel body / tiny legs / flustered* vs *smooth / fitted / confident upright*. The PROMPT.txt files for both share the exact same six-color palette (`#1a1410, #f0c8c0, #1c2a40, #e8e4d8, #7a1f2a, #0d0a08`). The prompts ask Pixellab to invent the posture difference and Pixellab does not. The barrel-body/tiny-legs is not present in Pig; the well-groomed taper is not present in Swine.
**Remediation:** Force one of these to be a profile-readable size or shape difference baked into the sprite, not a prompt-level adjective. Options: give Swine a different tie color (cravat is in the prompt — execute it; e.g., cream cravat against navy reads at silhouette scale), or give Pig a clearly oval/round outline by pushing his shoulder width and shortening his leg run in the prompt with explicit `wider body, shorter legs, no neck`. Regenerate, then black-out test side-by-side before approving. Negative anchor for Swine: `barrel body, tiny legs`.
**Severity:** S1

### F4 — Cula and Crab collapse into the same dark-suited junior
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/cula/cula_idle_front.png` vs `godot/art/sprites/crab/crab_idle_front.png`. Both: late-20s male, dark hair, navy suit, red tie, hands at sides, plain stance. The "rumpled jacket with sleeves pushed up" and "tie loose at collar" in `godot/art/sprites/crab/PROMPT.txt` does not survive at 92×92 — there is no visible sleeve roll, no slack tie, no notebook clipped to belt. Crab's hair is mid-brown #6a4a30; Cula's is darker. At 10–12 pixels of hair mass that distinction does not read.
**Why this fails:** The junior trio (Cula, Crab, Whimsy) was supposed to be silhouette-distinguished — Whimsy via eggplant blazer (works), Crab via rumple, Cula via dark formal. Crab's rumple was always going to fail at this size. The PROMPT.txt was written for a level of detail the resolution does not afford.
**Remediation:** Pick a hard silhouette differentiator for Crab that survives downscaling. Options: a flat cap (Polish junior-investigator coding); rolled-up sleeves rendered as a forearm color block on both arms in idle (skin tone against jacket); or a darker brown jacket against Cula's near-black navy so the suit value itself reads. The notebook on the belt is a held object — it violates the no-held-object rule and would be a layered Sprite2D anyway. Remove from prompt.
**Severity:** S1

### F5 — `held objects` negative anchor is being ignored
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/mail_carrier_ch1/idle/front.png` ships with a brown satchel/messenger bag clearly held against the carrier's hip — directly contradicting `godot/CONVENTIONS.md` §World sprite generation rules: "No held objects, no raised-hand gestures."
**Why this fails:** Pixellab routinely overrides negative anchors when the role implies an object. A mail carrier without a bag is uncanny to the model. The rule says these objects must be runtime-layered Sprite2D — yet here the bag is baked. If/when the carrier is animated, the bag will float and tear. The rule was set precisely to prevent this.
**Remediation:** Regenerate the mail carrier with an explicit negative anchor `satchel, bag, mail bag, messenger bag, parcel, envelope, held object` and a stance prompt `arms at sides, palms empty`. If Pixellab still bakes the bag, accept that the carrier needs a custom non-Pixellab sprite. Audit every chapter-1 NPC (`route_blocker_business_ch1`, `route_blocker_residential_ch1`, `smokers_lawyer_ch1`, `tram_waiter_ch1`, `mail_carrier_ch1`) for held-object violations and queue regenerations.
**Severity:** S1

### F6 — `Halina` and `Sikorska` are two folders for the same character
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/halina/PROMPT.txt` and `godot/art/sprites/sikorska/PROMPT.txt` are byte-similar (same Prompt, same Negative, same Notes). `sikorska/` ships only `PROMPT.txt` and `sikorska_sprite_frames.tres`; no PNGs. `halina/` has the full 8-direction idle set.
**Why this fails:** The character is Halina Sikorska, addressed as Mrs. Sikorska in dialogue per `data/dialogues/halina.json`. Two folders for one character means the next regeneration pass will land in whichever folder the operator opens first, and one will silently drift. A `sikorska_sprite_frames.tres` referencing no PNGs is a load-time error waiting to fire.
**Remediation:** Delete `godot/art/sprites/sikorska/` entirely. Confirm no scene references `sikorska_sprite_frames.tres` (grep first). If a scene references Sikorska's frames, point it at `halina_sprite_frames.tres`. Keep the single-character convention: one folder, character canon name, full alias list in PROMPT.txt as a comment.
**Severity:** S2

### F7 — `Polish satirical illustration` anchor is still in every prompt
**Flaw:** Every main-cast PROMPT.txt (`cula`, `mr_pig`, `mr_swine`, `murrow`, `whimsy`, `asia`, `halina`, `sikorska`) opens its style anchor with "Minimal synthetic line drawing, sparse flat shapes, Polish satirical illustration, institutional mood…". `godot/CONVENTIONS.md` line 313 codifies "Polish satirical illustration" as the canonical style anchor.
**Why this fails:** The brief was reframed to neutral-institutional rendering of a satirical premise. Pixellab reads "satirical illustration" as caricature instruction — exaggerated features, comic-book outline weight, big-eyed mascot drift — which is exactly what the world-sprite layer is trying not to be. The portrait layer carries the satirical register; the world layer should not. Every "pending regeneration" PROMPT.txt is targeting the wrong style.
**Remediation:** Strip `Polish satirical illustration` from `CONVENTIONS.md` §World sprite generation rules and every PROMPT.txt. Replace with a stance-neutral institutional anchor (e.g., "minimal synthetic line drawing, flat institutional figure, sparse shapes, palette-snapped"). Add `caricature, exaggerated features, comic, mascot` to the negative anchor list. Re-run a single test character (Crab is a useful smoke test because his rumple is failing now) and compare before doing the full pass.
**Severity:** S1

### F8 — `archive_room.tscn` and `cafe_paragraf.tscn` are colored rectangles, not rooms
**Flaw:** `godot/scenes/interiors/archive_room.tscn` line 37 — `Floor` is a `ColorRect` at `Color(0.16, 0.16, 0.18)` covering 640×480. No tilemap. No walls art (only collision rectangles). No props except `RightsMemo` pickup and `Crab`. `godot/scenes/interiors/cafe_paragraf.tscn` line 36 — same pattern, `Color(0.35, 0.23, 0.13)` brown rectangle, no tiles, no counter, no chairs, no signage. The Barista is an Area2D with no visual.
**Why this fails:** These rooms exist in the chapter-1 critical path. The player exits the office and is presented with a flat brown rectangle that the dialogue insists is "Café Paragraf." There is no compositional hierarchy guiding the eye to the coffee machine; there's nothing for the eye to read at all. The §Game palettes section of `CONVENTIONS.md` defines eight palettes for environmental work; none of them are applied here.
**Remediation:** Two scenes, two TileMap layers each (floor + walls), even if the first pass uses placeholder palette-snapped tiles. Archive room: use Palette E "Praga Północ" — rust, courtyard moss, faded walls. Café Paragraf: use Palette A "Milk Bar" — warm institutional. Add a counter Sprite2D, a window Sprite2D, and at minimum a single light source (lamp). The job per room is: tilemap-painted floor and walls + 3 layered props + one accent that does the compositional pointing. One sprint.
**Severity:** S1

### F9 — `pig_swine_office.tscn` camera limit contradicts the documented rebuild
**Flaw:** `godot/scenes/interiors/pig_swine_office.tscn` lines 532–535 set `limit_right = 1024` and `limit_bottom = 576`. `CONVENTIONS.md` §Floor system claims the office was rebuilt 2026-05-11 to 20×11 tiles = 1280×704, "fits entirely in the viewport, no scrolling," with `camera limits set to (0, 0, 1280, 704)`.
**Why this fails:** The scene file disagrees with the canon doc by 256 pixels horizontally and 128 pixels vertically. The camera will not show 256 columns × 128 rows of the room the doc says exists. Either the rebuild was partial and the doc lied, or the rebuild happened and the camera was missed. Either way the canon doc is unreliable as a source-of-truth on what the player sees.
**Remediation:** Open the scene, set `limit_right = 1280` and `limit_bottom = 704`, verify the TileMap actually fills those bounds (probe with `_probe_tilemap_format.py`). If the TileMap is short of 1280×704, the doc is wrong and needs amending. If the TileMap fills it, the camera fix is one edit.
**Severity:** S2

### F10 — `_alt/back_left_v2/` legacy frames bleed into the active Cula folder
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/cula/walk/_alt/back_left_v2/` contains six 92×92 frames named `cula_walk_back_left_v2_*.png`. Per `CONVENTIONS.md` §Current divergences, these are legacy artifacts. They sit inside the active `walk/` tree, not in a sibling `_legacy/` folder.
**Why this fails:** A scene or .tres referencing `walk/_alt/back_left_v2/` will load broken frames (I viewed `cula_walk_back_left_v2_00.png` — the figure is half-cut, missing limb detail). Tooling that globs `walk/**/*.png` will pick these up. The `cula_128_animation_preview.png` and `cula.aseprite` files also sit at the top of `cula/` — work-in-progress artifacts mixed with shipping assets.
**Remediation:** Move `_alt/` out of `walk/` to `godot/art/sprites/_legacy/cula/walk_back_left_v2/`. Move `cula.aseprite` and `cula_128_animation_preview.png` to `art/_sources/cula/` (the .aseprite is a source file, not a runtime asset; the .png.import alongside it suggests Godot is importing the preview as a usable texture, which it isn't). Add the same hygiene to `asia/`, `crab/` (both contain `.aseprite` files at top level).
**Severity:** S2

### F11 — Asia's cardigan is bright yellow, not Mustard
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/asia/asia_idle_front.png` renders the cardigan in a saturated bright yellow (close to `#e8c840` Cheap Gold from Palette C). The character-accent palette in `godot/art/sprites/asia/PROMPT.txt` line 9 specifies `#c8a868` Mustard (paint).
**Why this fails:** `CONVENTIONS.md` §Palette C: "Mustard now means warm building paint; Cheap Gold means signage — keep them distinct in use." Asia's character accent is supposed to be the warm-paint mustard. Pixellab landed on the signage gold instead. The single palette-safe accent is the only thing distinguishing her silhouette from any other female front-desk archetype — and it's the wrong hue.
**Remediation:** Regenerate with explicit `cardigan color: muted mustard ochre #c8a868, NOT bright yellow`. Snap the rendered cardigan to the palette in post by indexing on the dominant cardigan pixel cluster — a 5-minute `convert -fuzz 12% -fill '#c8a868' -opaque '#e8c840'` pass with ImageMagick if the regeneration fails. Run the same palette-snap audit on Whimsy's blazer (should be `#5a2a4a` Eggplant) and Cula's tie (should be `#7a1f2a` Oxblood — currently rendering as a cream rectangle on the front idle; the tie is wrong color entirely).
**Severity:** S2

### F12 — Cula's tie is the wrong color
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/cula/cula_idle_front.png` shows a light cream/beige tie. The PROMPT.txt specifies `oxblood tie` and the palette includes `#7a1f2a`. The current render is not oxblood; it is closer to `#e8e4d8` Parchment.
**Why this fails:** Cula's tie color is one of his three silhouette markers (dark hair / navy suit / oxblood tie). Render it cream and he reads as Generic Junior Lawyer instead of *the* junior lawyer. The shared palette-safe set in `CONVENTIONS.md` §Shared character colors does not include oxblood, which means Cula's accent has to come from his per-character extension — and the extension didn't survive generation.
**Remediation:** Index-replace the tie pixel cluster against `#7a1f2a` until the regeneration pass. This is one ImageMagick call per facing × 8 facings = 8 calls scripted in 10 minutes. Add a `tests/test_palette_snapped.gd` that loads each character sprite and asserts every non-transparent pixel is in the union of the shared palette + that character's accent list. This catches future drift the way `test_ysort_canon.gd` catches Y-sort regressions.
**Severity:** S2

### F13 — Style cohort split: legacy main cast vs new minor NPCs
**Flaw:** Main cast (Cula, Mr. Pig, Mr. Swine, Murrow, Crab, Whimsy, Asia, Halina) ships at 92×92 in a softly-shaded illustration style with visible value gradients on suit jackets and facial detail. Newer NPC folders (`mail_carrier_ch1`, `route_blocker_business_ch1`, `smokers_lawyer_ch1`, `tram_waiter_ch1`) show a different rendering register — see the mail carrier's front idle, which has heavier outline weight and a different value structure than Cula's front idle.
**Why this fails:** Chapter 1 has the player walking from the office (legacy cast) onto the street (new minor NPCs). Two registers in the same scene reads as a stylesheet failure, not a deliberate character choice. The whole project's "one game, not a sticker collection" palette discipline (`CONVENTIONS.md` §Palette discipline) is violated by adjacent-frame style drift.
**Remediation:** Before any further chapter-1 minor-NPC generation, do the main cast regeneration first (F1, F7). The minor NPCs become the style anchor for the main cast only if the main cast is dragged toward them — pick one direction and commit. The pre-rejected Świdziński-minimal at 64×64 was the picked direction once; if that target stands, the main cast is the work, not more new minor NPCs.
**Severity:** S2

### F14 — `art/props/office/wall_calendar.png` is character-scaled
**Flaw:** Per `art_dims.txt`, `wall_calendar.png` is 64×64, `wall_clock.png` is 56×56, `coffee_machine.png` is 64×64 — the same order of magnitude as a 92×92 character. `bookshelf.png` and `desk_pig.png` are 128×128, larger than the characters.
**Why this fails:** A wall calendar at 64×64 will read as roughly two-thirds the size of an adult human standing next to it. The player will not parse "calendar on the wall, two years out of date" (a canonical visual joke) — they will parse "weird square mounted at body height." At top-down perspective, props need to be sized to their real-world footprint relative to the figure, not generated at the convenience size of the generator.
**Remediation:** Audit `godot/art/props/office/` against character height. Wall calendar should be 24–32 px tall at 92×92 character scale (~25% of figure height). Wall clock similar. Coffee machine 48–64 px tall, narrower than a character. Re-export the existing PNGs at correct dimensions in the .import file (`size_override`) as a quick fix; regenerate at correct dimensions on the next prop pass. Verify against `pig_swine_office.tscn` after the change — the Y-sort offset math depends on the actual PNG dimensions per `CONVENTIONS.md` §Y-sort.
**Severity:** S2

## Standing concerns

The PROMPT.txt corpus carries "Last updated: 2026-05-11 — prompts rewritten for Świdziński-minimal direction (128→64 workflow). Pending regeneration." across every main-cast character. Nine days of pending. The regeneration is the work that retires every S1 above; until it ships, the project is critiquing a snapshot of a half-finished cutover. Block other art work until the cutover lands.

`art/portraits/` was not inspected in this pass because the brief is world-sprite-centric, but the hybrid Świdziński+Waliszewska doctrine in `CONVENTIONS.md` §Art direction is load-bearing for the comedy. If the portrait layer is also "pending regeneration" the contrast-is-the-joke premise is hypothetical, not shipped.

The eight game palettes documented in `CONVENTIONS.md` are excellent on paper. Zero of them are visibly applied to the two non-office interior scenes audited. Document discipline ahead of execution discipline is the failure mode to watch.
