# Narrative & Dialogue — Hostile Critique, 2026-05-25

## Verdict

The chapter has procedural spine and rolled-up sleeves; it does not yet have voices. Murrow, Crab and (now) Halina speak the same clipped two-sentence declarative; the courtroom prose has the texture of a checklist read aloud; the load-bearing Beat 8 plants (cardiologist, literary epigram) are buried inside a busy stage-management block; and Whimsy — the character whose theatrical flourish style_canon makes load-bearing for Court Round 2 — has zero committed court line. The project's craft documentation is more polished than its craft.

## Findings

### F1 — Murrow and Crab share one diction
**Flaw:** Their committed lines collapse into a single voice: clipped fragments, verbless lists, single-word verdicts. `godot/data/dialogues/murrow.json::court_readiness_check` runs "Procedural binder. On file. / Rights memo. On file. / Crab and Whimsy on the appearance. Confirmed. / Hearing at fourteen hundred. Courtroom four." `godot/data/dialogues/crab.json::after_binder_first_engagement` runs "Envelope: number seven, eighth of April. Renewal: number twelve, two thousand and nineteen, countersigned. Renumbering: two thousand and fifteen." If the speaker-tag is hidden, no playtester will diagnose which one is talking. Murrow's one-word "Acceptable." (murrow_coffee_reaction_perfect) and Crab's one-word "Heard." (crab.json::first_meeting_with_binder) are the same comedy engine performed twice.
**Why this fails:** style_canon §2 Murrow ("understated, archival, faintly amused, almost never uses adjectives — when he does, it lands") and Crab ("two-sentence rhythm: observation, then implication") describe distinct registers. The committed text has flattened both into procedural recitation. A character voice that cannot be diagnosed from a single line is not a voice; it is a sentence-length convention.
**Remediation:** Reauthor Murrow's archival-aside register with at least one adjective per scene that earns its load-bearing weight (the Mrożek-paperback dryness promised in style_canon §2 is absent from every committed Murrow line); cut Crab's verbless date-lists in `first_meeting_with_binder` / `after_binder_first_engagement` and rebuild as the canonical observation-then-implication paired sentence ("Renumbering was 2015. The notice was sent to a building that stopped existing eleven years ago. That is not service."). The single-word verdicts ("Acceptable." / "Heard.") cannot both belong to this game; pick one mouth.
**Severity:** S2

### F2 — Halina speaks her lawyer's lines
**Flaw:** Halina was specced as a 71-year-old retired Polish-language teacher whose biographical depth (1962 lease, 38 years teaching, Iwaszkiewicz) was supposed to colour her speech. Her committed lines in `godot/data/dialogues/halina.json::client_meeting_intro` are: "Good morning. Halina Sikorska. I've been told you can help." / "Thank you." / "Mr. Murrow. Mr. Crab. Mr. Whimsy." Her rebuke in `halina_post_meeting_decoy_incapacity_cold` ("I am not too old to be served with a letter. I am too inconvenient to be served with the right letter.") is the cleanest sentence in the chapter and reads as Murrow's hand on her mouth — same two-sentence reversal, same verbless cadence. Her biographical answers in `client_meeting_r0_response_high` recite dates ("seventy-four", "two thousand and nine") with no texture from a literary teacher's ear.
**Why this fails:** Disco Elysium and Pentiment build minor speakers by giving them one verbal habit no one else has. Halina has none. The "I do not know whether it was relevant to mention" in `client_meeting_r2_response_high` is the only place she sounds like an actual older woman who has carried this case on a kitchen table for three weeks. Style_canon §2 Cula warns "anything that mocks the client" is forbidden; the deeper failure is to render the client as a transcription engine.
**Remediation:** Author one habit only Halina has — a teacher's tic (correcting verb-tense gently, naming what a sentence is doing, a domestic-classroom idiom). Apply it across the four `client_meeting_r0/r1/r2_response_*` states. The Iwaszkiewicz reference flagged for phase-7 voice work in story.txt Beat 8 has never been written; write it. The Beat 8 literary epigram cannot land if every preceding Halina line is procedural fact-recitation.
**Severity:** S2

### F3 — The incapacity blunder collects five scoldings
**Flaw:** When the player picks D5 (incapacity), the chapter fires `murrow.json::murrow_post_decoy_incapacity`, `crab.json::crab_post_halina_incapacity_refuses`, `whimsy.json::whimsy_post_decoy_incapacity`, `halina.json::halina_post_meeting_decoy_incapacity_cold`, and `judge_district_ch1.json::judge_b12_remedy_narrow_incapacity`. Five scenes shaming the same choice. Whimsy's contribution ("I sing for the case. I do not sing for that.") adds nothing the others did not deliver more sharply.
**Why this fails:** The Dekalog discipline that style_canon §9 invokes works because each moral question lands once and refuses elaboration. The current implementation makes the firm a Greek chorus moralising at the player, which is exactly the tonal failure mode style_canon §3 calls out ("comedy steps back; the line lands plain"). The pile-on also collapses Crab's, Murrow's and Halina's distinct calibrations into the same register: they all say the same thing in slightly different syntax.
**Remediation:** Keep Crab's `crab_post_halina_incapacity_refuses` (the work-walkaway is the load-bearing in-fiction consequence). Keep Halina's `halina_post_meeting_decoy_incapacity_cold` (the client's voice is the only one that earns this scene). Delete `whimsy_post_decoy_incapacity` entirely. Collapse `murrow_post_decoy_incapacity` to a single line ("She is not incompetent. I will file the motion myself.") and leave the rest to staging. The judge's `judge_b12_remedy_narrow_incapacity` is the last word and should stay.
**Severity:** S2

### F4 — Whimsy is unauthored in the court he is supposed to carry
**Flaw:** story.txt Beat 12 Round 2 specifies "Whimsy's flourish carries the round." The judge in `godot/data/dialogues/judge_district_ch1.json::judge_b12_react_r2` reacts to that flourish: "The court is not, in fact, a spoon-allocation bureau, but the underlying proposition is correct." The metaphor the judge is reacting to does not exist in `godot/data/dialogues/whimsy.json`; the file has no court-round state. Cula carries Round 2 alone via `cula.json::cula_b12_round2_response`. Style_canon §2 Whimsy further specifies "one Wordsworth or Shakespeare cite per Court Round Phase 2 closing where Whimsy is on stage" — grep across `godot/data/dialogues/` returns zero. Whimsy's literary-quote canon is wholly unimplemented.
**Why this fails:** A character whose entire mechanical and rhetorical function is to perform in court has been written out of the only court scene. The judge reacts to thin air. A playtester who lands on a strong Round 2 will hear the judge praise a metaphor the game never spoke.
**Remediation:** Author `whimsy_b12_round2_open` in `godot/data/dialogues/whimsy.json` carrying the canonical soup/spoon/fork-of-bees metaphor (style_canon §1 has the canonical text), plus one Wordsworth or Shakespeare cite per style_canon §2. Three stance variants per `chapter1.client_meeting_stance` are specced; write them. Until this state exists, Round 2 fails the Taste Standard at the chapter's most load-bearing rhetorical moment.
**Severity:** S1

### F5 — cula.json's dwell library is unreachable at runtime
**Flaw:** `godot/data/dialogues/cula.json::_authoring_note` declares: "Cula's dialogue file is dispatched ONLY by the family-photo interactable... Beat states authored here are reachable only after engineering fan-out into the appropriate NPC files... fan-out is a tracked engineering task." The file then contains roughly 30 committed states, including 14 dwell-options gated on `chapter1.state_choice`, every one of which the runtime will never reach. The Beat 12 Round 1/2/3 court responses, the Beat 13 ledger silent state, the Kundera once-per-chapter interior beat in `cula_b9_kundera_beat` — all unreachable.
**Why this fails:** This is the largest single file in `data/dialogues/` and almost none of it ships. A writer authoring Cula's next chapter cannot trust this file as a voice document because half the lines have been duplicated into `pig.json` / `murrow.json` / `halina.json` (e.g. Cula's "Six weeks. Understood." appears in `pig.json::pig_first_meeting` and is re-staged in `cula.json::cula_b3_pig_rent_reaction`); the two copies will drift.
**Remediation:** Either (a) finish the fan-out — move every Cula state in `cula.json` into the receiving NPC files and reduce `cula.json` to the family-photo states only, then delete the orphaned duplicates; or (b) write the dialogue runner support that walks `cula.json` as an internal-voice channel on every state transition. Choose one this sprint. Continuing to author into a file the runtime ignores is silent work.
**Severity:** S1

### F6 — "Wojcik" without the ó is a canon violation
**Flaw:** `godot/data/dialogues/asia_hint_states_ch1.json::hint_bonus_evidence_wojcik` line 101: "Mrs. Wojcik rang earlier." Every other file (`halina.json`, `cula.json`, `judge_district_ch1.json`) spells the same character "Wójcik." godot/AGENTS.md §Stack invariants: "Polish institutional names appear with correct diacritics." The rule's spirit covers Polish surnames; the project applies it everywhere else.
**Why this fails:** The Polish-legal-flavour discipline lives in the diacritics. A single line drops it in a hint a player will see repeatedly (hint-NPC repeat is the only state-class style_canon §7 exempts from the no-filler rule). The visible misspelling reads as carelessness about the very surface texture the game is selling.
**Remediation:** Edit `godot/data/dialogues/asia_hint_states_ch1.json` line 101: replace "Mrs. Wojcik" with "Mrs. Wójcik." Add a `tools/voice_audit.py` rule that fails CI on any committed Polish surname appearing in two spelling variants across `data/`.
**Severity:** S1

### F7 — The Court Round openers are robot-shaped
**Flaw:** `judge_district_ch1.json::judge_b12_round1_open` says "Counsel for the petitioner. The first question is service." `judge_b12_round2_open` says "Second question. Why does the defect matter to the client's right to be heard?" `judge_b12_round3_open` says "Third question. What exactly does counsel ask the court to do today?" The three openers are interchangeable templates with the integer swapped. Halina's three round prompts in `halina.json` ("A few more questions." / "One more area.") fail the same way — they are placeholder strings dressed as dialogue.
**Why this fails:** Style_canon §6 explicitly templates judicial reactions as dry-surprise ("Counsel, surprisingly, that is a point.") to disguise the procedural skeleton. The bench is supposed to sound like a person being unexpectedly persuaded. Sequential "first question / second question / third question" is the writing equivalent of `print(f"Round {n}")` — it's the spec talking, not the judge.
**Remediation:** Rewrite the three round openers in `judge_district_ch1.json` so the bench's procedural framing carries one diagnosable judicial habit per round (a single-page wryness, a glance at the docket, a tired correction of how counsel framed it). Reauthor Halina's `client_meeting_r1` / `client_meeting_r2` round-openers as actual lines a 71-year-old former Polish-literature teacher would say at a kitchen-table interview, not as scene-transitions.
**Severity:** S2

### F8 — Round 1 reactions are tagged dry_surprise but contain none
**Flaw:** `judge_b12_react_r1_blunt_procedural` ("Counsel. The renumbering, the prior return, and the countersignature are before the court. The court has seen many excuses. It has also seen real procedural defects. This is the latter.") is a checklist. `judge_b12_react_r1_sympathetic` ("Counsel. The affidavit is noted. The renumbering is sufficient on the documents...") is also a checklist. The canonical dry-surprise template ("Against several expectations, counsel has produced a coherent submission") only fires in the *strong* outcome state, which requires perfect packet + zero decoys. The standard player path will never hear surprised judicial register.
**Why this fails:** Style_canon §6 names dry-surprise as the load-bearing comedic engine of the bench. Restricting it to a single-state perfect-play path means the bench's comic voice ships to perhaps 15% of playthroughs. The standard-path judge sounds like an examiner reading rubric points.
**Remediation:** Bring the dry-surprise template into `judge_b12_react_r1_blunt_procedural` and `judge_b12_react_r1_sympathetic` directly. One sentence per round of "the court did not expect this to land, and now must accept it." Move the dry-disappointment register into the wrong-answer wrong-evidence reactions where it currently isn't doing work.
**Severity:** S2

### F9 — Beat 8's load-bearing plants ride a stage-management bus
**Flaw:** `halina.json::client_meeting_close` is a 26-line monologue that has to deliver: Cula's summary, Murrow's fee terms, Halina's fee acceptance, the cardiologist plant, Pig's interruption with a "Hennessy retainer phone" line that no setup explains, Murrow's redirect, the retention signing, Halina's literary epigram, and the farewells. The cardiologist plant ("I will need to reschedule with my cardiologist again. He is patient with me.") and the literary epigram ("You go to a lawyer like you go to a doctor: too late.") are the two beats that have to recolor at Ch4. Both are jammed into one block, both immediately followed by Murrow saying "Two signatures. Here and here. I will witness." — no pause, no Cula register acknowledgment.
**Why this fails:** story.txt Beat 8 says explicitly of the cardiologist plant: "framed neutrally — Halina mentions she had to reschedule a cardiology follow-up... no italics, no clutched chest, no Cula reaction line." Neutral framing is not the same as buried framing. The plant has to be *heard* to recolor later; here it lands inside a paragraph the player will skim. The literary epigram has the same problem and is additionally undercut by the Pig/Murrow scuffle two lines earlier — the player's attention is on the interruption, not on Halina's wisdom.
**Remediation:** Split `client_meeting_close` into three states triggered sequentially: (a) fee + retention + Pig interruption + Murrow redirect; (b) cardiologist plant as a discrete short state with one beat of silence after; (c) literary epigram as a discrete short state, also with breath. The "Hennessy retainer phone" reference has no setup anywhere in chapter 1 (no earlier Hennessy mention in any committed JSON) and should be cut or replaced with a callback to an established firm detail (the printer, the missing-Swine-retainer, the rent envelope).
**Severity:** S2

### F10 — Manual met_<x> bookkeeping persists at 70 callsites
**Flaw:** Grep across `godot/data/dialogues/*.json` returns 70 references to `chapter1.met_*`. SAVE_VERSION 12 added declarative `once: true` fire-once via `dialogue_states_seen` (correctly used in `pig.json::pig_b13_celebration` with the note "once:true uses SAVE_VERSION 12 declarative fire-once... no manual flag needed"). Adoption stops there. Every other state still gates on `!chapter1.met_pig` / `!chapter1.met_murrow` / `!chapter1.met_crab` and writes the matching flag on dismiss.
**Why this fails:** Repeated greetings — "Good morning, Mr. Pig" landing twice because two states race on the same flag — are a craft failure style_canon §7 calls out as filler. The longer the manual-flag inventory survives, the more brittle the chain re-fires become. The dialogue editor work documented in MEMORY.md confirms `dialogue_editor.html` exposes the `once: true` checkbox; the bookkeeping is migration, not invention.
**Remediation:** Schedule a sprint sweep of `data/dialogues/*.json`: for every state whose only `on_dismiss` write is a `met_<npc>` flag, replace with `"once": true` and update downstream triggers to use `dialogue_states_seen` membership where appropriate. Keep `chapter1.met_<npc>` only where downstream gameplay state (route blockers, asia hints, etc.) reads it. Test fixture: a save loaded after the sweep that walks a known dwell tree and confirms no greeting repeats.
**Severity:** S3

### F11 — Mr. Pig's maritime tic has become his only register
**Flaw:** style_canon §2 Mr. Pig: "Use sparingly: roughly one in four lines, more frequent in panic states, absent in calm or sincere beats." Reality in committed text — `pig.json::pig_first_meeting` alone contains "six weeks under the surface" + "the polite knock is somehow worse" + "below deck" (`met_murrow_pre_binder`). `pig.json::court_readiness_check` has "kraken" / "bait." `halina.json::client_meeting_close` has Pig saying "the Hennessy retainer phone has gone overboard" + "small craft warning." `postcard_swine_ch1.json::pig_postcard_reaction` is "The Sea of Japan, then." Every Pig appearance in chapter 1 carries maritime imagery; the ratio is closer to one-in-one.
**Why this fails:** Style_canon explicitly warns the maritime tic must recede in Pig's Ch5 sincere line for that beat to land. By making it Pig's default register in Ch1, the Ch5 absence becomes invisible — Pig's voice is the maritime tic, not a panic-state spike of it. Hrabal's digressive-rhythm influence (style_canon §9) requires Pig to occasionally land somewhere true by accident; he cannot land anywhere true if every sentence ends in a metaphor.
**Remediation:** Rewrite `pig.json::pig_first_meeting` to keep at most one maritime line (the "six weeks under the surface" earns its keep); cut "below deck" from `met_murrow_pre_binder` and replace with a Hrabal-rhythm digression that wanders back to a printer-lease detail. Cut "kraken" from `court_readiness_check` (the lecture aside two lines later is already loaded). Decide whether `pig_b13_celebration`'s toast joke is doing different work than the maritime register — if not, recolor it as a Hrabalian sincere undercut.
**Severity:** S3

### F12 — Whimsy's recruitment line patronises the case shape
**Flaw:** `whimsy.json::before_meeting` Whimsy line: "Ah. A wrong-door file, with the right door also in the file. I have heard worse music." Followed three lines later by "I will lend my voice tomorrow at fourteen hundred. The shape you bring, I will sing." Style_canon §2 Whimsy: "Pairing rule: useful for rhetoric and proportionality. Dangerous on factual or procurement work." The committed lines instead read as a cynic auditioning the case for his approval — "I have heard worse music" treats Halina's situation as a draft pitch he's blessing.
**Why this fails:** The Beat 8 audit point preserved at multiple places in the spec is explicit: "Whimsy's flourish must address the case, not the person." The recruitment line gets it backwards by addressing the case's *aesthetics* — its musicality — from above. The cynic register collapses into a posture style_canon §3 warns against ("the joke is the *scale*: a three-person union for a half-collapsing firm. Resist making STUB ever competent or effective"). Whimsy here is not flamboyant-when-aimed; he is condescending-when-bored.
**Remediation:** Rewrite `before_meeting` so Whimsy's first move is a rhetorical question that engages the case's actual leverage (the fair-hearing failure), not a critic's verdict on its file shape. The "I have heard worse music" line should be retained for a later state where the procedural shape genuinely fails and Whimsy reaches for the metaphor in earnest. The "The shape you bring, I will sing" closer can stay if the audition register above it is cut.
**Severity:** S2

## Standing concerns

The voice-pack production line authors `_comment` fields that explain what the line is supposed to do, then writes a line that performs the explanation rather than the work — Cula's "client_meeting_stance" carrier in `cula.json::cula_b8_approach_choice` is the cleanest example; the comment is more diagnostic than any committed line. Watch for `_comment` fields growing longer than the line they justify.

The chapter ships extensive structural documentation (provenance fields, anti-AI-voice-pass declarations, address-form audit trails) and minimal authored character habit; the engineering is well-resourced and the writing is under-resourced relative to it. Future chapters will inherit this imbalance unless the next sprint allocates writing time at parity with structural-fan-out time.

`cula.json` is the diagnostic — a file the runtime cannot reach has accumulated 30 states because authoring against it is easier than authoring against the engineering contract. Watch for the same pattern in chapter 2's voice work; the easiest place to write is rarely the place the player will hear.
