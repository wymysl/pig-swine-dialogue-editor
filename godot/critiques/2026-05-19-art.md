# Pixel Art & Visual Direction — Hostile Critique, 2026-05-19

## Verdict
There is no game-wide visual thesis on disk — there are three. The main cast is rendered at 112×112 as chunky 1:4 cartoon dolls; Halina and the named Ch1 NPCs ship at 180×180 as smoothly-shaded illustrated figures with anti-aliased outlines; the canon document claims 124×124 is the standard and a separate divergence note insists the standard is 64×64. None of these match. Inside a single character (Asia) three different costumes appear across the 8 idle facings and even inside one walk cycle. Two interior rooms ship as flat ColorRect floors with no tilework. Forbidden cast names (Rak, Wymysl, Kula) are still load-bearing in scene resource paths. The project is shipping placeholder art at production paths and treating the gap as a deferred-divergence note rather than the structural problem it is.

## Findings

### F1 — Asia ships three different costumes across her 8 facings
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/asia/asia_idle_front.png` shows a yellow cardigan with a long navy A-line skirt, ankle boots, short auburn bob. `godot/art/sprites/asia/asia_idle_right.png` and `asia_idle_left.png` show the same yellow cardigan over blue full-length jeans, brown ponytail, no skirt. `godot/art/sprites/asia/asia_idle_back.png` reverts to skirt-and-up-do but in a different hair color again. The walk cycle `godot/art/sprites/asia/walk/north/frame_000.png` (jeans) and `walk/north/frame_003.png` (skirt) flicker between two outfits inside a single animation.
**Why this fails:** Costume continuity is the single non-negotiable of character art. The player will read this as the character changing clothes mid-step. Pixellab sessions were not seed-locked across passes; the PROMPT.txt's "ponytail, mustard cardigan, cream shirt, navy pencil skirt" was not enforced — the generator produced two valid interpretations and both shipped. The "Pixellab Character ID" field exists for exactly this reason and was not honored.
**Remediation:** Delete every asia_idle_*.png and the walk/* tree. Re-run a single Pixellab session against `Character ID: 0f78d397-...` with one locked seed; pick one of the two outfits (the jeans+ponytail set reads cleaner at distance — keep that one) and discard the other. Verify all 8 facings + every walk frame share hair color, top color, bottom silhouette before committing.
**Severity:** S1

### F2 — Mr. Pig and Mr. Swine collapse to the same silhouette
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/mr_pig/mr_pig_idle_front.png` and `godot/art/sprites/mr_swine/mr_swine_idle_front.png` are both pink anthropomorphic pigs in navy suits with red ties at the same body proportion and same head shape. The "round heavyset / barrel body / tiny legs" promised in `mr_pig/PROMPT.txt` did not survive; the "smooth well-groomed / fitted dark charcoal suit / oxblood cravat" promised in `mr_swine/PROMPT.txt` did not survive — Swine is in the same blue suit as Pig with the same red tie. Cover both in black and you cannot tell which founder is panicking and which is the absent one.
**Why this fails:** Pig and Swine are the title characters of a firm called Pig & Swine. They must be the two most distinguishable silhouettes in the cast. They are currently the two most overlapping. The whole "round/barrel vs. tall/smooth" contrast that the writing assumes is absent from the generated sprites.
**Remediation:** Rewrite Mr. Swine's prompt to enforce contrast: charcoal suit (not navy), taller and slimmer frame (1:5 not 1:4), narrower shoulders, longer snout angle. Drop the red tie from Swine entirely — the cravat color in his PROMPT (oxblood) is already palette-distinct; render it. For Mr. Pig, push the barrel volume harder — widen the torso silhouette, shorten the legs visibly. Generate side-by-side and reject any pass where the two front-facing silhouettes are confusable in pure black.
**Severity:** S1

### F3 — Three incompatible sprite resolutions ship simultaneously
**Flaw:** Main cast (`cula`, `mr_pig`, `mr_swine`, `murrow`, `crab`, `whimsy`, `asia`, `halina`) ships at 112×112 (and a 124×124 Cula). Mr. Swine ships at 144×144. Ch1 named NPCs (`mail_carrier_ch1`, `smokers_lawyer_ch1`, `tram_waiter_ch1`, `route_blocker_business_ch1`, `route_blocker_residential_ch1`) ship at 180×180. CONVENTIONS.md §Canonical numbers claims 124×124 is canonical. The "Current divergences" section at the bottom of the same file claims 64×64 is canonical and existing sprites are 112×112 pending regeneration. Every PROMPT.txt says "Size: 64×64". The art_dims.txt scan shows files at 92×92 too (`crab/`, `whimsy/`, `asia/`, `mr_pig/`, `cula/walk/`, `murrow/`).
**Why this fails:** Three different sprite scales standing on the same TileMap is the most visible art-direction failure a top-down game can ship. A 180px route blocker next to a 112px Mr. Pig reads as the route blocker being a giant. There is no Y-sort or texture-filter trick that disguises this. Worse, the canon doc disagrees with itself, so there is no authoritative target to regenerate against.
**Remediation:** Pick one number — 124 is defensible for legibility at 1280×720, 96 is defensible for grid-snap purity, 64 is canon-claimed and currently fictional. Document the choice in one place, update every other place to match, then regenerate every sprite in one Pixellab campaign with a fixed canvas size. Until that pass lands, scale the 180px Ch1 NPCs down at runtime via `scale = Vector2(112.0/180.0, 112.0/180.0)` as a stopgap — but only after asserting nearest-neighbor filtering survives the fractional ratio (it usually does not; consider 0.625 or 0.5 only).
**Severity:** S1

### F4 — Two interior rooms have no floors
**Flaw:** `godot/scenes/interiors/cafe_paragraf.tscn` Floor node is `ColorRect ... color = Color(0.35, 0.23, 0.13, 1)` — a flat brown rectangle 640×480. `godot/scenes/interiors/archive_room.tscn` Floor node is `ColorRect ... color = Color(0.16, 0.16, 0.18, 1)` — a flat near-black rectangle 640×480. No tiles, no pattern, no shading, no perspective cue.
**Why this fails:** These are placeholder rooms ported into shipping scenes. The player walks into Café Paragraf and the floor is one continuous mud-colored slab; into the archive and the floor is a black void. CONVENTIONS.md §TileMap vs Sprite2D placement explicitly requires tilemap layers for floors. Both rooms are at 640×480 — half the viewport — so the room itself reads as a postage stamp inside the camera view. The undersized floor is a separate divergence already flagged in CONVENTIONS.md but not yet fixed.
**Remediation:** For Café Paragraf, author a parquet wood tile (8 variants, 4 with grain rotation, 64×64) and bake into a TileSet `cafe_paragraf_tileset.tres`; floor area extends to 1280×720 minimum, walled ring around it. For Archive Room, author a worn linoleum tile (3 variants, deliberate scuff in 1/3, 64×64) and bake into `archive_room_tileset.tres`; use Palette E (Praga) per CONVENTIONS.md §Game palettes — the archive is exactly where "rust, crumbling facades, courtyard moss" belongs. Both rooms get the same wall-ring treatment as `pig_swine_office.tscn`. Delete the ColorRect Floor nodes once tilemaps land.
**Severity:** S1

### F5 — Ch1 named NPCs are a different art style entirely
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/route_blocker_residential_ch1/idle/front.png` and `godot/art/sprites/smokers_lawyer_ch1/idle/front.png` and `godot/art/sprites/mail_carrier_ch1/idle/front.png` are 180×180 illustrated figures with smooth anti-aliased outlines, soft shading on the suit folds, realistic adult 1:6 proportions. The main cast at 112×112 is 1:4, chunky, with hard pixel outlines and no shading. The Ch1 NPCs look like assets from a different game pasted into the same scene (`scenes/world/routes/office_street.tscn` instantiates all of them next to player Cula).
**Why this fails:** This is the single most jarring visual failure in the build. The route_blocker_residential — clean cel-shaded illustrated workman in yellow overalls — and Cula — pixelated 1:4 doll — share screen space. The illusion that they live in the same world dies on contact. The 180×180 set looks like Pixellab's default "high-detail character" template at a much larger canvas; the main cast looks like the same tool throttled to a lower canvas. Same generator, two incompatible briefs.
**Remediation:** Regenerate the five Ch1 NPC sprite sets at the same canvas size as the main cast (whatever F3's resolution decision is), against the same PROMPT.txt anchor block the main cast uses (minus the prohibited anchors — see F6). Match the line density of `cula_idle_front.png` exactly: hard 1-pixel outlines, flat-fill body, two-color shadow at most. The route_blocker should be readable as a man-in-overalls, not a man-painted-in-pixels.
**Severity:** S1

### F6 — Every PROMPT.txt still contains a pre-rejected style anchor
**Flaw:** Every PROMPT.txt under `godot/art/sprites/{cula,mr_pig,mr_swine,murrow,crab,whimsy,asia,halina,sikorska}/` contains the literal string "Polish satirical illustration" as part of its style anchor. This phrasing was rejected — the brief is neutral-institutional rendering of a satirical premise, not caricatured satirical rendering. The PROMPT files were last touched 2026-05-11 and have not been updated since the rejection.
**Why this fails:** Every future regeneration pass will re-bake the rejected aesthetic into the result. The PROMPT files are load-bearing: they're the seed contract Pixellab honors. As long as "Polish satirical illustration" is in the prompt, the generator will push toward broad-strokes caricature on each regen, undoing the neutral-institutional brief before the cleanup pass even begins.
**Remediation:** Bulk find-replace across `godot/art/sprites/*/PROMPT.txt`: remove "Polish satirical illustration, " from the Prompt: line. Replace with "neutral institutional pixel-art illustration, " or similar non-caricatural framing. Re-pin the seed and Pixellab Character ID on each file so future regens are reproducible. Verify CONVENTIONS.md §World sprite generation rules — its "Style anchor" block on line ~312 also contains "Polish satirical illustration" and must be updated in parallel, otherwise the next agent regenerates from the canon doc instead of the PROMPT.txt and reintroduces the anchor.
**Severity:** S1

### F7 — Halina and Sikorska are the same character authored twice
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/halina/PROMPT.txt` and `godot/art/sprites/sikorska/PROMPT.txt` contain identical Prompt: text ("Elderly slim upright woman, short gray hair, beige cardigan, cream blouse, navy skirt, dignified stiff posture..."). The character is Halina Sikorska. The `sikorska/` folder contains only `PROMPT.txt` and `sikorska_sprite_frames.tres` — no PNGs — while `halina/` contains the full 8-facing idle set. The `sikorska_sprite_frames.tres` resource points to nothing renderable.
**Why this fails:** Two folders, one character, one set of art, one orphaned `.tres` that any scene loading it will fail to render. The split signals to future agents that there are two distinct NPCs and may produce a phantom Sikorska sprite generation pass against the same prompt — duplicate work, certain drift.
**Remediation:** Delete `godot/art/sprites/sikorska/` entirely. Grep the project for `sikorska_sprite_frames.tres` and `art/sprites/sikorska` — every reference should point at `art/sprites/halina/halina_sprite_frames.tres` instead. Add one line to CONVENTIONS.md or godot/AGENTS.md cast section: "Halina Sikorska is one character; the canonical sprite folder is `art/sprites/halina/`."
**Severity:** S2

### F8 — Whimsy's mismatched mustard tie is invisible
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/whimsy/PROMPT.txt` specifies "eggplant velvet blazer, mismatched mustard tie, swept-back dark hair, theatrical posture." `godot/art/sprites/whimsy/whimsy_idle_front.png` shows a uniformly eggplant 3-piece suit — blazer, trousers, vest all the same purple — and no visible tie color contrast. The "mismatched" silhouette signal that distinguishes Whimsy at thumbnail scale from a generic dark-suited man is absent.
**Why this fails:** style_canon.txt §Whimsy explicitly calls the eggplant blazer + mustard contrast the visual marker that survives downscaling. Whimsy is meant to read as theatrical-defiance even at 112×112 — the mustard against the eggplant is how. The current sprite reads as a slim man in monochrome purple, which at scale is indistinguishable from any other slim suited figure. The "one attribute distinguishes the silhouette" rule the Świdziński direction depends on is violated.
**Remediation:** Regenerate Whimsy with the tie color escalated in the prompt: "thin mustard yellow #c8a868 tie, contrasting hard against eggplant blazer, tie visible across entire torso." Inspect the front, front_left, and front_right facings at 100% zoom and verify the tie occupies at least 4 contiguous pixels of mustard at the chest. If the back facings hide the tie naturally, that is correct.
**Severity:** S2

### F9 — Pig & Swine office floor zones are debug overlays shipped in production
**Flaw:** `godot/scenes/interiors/pig_swine_office.tscn` `FloorZones` node contains six semi-transparent ColorRect children: `MeetingFloor` (green tint 24% alpha), `PigOfficeFloor` (purple 22%), `DesksFloor` (blue 22%), `FilesFloor` (deeper blue 26%), `HallFloor` (warm brown 32%), `ReceptionFloor` (tan 26%). They sit at z_index = -19, above the marble tile floor at z_index = -20. The result: the marble tilework reads through a patchwork of overlapping colored haze.
**Why this fails:** These are designer's-eye zone markers — useful for blocking out which area is which during authoring. They are not authored lighting. Real environmental lighting in a top-down 2D scene would be a single lighting pass with a CanvasModulate or a Light2D, not six semi-transparent rectangles abutting each other in a grid. The seams between rectangles are visible. The marble tile work the project paid for is masked by them.
**Remediation:** Either (a) delete all six FloorZones ColorRects — the marble TileMap floor is the ground truth and the room reads as one connected space — or (b) replace them with a single CanvasModulate at the room root that warm-tints the whole interior, plus targeted Light2D nodes for the lamps (`desk_lamp.png` is already a referenced asset). If zone identification is needed for design legibility, do it with rug Sprite2D children (real rug textures) at specific zone centers, not with full-area color rects.
**Severity:** S2

### F10 — Office prop filenames use forbidden cast names
**Flaw:** `godot/art/props/office/desk_rak.png`, `godot/art/props/office/desk_wymysl.png`, `godot/art/props/office/desk_kula.png` are referenced from `godot/scenes/interiors/pig_swine_office.tscn`. AGENTS.md §Cast canonical names explicitly forbids "Rak", "Wymysl", "Kula" in code identifiers and JSON keys, and the CONSISTENCY_AUDIT.md flagged this exact category of drift (B4) for the world.txt narrative.
**Why this fails:** Asset path is part of the project's identifier surface. Every grep over the project for "Crab" or "Whimsy" or "Cula" misses these desks. Any future Code agent renaming or relocating desks against the canonical names will not find them. This is governance debt with a tested precedent — the same audit that caught "Rak" in world.txt missed these because the audit covered .txt and .md files, not .png paths.
**Remediation:** Rename: `desk_rak.png → desk_crab.png`, `desk_wymysl.png → desk_whimsy.png`, `desk_kula.png → desk_cula.png`. Update the three `ExtResource` paths in `pig_swine_office.tscn` and any `.import` files. Run the smoke test (`godot --headless --path . --script tests/test_smoke.gd`) afterward to verify scene load.
**Severity:** S2

### F11 — Building sprites in office_street use fractional non-integer scale
**Flaw:** `godot/scenes/world/routes/office_street.tscn`: the `Building` node sets `scale = Vector2(0.7515, 0.7515)` on `ps_paragraf_facade.png`. `BuildingBooksLegal` sets `scale = Vector2(0.8188, 0.8188)`. The kamienica buildings follow with similar fractional ratios. Texture filter is nearest-neighbor per CONVENTIONS.md §Texture filter.
**Why this fails:** Nearest-neighbor sampling at a fractional scale produces uneven pixel ratios — some source pixels span 1 screen pixel, others span 2, in arbitrary patterns. The result is moiré on regular features (windows, brick courses, signage), visible at every distance. CONVENTIONS.md §Canonical numbers calls for pixel-perfect rendering; that promise dies the moment a Sprite2D ships at scale 0.7515.
**Remediation:** Re-author each building texture at the correct native size — generate `ps_paragraf_facade.png` directly at the dimensions it needs to be on screen (multiply the current pixel dims by 0.7515 and re-export from the source). The runtime Sprite2D `scale` must read exactly `Vector2(1, 1)`. If the building must size-flex per camera distance, snap scale to integer ratios (0.5, 1.0, 2.0) only.
**Severity:** S2

### F12 — No project-wide character palette is being enforced
**Flaw:** Every PROMPT.txt declares an individual 5–6 color "Palette:" line, and the lists drift: Cula `#1c2a40, #7a1f2a`; Crab `#1c2a40, #7a1f2a, #6a4a30`; Murrow `#1c2a40, #6a4a30, #d4b878, #e6b08a`; Whimsy `#1c2a40, #5a2a4a, #c8a868`; Asia `#1c2a40, #c8a868, #e8e4d8`; Halina `#1c2a40, #d4b878, #9a9088`. CONVENTIONS.md §Shared character colors lists 8 "palette-safe" hexes; the prompts don't cite that table by name, they re-pick. Skin tone `#e6b08a` (Warm Skin) appears in Murrow and Crab but not in Cula's prompt — Cula has no skin-tone hex listed at all, and the rendered Cula face is a different pink-tan than Crab's. The shared character palette exists on paper and is not being enforced at the prompt-authoring layer.
**Why this fails:** CONVENTIONS.md §Palette discipline promises the world looks like one game by sharing skin, shadow, paper-tone, and frame palettes. When each PROMPT enumerates a private palette without referencing the shared table, the generator drifts on a per-character basis and the cast looks like a sticker collection at scene scale. The drift compounds with each regeneration pass.
**Remediation:** Pull skin tone `#e6b08a` and outline `#1a1410` + `#0d0a08` into a fixed leading block of every human-character PROMPT.txt's Palette line, in identical order. Each character then gets exactly 2 character-specific accent hexes after the shared block. For Cula specifically: add `#e6b08a` skin and a defined hair color (Cula's "dark / near-black" per style_canon.txt §Dr. A. Cula needs a hex; pick `#1a1410` for hair, distinct from outline). Document the per-character accent table in CONVENTIONS.md so any future regen pulls from the same source.
**Severity:** S2

### F13 — Cula back-view legacy folder is still on the active sprite tree
**Flaw:** `godot/art/sprites/cula/walk/_alt/back_left_v2/` contains six 92×92 PNGs of an earlier back_left walk pass. Per CONVENTIONS.md §Current divergences, this is acknowledged legacy material. The active `godot/art/sprites/cula/walk/back_left/` is the 124×124 canonical set. The `_alt` folder remains live on disk inside the production sprite tree.
**Why this fails:** An agent doing a glob search like `godot/art/sprites/cula/walk/back_left/**/*.png` will hit both folders. The `_alt` files are smaller-resolution and will round-trip into a Pixellab regen pass or a sprite_frames re-import if anyone treats them as canonical. Legacy material inside production trees is a slow-leak corruption source.
**Remediation:** Move `godot/art/sprites/cula/walk/_alt/` to a top-level `_legacy/cula_walk_v2/` outside the active `art/sprites/` tree. Update CONVENTIONS.md §Current divergences to note the move. Same treatment for the `godot/art/sprites/new/` folder, which contains a `cula/states/DR._A._CULA/sitting_down` subtree — that is either canonical (in which case it belongs at `art/sprites/cula/sitting/`) or legacy (in which case it belongs in `_legacy/`).
**Severity:** S3

### F14 — Camera and player-sprite offsets do not enforce a consistent feet-origin
**Flaw:** `pig_swine_office.tscn` Player has `position = Vector2(0, 24)` on its CollisionShape2D and `position = Vector2(0, -8)` on its `Visual` AnimatedSprite2D. `cafe_paragraf.tscn` and `archive_room.tscn` repeat the same pair of offsets verbatim. CONVENTIONS.md §Y-sort and Sprite2D origin convention prescribes `offset.y = -(texture_height / 2)` — `-56` for a 112×112 sprite, `-62` for a 124×124 sprite, `-32` for a 64×64. None of the three offsets match any of those values.
**Why this fails:** When the prescribed formula is not followed, Y-sort orders by node origin rather than figure feet; characters render in front of or behind props they should not. This is the exact failure mode `tests/test_ysort_canon.gd` was written to catch — yet the test (per CONVENTIONS.md) checks Sprite2D, while the player uses AnimatedSprite2D, which may bypass the assertion.
**Remediation:** In each room scene, set the Player Visual's `offset` (or per-frame anchor in the SpriteFrames resource) to `-(actual_texture_height/2)`. Read the PNG header to pick the value; do not hardcode. Extend `tests/test_ysort_canon.gd` to assert the rule on AnimatedSprite2D children as well. Run the test against `pig_swine_office.tscn`, `cafe_paragraf.tscn`, `archive_room.tscn`, `office_street.tscn`.
**Severity:** S3

## Standing concerns
The PROMPT.txt files claim Pixellab Character IDs (`94b17c50-...`, `758ed0ea-...`, etc.) per character. Pixellab honors seeded character IDs for consistency across regeneration passes. The drift across Asia's facings indicates the seed lock is being broken between passes — either the ID is being lost, or the prompt is being edited between facings, or both. Before any further regeneration, confirm that running the same prompt + ID + seed produces identical output across two consecutive sessions. If not, the consistency contract Pixellab promises does not hold for this project and a different generator (or a manual cleanup discipline budget) is needed.

The canon doc disagrees with itself on sprite resolution (124×124 in §Canonical numbers, 64×64 in §Current divergences). That contradiction is the upstream cause of three findings above. Resolve it as a documentation pass — one number, one place, the rest deleted — before authorizing any regeneration sprint.
