### F2 â€” Two competing schemas exist for Chapter 1, Round 1
**Flaw:** `godot/data/court_rounds/` contains both `chapter1_round_1.json` (court_clerk + resident_no_7 witnesses, motion-packet model, 1000 lines) and `ch1_round1_halina_examination.json` (Halina-as-witness, four-press model, witness_cooperation_budget 10, 97 lines). **Why this fails:** A new agent or contributor reading the directory cannot tell which file the controller loads. The orphan presents a competing design (Halina as her own witness, no court clerk) that contradicts the canonical model (a Polish district-court file-jacket inspection conducted before the defendant testifies). Source-of-truth ambiguity is worse than missing content.
**Remediation:** Delete `godot/data/court_rounds/ch1_round1_halina_examination.json`. If any of its English prose is reusable, lift the four press questions into `chapter1_round_1.json` `resident_no_7.statements[].press_options[].follow_up_text` before deletion. **Severity:** S2

### F3 â€” Phase 1 â†’ Phase 2 carry-over is mostly cosmetic
**Flaw:** `chapter1_round_1.json::phase_2_closing.judge_counter_questions` has three counter-questions. Only `jq_third_party_cure` carries `requires_fact_flags: ["_fact.resident_no_authority"]`. `jq_article_6_civil` and `jq_remedy_scope` have `requires_fact_flags: []`. The flagship "load-bearing fact-flag carry-over" PROPOSALS.md Â§10 promises â€” the rule that a sloppy Phase 1 makes Phase 2 citations unreachable â€” fires once across the round. **Why this fails:** This is exactly the "flashcards" failure mode Â§10 names as the reason the two-phase split exists. If the player cannot lose Phase 2 access by mishandling Phase 1, the split is presentational. The schema is doing the work; the data is opting out.
**Remediation:** Add `requires_fact_flags` to every Phase 2 counter-question whose underlying fact is established in Phase 1. `jq_article_6_civil` requires `_fact.notice_received_april_28` (it asks whether civil-side Article 6 still has procedural bite â€” the actual-notice date is the answer). `jq_remedy_scope` requires `(_fact.renumbering_2015_documented || _fact.landlord_knew_address)` â€” the remedy is calibrated to the procedural defect that was established. **Severity:** S2

### F4 â€” `motion_to_set_aside` cites with zero fact prerequisites
**Flaw:** `chapter1_round_1.json::phase_2_closing.judge_counter_questions[0].available_citations[0]` is `motion_to_set_aside` with `requires_fact_flags: []` and `judicial_patience_delta_on_hit: 1`. The motion to set aside is the named target of the entire packet-assembly metaphor (PROPOSAL_player_driven_argument.md Â§1.1). A player who establishes zero facts in Phase 1 can still cite the motion and gain patience.
**Why this fails:** The four-element packet metaphor (non-current address; landlord knowledge; timely actual-notice motion; no third-party cure) is the chapter's stated synthesis. If the canonical citation that names that motion does not require any of the four element flags, the metaphor lives only in `victory_resolution.branches[]` predicates. Mid-round, the player's choices read identical whether they assembled the packet or not.
**Remediation:** Set `motion_to_set_aside.requires_fact_flags` to at least `["_fact.renumbering_2015_documented", "_fact.notice_received_april_28"]` (two of four elements). Add a second-tier citation `motion_to_set_aside_full` that requires all four elements and grants `judicial_patience_delta_on_hit: 2`. Now the citation list itself surfaces packet quality at the moment of play.
**Severity:** S2

### F5 â€” `victory_resolution.evaluation_order` makes `narrow_victory` unreachable in the common decoy path
**Flaw:** `chapter1_round_1.json::phase_2_closing.victory_resolution.evaluation_order` is `["strong_victory", "standard_victory", "narrow_victory", "blunder_recovered"]`. `narrow_victory.when` is `"primary_fact_count >= 2 || chapter1.proposed_frame != 'defective_service_135bis'"`. `standard_victory.when` is `"primary_fact_count >= 2 && judicial_patience >= 2"`. A player who established 2+ facts and kept patience â‰Ą 2 lands in `standard_victory` regardless of whether they proposed a decoy frame; `narrow_victory` is unreachable for that case. The OR-clause on `proposed_frame != 'defective_service_135bis'` is silent in the canonical evaluation path. `blunder_recovered.when` includes `chapter1.decoy_incapacity == true` â€” but if a player who filed incapacity established four facts and held patience â‰Ą 2 (possible if `icy_silence` drain math is gentle), `standard_victory` still resolves first.
**Why this fails:** Wins should classify by what the player actually did, not by which predicate the evaluator hits first. The current ordering hides decoy choice behind a more permissive earlier branch.
**Remediation:** Reorder to evaluate the blunder branch first: `["blunder_recovered", "narrow_victory", "standard_victory", "strong_victory"]` and rewrite predicates as exclusive. `blunder_recovered` becomes `decoy_incapacity == true`; `narrow_victory` becomes `primary_fact_count >= 2 && decoy_count >= 2`; `standard_victory` becomes `primary_fact_count >= 2 && decoy_count <= 1 && judicial_patience >= 2 && !decoy_incapacity`; `strong_victory` becomes `primary_fact_count == 4 && decoy_count == 0 && judicial_patience >= 4`. First-match becomes legible.
**Severity:** S2

### F6 â€” Halina trust meter is a single boolean dressed in eleven values
**Flaw:** `state.gd::chapter1.halina_trust` (int) accumulates `trust_delta` across `halina.json` choices, gates a reveal at `>= 5`, and feeds the `halina_trust_delta_on_select` per-decoy values in `argument_frames_ch1.json` (0, -1, -1, 0, -4). There is no in-fiction surface during the meeting that shows the meter moving. The reveal at 5 is a binary outcome (landlord_tip surfaces or it does not). The four trust-relevant states (intact / cooled / strained / cold) compress an integer into four states that drive at most one branch each.
**Why this fails:** A points-counter the player cannot see is a points-counter pretending to be drama. The trust meter is justified by the Tokarczuk-Kundera literary register (PROPOSAL Â§11), but Cula's observer perspective requires the player to *read* the relationship moment-to-moment, not retroactively. Either the meter is a visible diegetic affordance (Halina's posture, her sentence rhythm, her gaze tracking) authored as portrait/line variants the player perceives at the moment of choice, or it is three booleans (`trust_intact`, `trust_strained`, `trust_cold`) and lose nothing.
**Remediation:** Pick one. If diegetic: write three Halina portrait variants and 6â€“9 line variants gated on the trust value at each choice; the integer is not surfaced as a number but every cost is read at the moment paid. If reduced: replace the int with the three-bool set, migrate at next save bump, and audit the decoy `halina_trust_delta_on_select` values to map to bool transitions (`-1 â†’ strained`, `-4 â†’ cold`). The current "invisible meter with paper consequences" shape gives the player neither a legible meter nor reliable diegesis.
**Severity:** S2

### F7 â€” Decoy round-burn is real, in-fiction signal is absent
**Flaw:** Four of five decoys in `argument_frames_ch1.json` carry `burns_round_attempt: true`. The chapter has three court rounds (round_1_open / round_2_open / round_3_open). Two decoys consume the round budget; three end the round system. The decoys are surfaced inside NPC dialogue states (`crab.json`, `murrow.json`, `whimsy.json`) framed as "include this?" â€” there is no diegetic cue that "include the standing theory" is the same action as "burn a court round."
**Why this fails:** Severe costs without legible signals produce frustration the player attributes to the system, not to their choice. The juniors are pitching these decoys in dialogue; the player has no reason to know the pitch is a trap of round-burn scale. The chapter never forks into "Halina is evicted" (PROPOSAL Â§9), which is correct policy â€” but the player who burned three rounds on three decoys still gets the bench_initiative win, which makes the round-burn invisible at outcome time too.
**Remediation:** Either reduce the cost (decoys cost patience, do not burn round attempts) or make the cost diegetic at the choice point. Add a Cula internal beat after each decoy-include option that names the procedural cost ("this would commit a round attempt â€” Murrow's blessing or my gut?"). Add a Murrow caution line on the second decoy include. The cost stays harsh; the signal becomes load-bearing.
**Severity:** S2

### F8 â€” `pressure_strength_tags` collapse the taxonomy to canned dictionaries

## Standing concerns

The repo's proposal-to-ship ratio is upside-down. Eleven PROPOSALS.md entries; PROPOSAL_player_driven_argument.md on v3; PROPOSAL_mechanical_depth_2026-05-18 layering four more items. Meanwhile the canonical Phase 1 round file is `DESIGN_TODO` everywhere it matters and no stranger has played the chapter. Iteration on paper is cheaper than iteration in play; the project keeps choosing the cheaper one. A stranger-playtest gate on every new proposal (no new proposal lands until the previous one is shipped and someone-not-the-author has played it) would surface which proposals were solving real problems.

The "soft failure + modest remedy + Halina always wins" canon (PROPOSAL Â§9) is intentional and correct for the literary register the project is reaching for. It also means the load-bearing Casebook engine must produce dramatic stakes from texture alone â€” different victory grades, different judge tone, different Day-One Summary, different corridor sighting in Ch4. None of the current authoring delivers that texture at production density (every `victory_resolution.branches[].result_text` is `DESIGN_TODO`). A Kundera ending depends on the player feeling the gradient between strong/standard/narrow/blunder-recovered. The data structure for the gradient exists; the prose that earns it does not.
