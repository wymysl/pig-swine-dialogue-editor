# Pig & Swine Design Bible

## North Star

*Dr. A. Kula and the Pig & Swine Crisis* is a charming retro isometric legal RPG about Dr. A. Kula trying to save the chaotic Warsaw law firm Pig & Swine through friendship, Polish law, courts, and human rights.

The game should feel like: **Pokémon Red structure + Ace Attorney logic + EarthBound humor + Warsaw kancelaria satire**.

The player fantasy: **be a clever, stressed, principled legal adventurer surviving absurd bureaucracy with real friends**.

## Taste Standard

Every change must improve at least one:

1. Make the player laugh.
2. Make the player feel clever.
3. Make the world feel alive.
4. Make objectives clearer.
5. Make future content easier to create.

Prefer a better small game over a bigger prototype.

## Core Characters

### Dr. A. Kula
- Player character.
- Clever, principled, slightly overwhelmed.
- Learns to combine facts, law, human rights, and friendship.

### Mr. Pig
- Patron / quest giver / financial panic engine.
- Dramatic, anxious, grandiose, not evil.
- Recurring line shape: justice is noble, but rent is immediate.
- Tracks firm liquidity and main plot stakes.

### Mr. Swine
- Absent partner skiing in Japan.
- Appears via postcards, emails, expenses, rumors.
- Cheerful, evasive, luxuriously irresponsible.
- Running joke and possible mystery source.

### Muraś
- Guide / tutorial / keeper of firm lore.
- Calm, practical, dryly funny.
- Knows how Pig & Swine actually works.
- Explains mechanics without becoming bland.

### Rak
- Real friend; fact and evidence specialist.
- Sharp, observant, skeptical.
- Helps identify contradictions and missing evidence.

### Wymysl
- Real friend; strategy and argument specialist.
- Inventive, ironic, a little chaotic.
- Helps construct legal theories and human rights angles.

## Quest Formula

Every quest should follow:

**Hook → Investigation → Choice → Consequence → Payoff**

Every quest needs one clever moment:
- spot contradiction
- match evidence to argument
- choose correct legal basis
- prioritize deadline
- persuade NPC
- distinguish useful facts from nonsense

Every failure should be funny and recoverable when possible.

## Legal Mechanics

Legal gameplay should be simplified but respectful. Use three mechanics:

1. **Issue Spotting** — identify the legal/human rights problem from facts.
2. **Evidence Matching** — choose which document/fact supports the argument.
3. **Court Argument Choice** — select a legal argument from plausible/funny options.

Wrong answers should entertain, not just punish.

## Humor Rules

Humor must be specific, legal/bureaucratic, and character-based.

Running jokes:
- Mr. Swine skiing in Japan.
- Pig & Swine’s financial crisis.
- Printer as final boss.
- Court clerk as gatekeeper of reality.
- Coffee as legal fuel.
- “Simple case” means disaster.
- Missing annexes.
- Human rights memo appearing in absurd places.
- Muraś knows too much.

Cut jokes that do not reveal character, reward exploration, clarify the world, or make failure enjoyable.

## UI Aesthetic

Retro RPG + legal stationery.

Preferred labels:
- Quest Log → **Docket**
- Inventory → **Case Bag**
- Money → **Firm Liquidity**
- Reputation → **Bar Reputation**
- Stress → **Procedural Anxiety**

## Visual Direction

Pixelated, readable, warm, Warsaw autumn/rain, law-office clutter. Visual hierarchy:

1. Dr. A. Kula
2. interactables
3. objective areas
4. obstacles
5. decoration

Never add detail that harms readability.

## Autonomous Development Rule

Each loop: PLAY → DIAGNOSE → PRIORITIZE → SPEC → TEST FIRST → IMPLEMENT → VERIFY → CURATE → DOCUMENT → NEXT.

Always append build notes. Always keep a playable build.
